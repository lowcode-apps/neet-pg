# ANTEPARTUM HEMORRHAGE (APH) - NEET PG Study Material

## Definition
**APH**: Bleeding from or into the genital tract **after 20 weeks** (or 28 weeks - varies) **before delivery**

**Incidence**: 2-5% of pregnancies

---

## CLASSIFICATION

### Major Causes (Placental)
1. **Placenta previa** (30%)
2. **Placental abruption** (30%)

### Minor Causes (20%)
- **Vasa previa**
- **Marginal sinus rupture**
- **Cervical causes**: Polyp, ectropion, carcinoma
- **Vaginal causes**: Trauma, infection
- **"Show"** (bloody mucus plug - normal labor onset)

### Unexplained (20%)

**NEET PG**: Placenta previa & Abruption = 60% of APH

---

## PLACENTA PREVIA - HIGH YIELD

### Definition
**Placenta implanted in lower uterine segment**, covering or near internal os

---

### Classification (by location)

**Old classification**:
1. **Type I (Low-lying)**: Placenta in lower segment, NOT reaching internal os
2. **Type II (Marginal)**: Placenta reaches but doesn't cover internal os
3. **Type III (Partial)**: Partially covers internal os
4. **Type IV (Complete/Central)**: Completely covers internal os

**New classification** (RCOG):
- **Minor (Low-lying)**: <2 cm from internal os
- **Major**: Covering or ≥2 cm from os

**NEET PG**: Type IV (complete) = Most bleeding, cesarean mandatory

---

### Risk Factors

- **Previous cesarean section** (Most important) - scar tissue ↑ risk
- **Multiparity** (↑ with parity)
- **Previous placenta previa** (4-8% recurrence)
- **Multiple pregnancy**
- **Advanced maternal age** (>35 years)
- **Smoking**
- **Previous uterine surgery** (myomectomy, D&C)
- **Endometrial damage**

**NEET PG**: Previous CS = Strongest risk factor

---

### Clinical Features

**Classic triad**:
1. **Painless** bleeding
2. **Bright red** blood
3. **Recurrent** episodes

**Characteristics**:
- **Onset**: Usually after 28 weeks (more common 3rd trimester)
- **First bleed**: Usually mild ("warning hemorrhage")
- **Provoked by**: Coitus, vaginal examination, labor
- **Shock**: Proportionate to visible blood loss

**Examination**:
- **Soft, non-tender uterus**
- **Malpresentation** common (oblique, transverse, breech - 30%)
- **High presenting part** (placenta prevents engagement)
- **Fetal heart**: Usually normal (unless massive bleed)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Painless** bright red bleeding (vs abruption - painful, dark)
- **NO vaginal examination** until placenta previa excluded (can provoke massive bleeding)

---

### Investigations

**USG** (Transabdominal + **Transvaginal**):
- **Diagnosis**: Placental localization
- **Transvaginal USG**: Gold standard (safe, more accurate)
- **Timing**: 18-20 weeks (anomaly scan), repeat at 32 weeks if low-lying

**Important**: 90% of low-lying placentas at 20 weeks **migrate up** (placental migration)

**MRI**: If posterior placenta, concern for accreta

**NEET PG**:
- **TVS safe** in placenta previa (NOT per vaginal examination)
- **Repeat USG at 32 weeks** if low-lying at 20 weeks

---

### Management

#### Conservative (if bleeding stopped, stable)

**Before 37 weeks**:
- **Admit & observe**
- **Bed rest** (until bleeding stops)
- **IV access**, **Blood grouping & cross-match**
- **Tocolytics** (if uterine contractions)
- **Corticosteroids** (if <34 weeks for fetal lung maturity)
- **Iron supplementation** (correct anemia)
- **Monitor fetus**: NST, Doppler
- **Aim**: Prolong pregnancy to 37 weeks

**Criteria for discharge**:
- No bleeding for 48-72 hours
- Hemoglobin stable
- Fetal well-being confirmed

#### Delivery

**Indications**:
- **Maternal**: Massive/uncontrolled bleeding, hemodynamic instability
- **Fetal**: Distress, maturity (≥37 weeks)

**Mode**:
- **Cesarean section**: If major placenta previa (covering os)
- **Vaginal delivery**: Possible if minor (low-lying, NOT covering os) & vertex

**Timing**: **Elective CS at 37-38 weeks** if asymptomatic

**NEET PG**:
- **Major placenta previa**: CS mandatory
- **Elective CS at 37-38 weeks**

---

### Complications

**Maternal**:
- **Hemorrhage** (antepartum, intrapartum, postpartum)
- **Placenta accreta/increta/percreta** (↑ risk if previous CS)
- **PPH** (atonic - lower segment doesn't contract well)
- **Hysterectomy** (if uncontrolled bleeding)
- **Shock, DIC**

**Fetal**:
- **Prematurity**
- **IUGR**
- **Malpresentation**
- **Vasa previa** (if velamentous cord insertion)

**NEET PG**: Placenta accreta common with placenta previa + previous CS

---

## PLACENTAL ABRUPTION (Abruptio Placentae) - HIGH YIELD

### Definition
**Premature separation of normally situated placenta** from uterine wall **before delivery**

---

### Classification

**By severity**:
1. **Grade 0 (Asymptomatic)**: Diagnosed after delivery (retroplacental clot)
2. **Grade 1 (Mild)**: Vaginal bleeding, no fetal distress
3. **Grade 2 (Moderate)**: Concealed/revealed bleeding, fetal distress, uterine tenderness
4. **Grade 3 (Severe)**: Severe bleeding, fetal death, maternal shock, DIC

**By type of bleeding**:
- **Revealed** (80%): Blood escapes through vagina
- **Concealed** (20%): Blood trapped behind placenta (more dangerous)

**NEET PG**: Concealed hemorrhage more dangerous (no visible bleeding, hypovolemic shock)

---

### Risk Factors

- **Hypertension** (Chronic HTN, pre-eclampsia) - **Most important**
- **Previous abruption** (10-15% recurrence)
- **Trauma** (RTA, domestic violence, external cephalic version)
- **Smoking, cocaine use**
- **Multiparity**
- **PROM** (Premature rupture of membranes)
- **Thrombophilia** (Factor V Leiden, protein C/S deficiency)
- **Advanced maternal age**
- **Polyhydramnios** (sudden decompression)

**NEET PG**: Hypertension (pre-eclampsia) = Most important risk factor

---

### Clinical Features

**Classic presentation**:
1. **Painful** bleeding (constant pain)
2. **Dark red/clotted** blood
3. **Tender, tense, "woody hard" uterus** (tetanic contractions)
4. **Fetal distress** or death

**Additional**:
- **Abdominal pain** (constant, severe)
- **Back pain** (if posterior placenta)
- **Uterine contractions** (frequent, hypertonic)
- **Shock**: Out of proportion to visible blood loss (if concealed)
- **Couvelaire uterus**: Blood infiltration into myometrium (bluish discoloration)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Painful**, **dark** blood, **tender rigid uterus** (vs previa - painless, bright red, soft uterus)
- **Shock > visible bleeding** (concealed)

---

### Investigations

**Laboratory**:
- **CBC**: Anemia, thrombocytopenia (if DIC)
- **Coagulation profile**: PT, aPTT, fibrinogen, D-dimer
  - **DIC**: ↓ Platelets, ↓ fibrinogen, ↑ PT/aPTT, ↑ D-dimer
- **Renal function**: ↑ Creatinine (acute tubular necrosis)
- **Kleihauer-Betke test**: Feto-maternal hemorrhage (Rh-negative mothers)

**USG**:
- **Often normal** (50% cases - USG insensitive for abruption)
- May show: Retroplacental hematoma, placental thickening
- **Main role**: Exclude placenta previa

**NEET PG**: USG often **normal** in abruption (clinical diagnosis)

---

### Management

**Resuscitation** (ABCD):
- **Airway, Breathing, Circulation**
- **Large bore IV cannulas** (2 lines)
- **Aggressive fluid resuscitation** (crystalloids, blood products)
- **Blood transfusion**: Aim Hb >7 g/dl
- **Correct coagulopathy**: FFP, platelets, cryoprecipitate
- **Monitor**: Vitals, urine output, fetal heart

**Delivery**:
- **If fetus alive**:
  - **Cesarean section** (urgent) - if fetal distress, severe abruption
  - **Vaginal delivery** (expedite) - if mild, no fetal distress, rapid progress
    - **ARM** (artificial rupture of membranes) to ↓ intrauterine pressure
    - **Oxytocin augmentation**
- **If fetus dead**:
  - **Vaginal delivery** (preferred) unless maternal instability
  - **Cesarean** only if uncontrolled bleeding or failed vaginal delivery

**Postpartum**:
- **Active management of 3rd stage** (oxytocin)
- **Monitor for PPH** (atonic uterus)
- **DIC monitoring**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Live fetus + severe abruption**: Emergency CS
- **Dead fetus**: Vaginal delivery (unless uncontrolled bleeding)
- **ARM**: Reduce intrauterine pressure

---

### Complications

**Maternal**:
- **Hemorrhagic shock**
- **DIC** (consumptive coagulopathy)
- **Acute kidney injury** (ATN)
- **Couvelaire uterus**: May need hysterectomy (rare)
- **PPH**
- **Maternal death**

**Fetal**:
- **Fetal distress**
- **Fetal death** (intrauterine)
- **Prematurity**
- **IUGR**
- **Anemia** (feto-maternal hemorrhage)

**NEET PG**: DIC common in severe abruption (fetal thromboplastin release)

---

## VASA PREVIA - RARE but HIGH YIELD

### Definition
**Fetal vessels** (from velamentous cord insertion or accessory lobe) **crossing internal os** ahead of presenting part

---

### Risk Factors
- **Velamentous cord insertion**
- **Succenturiate (accessory) placental lobe**
- **Low-lying placenta**
- **IVF pregnancy**

---

### Clinical Features

**Classic triad**:
1. **Rupture of membranes** (spontaneous or amniotomy)
2. **Painless vaginal bleeding**
3. **Fetal distress** (sudden bradycardia)

**Key**: Bleeding after **rupture of membranes** + **fetal distress**

**NEET PG**: Vasa previa = Bleeding after ROM + sudden fetal distress

---

### Diagnosis

**Antenatal**:
- **Color Doppler USG**: Vessels over internal os
- **Transvaginal USG**: Gold standard

**Intrapartum**:
- **Apt test**: Differentiate fetal vs maternal blood
  - Fetal blood: Pink (resistant to alkali)
  - Maternal blood: Brown (denatures)

---

### Management

**Antenatal**:
- **Hospitalization** at 30-32 weeks
- **Corticosteroids** for lung maturity
- **Elective CS at 34-36 weeks** (before labor/ROM)

**Intrapartum**: **Emergency CS** (if ROM + bleeding)

**NEET PG**:
- **Vasa previa**: Elective CS before labor (prevent rupture)
- **Apt test**: Fetal vs maternal blood differentiation

---

## PLACENTA PREVIA vs ABRUPTION - Comparison

| Feature | Placenta Previa | Placental Abruption |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Bleeding** | **Painless** | **Painful** |
| **Color** | **Bright red** | **Dark red**, clotted |
| **Onset** | Recurrent, small amounts | Sudden, severe |
| **Uterus** | **Soft**, non-tender | **Tender, rigid** (woody) |
| **Shock** | Proportionate to bleeding | **Out of proportion** (if concealed) |
| **Fetal heart** | Usually normal | **Distress** or absent |
| **Lie** | Malpresentation common | Usually normal |
| **USG** | **Diagnostic** (placenta location) | **Often normal** |
| **Risk factor** | **Previous CS** | **Hypertension** |

**NEET PG**: Key differentiator = Pain (absent in previa, present in abruption)

## Practice MCQs

1. **Most common cause of APH?**
   Answer: Placenta previa & Abruption (30% each)

2. **Painless bright red bleeding in 3rd trimester?**
   Answer: Placenta previa

3. **Why avoid PV examination in APH?**
   Answer: Can provoke massive bleeding in placenta previa

4. **Most important risk factor for placenta previa?**
   Answer: Previous cesarean section

5. **Most important risk factor for abruption?**
   Answer: Hypertension (pre-eclampsia)

6. **Concealed hemorrhage more dangerous than revealed?**
   Answer: Yes (shock > visible bleeding, hypovolemia)

7. **Bleeding after ROM + fetal distress?**
   Answer: Vasa previa

8. **Woody hard uterus indicates?**
   Answer: Placental abruption (severe)
