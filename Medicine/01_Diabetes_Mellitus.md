# DIABETES MELLITUS - NEET PG Study Material

## Definition
Metabolic disorder characterized by **chronic hyperglycemia** due to:
- Insulin deficiency (Type 1)
- Insulin resistance (Type 2)
- Both

---

## Classification (ADA 2023)

### 1. TYPE 1 DIABETES (5-10%)
**Pathophysiology**: Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic β-cells
**Age**: Usually <30 years (can occur at any age)
**Presentation**: Acute onset, DKA common
**Body habitus**: Lean
**Insulin**: **Absolute deficiency**
**Ketosis**: Prone
**Antibodies**: **Positive** (GAD, IA-2, Insulin autoantibodies)

**NEET PG**: Anti-GAD antibodies = Type 1 DM

---

### 2. TYPE 2 DIABETES (90-95%)
**Pathophysiology**: Insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency
**Age**: Usually >40 years (increasing in youth)
**Presentation**: Gradual onset, often asymptomatic
**Body habitus**: Obese (80%)
**Insulin**: Resistance ± relative deficiency
**Ketosis**: Resistant (can develop HHS)
**Antibodies**: Negative

**NEET PG**: Most common type, associated with obesity & metabolic syndrome

---

### 3. GESTATIONAL DIABETES (GDM)
**Definition**: Diabetes diagnosed during pregnancy
**Screening**: 24-28 weeks (75g OGTT)
**Risks**: Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycemia, maternal pre-eclampsia

---

### 4. OTHER SPECIFIC TYPES
- **MODY** (Maturity-Onset Diabetes of Young): Monogenic
- **Secondary**: Pancreatitis, Cushing's syndrome, acromegaly, drugs (steroids, thiazides)
- **Pancreatic**: Chronic pancreatitis, hemochromatosis, cystic fibrosis

**NEET PG**: MODY = Young onset, autosomal dominant, no insulin resistance

---

## Diagnostic Criteria (ADA 2023) - HIGH YIELD

Any ONE of the following:

| Test | Criteria | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Fasting plasma glucose** | ≥126 mg/dL | Fasting = No caloric intake for ≥8 hours |
| **2-hour OGTT** | ≥200 mg/dL | 75g glucose load |
| **HbA1c** | ≥6.5% | Reflects 3-month average glucose |
| **Random plasma glucose** | ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms | Polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss |

**Prediabetes** (IFG/IGT):

| Test | Prediabetes | Normal |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Fasting glucose** | 100-125 mg/dL | <100 mg/dL |
| **2-hour OGTT** | 140-199 mg/dL | <140 mg/dL |
| **HbA1c** | 5.7-6.4% | <5.7% |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- HbA1c ≥6.5% = Diabetes
- HbA1c 5.7-6.4% = Prediabetes
- Fasting glucose ≥126 mg/dL = Diabetes
- HbA1c preferred (no fasting required)

---

## Clinical Features

### Classic Triad
1. **Polyuria**: ↑ Glucose → Osmotic diuresis
2. **Polydipsia**: Compensatory thirst
3. **Polyphagia**: Cellular starvation despite hyperglycemia
4. **Weight loss**: Especially Type 1 (catabolism)

### Additional Features
- Blurred vision
- Recurrent infections (UTI, candidiasis)
- Slow wound healing
- Fatigue
- Acanthosis nigricans (Type 2, insulin resistance)

**NEET PG**: Acanthosis nigricans = Marker of insulin resistance

---

## Acute Complications

### 1. DIABETIC KETOACIDOSIS (DKA)

**Triad**:
1. **Hyperglycemia** (>250 mg/dL)
2. **Ketosis** (↑ Ketones in blood/urine)
3. **Metabolic acidosis** (pH <7.3, HCO3 <15)

**Causes**:
- Insulin omission (most common)
- Infection (pneumonia, UTI)
- MI, stroke, pancreatitis
- New-onset Type 1 DM

**Pathophysiology**:
```
Insulin deficiency + ↑ Counter-regulatory hormones
         ↓
    Hyperglycemia + Lipolysis
         ↓
    Ketogenesis (acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate)
         ↓
    Metabolic acidosis
```

**Clinical Features**:
- Polyuria, polydipsia
- Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
- **Kussmaul breathing** (deep, rapid)
- **Fruity odor** (acetone)
- Dehydration
- Altered sensorium

**Investigations**:
- **Hyperglycemia**: >250 mg/dL
- **Anion gap metabolic acidosis**: pH <7.3, ↑ Anion gap
- **Ketones**: Positive (urine/blood)
- **K+**: Initially high (acidosis) → Drops with treatment

**NEET PG**:
- **Kussmaul breathing**: Compensatory respiratory alkalosis
- **Pseudohyponatremia**: Corrected Na+ = Measured Na+ + 1.6 × [(Glucose - 100) / 100]
- **K+ monitoring**: Critical (initially high → drops with insulin)

**Treatment**:
1. **IV fluids**: Normal saline (1 L in 1st hour)
2. **Insulin**: Regular insulin IV (0.1 U/kg/hr)
3. **K+ replacement**: When K+ <5.2 mEq/L (prevent hypokalemia)
4. **Treat precipitating cause**: Antibiotics for infection

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Do NOT give K+ if**: K+ >5.2 mEq/L or oliguria
- **Bicarbonate**: Only if pH <6.9 (controversial)
- **Cerebral edema**: Complication in children (too rapid correction)

---

### 2. HYPEROSMOLAR HYPERGLYCEMIC STATE (HHS)

**Features**:
- **Severe hyperglycemia**: >600 mg/dL
- **Hyperosmolality**: >320 mOsm/kg
- **NO significant ketosis**: pH >7.3
- **Altered sensorium**: Confusion, coma

**Occurs in**: Type 2 DM (elderly)

**NEET PG**: HHS vs DKA:
- HHS: Higher glucose (>600), no ketosis, higher mortality
- DKA: Lower glucose (>250), ketosis present

**Treatment**: Similar to DKA but:
- More fluids (severe dehydration)
- Lower insulin doses (more insulin-sensitive)

---

### 3. HYPOGLYCEMIA
**Definition**: Blood glucose <70 mg/dL

**Causes**:
- Excess insulin/sulfonylureas
- Missed meals
- Exercise
- Alcohol

**Whipple's Triad**:
1. Symptoms of hypoglycemia
2. Low blood glucose
3. Relief with glucose

**Symptoms**:
- **Autonomic**: Sweating, tremor, palpitations, anxiety, hunger
- **Neuroglycopenic**: Confusion, seizures, coma

**Treatment**:
- **Conscious**: 15g oral glucose (3 glucose tablets, juice)
- **Unconscious**: IV dextrose (D50) or IM glucagon

**NEET PG**: Hypoglycemia most common acute complication of DM treatment

---

## Chronic Complications

### Microvascular Complications

#### 1. DIABETIC RETINOPATHY
**Most common cause of blindness** in adults (20-74 years)

**Classification**:

**A. Non-Proliferative (NPDR)**:
- **Mild**: Microaneurysms
- **Moderate**: Microaneurysms + dot-blot hemorrhages + hard exudates
- **Severe**: 4-2-1 rule:
  - Hemorrhages in **4** quadrants
  - Venous beading in **2** quadrants
  - IRMA in **1** quadrant

**B. Proliferative (PDR)**:
- **Neovascularization** (NVD - disc, NVE - elsewhere)
- Vitreous hemorrhage
- Retinal detachment

**Macular Edema**: Leading cause of vision loss

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Screening**: Annual fundoscopy from diagnosis (Type 2), 5 years after diagnosis (Type 1)
- **Treatment**: Laser photocoagulation, anti-VEGF (bevacizumab, ranibizumab)
- **4-2-1 rule**: Severe NPDR (high risk of progression)

---

#### 2. DIABETIC NEPHROPATHY
**Leading cause of ESRD** worldwide

**Stages** (Mogensen):

| Stage | Name | GFR | Albuminuria | Pathology |
|-------|------|-----|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Hyperfiltration | ↑ | - | Glomerular hypertrophy |
| 2 | Silent | Normal/↑ | - | Thickening of GBM |
| 3 | Microalbuminuria | Normal/↓ | 30-300 mg/day | Mesangial expansion |
| 4 | Overt nephropathy | ↓ | >300 mg/day | Nodular glomerulosclerosis |
| 5 | ESRD | ↓↓ | Massive | End-stage kidney |

**Pathology**:
- **Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules**: Nodular glomerulosclerosis (pathognomonic)
- Hyaline arteriolosclerosis (both afferent & efferent)

**NEET PG**:
- **Microalbuminuria**: Earliest clinical sign (30-300 mg/day)
- **ACE inhibitors/ARBs**: Slow progression (reduce intraglomerular pressure)
- **Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules**: Diabetic nephropathy

**Screening**: Annual urine albumin-creatinine ratio

---

#### 3. DIABETIC NEUROPATHY

**A. Peripheral Neuropathy** (Most common)
- **Distal symmetric polyneuropathy**: "Glove & stocking" distribution
- **Symptoms**: Numbness, tingling, burning pain (worse at night)
- **Loss of**: Vibration, proprioception, ankle reflex
- **Complications**: Diabetic foot ulcers, Charcot joint

**B. Autonomic Neuropathy**
- **Cardiovascular**: Resting tachycardia, orthostatic hypotension, silent MI
- **GI**: Gastroparesis, diarrhea, constipation
- **GU**: Erectile dysfunction, neurogenic bladder
- **Sudomotor**: Anhidrosis

**C. Mononeuropathy**
- **Cranial nerves**: CN III (pupil-sparing), CN VI
- **Median, ulnar, peroneal** nerves

**NEET PG**:
- **Loss of ankle reflex**: Earliest sign
- **Treatment**: Gabapentin, pregabalin, duloxetine (for neuropathic pain)
- **CN III palsy**: Pupil-sparing (microvascular, not aneurysm)

---

### Macrovascular Complications

#### 1. Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
- **2-4× risk** in diabetics
- **Silent MI**: Autonomic neuropathy

#### 2. Stroke
- **2-3× risk**

#### 3. Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD)
- Claudication, gangrene
- **Diabetic foot**: Neuropathy + PAD + infection

**NEET PG**: Diabetes = CAD equivalent (same treatment as CAD)

---

## Diabetic Foot - HIGH YIELD

**Wagner Classification**:
- **Grade 0**: No ulcer, high-risk foot
- **Grade 1**: Superficial ulcer
- **Grade 2**: Deep ulcer (tendon/joint)
- **Grade 3**: Abscess/osteomyelitis
- **Grade 4**: Forefoot gangrene
- **Grade 5**: Whole foot gangrene

**Management**:
- Debridement
- Antibiotics (if infected)
- Offloading (avoid weight-bearing)
- Glycemic control
- Revascularization (if PAD)

**NEET PG**: Neuropathy + PAD + infection = Diabetic foot

---

## Treatment

### Type 1 Diabetes
**Insulin** (lifelong)

**Regimens**:
1. **Basal-bolus**: Most physiologic
   - **Basal**: Long-acting (glargine, detemir) - once/twice daily
   - **Bolus**: Rapid-acting (lispro, aspart) - before meals

2. **Premixed insulin**: Fixed ratio (70/30, 50/50)

**NEET PG**: Basal-bolus most physiologic, best HbA1c control

---

### Type 2 Diabetes

**Stepwise Approach**:

**1st Line**: **Metformin**
- Mechanism: ↓ Hepatic gluconeogenesis, ↑ Peripheral glucose uptake
- Advantages: Weight loss, ↓ CV risk, no hypoglycemia
- Adverse effects: GI upset, **lactic acidosis** (rare)
- **Contraindications**: eGFR <30 ml/min, contrast studies, acidosis

**2nd Line** (if HbA1c target not met):
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin)
  - ↓ Glucose reabsorption in kidney
  - Benefits: Weight loss, ↓ CV risk, ↓ HF hospitalization
  - Adverse effects: UTI, genital infections, euglycemic DKA

- **GLP-1 agonists** (Liraglutide, Semaglutide)
  - ↑ Insulin, ↓ Glucagon, ↓ Appetite
  - Benefits: Weight loss, ↓ CV risk
  - Adverse effects: Nausea, pancreatitis

- **DPP-4 inhibitors** (Sitagliptin, Vildagliptin)
  - ↑ GLP-1, ↑ Insulin
  - Neutral on weight, CV

- **Sulfonylureas** (Glimepiride, Gliclazide)
  - ↑ Insulin secretion
  - Adverse effects: **Hypoglycemia**, weight gain

- **Thiazolidinediones** (Pioglitazone)
  - ↑ Insulin sensitivity
  - Adverse effects: Weight gain, edema, **heart failure**, fractures

**3rd Line**: **Insulin** (if inadequate control)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Metformin**: 1st line, contraindicated if eGFR <30
- **SGLT2i**: Preferred if HF or CKD
- **GLP-1 agonist**: Preferred if obesity or ASCVD
- **Sulfonylureas**: Risk of hypoglycemia
- **Pioglitazone**: Contraindicated in heart failure

## Target Goals (ADA 2023)

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **HbA1c** | <7% (individualize) |
| **Fasting glucose** | 80-130 mg/dL |
| **Postprandial glucose** | <180 mg/dL |
| **BP** | <130/80 mmHg |
| **LDL cholesterol** | <100 mg/dL (<70 if CAD) |

**NEET PG**: HbA1c target individualized (stricter in young, relaxed in elderly/comorbid)

## Practice MCQs

1. **Gold standard for DM diagnosis?**
   Answer: HbA1c ≥6.5%

2. **Most common chronic complication?**
   Answer: Diabetic neuropathy

3. **Leading cause of blindness in adults?**
   Answer: Diabetic retinopathy

4. **Leading cause of ESRD?**
   Answer: Diabetic nephropathy

5. **Earliest sign of nephropathy?**
   Answer: Microalbuminuria

6. **1st line drug for Type 2 DM?**
   Answer: Metformin

7. **DKA vs HHS - key difference?**
   Answer: HHS has higher glucose, no ketosis

8. **Pathognomonic for diabetic nephropathy?**
   Answer: Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules
