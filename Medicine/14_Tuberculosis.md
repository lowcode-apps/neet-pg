## Overview & Epidemiology

### Definition
**Tuberculosis (TB)** is a chronic infectious disease caused by **Mycobacterium tuberculosis** (MTB), primarily affecting the **lungs** (Pulmonary TB - 80%) but can affect any organ (Extrapulmonary TB).

### Epidemiology
- **Leading infectious cause of death worldwide** (Before COVID-19)
- **India:** Highest TB burden globally (~27% of global cases)
- **HIV-TB coinfection:** Major problem (TB is leading cause of death in HIV)
- **One-third of world population** has latent TB infection (LTBI)

### Causative Organism

**Mycobacterium tuberculosis Complex:**
- **M. tuberculosis** (Most common - 95%)
- **M. bovis** (Cattle - Milk-borne, Rare now)
- **M. africanum**

**Characteristics:**
- **Acid-Fast Bacillus (AFB)** - Ziehl-Neelsen (ZN) stain
- **Aerobic** (Prefers upper lobes - high O₂)
- **Slow-growing** (Doubling time 15-20 hours)
- **Intracellular** (Survives in macrophages)
- **Cell wall:** High lipid content (Mycolic acid) → Resistance to drying, Acid-fast

---

## Pathogenesis

### Transmission
**Airborne droplet nuclei** (<5 μm) from infected person (Cough, Sneeze, Speak)

**Risk of Transmission:**
- **Sputum-positive pulmonary TB** (Cavitary >> Non-cavitary)
- **Close contact, Prolonged exposure**

### Natural History

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         NATURAL HISTORY OF TB                      │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                    │
│ INFECTION (Inhalation of MTB)                      │
│         ↓                                          │
│         ├─ 90% → PRIMARY TB (Usually asymptomatic) │
│         │         ↓                                │
│         │         ├─ 90% → Latent TB (LTBI)        │
│         │         │         • No symptoms          │
│         │         │         • Not infectious       │
│         │         │         • TST/IGRA positive    │
│         │         │         • 5-10% lifetime risk  │
│         │         │           of reactivation      │
│         │         │                                │
│         │         └─ 10% → Progressive Primary TB  │
│         │                   (Children, HIV)        │
│         │                                          │
│         └─ 10% → Direct progression to disease     │
│                                                    │
│ REACTIVATION (Post-Primary/Secondary TB)           │
│ • From latent TB (5-10% lifetime)                  │
│ • ↑ Risk: HIV, Immunosuppression, Malnutrition    │
│ • Usually PULMONARY (Upper lobes)                  │
│                                                    │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Pathology

**Primary TB (Ghon Complex):**
1. **Ghon focus:** Subpleural granuloma (Usually lower/middle lobe)
2. **Lymphangitis**
3. **Hilar lymphadenopathy**

**Ghon Complex** = Ghon focus + Hilar LN

**Ranke Complex** = Healed Ghon complex (Calcified)

**Secondary (Reactivation) TB:**
- **Upper lobes** (Apical/Posterior segments) - High O₂
- **Cavitation** (Caseous necrosis → Liquefaction → Cavity)
- **Bronchogenic spread** (To other lobes)

**Granuloma:**
- **Caseating granuloma** (Hallmark of TB)
- **Central caseous necrosis** (Cheese-like)
- **Epithelioid cells, Langerhans giant cells**
- **Lymphocytes** (Periphery)

## Pulmonary Tuberculosis

### Classification

**Primary TB:**
- **First infection** with MTB
- **Usually asymptomatic** (Children, Young adults)
- **Lower/Middle lobes**
- **Hilar lymphadenopathy** (Prominent)
- **Pleural effusion** (Common in primary)

**Post-Primary (Reactivation/Secondary) TB:**
- **Reactivation of latent infection** OR **Reinfection**
- **Symptomatic** (Adults)
- **Upper lobes** (Apical/Posterior)
- **Cavitation** (Common)
- **Most contagious** (Sputum-positive, Cavitary)

---

### Clinical Features

**Symptoms:**

**Constitutional ("B Symptoms"):**
- **Fever:** Low-grade, **Evening rise** (Characteristic)
- **Night sweats** (Drenching)
- **Weight loss** (Significant)
- **Anorexia, Fatigue**

**Respiratory:**
- **Chronic cough** (>2-3 weeks) - **Most common symptom**
  - Initially dry, Later productive
- **Hemoptysis** (Blood-streaked sputum)
  - **Massive hemoptysis:** Erosion into bronchial artery (Rasmussen aneurysm)
- **Dyspnea** (Advanced disease)
- **Chest pain** (Pleuritic - if pleural involvement)

**Duration:** **Insidious onset, Chronic course** (Weeks to months)

**Signs:**
- Often **minimal** (Early disease)
- **Fever**
- **Wasting, Cachexia**
- **Crackles** (Apical - Upper lobes)
- **Amphoric breath sounds** (Large cavity)
- **Lymphadenopathy** (Cervical - Scrofula)

---

```
│ • Bronchogenic spread (Tree-in-bud pattern)        │

└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Miliary TB:**
- **Millet-seed** sized nodules (1-3 mm)
- **Diffuse, Bilateral**
- **Hematogenous dissemination**

## Extrapulmonary Tuberculosis

**15-20% of TB cases** (↑ In HIV - up to 50%)

### 1. Tuberculous Lymphadenitis (Most Common EPTB)

**Sites:** **Cervical** (Most common - **Scrofula**)

**Clinical:**
- **Painless, Matted lymph nodes**
- **Cold abscess** (Fluctuant, Non-tender)
- **Sinus formation** (Chronic)

**Diagnosis:**
- **FNAC:** Caseating granulomas, AFB
- **Excision biopsy** (If FNAC non-diagnostic)

---

### 2. Tuberculous Pleural Effusion

**Pathogenesis:**
- **Hypersensitivity reaction** to TB antigens in pleura (NOT direct infection usually)

**Clinical:**
- **Pleuritic chest pain, Dyspnea**
- **Fever**

**Pleural Fluid Analysis:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      TB PLEURAL EFFUSION CHARACTERISTICS           │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Exudate (Light's criteria)                       │
│ • Straw-colored to bloody                          │
│ • Lymphocytic predominance (>50%, Usually >80%)    │
│ • Protein >5 g/dL                                  │
│ • Glucose <60 mg/dL (Low)                          │
│ • LDH elevated                                     │
│ • ADA (Adenosine Deaminase) >40 U/L (Diagnostic)   │
│   - Sensitivity/Specificity >90%                   │
│ • AFB smear: Usually NEGATIVE (Low yield <10%)     │
│ • TB culture: Positive 20-40%                      │
│ • GeneXpert: Sensitivity 20-50% (Lower than sputum)│
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**ADA >40 U/L:** **HIGHLY SUGGESTIVE** of TB pleural effusion

**Pleural Biopsy:**
- **Caseating granulomas** (Definitive diagnosis)
- Higher yield than fluid AFB/culture

---

### 3. Tuberculous Meningitis (TBM)

**Most severe form** of TB (High mortality/morbidity)

**Clinical:**
```
THREE STAGES:
Stage I (Prodromal): 1-3 weeks
  • Low-grade fever, Headache, Malaise
  • Personality changes

Stage II (Meningitic): 2-3 weeks
  • Meningismus (Neck stiffness, Kernig's, Brudzinski's)
  • Cranial nerve palsies (CN VI most common)
  • Confusion, Focal deficits

Stage III (Paralytic): >3 weeks
  • Coma, Seizures
  • Hemiplegia, Paraplegia
  • Decerebrate rigidity
```

**CSF Analysis:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      TB MENINGITIS CSF                             │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Appearance: Clear to cloudy (Xanthochromic)      │
│ • Pressure: Elevated (>250 mm H₂O)                 │
│ • WBC: 100-500/μL (Lymphocytic predominance >50%)  │
│   - Early: PMN may predominate                     │
│ • Protein: Markedly elevated (>100 mg/dL, Often    │
│   100-500 mg/dL)                                   │
│ • Glucose: Low (<45 mg/dL or CSF/Blood <0.5)       │
│ • Chloride: <110 mEq/L (Old teaching - not used)   │
│ • AFB smear: Low sensitivity (10-20%)              │
│   - ↑ Yield: Large volume (6-10 mL), Centrifuge    │
│ • TB culture: Positive 50-80% (Takes 4-8 weeks)    │
│ • GeneXpert: Sensitivity 50-60% (Better than smear)│
│ • ADA: >10 U/L suggestive (Less specific than      │
│   pleural)                                         │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Imaging:**
- **CT/MRI:** Basal meningeal enhancement, Hydrocephalus, Tuberculomas, Infarcts

**Treatment:**
- **ATT** (4 drugs - HRZE) + **Steroids** (Dexamethasone or Prednisolone)
  - **Steroids ↓ mortality/disability** (Proven in TBM)
- **Treat for 12 months** (Longer than pulmonary TB)

**Complications:**
- **Hydrocephalus** (Basal exudate blocks CSF flow)
- **Vasculitis → Infarcts**
- **Cranial nerve palsies** (CN VI most common)
- **SIADH**

---

### 4. Spinal TB (Pott's Disease)

**Most common site of skeletal TB**

**Location:** **Lower thoracic/Lumbar spine** (Most common)

**Pathology:**
- **Vertebral body destruction** (Anterior > Posterior)
- **Disc space narrowing** (Unlike pyogenic - usually preserves disc)
- **Paravertebral abscess** (Cold abscess)
- **Gibbus deformity** (Angular kyphosis)

**Clinical:**
- **Back pain** (Insidious, Progressive)
- **Neurological deficits:** Paraparesis, Paraplegia (Cord compression)
- **Gibbus** (Visible angular deformity)

**Imaging:**
- **MRI:** Gold standard
  - Vertebral body destruction
  - Disc involvement
  - Paravertebral abscess
  - Cord compression

**Diagnosis:**
- **CT-guided biopsy:** AFB, Culture, Histology

**Treatment:**
- **ATT** (Standard 6-9 months, Longer if extensive)
- **Surgery:** If neurological deficit, Spinal instability, Large abscess

---

### 5. Abdominal TB

**Sites:**
- **Intestinal:** Terminal ileum, Ileocecal region (Most common)
- **Peritoneal:** Ascites
- **Lymph nodes:** Mesenteric

**Clinical:**
- **Chronic abdominal pain**
- **Fever, Weight loss**
- **Diarrhea/Constipation**
- **Abdominal mass** (Ileocecal)
- **Ascites** (Peritoneal TB)

**Ascitic Fluid:**
- **Exudate**
- **Lymphocytic** (>250 cells/μL)
- **SAAG <1.1 g/dL** (Exudate)
- **ADA >40 U/L** (Suggestive)
- **AFB smear:** Low yield

**Laparoscopy with Biopsy:** Definitive (Caseating granulomas, AFB)

---

### 6. Genitourinary TB

**Renal TB:**
- **Sterile pyuria** (WBC in urine, Negative bacterial culture)
- **Hematuria**
- **Dysuria, Frequency**
- **IVP:** "Moth-eaten" calyces, Hydronephrosis

**Genital TB:**
- **Females:** Infertility (Endometrial TB), Menstrual irregularities
- **Males:** Epididymo-orchitis (Beaded vas)

---

### 7. Miliary TB

**Hematogenous dissemination** → Multiple organs

**Clinical:**
- **Fever, Night sweats, Weight loss** (Severe)
- **Hepatosplenomegaly**
- **Choroidal tubercles** (Ophthalmoscopy - Pathognomonic)

**CXR:**
- **Miliary pattern** (1-3 mm nodules, Diffuse, Bilateral)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Sputum AFB:** Often negative
- **Blood culture** for MTB (BACTEC)
- **Liver/Bone marrow biopsy:** Granulomas

## Diagnosis

### Microbiological

#### **1. Sputum Smear Microscopy (Ziehl-Neelsen Stain)**

**Technique:**
- **Acid-fast stain** (AFB - Pink/Red rods on blue background)
- **Fluorescent stain** (Auramine-rhodamine)

**Criteria:**
- **≥3 sputum samples** (Spot-Morning-Spot or 2 consecutive morning samples)

**Grading:**
- **1+:** 10-99 AFB in 100 fields
- **2+:** 1-10 AFB per field (50 fields)
- **3+:** >10 AFB per field (20 fields)

**Sensitivity:** 50-60% (Depends on bacillary load - Cavitary >> Non-cavitary)

**Specificity:** High (But can't distinguish MTB from NTM)

**Advantages:** Fast (2 hours), Cheap

**Limitations:**
- **Low sensitivity** (Needs 5,000-10,000 bacilli/mL)
- Cannot distinguish MTB from NTM (Non-tuberculous mycobacteria)
- Cannot detect drug resistance

---

#### **2. Sputum Culture**

**Media:**
- **Liquid:** BACTEC MGIT (Mycobacteria Growth Indicator Tube) - **Faster** (1-3 weeks)
- **Solid:** Lowenstein-Jensen (LJ) medium - **Slower** (4-8 weeks)

**Gold Standard** for diagnosis

**Advantages:**
- **Most sensitive** (100-1000 bacilli/mL)
- **Confirms MTB**
- **Drug Sensitivity Testing (DST)** possible

**Limitations:**
- **Slow** (Weeks)
- Requires biosafety lab

---

#### **3. GeneXpert MTB/RIF (Molecular - Cartridge-Based PCR)**

**Revolutionized TB diagnosis**

**Technique:**
- **Real-time PCR** detects MTB DNA + **Rifampicin resistance** (rpoB gene mutations)

**Advantages:**
- **Rapid:** 2 hours
- **High sensitivity:** 90-95% (Smear-positive), 70-75% (Smear-negative)
- **Detects Rifampicin resistance** (Proxy for MDR-TB)
- **WHO-recommended** initial test (Replacing smear)

**Specimen:** Sputum, CSF, Pleural fluid, Lymph node aspirate

**Limitations:**
- **Cost** (Higher than smear)
- Cannot detect Isoniazid resistance alone
- Lower sensitivity in extrapulmonary TB (CSF ~50-60%)

**Indications (WHO):**
- **All presumptive TB cases**
- **HIV-TB** (Initial test)
- **Suspected MDR-TB**
- **Extrapulmonary TB**

---

#### **4. Line Probe Assay (LPA)**

**Molecular test** for drug resistance

**Types:**
- **First-line LPA:** Detects resistance to Rifampicin + Isoniazid (MDR-TB)
- **Second-line LPA:** Fluoroquinolones + Injectable agents (XDR-TB)

**Advantages:**
- **Faster** than culture DST (1-2 days)

**Specimen:** Sputum (Smear-positive) or Culture isolate

---

### Immunological

#### **1. Tuberculin Skin Test (TST / Mantoux Test)**

**Technique:**
- **Intradermal injection** of **0.1 mL PPD** (Purified Protein Derivative - 5 TU)
- **Volar aspect of forearm**
- **Read at 48-72 hours** (Measure **induration**, NOT erythema)

**Interpretation:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      TST (MANTOUX) INTERPRETATION                  │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ≥5 mm: Positive if                                 │
│   • HIV-positive                                   │
│   • Recent TB contact                              │
│   • CXR suggestive of old TB                       │
│   • Immunosuppressed (Steroids, Transplant)        │
│                                                    │
│ ≥10 mm: Positive if                                │
│   • Healthcare workers                             │
│   • IVDU, Homeless                                 │
│   • Immigrants from high-burden countries          │
│   • Chronic diseases (DM, CKD, Malignancy)         │
│                                                    │
│ ≥15 mm: Positive in                                │
│   • Healthy individuals (Low risk)                 │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Indicates:** **TB infection** (LTBI or Active TB) - Does NOT distinguish

**False Positive:**
- **BCG vaccination** (Wanes over time - Usually <15 mm)
- **NTM infection**

**False Negative:**
- **Anergy** (Severe TB, HIV, Malnutrition, Steroids)
- **Recent infection** (<8 weeks)
- **Very young (<6 months) or very old**

---

#### **2. Interferon-Gamma Release Assay (IGRA)**

**Blood test** measuring IFN-γ release by T cells when exposed to TB antigens (ESAT-6, CFP-10)

**Types:**
- **QuantiFERON-TB Gold**
- **T-SPOT.TB**

**Advantages over TST:**
- **NOT affected by BCG** (More specific)
- **Single visit** (No return for reading)
- **Less subjective**

**Limitations:**
- **Cost** (Higher than TST)
- Cannot distinguish LTBI from active TB
- False negative in immunosuppressed

**Indications:**
- **Latent TB screening** (Especially BCG-vaccinated)
- **Contact tracing**

---

**Chest X-ray:** Described above

**CT Chest:**

## Treatment

### Anti-Tubercular Therapy (ATT)

**Goals:**
- **Cure** TB
- **Prevent relapse**
- **Prevent transmission**
- **Prevent drug resistance**

---

### Treatment Regimens (RNTCP - India)

#### **New Drug-Sensitive Pulmonary TB**

**WHO 2021 Recommendation:**
- **6 months** regimen: **2HRZE / 4HR**
  - **Intensive Phase:** 2 months of HRZE (4 drugs daily)
  - **Continuation Phase:** 4 months of HR (2 drugs daily)

**India (NTEP - National TB Elimination Programme):**
- **Daily regimen** (Preferred): 2HRZE / 4HR (6 months total)
- **Weight-based dosing** (FDC - Fixed Dose Combination tablets)

**Monitoring:**
- **Sputum smear/GeneXpert:** End of intensive phase (2 months), End of treatment
- **LFTs:** Baseline, Monthly (if symptomatic)

**Treatment Success:** **Cure + Treatment completed** (>85% target)

---

#### **Extrapulmonary TB**

**Most Sites:**
- **Same as pulmonary:** 2HRZE / 4HR (6 months)

**Exceptions (Longer duration):**
- **TB Meningitis:** 2HRZE / 10HR (**12 months total**)
  - **Add Steroids:** Dexamethasone (taper over 6-8 weeks) or Prednisolone
  - Steroids ↓ mortality/disability (Proven)
- **Spinal TB (Pott's):** 2HRZE / 7HR (9 months) to 12 months
- **Bone/Joint TB:** 9-12 months

---

#### **Retreatment TB (Previously Treated)**

**If relapse/failure:**
- **DO NOT use Category II** (2HRZES/1HRZE/5HRE) - **Abandoned due to high failure/MDR**
- **Treat as Drug-Resistant TB** until DST available
- **GeneXpert + Culture DST** (Mandatory)

---

### Directly Observed Therapy (DOT)

**DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course):**
- **Healthcare worker watches patient swallow pills**
- **Improves adherence**
- **↓ Drug resistance**
- **Cornerstone of TB control**

---

### Monitoring & Adverse Effects

**Hepatotoxicity** (Most common serious ADR):
- **Drugs:** H, R, Z (All three)
- **Monitor:** LFTs (Baseline, then if symptoms)
- **Action:**
  - **Transaminases >3x ULN + Symptoms** OR **>5x ULN:** **STOP all hepatotoxic drugs**
  - Restart sequentially when LFTs normalize (R first, then H, then Z)

**Peripheral Neuropathy:**
- **Isoniazid**
- **Prevention:** Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) 10 mg/day (Give to all patients - cheap, safe)

**Optic Neuritis:**
- **Ethambutol**
- **Monitor:** Visual acuity, Color vision (Monthly - especially >2 months)
- **Action:** STOP Ethambutol immediately if symptoms

## Drug-Resistant TB

### Definitions

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      DRUG-RESISTANT TB DEFINITIONS                 │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Mono-resistant: Resistance to 1 drug             │
│                                                    │
│ • Poly-resistant: Resistance to >1 drug (NOT HR)   │
│                                                    │
│ • MDR-TB: Resistance to H + R (± others)           │
│   (MOST IMPORTANT DEFINITION)                      │
│                                                    │
│ • Pre-XDR TB: MDR-TB + Resistance to any           │
│   Fluoroquinolone                                  │
│                                                    │
│ • XDR-TB: Pre-XDR-TB + Resistance to ≥1 Group A   │
│   drug (Levofloxacin/Moxifloxacin, Bedaquiline,   │
│   Linezolid)                                       │
│                                                    │
│ • RR-TB: Rifampicin-resistant (Detected by        │
│   GeneXpert - Treat as MDR)                        │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**MDR-TB = Resistance to at least Isoniazid + Rifampicin**

---

### Risk Factors for MDR-TB

**"PRIOR TB"**
```
P - Previous TB treatment (Biggest risk - Relapse, Failure)
R - Rifampicin resistance on GeneXpert
I - Immunocompromised (HIV)
O - Occupational exposure (Healthcare workers)
R - Residence in high MDR area

T - Treatment failure
B - Bad adherence (Non-compliance)
```

**Suspect MDR if:**
- **Previously treated** (Relapse, Failure, LTFU)
- **Treatment failure** at 4-5 months
- **Contact of MDR-TB** patient
- **HIV-positive**

---

### Diagnosis of DR-TB

**1. GeneXpert MTB/RIF:**
- Detects **Rifampicin resistance** (rpoB mutations)
- **RR-TB = Proxy for MDR-TB** (95% of RR-TB is MDR)

**2. Culture + Drug Sensitivity Testing (DST):**
- **Gold standard**
- **Phenotypic DST:** Tests drug susceptibility (Takes weeks)
- **LPA (Line Probe Assay):** Faster (1-2 days)
  - First-line LPA: H + R resistance
  - Second-line LPA: Fluoroquinolones + Injectables

---

### Treatment of MDR-TB

**WHO 2021 Guidelines:**

**Shorter Regimen (9-12 months) - BPaLM:**
- **Bedaquiline** (B) + **Pretomanid** (Pa) + **Linezolid** (L) + **Moxifloxacin** (M)
- **9-11 months** for non-severe, non-extensive disease
- **All-oral**, Shorter duration

**Longer Regimen (18-24 months):**
- **≥4 effective drugs** for 18-24 months
- **Group A** (Include all 3 if susceptible):
  - **Levofloxacin/Moxifloxacin** (Fluoroquinolone)
  - **Bedaquiline**
  - **Linezolid**
- **Group B** (Add 1-2):
  - **Clofazimine, Cycloserine**
- **Group C** (If needed):
  - Ethambutol, Delamanid, Pyrazinamide, etc.

**Monitoring:**
- **Monthly sputum culture**
- **QTc interval** (Bedaquiline, Fluoroquinolones prolong QT)
- **Audiometry** (Aminoglycosides - if used)
- **Visual acuity** (Linezolid - optic neuropathy)

**Treatment Success:** Lower than DS-TB (~60-70%)

## NEET PG High-Yield Points

### 🔥 MUST KNOW

1. **Most Common Site:** **Lungs** (Pulmonary TB - 80%)

2. **Transmission:** **Airborne droplets** (<5 μm)

3. **Organism:** **Mycobacterium tuberculosis** (Acid-fast bacillus)

4. **Primary TB:**
   - **Ghon complex:** Ghon focus + Hilar lymphadenopathy
   - **Ranke complex:** Healed (Calcified) Ghon complex
   - **Lower/Middle lobes**, **Pleural effusion** common

5. **Reactivation (Secondary) TB:**
   - **Upper lobes** (Apical/Posterior) - High O₂
   - **Cavitation** common
   - **Most infectious** (Sputum-positive, Cavitary)

6. **Most Common Symptom:** **Chronic cough** (>2-3 weeks)

7. **Classical Features:** Fever (Evening rise), Night sweats, Weight loss

8. **Diagnosis:**
   - **Sputum smear** (AFB - ZN stain): Fast, Cheap, Low sensitivity (50-60%)
   - **Sputum culture:** Gold standard, Slow (Weeks)
   - **GeneXpert MTB/RIF:** Rapid (2 hours), Detects MTB + Rifampicin resistance (WHO-recommended initial test)

9. **Treatment (New Drug-Sensitive Pulmonary TB):**
   - **2HRZE / 4HR** (6 months total)
   - **Intensive:** 2 months HRZE (4 drugs daily)
   - **Continuation:** 4 months HR (2 drugs daily)

10. **First-Line Drugs - "HRZE-S":**
    - **H:** Isoniazid - Hepatitis, Peripheral neuropathy (Give Pyridoxine)
    - **R:** Rifampicin - Orange discoloration, Hepatitis, Enzyme inducer
    - **Z:** Pyrazinamide - Hepatitis, Hyperuricemia (Gout)
    - **E:** Ethambutol - Optic neuritis (Red-green color blindness)
    - **S:** Streptomycin - Ototoxicity, Nephrotoxicity

11. **Extrapulmonary TB:**
    - **Most common:** **Lymph node** (Cervical - Scrofula)
    - **TB Meningitis:** 12 months treatment + **Steroids** (Proven benefit)
    - **Pott's Disease:** Spinal TB, Gibbus deformity, Paraplegia

12. **TB Pleural Effusion:**
    - **Lymphocytic exudate**
    - **ADA >40 U/L** (Highly suggestive)
    - **AFB smear:** Low yield (<10%)

13. **TB Meningitis CSF:**
    - **↑ Protein** (>100 mg/dL), **↓ Glucose**, **↓ Chloride**
    - **Lymphocytic** (>50%)
    - **AFB smear:** Low yield (10-20% - ↑ Yield with large volume)

14. **MDR-TB Definition:** **Resistance to H + R** (± others)

15. **GeneXpert:** Detects **Rifampicin resistance** → **RR-TB = Treat as MDR-TB**

16. **Latent TB (LTBI):**
    - **No symptoms**, **Not infectious**
    - **TST or IGRA positive**
    - **5-10% lifetime risk** of reactivation
    - **Treatment:** Isoniazid 300 mg daily × 6-9 months (or Rifampicin 4 months)

17. **DOTS:** Directly Observed Therapy, Short-course (Improves adherence, ↓ Resistance)

18. **Most Common ADR:** **Hepatotoxicity** (H, R, Z)

19. **Miliary TB:** Hematogenous dissemination, Millet-seed nodules (1-3 mm) on CXR, Choroidal tubercles

20. **Tuberculoma:** CNS mass lesion, Ring-enhancing (Target/Bull's eye on MRI)

### Clinical Scenarios

**Scenario 1:** Chronic cough, fever, night sweats, weight loss, upper lobe cavitation on CXR → **Pulmonary TB (Reactivation)**

**Scenario 2:** AFB-positive sputum, start ATT → **2HRZE / 4HR** (6 months)

**Scenario 3:** Painless cervical lymph node, matted, cold abscess → **TB lymphadenitis (Scrofula)**

**Scenario 4:** Lymphocytic pleural effusion, ADA >40 U/L → **TB pleural effusion**

**Scenario 5:** Headache, neck stiffness, cranial nerve palsy, CSF: ↑ Protein, ↓ Glucose, Lymphocytic → **TB meningitis** → Treat **12 months + Steroids**

**Scenario 6:** Back pain, paraplegia, gibbus deformity, vertebral destruction on MRI → **Pott's Disease** (Spinal TB)

**Scenario 7:** GeneXpert shows Rifampicin resistance → **RR-TB** → **Treat as MDR-TB**

**Scenario 8:** Previously treated TB, relapse → **Suspect MDR-TB** → GeneXpert + Culture DST

**Scenario 9:** On ATT, develops red-green color blindness → **Ethambutol-induced optic neuritis** → **STOP Ethambutol**

**Scenario 10:** On ATT, orange discoloration of urine → **Rifampicin** (Normal, Reassure)

- [ ] TB: Airborne, Acid-fast bacillus, Lungs (80%)

### Question 1
A 30-year-old presents with chronic cough, hemoptysis, and weight loss for 2 months. CXR shows right upper lobe cavitation. Sputum is AFB-positive. What is the most appropriate initial treatment?

**A)** 2HRZE / 4HR
**B)** 2HRZES / 1HRZE / 5HRE
**C)** Bedaquiline + Pretomanid + Linezolid + Moxifloxacin
**D)** Isoniazid alone for 9 months

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: A) 2HRZE / 4HR**

**Explanation:**
- **New drug-sensitive pulmonary TB**
- **Standard 6-month regimen:**
  - **Intensive Phase:** 2 months of HRZE (4 drugs daily)
  - **Continuation Phase:** 4 months of HR (2 drugs daily)

**Why not others:**
- **Category II (2HRZES/1HRZE/5HRE):** Abandoned (High failure/MDR rates)
- **BPaLM:** For MDR-TB (No indication here)
- **Isoniazid alone:** For Latent TB, NOT active TB
</details>

### Question 2
A patient with TB pleural effusion has pleural fluid ADA level of 55 U/L. What does this indicate?

**A)** Pyogenic infection
**B)** Malignant effusion
**C)** Highly suggestive of TB
**D)** Heart failure

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: C) Highly suggestive of TB**

**Explanation:**
- **ADA (Adenosine Deaminase) >40 U/L** is **HIGHLY SUGGESTIVE** of TB pleural effusion
- **Sensitivity/Specificity >90%**

**TB Pleural Effusion:**
- **Lymphocytic exudate**
- **ADA >40 U/L**
- **AFB smear:** Low yield (<10%)
- **Pleural biopsy:** Caseating granulomas (Definitive)

**Other causes of ↑ ADA:**
- **Empyema** (But usually neutrophilic, Not lymphocytic)
- **Lymphoma** (But usually <40 U/L)
</details>

### Question 3
A patient with TB meningitis is started on ATT. Which additional medication has PROVEN mortality/disability benefit?

**A)** Pyridoxine
**B)** Corticosteroids
**C)** Aspirin
**D)** Mannitol

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: B) Corticosteroids**

**Explanation:**
- **TB Meningitis:** **Steroids ↓ mortality and disability** (PROVEN in multiple RCTs)
- **Regimen:**
  - **Dexamethasone** (Taper over 6-8 weeks), OR
  - **Prednisolone** (Taper)

**Mechanism:**
- ↓ Inflammation
- ↓ Basal exudate → ↓ Hydrocephalus
- ↓ Vasculitis → ↓ Infarcts

**Treatment:**
- **2HRZE / 10HR** (12 months total) + **Steroids**

**Why not others:**
- Pyridoxine: Prevents peripheral neuropathy (Isoniazid), Does NOT ↓ mortality
- Aspirin, Mannitol: No proven benefit in TBM
</details>
