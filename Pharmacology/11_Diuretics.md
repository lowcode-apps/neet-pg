# DIURETICS - NEET PG Study Material

## DEFINITION

**Diuretics**: Drugs that ↑ urine output by ↑ Na⁺ and water excretion from kidneys

**Natriuresis**: ↑ Na⁺ excretion
**Diuresis**: ↑ Water excretion

---

## NEPHRON PHYSIOLOGY - Brief Review

```
Glomerulus (filtration)
    ↓
Proximal tubule (65% Na+ reabsorption)
    ↓
Loop of Henle (25% Na+ reabsorption)
    ↓
Distal tubule (5% Na+ reabsorption)
    ↓
Collecting duct (2-3% Na+ reabsorption)
```

**NEET PG**: Most reabsorption in proximal tubule (65%)

---

## CLASSIFICATION OF DIURETICS

### By Site of Action

**1. Proximal tubule**:
- Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors (Acetazolamide)
- Osmotic diuretics (Mannitol)

**2. Thick ascending limb (Loop of Henle)**:
- Loop diuretics (Furosemide, Bumetanide, Torsemide, Ethacrynic acid)

**3. Early distal tubule**:
- Thiazides (Hydrochlorothiazide, Chlorthalidone, Indapamide)

**4. Late distal tubule & collecting duct**:
- K⁺-sparing diuretics
  - Aldosterone antagonists (Spironolactone, Eplerenone)
  - ENaC blockers (Amiloride, Triamterene)

---

### By Efficacy

**High-efficacy (High-ceiling)**:
- **Loop diuretics** (can excrete 15-25% filtered Na⁺)

**Medium-efficacy**:
- **Thiazides** (5-10% filtered Na⁺)

**Low-efficacy**:
- **K⁺-sparing**, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, osmotic

**NEET PG**: Loop diuretics = Most potent

---

## LOOP DIURETICS

**Examples**: **Furosemide** (prototype), Bumetanide, Torsemide, Ethacrynic acid

### Mechanism

**Inhibit Na⁺-K⁺-2Cl⁻ cotransporter** in **thick ascending limb** of Loop of Henle

**Effects**:
1. **↓ Na⁺ reabsorption** → Natriuresis
2. **↓ Medullary tonicity** (↓ concentrating ability)
3. **↓ Ca²⁺ reabsorption** → **Hypocalcemia**
4. **↓ Mg²⁺ reabsorption** → **Hypomagnesemia**
5. **↑ K⁺ excretion** → **Hypokalemia**
6. **↑ Renal blood flow** (prostaglandin-mediated)
7. **↑ Renin release** → ↑ Aldosterone

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Loop diuretics cause **hypocalcemia** (vs thiazides → hypercalcemia)

---

### Pharmacokinetics

**Route**: Oral, **IV** (rapid onset)

**Onset**:
- **IV**: 5 minutes
- **Oral**: 30-60 minutes

**Duration**: 2-6 hours (short-acting)

**Excretion**: Renal

**NEET PG**: Loop diuretics rapid onset (5 min IV)

---

### Clinical Uses

**1. Acute pulmonary edema** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **DOC**
- **IV furosemide**
- Rapid venodilation + diuresis

**2. Chronic heart failure**:
- Volume overload
- **Preferred** in severe HF (vs thiazides)

**3. Acute renal failure** (oliguric):
- Convert to non-oliguric (easier to manage)
- **No improvement in survival**

**4. Nephrotic syndrome**:
- Edema
- Often combined with albumin

**5. Cirrhosis with ascites**:
- With spironolactone

**6. Hypertension**:
- **Not first-line** (short duration)
- **Useful in renal impairment** (GFR <30)

**7. Hypercalcemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Causes calciuresis**
- With saline hydration

**8. Hyperkalemia**:
- Emergency (with other measures)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Furosemide = DOC for acute pulmonary edema
- Loop diuretics for hypercalcemia (calciuresis)
- Useful in renal failure (GFR <30)

---

### Adverse Effects

**1. Hypokalemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Most common**
- Can cause arrhythmias
- Prevent: K⁺ supplements, combine with K⁺-sparing diuretic

**2. Metabolic alkalosis**:
- ↑ H⁺ excretion
- **Contraction alkalosis**

**3. Hyponatremia, hypovolemia**

**4. Hypocalcemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- ↑ Ca²⁺ excretion

**5. Hypomagnesemia**:
- ↑ Mg²⁺ excretion

**6. Hyperuricemia** (gout):
- ↓ Uric acid excretion

**7. Ototoxicity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Dose-related**, reversible
- **Ethacrynic acid > Furosemide**
- **Increased risk** with:
  - Aminoglycosides
  - Rapid IV injection
  - Renal failure

**8. Hyperglycemia**:
- ↓ Insulin secretion

**9. Hyperlipidemia**:
- ↑ LDL, ↑ TG

**10. Allergic interstitial nephritis**:
- Sulfonamide allergy (except ethacrynic acid)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Loop diuretics cause hypokalemia, hypocalcemia, ototoxicity
- Ethacrynic acid = Non-sulfonamide (use if sulfa allergy)

---

### Drug Interactions

**1. Aminoglycosides**:
- **↑ Ototoxicity**
- **↑ Nephrotoxicity**

**2. NSAIDs**:
- **↓ Diuretic effect**
- Block prostaglandin-mediated renal vasodilation

**3. Digoxin**:
- Hypokalemia → **↑ Digoxin toxicity**

**4. Lithium**:
- ↓ Lithium excretion → toxicity

**NEET PG**: NSAIDs ↓ loop diuretic efficacy

---

### ETHACRYNIC ACID

**Unique features**:
- **NOT a sulfonamide** (vs other loop diuretics)
- **Use if sulfonamide allergy**
- **More ototoxic** than furosemide

**NEET PG**: Ethacrynic acid = Non-sulfonamide loop diuretic

---

## THIAZIDE DIURETICS

**Examples**: **Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ)**, Chlorthalidone, Indapamide, Metolazone

### Mechanism

**Inhibit Na⁺-Cl⁻ cotransporter** in **early distal convoluted tubule**

**Effects**:
1. **↓ Na⁺ reabsorption** → Natriuresis (moderate)
2. **↑ Ca²⁺ reabsorption** → **Hypercalcemia** (unique)
3. **↑ K⁺ excretion** → **Hypokalemia**
4. **Chronic use**: Vasodilation (main antihypertensive effect)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Thiazides cause **hypercalcemia** (opposite of loop diuretics)

---

### Pharmacokinetics

**Route**: Oral only (NOT IV)

**Onset**: 1-2 hours

**Duration**: 12-24 hours (longer than loop)

**Excretion**: Renal

**NEET PG**: Thiazides oral only, longer duration than loop

---

### Clinical Uses

**1. Hypertension** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **First-line** (along with ACE-I, ARB, CCB)
- Inexpensive, once daily
- **Preferred in**:
  - Elderly
  - Blacks
  - Isolated systolic hypertension

**2. Heart failure**:
- Mild to moderate
- **NOT in severe** (loop diuretics better)

**3. Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Paradoxical effect**
- ↓ Urine volume
- Mechanism: ↓ GFR, ↑ proximal reabsorption

**4. Hypercalciuria, calcium stones** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- ↑ Ca²⁺ reabsorption → ↓ urinary Ca²⁺

**5. Osteoporosis** (prevention):
- ↑ Bone density (↑ Ca²⁺ retention)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Thiazides = First-line for hypertension
- Nephrogenic DI (paradoxical effect)
- Hypercalciuria, calcium stones

---

### Adverse Effects

**1. Hypokalemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Most common**
- Arrhythmias
- Prevent: K⁺ supplements, K⁺-sparing diuretic

**2. Metabolic alkalosis**

**3. Hyponatremia**:
- **More common than with loop** (longer duration)

**4. Hypercalcemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Unique to thiazides**
- Can unmask hyperparathyroidism

**5. Hyperuricemia** (gout):
- ↓ Uric acid excretion

**6. Hyperglycemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- ↓ Insulin secretion
- Worsen diabetes

**7. Hyperlipidemia**:
- ↑ LDL, ↑ TG, ↑ cholesterol

**8. Hypomagnesemia**

**9. Erectile dysfunction**

**10. Sulfonamide allergy**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Thiazides cause hypokalemia, **hypercalcemia**, hyperuricemia, hyperglycemia

---

### Contraindications

- **Renal failure** (GFR <30) - **Ineffective** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Use loop diuretics instead
- Hypokalemia
- Hypercalcemia
- Gout (relative)

**NEET PG**: Thiazides ineffective if GFR <30 (use loop diuretics)

---

### METOLAZONE

**Unique features**:
- **Thiazide-like** diuretic
- **Effective even in renal failure** (unlike other thiazides)
- **Synergistic with loop diuretics** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Sequential nephron blockade**
  - For **refractory edema**

**NEET PG**: Metolazone + furosemide = Refractory edema

---

## POTASSIUM-SPARING DIURETICS

### A. ALDOSTERONE ANTAGONISTS

**Examples**: **Spironolactone**, Eplerenone

---

#### SPIRONOLACTONE

**Mechanism**:
- **Competitive antagonist** of **aldosterone** (mineralocorticoid receptor)
- Blocks Na⁺ reabsorption, K⁺/H⁺ excretion in collecting duct

**Effects**:
- **Weak diuresis**
- **K⁺ retention** (hyperkalemia)
- **Weak natriuresis**

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Primary hyperaldosteronism** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **DOC for treatment**
- **Diagnostic** (trial of spironolactone)

**2. Heart failure** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Systolic HF** (NYHA III-IV)
- **↓ Mortality** (RALES trial)
- Low dose (25-50 mg)

**3. Cirrhosis with ascites** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **DOC** (often first-line)
- **With furosemide** (ratio 100:40 mg)

**4. Resistant hypertension** (with other drugs)

**5. Hirsutism, acne** (women):
- Anti-androgen effect
- PCOS

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Spironolactone = DOC for primary hyperaldosteronism
- ↓ Mortality in heart failure (RALES trial)
- DOC for cirrhotic ascites

---

#### Adverse Effects

**1. Hyperkalemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Most serious**
- **Especially with**: ACE-I, ARBs, NSAIDs, K⁺ supplements, renal failure

**2. Gynecomastia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Anti-androgen effect**
- Binds to androgen receptors
- Common in men (10-20%)

**3. Menstrual irregularities**

**4. Metabolic acidosis**:
- ↓ H⁺ excretion

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Spironolactone causes hyperkalemia, gynecomastia
- Don't combine with ACE-I/ARB (hyperkalemia)

---

#### EPLERENONE

**Selective aldosterone antagonist**:
- **NO anti-androgen effects** (no gynecomastia)
- Otherwise similar to spironolactone

**Uses**:
- Heart failure (post-MI)
- Hypertension

**NEET PG**: Eplerenone = No gynecomastia (vs spironolactone)

---

### B. ENaC BLOCKERS

**Examples**: **Amiloride**, Triamterene

---

#### Mechanism

**Block epithelial Na⁺ channels (ENaC)** in collecting duct
- Independent of aldosterone

**Effects**:
- Weak diuresis
- **K⁺ retention**

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Combination with thiazides/loop** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Prevent hypokalemia**
- Fixed-dose combinations available (HCTZ + Amiloride)

**2. Lithium-induced nephrogenic DI**:
- Amiloride blocks Li⁺ entry (via ENaC)

**3. Liddle syndrome**:
- Genetic ↑ ENaC activity
- Amiloride specific treatment

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Amiloride + thiazide prevents hypokalemia
- Amiloride for lithium-induced DI, Liddle syndrome

---

#### Adverse Effects

**1. Hyperkalemia** (most serious)

**2. Metabolic acidosis**

**3. **Triamterene**: Kidney stones, megaloblastic anemia

---

## CARBONIC ANHYDRASE INHIBITORS

**Example**: **Acetazolamide**

### Mechanism

**Inhibit carbonic anhydrase** in proximal tubule

**Effect**:
- ↓ HCO₃⁻ reabsorption → **Bicarbonate diuresis**
- **Weak diuretic**
- **Causes metabolic acidosis**

---

### Clinical Uses

**1. Glaucoma** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **↓ Aqueous humor production**
- ↓ Intraocular pressure
- Oral or topical (dorzolamide)

**2. Altitude sickness** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Prophylaxis & treatment**
- ↑ Ventilation (metabolic acidosis → respiratory compensation)

**3. Metabolic alkalosis**:
- Correct alkalosis

**4. Epilepsy** (add-on):
- Absence seizures

**5. Pseudotumor cerebri** (idiopathic intracranial hypertension):
- ↓ CSF production

**6. Periodic paralysis** (hypokalemic)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Acetazolamide = Glaucoma, altitude sickness
- Causes metabolic acidosis

---

### Adverse Effects

**1. Metabolic acidosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- ↑ Cl⁻, ↓ HCO₃⁻

**2. Hypokalemia**

**3. Paresthesias** (hands, feet)

**4. Renal stones**:
- Alkaline urine → calcium phosphate stones

**5. Sulfonamide allergy**

**NEET PG**: Acetazolamide causes metabolic acidosis, stones

---

## OSMOTIC DIURETICS

**Example**: **Mannitol**

### Mechanism

**Freely filtered**, **NOT reabsorbed**
- ↑ Tubular fluid osmolality
- **↓ Water reabsorption** (osmotic effect)

**Effects**:
- **Water diuresis** > Na⁺ diuresis
- Initial **plasma expansion** (draws fluid from tissues)

---

### Clinical Uses

**1. Cerebral edema** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **↓ Intracranial pressure**
- **DOC** (along with hypertonic saline)
- Head injury, stroke, tumor

**2. Acute angle-closure glaucoma**:
- **↓ Intraocular pressure** (emergency)

**3. Prevent acute renal failure**:
- Rhabdomyolysis, hemolysis
- **No benefit if established ARF**

**4. Forced diuresis** (drug overdose):
- Salicylate, phenobarbital

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Mannitol = DOC for cerebral edema (↓ ICP)
- Also for acute glaucoma

---

### Route & Monitoring

**Route**: **IV only** (not absorbed orally)

**Monitor**: Serum osmolality (<320 mOsm/kg)

---

### Adverse Effects

**1. Volume overload** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Acute pulmonary edema**
- **Contraindicated in heart failure**
- Initial plasma expansion

**2. Hyponatremia, hypokalemia**

**3. Dehydration** (excessive diuresis)

**4. Headache, nausea**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Mannitol contraindicated in heart failure (volume overload)
- Can cause pulmonary edema

---

## DIURETIC COMBINATIONS

### Synergistic Combinations

**1. Loop + Thiazide** (or metolazone):
- **Sequential nephron blockade**
- **Refractory edema**

**2. Loop (or Thiazide) + K⁺-sparing**:
- **Prevent hypokalemia**
- Commonly used

**3. Spironolactone + Furosemide**:
- Cirrhotic ascites (ratio 100:40)

**NEET PG**: Loop + thiazide = Refractory edema

---

### Avoid

**K⁺-sparing + ACE-I/ARB**:
- **Severe hyperkalemia** risk

---

## DIURETIC RESISTANCE

**Causes**:
1. **Non-compliance**
2. **High Na⁺ intake**
3. **NSAIDs** (↓ efficacy)
4. **Renal failure** (severe)
5. **Hypoalbuminemia** (nephrotic, cirrhosis)

**Management**:
- **Increase dose**
- **Switch to IV** (better bioavailability)
- **Combination** (loop + thiazide)
- **Albumin infusion** (if hypoalbuminemic)
- **Treat underlying cause**

**NEET PG**: Diuretic resistance → Increase dose, combine drugs

---

## HIGH-YIELD COMPARISONS

| Feature | Loop | Thiazide | K⁺-sparing |
|---------|------|----------|------------|
| **Site** | Thick ascending limb | Distal tubule | Collecting duct |
| **Efficacy** | **High** | Moderate | Low |
| **K⁺** | **Hypokalemia** | **Hypokalemia** | **Hyperkalemia** |
| **Ca²⁺** | **Hypocalcemia** | **Hypercalcemia** | - |
| **Uric acid** | ↑ (Hyperuricemia) | ↑ (Hyperuricemia) | - |
| **Renal failure** | **Effective** | **Ineffective** (GFR <30) | Caution |
| **First-line HTN** | NO | **YES** | NO |
| **Heart failure** | **Severe HF** | Mild-moderate HF | Add-on (mortality benefit) |

**NEET PG**: Know Ca²⁺ effects (loop ↓, thiazide ↑)

## Practice MCQs

1. **DOC for acute pulmonary edema?**
   Answer: IV Furosemide (loop diuretic)

2. **Diuretic causing hypercalcemia?**
   Answer: Thiazides

3. **Diuretic for hypercalcemia?**
   Answer: Loop diuretics (cause calciuresis)

4. **Diuretic for nephrogenic diabetes insipidus?**
   Answer: Thiazides

5. **Diuretic ineffective in renal failure (GFR <30)?**
   Answer: Thiazides

6. **DOC for cerebral edema?**
   Answer: Mannitol (osmotic diuretic)

7. **Diuretic contraindicated in heart failure?**
   Answer: Mannitol (causes volume overload)

8. **Diuretic reducing mortality in heart failure?**
   Answer: Spironolactone (aldosterone antagonist)

9. **Diuretic causing gynecomastia?**
   Answer: Spironolactone

10. **Diuretic for glaucoma?**
    Answer: Acetazolamide (carbonic anhydrase inhibitor)
