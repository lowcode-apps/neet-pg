# SKELETAL MUSCLE RELAXANTS - NEET PG Study Material

## CLASSIFICATION

### A. Neuromuscular Blockers (Peripherally Acting)
1. **Non-depolarizing (Competitive)**:
   - Long-acting: Tubocurarine, Pancuronium
   - Intermediate-acting: Atracurium, Vecuronium, Rocuronium
   - Short-acting: Mivacurium
2. **Depolarizing**: Succinylcholine (Suxamethonium)

### B. Centrally Acting Muscle Relaxants
- Baclofen
- Diazepam
- Tizanidine
- Dantrolene (acts on muscle directly)

---

## NEUROMUSCULAR JUNCTION - PHYSIOLOGY

### Normal Transmission

```
Motor nerve action potential
         ↓
   ACh release
         ↓
   ACh binds to nicotinic (NM) receptors
         ↓
   Na⁺ influx → Depolarization
         ↓
   Muscle contraction
         ↓
   ACh hydrolyzed by AChE
```

**Key point**: **TWO** α-subunits of nicotinic receptor must bind ACh for channel opening

**NEET PG**: Neuromuscular blockers act at nicotinic (NM) receptors

---

## NON-DEPOLARIZING MUSCLE RELAXANTS

### TUBOCURARINE - Prototype

**Mechanism**: **Competitive antagonist** at nicotinic (NM) receptors
- Blocks ACh binding
- Prevents depolarization
- **Reversible**

---

#### Pharmacological Actions

**1. Neuromuscular Blockade** (Sequential paralysis):

**Order of paralysis**:
```
Small, rapidly moving muscles FIRST
         ↓
Eyes, face → Fingers → Limbs → Trunk → Intercostals → Diaphragm (LAST)
```

**Recovery order**: **Reverse** (diaphragm recovers first)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Diaphragm paralyzed LAST, recovers FIRST

---

**2. Autonomic Effects**:
- **Ganglionic blockade** (weak)
- **Histamine release** → hypotension, bronchospasm
- No effect on consciousness, pain sensation

**3. No CNS effects** (doesn't cross BBB - quaternary)

**NEET PG**: Patient awake & aware during paralysis (no anesthesia)

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Adjunct to general anesthesia**:
- Muscle relaxation (surgery)
- Facilitates intubation
- ↓ Anesthetic dose requirement

**2. Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)**:
- Prevents fractures during seizures

**3. Controlled ventilation**

**NEET PG**: Muscle relaxants used with anesthesia (patient still conscious)

---

#### Adverse Effects

- **Prolonged apnea** (respiratory paralysis)
- **Histamine release**: Hypotension, bronchospasm, flushing
- Ganglionic blockade

**NEET PG**: Tubocurarine releases histamine

---

#### Reversal of Non-Depolarizing Block

**Anticholinesterases** (↑ ACh at NMJ):
- **Neostigmine** (most common)
- Pyridostigmine
- Edrophonium

**Always give with**:
- **Atropine** or **Glycopyrrolate** (prevent muscarinic effects)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Neostigmine + Atropine to reverse non-depolarizing block

---

### Other Non-Depolarizing Agents

#### PANCURONIUM

**Properties**:
- **Long-acting**
- **No histamine release**
- **Vagolytic** (↑ HR) - releases NE

**Uses**: Long surgeries

**NEET PG**: Pancuronium causes tachycardia (vagolytic)

---

#### VECURONIUM

**Properties**:
- **Intermediate-acting**
- **No histamine release**
- **No cardiovascular effects** (most stable)
- **Hepatic elimination**

**Uses**: Preferred for most surgeries

**NEET PG**: Vecuronium = Hemodynamically stable

---

#### ATRACURIUM

**Properties**:
- **Intermediate-acting**
- **Hofmann elimination** (spontaneous degradation - non-enzymatic)
- **Ester hydrolysis**
- **Safe in renal/hepatic failure**
- Releases histamine (mild)

**Uses**:
- Renal/hepatic impairment
- ICU sedation

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Atracurium = Safe in renal/hepatic failure (Hofmann elimination)

---

#### ROCURONIUM

**Properties**:
- **Intermediate-acting**
- **Rapid onset** (similar to succinylcholine)
- No histamine release

**Uses**:
- **Rapid sequence intubation** (alternative to succinylcholine)

**Reversal**:
- **Sugammadex** (specific reversal agent - encapsulates rocuronium)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Rocuronium = Rapid sequence intubation (non-depolarizing)
- Sugammadex = Specific reversal for rocuronium

---

#### MIVACURIUM

**Properties**:
- **Short-acting**
- Metabolized by **plasma cholinesterase** (like succinylcholine)
- Prolonged in cholinesterase deficiency

**NEET PG**: Mivacurium metabolized by plasma cholinesterase

---

## DEPOLARIZING MUSCLE RELAXANT

### SUCCINYLCHOLINE (Suxamethonium)

**Structure**: Two ACh molecules joined together

**Mechanism**: **Depolarizing** (NOT competitive)
1. Binds to nicotinic (NM) receptors
2. **Causes persistent depolarization**
3. Initial fasciculations (muscle twitching)
4. **Desensitization** → flaccid paralysis
5. Metabolized by **plasma cholinesterase**

**NEET PG**: Succinylcholine = Depolarizing, causes fasciculations

---

#### Pharmacological Actions

**Phase I Block** (Depolarizing):
- Initial fasciculations
- **NOT reversible by anticholinesterases**
- Enhanced by anticholinesterases

**Phase II Block** (Desensitization):
- After prolonged use
- Resembles non-depolarizing block
- May be reversed by anticholinesterases

**NEET PG**: Phase I succinylcholine block NOT reversed by neostigmine

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Rapid sequence intubation**:
- **Fastest onset** (30-60 seconds)
- **Shortest duration** (5-10 minutes)
- Emergency situations

**2. Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)**

**3. Endoscopy** (short procedures)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Succinylcholine = DOC for rapid intubation (fastest onset)

---

#### Adverse Effects

**1. Fasciculations**:
- Muscle pain (postoperative myalgia)

**2. Hyperkalemia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- K⁺ release from muscles
- **DANGEROUS in**:
  - **Burns** (>24 hours old)
  - **Denervation injuries** (stroke, spinal cord injury)
  - **Crush injuries**
  - **Prolonged immobilization**
  - Neuromuscular diseases
- Can cause **cardiac arrest**

**3. Malignant hyperthermia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Hypermetabolic crisis**
- **Triggers**: Succinylcholine + Volatile anesthetics
- Symptoms: ↑ Temperature, ↑ CO2, muscle rigidity, acidosis, hyperkalemia
- **Treatment**: **Dantrolene** (IV)

**4. Prolonged apnea**:
- **Plasma cholinesterase deficiency** (genetic)
- Duration: Hours instead of minutes

**5. Bradycardia**:
- Especially in children, after 2nd dose

**6. ↑ Intraocular pressure**:
- Contraindicated in **open eye injury**

**7. ↑ Intragastric pressure**:
- Risk of aspiration

**8. ↑ Intracranial pressure**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Succinylcholine contraindicated in burns, denervation (hyperkalemia)
- Contraindicated in open eye injury (↑ IOP)
- Malignant hyperthermia treated with Dantrolene

---

#### Contraindications

**Absolute**:
- **Burns** (>24 hours old)
- **Denervation** (stroke, spinal cord injury)
- **Crush injuries**
- **Neuromuscular diseases** (muscular dystrophy, myasthenia gravis)
- **Open eye injury** (↑ IOP)
- **Malignant hyperthermia** (personal/family history)
- **Hyperkalemia**

**Relative**:
- Plasma cholinesterase deficiency

**NEET PG**: Know contraindications (burns, denervation, eye injury)

---

## CENTRALLY ACTING MUSCLE RELAXANTS

### BACLOFEN

**Mechanism**: **GABA-B receptor agonist** (spinal cord)

**Actions**:
- ↓ Muscle spasticity
- ↓ Polysynaptic reflexes

**Uses**:
- **Spasticity** (multiple sclerosis, spinal cord injury, cerebral palsy)

**Adverse effects**:
- Sedation, drowsiness
- Muscle weakness
- **Withdrawal seizures** (if stopped abruptly)

**NEET PG**: Baclofen = GABA-B agonist for spasticity

---

### DIAZEPAM

**Mechanism**: **GABA-A receptor** potentiation

**Uses**:
- Muscle spasm (acute)
- Status epilepticus
- Anxiety

**NEET PG**: Diazepam for acute muscle spasm

---

### TIZANIDINE

**Mechanism**: **Central α2 agonist** (like clonidine)

**Uses**:
- Spasticity (multiple sclerosis)

**Adverse effects**:
- Sedation
- Hypotension

---

## DANTROLENE - Direct-Acting

**Mechanism**: Acts **directly on skeletal muscle**
- Blocks **ryanodine receptor** (Ca²⁺ release from sarcoplasmic reticulum)
- ↓ Muscle contraction

**Uses**:

**1. Malignant hyperthermia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **ONLY effective treatment**
- IV administration
- Life-saving

**2. Neuroleptic malignant syndrome** (NMS)

**3. Spasticity** (chronic - oral)

**Adverse effects**:
- Hepatotoxicity (chronic use)
- Muscle weakness

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Dantrolene = ONLY treatment for malignant hyperthermia

---

## DRUG INTERACTIONS

### Factors Potentiating Non-Depolarizing Block

1. **Volatile anesthetics** (halothane, isoflurane)
2. **Aminoglycosides** (gentamicin)
3. **Magnesium sulfate**
4. **Hypothermia**
5. **Acidosis**
6. **Myasthenia gravis**

**NEET PG**: Aminoglycosides potentiate muscle relaxants

---

### Factors Potentiating Succinylcholine

1. **Anticholinesterases** (neostigmine) - Phase I
2. **Organophosphates**
3. **Plasma cholinesterase deficiency**

---

## HIGH-YIELD COMPARISONS

| Feature | Non-Depolarizing | Depolarizing (Succinylcholine) |
|---------|------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Mechanism** | Competitive antagonist | Persistent depolarization |
| **Fasciculations** | NO | YES |
| **Initial effect** | Flaccid paralysis | Fasciculations → paralysis |
| **Reversal** | Anticholinesterases (neostigmine) | NOT reversible (Phase I) |
| **Onset** | Slower (except rocuronium) | **Fastest** (30-60 sec) |
| **Duration** | Long to intermediate | **Shortest** (5-10 min) |
| **Hyperkalemia** | NO | **YES** (dangerous) |
| **Malignant hyperthermia** | NO | **YES** |
| **Contraindications** | Myasthenia gravis | Burns, denervation, eye injury |

---

| Drug | Type | Duration | Key Feature |
|------|------|----------|-------------|
| **Tubocurarine** | Non-depolarizing | Long | Histamine release |
| **Pancuronium** | Non-depolarizing | Long | Tachycardia (vagolytic) |
| **Vecuronium** | Non-depolarizing | Intermediate | Hemodynamically stable |
| **Atracurium** | Non-depolarizing | Intermediate | Safe in renal/hepatic failure |
| **Rocuronium** | Non-depolarizing | Intermediate | Rapid onset, sugammadex reversal |
| **Succinylcholine** | Depolarizing | **Very short** | Fastest onset, hyperkalemia risk |

## Practice MCQs

1. **Drug of choice for rapid sequence intubation?**
   Answer: Succinylcholine (fastest onset)

2. **How to reverse non-depolarizing neuromuscular block?**
   Answer: Neostigmine + Atropine

3. **Specific reversal agent for rocuronium?**
   Answer: Sugammadex

4. **Muscle relaxant safe in renal and hepatic failure?**
   Answer: Atracurium (Hofmann elimination)

5. **Treatment for malignant hyperthermia?**
   Answer: Dantrolene (IV)

6. **Contraindications for succinylcholine?**
   Answer: Burns (>24 hr), denervation, open eye injury, hyperkalemia

7. **Which muscle paralyzed last, recovers first?**
   Answer: Diaphragm

8. **Drug causing fasciculations before paralysis?**
   Answer: Succinylcholine

9. **Muscle relaxant causing tachycardia?**
   Answer: Pancuronium

10. **GABA-B agonist for spasticity?**
    Answer: Baclofen
