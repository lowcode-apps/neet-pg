# RESPIRATORY PHYSIOLOGY - NEET PG Study Material

## Overview
**Functions of Respiratory System**:
1. Gas exchange (O2 and CO2)
2. Acid-base balance (CO2 excretion)
3. Phonation (voice production)
4. Olfaction (smell)
5. Heat & water loss regulation
6. Metabolism (ACE in lungs)

---

## LUNG VOLUMES & CAPACITIES - HIGH YIELD

### Volumes (cannot be subdivided)

| Volume | Definition | Value | Measured by |
|--------|------------|-------|-------------|
| **Tidal Volume (TV)** | Volume inspired/expired in normal breathing | **500 ml** | Spirometry |
| **Inspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV)** | Extra volume that can be inspired | 3000 ml | Spirometry |
| **Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV)** | Extra volume that can be expired | 1100 ml | Spirometry |
| **Residual Volume (RV)** | Volume remaining after maximal expiration | 1200 ml | **Body plethysmography** |

**NEET PG**: RV cannot be measured by spirometry (requires body plethysmography or helium dilution)

---

### Capacities (sum of 2 or more volumes)

| Capacity | Formula | Value | Significance |
|----------|---------|-------|--------------|
| **Inspiratory Capacity (IC)** | TV + IRV | 3500 ml | Maximum inspiration from FRC |
| **Functional Residual Capacity (FRC)** | **ERV + RV** | **2300 ml** | Lung volume at end of normal expiration |
| **Vital Capacity (VC)** | TV + IRV + ERV | 4600 ml | Maximum breath (inspiration → expiration) |
| **Total Lung Capacity (TLC)** | VC + RV (or IC + FRC) | **5800 ml** | Total lung volume |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **FRC = ERV + RV**: Most important capacity (gas exchange occurs here)
- **VC**: Decreased in restrictive lung disease
- **RV/TLC ratio**: Increased in obstructive lung disease

---

### Spirometry Values

**Forced Vital Capacity (FVC)**: Maximum volume expired forcefully after max inspiration
**FEV1 (Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 sec)**: Volume expired in first second of FVC
**FEV1/FVC ratio**: Normally >70% (0.7)

**NEET PG**:
- **Obstructive disease**: ↓ FEV1/FVC (<70%)
- **Restrictive disease**: Normal or ↑ FEV1/FVC, ↓ FVC

## DEAD SPACE

### Anatomical Dead Space
**Definition**: Air in conducting airways (no gas exchange)
**Volume**: **150 ml** (≈ 2 ml/kg)
**Includes**: Nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi, bronchioles (up to terminal bronchioles)

### Alveolar Dead Space
**Definition**: Ventilated alveoli with no/poor perfusion
**Normal**: Negligible (≈ 0)
**Increased in**: Pulmonary embolism, shock

### Physiological Dead Space
**Definition**: Anatomical dead space + Alveolar dead space
**Formula**: VD/VT = (PaCO2 - PECO2) / PaCO2 (Bohr equation)

**NEET PG**:
- **Anatomical dead space**: 150 ml (1/3 of tidal volume)
- **Alveolar ventilation** = (TV - Dead space) × RR = (500-150) × 12 = **4200 ml/min**

---

## VENTILATION

### Minute Ventilation
**Definition**: Total volume breathed per minute
**Formula**: VE = TV × RR = 500 × 12 = **6000 ml/min** (6 L/min)

### Alveolar Ventilation
**Definition**: Volume reaching alveoli per minute (effective ventilation)
**Formula**: VA = (TV - VD) × RR = (500-150) × 12 = **4200 ml/min**

**NEET PG**: Alveolar ventilation more important than minute ventilation

---

## MECHANICS OF BREATHING

### Inspiratory Muscles

**Primary**:
- **Diaphragm** (most important - 75% of inspiration)
  - Nerve: Phrenic nerve (C3, C4, C5)
  - Contraction → Descends → ↑ Vertical diameter
- **External intercostals**
  - Elevate ribs → ↑ AP & transverse diameter

**Accessory** (in dyspnea):
- Sternocleidomastoid
- Scalenes
- Pectoralis major/minor

**NEET PG**: "**C3, 4, 5 keep the diaphragm alive**" (phrenic nerve)

### Expiratory Muscles

**Normal breathing**: Passive (elastic recoil)

**Active expiration**:
- **Internal intercostals**: Depress ribs
- **Abdominal muscles**: Compress abdomen, push diaphragm up

---

## COMPLIANCE & ELASTANCE

### Compliance (C)
**Definition**: Change in volume per unit change in pressure
**Formula**: C = ΔV / ΔP
**Normal**: 200 ml/cm H2O

**Increased compliance** (floppy lungs):
- **Emphysema** (loss of elastic tissue)
- Easy to inflate, difficult to deflate

**Decreased compliance** (stiff lungs):
- **Pulmonary fibrosis**
- **Pulmonary edema**
- **ARDS**
- Difficult to inflate

**NEET PG**: Emphysema = ↑ Compliance, Fibrosis = ↓ Compliance

### Elastance
**Definition**: Reciprocal of compliance (E = 1/C)
**Increased in**: Pulmonary fibrosis (stiff lungs)

---

## SURFACTANT - HIGH YIELD

**Produced by**: Type II pneumocytes
**Composition**: 90% lipid (dipalmitoyl phosphatidylcholine - DPPC), 10% protein

**Functions**:
1. **↓ Surface tension** in alveoli
2. **Prevents alveolar collapse** (especially small alveoli)
3. **Increases compliance**
4. **Prevents pulmonary edema** (opposes transudation)

**Laplace's Law**: P = 2T/r
- P = Pressure inside alveolus
- T = Surface tension
- r = Radius

**Without surfactant**: Smaller alveoli collapse (higher pressure needed)
**With surfactant**: Surface tension ↓ more in small alveoli → Equalizes pressure

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Surfactant deficiency**: Neonatal respiratory distress syndrome (NRDS)
- **Treatment**: Exogenous surfactant (beractant, poractant)
- **L/S ratio <2**: Immature lungs (risk of NRDS)
- **Corticosteroids** (betamethasone): Accelerate surfactant production

---

## VENTILATION-PERFUSION (V/Q) RATIO

**Definition**: Ratio of alveolar ventilation to pulmonary blood flow

**Normal V/Q ratio**: 0.8 (V = 4 L/min, Q = 5 L/min)

### Regional Variation

**Apex**:
- High V/Q ratio (≈3)
- Good ventilation, poor perfusion
- High PO2, low PCO2
- **Site for TB** (Mycobacterium likes high O2)

**Base**:
- Low V/Q ratio (≈0.6)
- Good perfusion, better than ventilation
- Low PO2, high PCO2

**NEET PG**: V/Q mismatch most common cause of hypoxemia

### Extreme V/Q States

| Condition | V/Q Ratio | Cause | Example |
|-----------|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Dead space** | ∞ (V, no Q) | No perfusion | Pulmonary embolism |
| **Shunt** | 0 (Q, no V) | No ventilation | Atelectasis, ARDS |

**Shunt**:
- **Anatomical**: Bronchial veins, Thebesian veins (2% of CO)
- **Physiological**: Unventilated but perfused alveoli

**NEET PG**:
- **100% O2 test**: Hypoxemia due to shunt does NOT correct with 100% O2
- **V/Q mismatch**: Hypoxemia corrects with 100% O2

## GAS TRANSPORT

### Oxygen Transport

**Forms**:
1. **Dissolved in plasma**: 0.3 ml/100 ml blood (1.5%)
2. **Bound to hemoglobin**: 19.7 ml/100 ml blood (98.5%)

**Total O2 content**: 20 ml/100 ml (20 vol%)

**Oxygen carrying capacity** = Hb (g/dl) × 1.34 ml O2/g Hb
**O2 content** = (Hb × 1.34 × SaO2) + (0.003 × PaO2)

**NEET PG**: Each gram of Hb binds 1.34 ml O2 when fully saturated

---

### Oxygen-Hemoglobin Dissociation Curve - HIGH YIELD

**Shape**: Sigmoid (S-shaped)
**P50**: PO2 at which Hb is 50% saturated = **27 mmHg**

**Plateau portion** (PO2 >60 mmHg):
- Large changes in PO2 → Small changes in SaO2
- Protects against hypoxemia (high altitude)

**Steep portion** (PO2 <60 mmHg):
- Small changes in PO2 → Large O2 release to tissues

#### Right Shift (↓ O2 affinity, ↑ O2 delivery to tissues)
**Mnemonic**: "**R**ight shift = **R**elease O2 to tissues"

**Causes** (CADET, face Right):
- **C**O2 ↑ (hypercapnia)
- **A**cid ↑ (↓ pH)
- **D**PG (2,3-DPG) ↑
- **E**xercise
- **T**emperature ↑

**NEET PG**: ↑ 2,3-DPG in chronic hypoxia, high altitude

#### Left Shift (↑ O2 affinity, ↓ O2 delivery)
**Causes**:
- ↓ Temperature (hypothermia)
- ↓ CO2, ↓ H+ (alkalosis)
- ↓ 2,3-DPG
- **Fetal hemoglobin (HbF)**
- **Carboxyhemoglobin** (CO poisoning)
- **Methemoglobin**

**NEET PG**:
- **Fetal Hb**: Left shift (higher O2 affinity - extracts O2 from maternal blood)
- **CO poisoning**: Left shift + ↓ O2 capacity (binds 200× more than O2)

---

### Bohr Effect
**Definition**: ↑ CO2 & ↓ pH → ↓ O2 affinity (right shift)
**Mechanism**: CO2 + H+ → Allosteric change in Hb → ↓ O2 binding
**Significance**: Facilitates O2 unloading in tissues (↑ CO2, ↓ pH)

**NEET PG**: Bohr effect helps O2 delivery to metabolically active tissues

---

### Haldane Effect
**Definition**: Deoxygenated Hb binds more CO2 than oxygenated Hb
**Mechanism**: Deoxygenated Hb is a better buffer for H+
**Significance**: Facilitates CO2 loading in tissues & unloading in lungs

**NEET PG**: Haldane effect explains 70% of CO2 transport difference between arterial & venous blood

---

### Carbon Dioxide Transport

**Forms**:
1. **Dissolved in plasma**: 7% (0.6 mmol/L per mmHg)
2. **Carbamino compounds** (bound to Hb): 23%
3. **Bicarbonate** (HCO3-): **70%** (most important)

**In RBCs**:
```
CO2 + H2O ⇌ H2CO3 ⇌ H+ + HCO3-
   (Carbonic anhydrase)
```

**Chloride shift (Hamburger phenomenon)**:
- HCO3- diffuses out of RBC → Cl- enters (maintain electroneutrality)
- Occurs in tissues (CO2 loading)
- Reversed in lungs

**NEET PG**:
- **70% CO2** transported as HCO3-
- **Carbonic anhydrase**: In RBCs, NOT in plasma
- **Chloride shift**: Maintains electrical neutrality

## CONTROL OF RESPIRATION

### Respiratory Centers

#### Medulla (Dorsal & Ventral Groups)
**Function**: Basic rhythm of respiration
- **Dorsal respiratory group (DRG)**: Inspiration (stimulates phrenic nerve)
- **Ventral respiratory group (VRG)**: Expiration (active expiration)

#### Pons
**Pneumotaxic center**: Limits inspiration (controls respiratory rate)
**Apneustic center**: Prolongs inspiration

**NEET PG**: Medulla = Basic rhythm, Pons = Fine-tuning

---

### Chemoreceptors - HIGH YIELD

#### Central Chemoreceptors (Medulla)
**Location**: Ventral surface of medulla
**Stimulus**: **↑ H+** in CSF (↑ CO2 crosses BBB → H2CO3 → H+)
**Response**: ↑ Ventilation
**Contribution**: **70-80%** of hypercapnic drive

**NEET PG**: Central chemoreceptors respond to CO2 (via H+), NOT O2

#### Peripheral Chemoreceptors
**Location**:
- **Carotid bodies** (bifurcation of common carotid) - CN IX
- **Aortic bodies** (arch of aorta) - CN X

**Stimuli**:
1. **↓ PO2** (most sensitive) - primary O2 sensor
2. ↑ PCO2
3. ↓ pH

**Response**: ↑ Ventilation
**Contribution**: 30% of hypercapnic drive, **100% of hypoxic drive**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Only peripheral chemoreceptors** respond to hypoxemia
- **Carotid bodies**: More sensitive to hypoxia than aortic bodies
- **Chronic hypercapnia** (COPD): Lose central chemoreceptor sensitivity → Hypoxic drive predominates

---

### Other Receptors

**Stretch receptors** (Hering-Breuer reflex):
- In airway smooth muscle
- Inflation → Inhibit inspiration (prevents over-inflation)

**Irritant receptors**:
- Rapid shallow breathing, cough, bronchoconstriction

**J receptors (Juxtacapillary)**:
- In alveolar walls
- Stimulated by: Pulmonary edema, PE, pneumonia
- Response: Rapid shallow breathing (tachypnea)

**NEET PG**: J receptors → Rapid shallow breathing in pulmonary edema

## HYPOXIA - TYPES

| Type | PaO2 | CaO2 | SaO2 | Cause | Example |
|------|------|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Hypoxic** | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ Inspired O2, hypoventilation, V/Q mismatch, diffusion defect | High altitude, COPD |
| **Anemic** | Normal | ↓ | Normal | ↓ Hemoglobin | Anemia |
| **Stagnant** | Normal | Normal | Normal | ↓ Blood flow | Shock, CHF |
| **Histotoxic** | Normal | Normal | Normal | Cells can't use O2 | Cyanide poisoning |

**NEET PG**:
- **Hypoxic hypoxia**: Only type with ↓ PaO2
- **Anemic hypoxia**: Normal PaO2 but ↓ O2 content
- **Cyanide poisoning**: Inhibits cytochrome oxidase (can't use O2)

## ALTITUDE PHYSIOLOGY

### Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS)
**Altitude**: >2500m (8000 ft)
**Time**: 6-12 hours after ascent
**Symptoms**: Headache, nausea, dizziness, fatigue, insomnia

### High Altitude Pulmonary Edema (HAPE)
**Mechanism**: Hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction → ↑ Pulmonary artery pressure → Edema
**Features**: Dyspnea, cough, frothy sputum
**Treatment**: Descent, O2, nifedipine

### High Altitude Cerebral Edema (HACE)
**Mechanism**: Hypoxia → ↑ Cerebral blood flow → Edema
**Features**: Ataxia, confusion, altered sensorium
**Treatment**: Descent, O2, dexamethasone

**NEET PG**: Acetazolamide prevents AMS (causes metabolic acidosis → stimulates breathing)

---

### Acclimatization to High Altitude

**Immediate**:
- ↑ Ventilation (hyperventilation)
- ↑ Heart rate

**Days-Weeks**:
- ↑ 2,3-DPG (right shift → ↑ O2 delivery)
- ↑ Erythropoietin → Polycythemia
- ↑ Capillary density

**Months**:
- ↑ Mitochondria
- ↑ Myoglobin

**NEET PG**: ↑ 2,3-DPG and polycythemia = Key adaptations

---

## DIVING PHYSIOLOGY

### Nitrogen Narcosis
**Cause**: ↑ Nitrogen dissolves in tissues (high pressure)
**Depth**: >30m (100 ft)
**Symptoms**: Euphoria, impaired judgment ("rapture of the deep")

### Decompression Sickness (Bends)
**Cause**: Rapid ascent → Nitrogen bubbles form
**Features**:
- **Type I**: Joint pain, skin rash
- **Type II**: CNS (paralysis), pulmonary (chokes)
**Treatment**: Recompression (hyperbaric O2)

**NEET PG**: Decompression sickness prevented by slow ascent
