# GASTROINTESTINAL PHYSIOLOGY - NEET PG Study Material

## Overview
**Functions of GI System**:
1. **Motility**: Propulsion of food
2. **Secretion**: Enzymes, hormones, mucus
3. **Digestion**: Breakdown of nutrients
4. **Absorption**: Uptake of nutrients
5. **Excretion**: Elimination of waste

---

## SALIVARY SECRETION

### Composition
**Daily secretion**: 1-1.5 L/day
**pH**: 6-7 (slightly acidic to neutral)

**Components**:
- **Water**: 99%
- **Electrolytes**: Na+, K+, Cl-, HCO3-
- **Enzymes**:
  - **α-amylase (ptyalin)**: Starch → Maltose
  - **Lingual lipase**: Triglycerides
- **Mucins**: Lubrication
- **Antibacterial**: Lysozyme, lactoferrin, IgA

**NEET PG**: Salivary amylase (ptyalin) digests starch at pH 6-7, inactivated in stomach (pH <4)

### Salivary Glands

| Gland | % Contribution | Type of Secretion |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Parotid** | 25% | Serous (watery, enzyme-rich) |
| **Submandibular** | 70% | Mixed (mainly serous) |
| **Sublingual** | 5% | Mucous (viscous) |

**Nerve supply**:
- **Parasympathetic** (CN VII, IX): **Stimulates** copious watery secretion
- **Sympathetic**: Small volume, viscous secretion

**NEET PG**: Parasympathetic = Main stimulator of salivation

### Functions of Saliva
1. **Lubrication**: Mucins
2. **Digestion**: Amylase (starch)
3. **Protection**: Lysozyme, IgA, buffer HCO3-
4. **Taste**: Dissolves food
5. **Speech**: Moistens mouth

## GASTRIC SECRETION - HIGH YIELD

### Gastric Juice Composition
**Daily volume**: 2-3 L
**pH**: 1-2 (highly acidic)

**Components**:
- **HCl** (hydrochloric acid)
- **Pepsinogen** (inactive) → **Pepsin** (active)
- **Intrinsic factor** (Vitamin B12 absorption)
- **Mucus** (protection)
- **Bicarbonate** (neutralization in mucus layer)

---

### Cell Types in Gastric Mucosa

| Cell Type | Location | Secretion | Function |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Parietal (Oxyntic)** | Body & Fundus | **HCl**, **Intrinsic factor** | Acid secretion, B12 absorption |
| **Chief (Peptic)** | Body & Fundus | **Pepsinogen**, Gastric lipase | Protein digestion |
| **Mucous neck cells** | Throughout | **Mucus**, **HCO3-** | Protection |
| **G cells** | **Antrum** | **Gastrin** | Stimulates HCl |
| **D cells** | Antrum & Body | **Somatostatin** | Inhibits gastrin & HCl |
| **ECL cells** | Body & Fundus | **Histamine** | Stimulates HCl |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Parietal cells**: HCl + Intrinsic factor
- **Chief cells**: Pepsinogen
- **G cells**: Gastrin (in antrum only)

---

### HCl Secretion Mechanism

**Parietal Cell**:
```
H2O + CO2 → H2CO3 → H+ + HCO3-
         (Carbonic anhydrase)

H+ secreted into lumen via H+/K+-ATPase (Proton pump)
HCO3- absorbed into blood (alkaline tide)
```

**Stimulators of HCl Secretion**:
1. **Gastrin** (from G cells) - Most potent
2. **Histamine** (from ECL cells) - via H2 receptors
3. **Acetylcholine** (vagus nerve) - via M3 receptors

**All act on parietal cells** to stimulate **H+/K+-ATPase** (proton pump)

**Inhibitors**:
1. **Somatostatin** (from D cells)
2. **Prostaglandins** (PGE2)
3. **Secretin** (from duodenum)

**NEET PG**:
- **Proton pump (H+/K+-ATPase)**: Final common pathway
- **PPIs** (omeprazole): Irreversibly inhibit H+/K+-ATPase
- **H2 blockers** (ranitidine): Block histamine H2 receptors

---

### Phases of Gastric Secretion

#### 1. Cephalic Phase (30%)
**Stimulus**: Sight, smell, taste, thought of food
**Mediator**: **Vagus nerve** (acetylcholine)
**Mechanism**:
- Direct: ACh → Parietal cells
- Indirect: ACh → G cells → Gastrin

#### 2. Gastric Phase (60%)
**Stimulus**: Food in stomach (distension, protein)
**Mediators**: **Gastrin** (main), vagus
**Mechanism**:
- Distension → Vagal reflex + Local reflexes
- Proteins → G cells → Gastrin

#### 3. Intestinal Phase (10%)
**Stimulus**: Food in duodenum
**Effect**: Initially stimulatory, then **inhibitory**
**Inhibitory mediators**:
- **Secretin** (from S cells): ↓ Gastrin
- **CCK** (from I cells): ↓ Gastric emptying
- **GIP** (Glucose-dependent insulinotropic peptide)

**NEET PG**: Gastric phase = Maximum secretion (60%)

---

### Pepsin

**Secreted as**: **Pepsinogen** (inactive zymogen) by chief cells

**Activation**:
```
Pepsinogen → Pepsin (by HCl, pH <5)
Pepsinogen → Pepsin (by pepsin - autocatalysis)
```

**Function**: Protein → Peptides (breaks peptide bonds, especially aromatic amino acids)

**Optimal pH**: 1.5-2.5

**NEET PG**: Pepsin activated by HCl, optimal pH 1.5-2.5

---

### Intrinsic Factor

**Secreted by**: Parietal cells
**Function**: Binds Vitamin B12 → Absorption in **terminal ileum**

**Deficiency**:
- **Pernicious anemia**: Autoimmune destruction of parietal cells
- **Features**: Megaloblastic anemia + neurological symptoms (subacute combined degeneration)

**NEET PG**: Intrinsic factor required for B12 absorption in terminal ileum

---

### Gastric Mucosal Barrier

**Protection mechanisms**:
1. **Mucus layer**: Bicarbonate-containing gel
2. **Bicarbonate secretion**: Neutralizes H+
3. **Tight junctions**: Prevent back-diffusion of H+
4. **Blood flow**: Removes excess H+
5. **Prostaglandins**: ↑ Mucus, ↑ HCO3-, ↑ Blood flow

**Damaged by**:
- **NSAIDs**: ↓ Prostaglandins (COX inhibition)
- **H. pylori**: Urease → Ammonia (damages mucosa)
- **Alcohol, bile salts**

**NEET PG**: NSAIDs damage gastric mucosa by inhibiting prostaglandins

## PANCREATIC SECRETION

### Composition
**Daily volume**: 1-2 L
**pH**: 8-8.3 (highly alkaline - rich in HCO3-)

**Two components**:
1. **Aqueous component** (from duct cells): HCO3-, water
2. **Enzymatic component** (from acinar cells): Digestive enzymes

---

### Pancreatic Enzymes

**Proteolytic** (secreted as inactive zymogens):
1. **Trypsinogen** → **Trypsin** (by enterokinase in duodenum)
2. **Chymotrypsinogen** → Chymotrypsin (by trypsin)
3. **Proelastase** → Elastase (by trypsin)
4. **Procarboxypeptidase** → Carboxypeptidase (by trypsin)

**Lipolytic**:
1. **Pancreatic lipase**: Triglycerides → Fatty acids + Monoglycerides
   - Requires **colipase** and **bile salts**
2. **Phospholipase A2**: Phospholipids

**Amylolytic**:
1. **Pancreatic amylase**: Starch → Maltose, maltotriose, α-limit dextrins

**Nucleases**: RNase, DNase

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Trypsinogen activated by enterokinase** (in duodenal brush border)
- **Trypsin activates other pancreatic zymogens** (key enzyme)
- **Pancreatic lipase requires colipase** (overcomes bile salt inhibition)

---

### Regulation of Pancreatic Secretion

#### Aqueous Component (HCO3-)
**Stimulated by**: **Secretin** (from duodenal S cells)
**Stimulus for secretin**: **Acid in duodenum** (pH <4.5)

**Mechanism**: Secretin → Pancreatic duct cells → ↑ cAMP → HCO3- secretion

#### Enzymatic Component
**Stimulated by**: **CCK (Cholecystokinin)** (from duodenal I cells)
**Stimulus for CCK**: **Fats & proteins** in duodenum

**Mechanism**: CCK → Pancreatic acinar cells → Enzyme secretion

**Vagus nerve**: Potentiates both (via ACh)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Secretin**: ↑ HCO3- (neutralizes acid)
- **CCK**: ↑ Enzymes (digests fats & proteins)
- **Secretin + CCK**: Synergistic effect

---

## BILE SECRETION

### Composition
**Daily secretion**: 500-1000 ml
**pH**: 7.8-8.6 (alkaline)

**Components**:
- **Bile salts** (67%): Primary (cholic, chenodeoxycholic), Secondary (deoxycholic, lithocholic)
- **Bile pigments**: **Bilirubin** (conjugated)
- **Cholesterol**
- **Phospholipids**: Lecithin
- **Electrolytes**: Na+, K+, HCO3-

**NEET PG**: Bile salts synthesized in liver from cholesterol

---

### Functions of Bile

1. **Fat digestion**: Emulsification (↑ surface area for lipase)
2. **Fat-soluble vitamin absorption**: A, D, E, K
3. **Excretion**: Bilirubin, cholesterol, drugs
4. **Antimicrobial**: Bile salts

**NEET PG**: Bile salts essential for fat-soluble vitamin absorption

---

### Enterohepatic Circulation

**Bile salts**:
- Secreted in bile (liver)
- Reabsorbed in **terminal ileum** (95%)
- Return to liver via portal blood
- Recycled 6-8 times/day

**Interruption**:
- **Ileal resection**: ↓ Bile salt reabsorption → Steatorrhea, cholesterol gallstones
- **Cholestyramine**: Binds bile salts (↓ cholesterol)

**NEET PG**: 95% bile salts reabsorbed in terminal ileum (enterohepatic circulation)

---

### Gallbladder Function

**Storage & concentration**: 5-10× concentrated
**Stimulated to contract by**: **CCK**
**Relaxation of sphincter of Oddi**: CCK, vagus

**NEET PG**: CCK causes gallbladder contraction + sphincter of Oddi relaxation

## GI HORMONES - HIGH YIELD

| Hormone | Source | Stimulus | Actions |
|---------|--------|----------|---------|
| **Gastrin** | G cells (antrum) | Proteins, distension, vagus | ↑ HCl, ↑ gastric motility, trophic (mucosa) |
| **Secretin** | S cells (duodenum) | **Acid** (pH <4.5) | ↑ Pancreatic HCO3-, ↑ Bile HCO3-, ↓ Gastric acid |
| **CCK** | I cells (duodenum) | **Fats, proteins** | ↑ Pancreatic enzymes, ↑ GB contraction, ↓ Gastric emptying |
| **GIP** | K cells (duodenum) | Glucose, fats | ↑ Insulin (incretin), ↓ Gastric acid |
| **Motilin** | M cells (duodenum) | Fasting | ↑ Migrating motor complex (MMC) |
| **VIP** | Neurons | - | ↑ Intestinal secretion, relaxes smooth muscle |
| **Somatostatin** | D cells | Acid, fats | **Inhibits all GI hormones** & secretions |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Gastrin**: ↑ HCl (most potent stimulator)
- **Secretin**: ↑ Pancreatic HCO3- (neutralizes acid)
- **CCK**: ↑ Pancreatic enzymes + GB contraction
- **GIP**: Incretin (↑ insulin) - "Gastric Inhibitory Peptide"
- **Motilin**: MMC (interdigestive motility)
- **Somatostatin**: Universal inhibitor

## SMALL INTESTINAL ABSORPTION - HIGH YIELD

### Carbohydrate Absorption

**Digestion**:
1. **Salivary amylase**: Starch → Maltose (mouth)
2. **Pancreatic amylase**: Starch → Maltose, maltotriose, α-limit dextrins (duodenum)
3. **Brush border enzymes**:
   - **Maltase**: Maltose → 2 Glucose
   - **Sucrase**: Sucrose → Glucose + Fructose
   - **Lactase**: Lactose → Glucose + Galactose
   - **Isomaltase**: α-limit dextrins → Glucose

**Absorption** (into enterocyte):
- **Glucose & Galactose**: **SGLT1** (Na+-coupled, secondary active transport)
- **Fructose**: **GLUT5** (facilitated diffusion)

**Exit** (into blood):
- All monosaccharides: **GLUT2** (basolateral, facilitated diffusion)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Glucose absorption**: SGLT1 (apical), GLUT2 (basolateral)
- **Lactase deficiency**: Lactose intolerance (osmotic diarrhea)

---

### Protein Absorption

**Digestion**:
1. **Pepsin** (stomach): Proteins → Peptides
2. **Pancreatic proteases**: Trypsin, chymotrypsin, elastase, carboxypeptidase
3. **Brush border peptidases**: Aminopeptidase, dipeptidases

**Absorption**:
- **Amino acids**: Na+-coupled cotransport (multiple transporters)
- **Di/tripeptides**: **PepT1** (H+-coupled)
- **Intact proteins**: Pinocytosis (small amount - antibodies in neonates)

**NEET PG**: Dipeptides absorbed via PepT1 (H+-coupled transporter)

---

### Fat Absorption - MOST COMPLEX

**Digestion**:
1. **Lingual lipase** (saliva): Starts in mouth
2. **Gastric lipase**: Continues in stomach
3. **Pancreatic lipase** (main enzyme): Triglycerides → 2-Monoglyceride + 2 Fatty acids
   - Requires **colipase** and **bile salts**

**Emulsification**: Bile salts break fat into small droplets

**Micelle formation**:
- Bile salts + Monoglycerides + Fatty acids + Fat-soluble vitamins
- Micelles transport to enterocyte

**Absorption**:
- **Short/medium-chain fatty acids** (<12 C): Directly into portal blood
- **Long-chain fatty acids** + Monoglycerides:
  - Re-esterified to triglycerides in enterocyte
  - Packaged into **chylomicrons** (with apolipoprotein B-48)
  - Released into **lymphatics** (lacteals) → Thoracic duct → Blood

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Pancreatic lipase** requires colipase & bile salts
- **Chylomicrons**: Transport dietary fats via lymphatics
- **Abetalipoproteinemia**: Cannot form chylomicrons (no apoB-48)

---

### Vitamin Absorption

**Fat-soluble** (A, D, E, K):
- Require **bile salts** and **micelles**
- Absorbed with fats into lymphatics

**Water-soluble**:
- **B12**: Intrinsic factor → Terminal ileum (specific receptor)
- **Folate**: Proximal jejunum
- **Others**: Various mechanisms

**NEET PG**:
- **Fat-soluble vitamins**: Need bile salts (steatorrhea → deficiency)
- **B12**: Terminal ileum (ileal resection → deficiency)

---

### Mineral Absorption

**Iron**:
- Absorbed as **Fe2+** (ferrous) in duodenum
- **DMT1** (divalent metal transporter 1)
- Stored as **ferritin**
- Exported by **ferroportin**
- Regulated by **hepcidin** (↓ absorption when iron stores high)

**Calcium**:
- **Active transport** in duodenum & jejunum
- Stimulated by **Vitamin D** (↑ calbindin)
- **Passive diffusion** in ileum

**NEET PG**:
- **Iron**: Absorbed in duodenum as Fe2+
- **Calcium**: Vitamin D-dependent (duodenum & jejunum)

---

## COLONIC FUNCTION

### Functions
1. **Water & electrolyte absorption**: 1-2 L/day
2. **Storage** of feces
3. **Bacterial fermentation**: Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)
4. **Synthesis**: Vitamin K, B vitamins (by bacteria)
5. **Defecation**

### Absorption
- **Na+ absorption**: Aldosterone-sensitive (ENaC channels)
- **K+ secretion**: Aldosterone stimulates
- **Water follows Na+** (osmosis)

**NEET PG**: Aldosterone ↑ Na+ absorption & K+ secretion in colon

## GI MOTILITY

### Esophageal Motility

**Primary peristalsis**: Initiated by swallowing
**Secondary peristalsis**: Initiated by esophageal distension (clears reflux)

**Lower Esophageal Sphincter (LES)**:
- **Tonic contraction** (prevents reflux)
- **Relaxes** during swallowing (VIP, NO)

**Achalasia**:
- **Defect**: Loss of myenteric plexus neurons
- **Features**: ↑ LES tone, absent peristalsis
- **Clinical**: Dysphagia to solids & liquids, regurgitation
- **Barium swallow**: **Bird's beak** appearance

**NEET PG**: Achalasia = ↑ LES tone + absent peristalsis

---

### Gastric Emptying

**Factors ↑ Emptying**:
- Gastric distension
- Liquid consistency
- Low caloric content

**Factors ↓ Emptying**:
- **Fat** (most potent inhibitor - CCK)
- **Acid** (secretin)
- Hypertonicity
- Amino acids

**NEET PG**: Fat most potent inhibitor of gastric emptying (via CCK)

---

### Small Intestinal Motility

**Segmentation**: Mixing (predominates during digestion)
**Peristalsis**: Propulsion

**Migrating Motor Complex (MMC)**:
- **Fasting state** (between meals)
- Clears undigested material
- Stimulated by **motilin**
- **3 phases**: Quiescence → Irregular → Regular contractions

**NEET PG**: Motilin stimulates MMC (interdigestive motility)

---

### Colonic Motility

**Haustral contractions**: Mixing
**Mass movements**: 1-3 times/day (after meals - gastrocolic reflex)

**Defecation Reflex**:
- Rectal distension → Relaxation of internal anal sphincter (involuntary)
- External anal sphincter: Voluntary control

---

## DIARRHEA - MECHANISMS

### 1. Osmotic Diarrhea
**Cause**: Non-absorbed solutes (lactose, Mg2+, sorbitol)
**Features**:
- Stops with fasting
- **Stool osmotic gap >50**
- Normal stool Na+ & K+

**Stool osmotic gap** = 290 - 2(stool Na+ + K+)

### 2. Secretory Diarrhea
**Cause**: ↑ Secretion or ↓ Absorption (cholera toxin, VIP)
**Features**:
- Continues despite fasting
- **Stool osmotic gap <50**
- High volume

**NEET PG**: Cholera toxin → ↑ cAMP → ↑ Cl- secretion

### 3. Inflammatory Diarrhea
**Cause**: IBD, infections (Shigella, Salmonella)
**Features**: Blood, WBCs in stool, fever

### 4. Motility Disorder
**Cause**: IBS, hyperthyroidism, diabetic neuropathy

**NEET PG**: Osmotic gap differentiates osmotic from secretory diarrhea

## Practice MCQs

1. **Enzyme activating trypsinogen?**
   Answer: Enterokinase

2. **Hormone stimulating pancreatic HCO3-?**
   Answer: Secretin

3. **Site of B12 absorption?**
   Answer: Terminal ileum

4. **Site of bile salt reabsorption?**
   Answer: Terminal ileum

5. **Glucose absorption transporter (apical)?**
   Answer: SGLT1

6. **Most potent inhibitor of gastric emptying?**
   Answer: Fat (via CCK)

7. **Phase of gastric secretion with maximum output?**
   Answer: Gastric phase (60%)

8. **Defect in achalasia?**
   Answer: Loss of myenteric plexus neurons
