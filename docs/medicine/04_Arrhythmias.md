## Overview

### Definition
Arrhythmias are disorders of cardiac rhythm caused by abnormalities in impulse formation, impulse conduction, or both.

### Classification

**By Heart Rate:**
- **Tachyarrhythmias:** Heart rate >100 bpm
- **Bradyarrhythmias:** Heart rate <60 bpm

**By Origin:**
- **Supraventricular:** Atria or AV node
- **Ventricular:** Ventricles

**By Mechanism:**
- **Re-entry:** Most common (AF, VT, AVNRT)
- **Abnormal Automaticity:** Ectopic pacemaker
- **Triggered Activity:** EADs (Torsades), DADs (Digoxin toxicity)

### Mechanisms - **"RAT"**
```
R - Re-entry (Most common)
A - Automaticity
T - Triggered activity
```

## Atrial Fibrillation (AF)

### Definition
**Most common sustained cardiac arrhythmia** characterized by rapid, irregular atrial activation without discrete P waves.

### Epidemiology
- Prevalence: 1-2% general population
- Increases with age: 10% in >80 years
- Risk of stroke: **5x increased**

### Etiology - **"PIRATES"**

```
P - Pulmonary disease (COPD, PE)
I - Ischemic heart disease (MI, CAD)
R - Rheumatic heart disease (Mitral stenosis)
A - Alcohol (Holiday heart syndrome)
T - Thyrotoxicosis
E - Embolism (Pulmonary)
S - Sepsis, Surgery
```

**Additional Causes:**
- Hypertension (**Most common** in developed countries)
- Mitral valve disease (**Most common** worldwide)
- Cardiomyopathies
- Congenital heart disease (ASD)
- Pericarditis
- Sick sinus syndrome

### Classification

#### **1. Based on Duration**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   AF CLASSIFICATION                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ First detected      → First episode documented          │
│ Paroxysmal          → Terminates within 7 days          │
│ Persistent          → Lasts >7 days                     │
│ Long-standing       → Lasts >12 months                  │
│ Permanent           → Accepted, no cardioversion        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### **2. Lone AF**
- AF in patients <60 years
- No structural heart disease
- Good prognosis

### Pathophysiology

**Mechanism:** Multiple micro-re-entry circuits in atria

**Key Changes:**
1. **Atrial rate:** 300-600 bpm (irregular)
2. **Ventricular rate:** 120-180 bpm (irregularly irregular)
3. **Loss of atrial kick:** ↓ Cardiac output by 20-30%
4. **Atrial stasis:** Thrombus formation (LAA - Left Atrial Appendage)

### Clinical Features

**Symptoms:**
- **Palpitations** (Most common)
- Dyspnea, fatigue
- Chest discomfort
- Dizziness, syncope
- **Some patients asymptomatic** (detected incidentally)

**Signs:**
- **Irregularly irregular pulse** (Hallmark)
- Pulse deficit (Radial pulse < Apical rate)
- Varying intensity of S1
- Absent 'a' wave in JVP

**Complications:**
1. **Thromboembolism** (Stroke - most serious)
2. **Heart failure** (Tachycardia-induced)
3. **Hypotension**
4. **Cardiomyopathy**

### Investigations

#### **ECG - Diagnostic**

**Key Features:**
```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              AF ON ECG                               │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 1. Absent P waves                                    │
│ 2. Fibrillatory waves (f waves)                      │
│ 3. Irregularly irregular QRS complexes               │
│ 4. Narrow QRS (unless bundle branch block)           │
│ 5. Ventricular rate: Usually 120-180 bpm             │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**AF with RVR** (Rapid Ventricular Response): Rate >100 bpm

#### **Other Investigations**
- **Echocardiography:**
  - LA size (>4 cm - high risk)
  - LV function
  - Valvular disease
  - Thrombus detection (TEE more sensitive)

- **Thyroid function tests:** Rule out thyrotoxicosis
- **Holter monitoring:** Paroxysmal AF
- **Chest X-ray:** Cardiomegaly, pulmonary congestion

### Management

#### **1. Rate Control vs Rhythm Control**

**Rate Control** (Preferred in most patients):
- Target: Resting HR <110 bpm (lenient) or <80 bpm (strict)
- **First-line drugs:**
  - **Beta-blockers** (Metoprolol, Bisoprolol) - DOC
  - **Calcium channel blockers** (Diltiazem, Verapamil)
  - **Digoxin** (Only in sedentary/HF patients)

**Rhythm Control** (Cardioversion):
- **Indications:**
  - Symptomatic despite rate control
  - First episode
  - Young patients
  - AF precipitated by reversible cause

- **Methods:**
  - **Electrical cardioversion:** 200-360 J (synchronized)
  - **Pharmacological:** Flecainide, Propafenone, Amiodarone

**<48 hours rule:**
- AF duration **<48 hours:** Cardioversion safe (low thrombus risk)
- AF duration **>48 hours or unknown:**
  - Anticoagulate 3 weeks before cardioversion
  - Or TEE to rule out thrombus
  - Continue anticoagulation 4 weeks after

#### **2. Anticoagulation - MOST IMPORTANT**

**CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score** (Stroke Risk):

```
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│          CHA₂DS₂-VASc SCORE                       │
├────────────────────────────────┬──────────────────┤
│ C - CHF/LV dysfunction         │      1 point     │
│ H - Hypertension               │      1 point     │
│ A₂ - Age ≥75 years             │      2 points    │
│ D - Diabetes mellitus          │      1 point     │
│ S₂ - Stroke/TIA/TE history     │      2 points    │
│ V - Vascular disease           │      1 point     │
│ A - Age 65-74 years            │      1 point     │
│ Sc - Sex category (Female)     │      1 point     │
├────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────┤
│ Maximum Score: 9                                  │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Interpretation:
Score 0 (Male) or 1 (Female): Low risk → No anticoagulation
Score 1 (Male): Consider anticoagulation
Score ≥2: Anticoagulation recommended
```

**HAS-BLED Score** (Bleeding Risk):

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           HAS-BLED SCORE                        │
├──────────────────────────────────┬──────────────┤
│ H - Hypertension (>160 mmHg)     │   1 point    │
│ A - Abnormal renal/liver function│   1-2 points │
│ S - Stroke history               │   1 point    │
│ B - Bleeding history             │   1 point    │
│ L - Labile INR                   │   1 point    │
│ E - Elderly (>65 years)          │   1 point    │
│ D - Drugs/Alcohol                │   1-2 points │
├──────────────────────────────────┴──────────────┤
│ Score ≥3: High bleeding risk (CAUTION)          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Anticoagulation Options:**

**1. Vitamin K Antagonists:**
- **Warfarin:** Target INR 2-3
- Requires regular monitoring

**2. DOACs (Direct Oral Anticoagulants)** - **Preferred:**
- **Dabigatran:** Direct thrombin inhibitor
- **Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Edoxaban:** Factor Xa inhibitors
- **Advantages:** No monitoring, fixed dose, fewer drug interactions
- **Disadvantages:** No reversal agent (except Idarucizumab for Dabigatran), cost

**DOAC Contraindications:**
- Severe renal impairment (CrCl <30 ml/min)
- Mechanical heart valves
- Mitral stenosis (Warfarin preferred)

#### **3. Special Situations**

**Hemodynamically Unstable AF:**
- **Immediate DC cardioversion**
- No waiting for anticoagulation

**Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) with AF:**
- **AVOID:** Digoxin, Beta-blockers, CCBs (may cause VF)
- **Use:** Procainamide, Ibutilide, or DC cardioversion

#### **4. Catheter Ablation**
- **Indications:**
  - Symptomatic paroxysmal AF refractory to drugs
  - Patient preference
  - Tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy

- **Procedure:** Pulmonary vein isolation (PVI)
- **Success rate:** 60-80%

#### **5. Surgical Options**
- **MAZE procedure:** Creates scar tissue to block abnormal circuits
- **LAA closure:** Watchman device (alternative to anticoagulation)

## Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)

### Definition
**≥3 consecutive ventricular beats at rate >100 bpm**

### Classification

**By Duration:**
- **Non-sustained VT (NSVT):** <30 seconds
- **Sustained VT:** ≥30 seconds or requires termination

**By Morphology:**
- **Monomorphic VT:** Same QRS morphology (single focus)
- **Polymorphic VT:** Varying QRS morphology
  - **Torsades de Pointes:** Special type with twisting QRS

### Etiology

**With Structural Heart Disease (90%):**
- **Ischemic heart disease** (Post-MI scar - most common)
- Cardiomyopathies (Dilated, Hypertrophic, ARVC)
- Valvular heart disease
- Congenital heart disease

**Without Structural Heart Disease (10%):**
- **Idiopathic VT:**
  - RVOT VT (Right Ventricular Outflow Tract)
  - Fascicular VT
- Channelopathies:
  - Long QT syndrome
  - Brugada syndrome
  - Catecholaminergic polymorphic VT

**Reversible Causes - "HIKE"**
```
H - Hypoxia
I - Ischemia/Infarction
K - K+ abnormalities (Hypo/Hyperkalemia)
E - Electrolyte imbalance, Drugs
```

### Pathophysiology

**Mechanism:**
- **Re-entry** (Most common) - around MI scar
- Abnormal automaticity
- Triggered activity

### Clinical Features

**Symptoms:**
- Palpitations
- Dizziness, syncope
- Chest pain
- Dyspnea
- **Sudden cardiac death** (if sustained)

**Signs:**
- Tachycardia (>100 bpm, usually 120-250)
- Hypotension
- Signs of poor perfusion
- **Cannon 'a' waves in JVP** (AV dissociation)
- Varying S1 intensity

**Hemodynamic Stability:**
- **Stable:** Conscious, BP maintained
- **Unstable:** Hypotension, altered consciousness, pulmonary edema

### Investigations

#### **ECG Features of VT**

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              VT ON ECG                               │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 1. Wide QRS complex (>120 ms)                        │
│ 2. Rate >100 bpm (usually 120-250)                   │
│ 3. Regular or slightly irregular                     │
│ 4. AV dissociation (P and QRS independent)           │
│ 5. Capture beats (occasional normal QRS)             │
│ 6. Fusion beats (QRS intermediate morphology)        │
│ 7. Positive or negative concordance in chest leads   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Brugada Criteria for VT vs SVT with Aberrancy:**

1. **Absence of RS complex** in all chest leads → VT
2. **R to S interval >100 ms** in any chest lead → VT
3. **AV dissociation** → VT
4. **Morphology criteria:**
   - **RBBB pattern:** R in V1 or QS/QR → VT
   - **LBBB pattern:** R in V1 >30ms, Slurred S downstroke → VT

**VT is more likely if:**
- History of MI/structural heart disease
- Age >35 years
- Wide QRS (>140 ms)

#### **Other Investigations**
- **Echocardiography:** LV function, structural disease
- **Coronary angiography:** CAD
- **Cardiac MRI:** Scar, infiltrative disease, ARVC
- **Electrophysiology study:** Inducibility, ablation

### Management

#### **Acute Management**

**Algorithm:**
```
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         VT ACUTE MANAGEMENT                        │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                    │
│  UNSTABLE (Hypotension/AMS/Chest pain/Pulm edema) │
│               ↓                                    │
│      DC Cardioversion (Synchronized)               │
│      Energy: 100-200 J (Biphasic)                  │
│                                                    │
│  ────────────────────────────────────────────────  │
│                                                    │
│  STABLE                                            │
│               ↓                                    │
│      Correct K+, Mg2+ (Target: K>4, Mg>2)          │
│               ↓                                    │
│      Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min              │
│      (or Procainamide, Sotalol)                    │
│               ↓                                    │
│      If fails → DC Cardioversion                   │
│                                                    │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**NEVER use:**
- **Verapamil in VT** → May cause cardiovascular collapse
- **Adenosine** → Only for VT vs SVT differentiation (if truly uncertain)

#### **Long-term Management**

**1. ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator)** - **MOST IMPORTANT**

**Indications:**
- **Secondary prevention:**
  - Survived cardiac arrest (VT/VF)
  - Sustained VT with hemodynamic compromise

- **Primary prevention:**
  - Post-MI with EF ≤30% (>40 days post-MI)
  - Non-ischemic cardiomyopathy with EF ≤35%
  - Genetic conditions (Brugada, LQTS, HCM)

**2. Anti-arrhythmic Drugs**
- **Amiodarone:** Most effective
- **Sotalol:** Beta-blocker + Class III
- **Beta-blockers:** Post-MI (reduce VT)

**3. Catheter Ablation**
- Monomorphic VT with identifiable scar
- Recurrent VT despite ICD/drugs
- Idiopathic VT (curative)

**4. Treat Underlying Cause**
- Revascularization for ischemia
- Optimize heart failure management
- Correct electrolytes

### Torsades de Pointes (TdP)

**Special type of polymorphic VT with prolonged QT**

**ECG Features:**
- **"Twisting of the points"** - QRS axis rotates around baseline
- Ventricular rate: 200-250 bpm
- Prolonged QT interval (>500 ms)

**Causes - "ABCDE"**
```
A - Anti-arrhythmics (Quinidine, Sotalol, Amiodarone)
B - Bradycardia (Heart block)
C - Congenital (Long QT syndrome)
D - Diuretics (Hypokalemia, Hypomagnesemia)
E - Erythromycin, other drugs (Antipsychotics, TCAs)
```

**Management:**
1. **Magnesium sulfate 2g IV** (Even if Mg2+ normal) - **DRUG OF CHOICE**
2. **Correct electrolytes:** K+ >4.5, Mg2+ >2
3. **Stop offending drugs**
4. **Temporary pacing** (to increase HR and shorten QT)
5. **Isoproterenol** (if pacing not available)
6. **DC cardioversion** (if hemodynamically unstable)

**AVOID:** Standard anti-arrhythmics (may prolong QT further)

## Heart Blocks

### Overview

**Definition:** Impaired conduction from atria to ventricles through AV node/His-Purkinje system

**Classification:**
1. **First-degree AV block**
2. **Second-degree AV block**
   - Mobitz Type I (Wenckebach)
   - Mobitz Type II
3. **Third-degree (Complete) AV block**

### First-Degree AV Block

**Definition:** Prolonged AV conduction without dropped beats

**ECG Features:**
```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      1° AV BLOCK ECG                         │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • PR interval >200 ms (>5 small squares)     │
│ • Every P wave followed by QRS               │
│ • PR interval constant                       │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Causes:**
- **Increased vagal tone** (Athletes)
- Drugs (Beta-blockers, CCBs, Digoxin)
- Inferior MI
- Myocarditis, Lyme disease
- Degenerative conduction disease

**Management:**
- Usually **no treatment required**
- Asymptomatic
- Monitor if drugs/ischemia involved
- **No pacemaker needed**

### Second-Degree AV Block

#### **Mobitz Type I (Wenckebach)**

**ECG Features:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      MOBITZ TYPE I (WENCKEBACH)                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Progressive PR prolongation                   │
│ • Until one P wave not conducted (dropped QRS)  │
│ • Then cycle repeats                            │
│ • "Progressively longer, until dropped, repeat" │
│ • RR interval progressively shortens            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Characteristics:**
- **Site:** AV node (above His bundle)
- Usually **benign**
- Common in **inferior MI** (RCA occlusion)
- Athletes, during sleep

**Management:**
- Usually **no treatment** if asymptomatic
- May resolve spontaneously
- **Temporary pacing** if symptomatic (rare)
- Treat underlying cause (e.g., inferior MI)

#### **Mobitz Type II**

**ECG Features:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      MOBITZ TYPE II                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Constant PR interval                          │
│ • Sudden dropped QRS (without PR prolongation)  │
│ • "Constant, constant, dropped"                 │
│ • Wide QRS if infra-Hisian                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Characteristics:**
- **Site:** Below AV node (His-Purkinje system)
- **More serious** - may progress to complete heart block
- Common in **anterior MI** (LAD occlusion)
- Wide QRS (bundle branch involvement)

**Management:**
- **Permanent pacemaker indicated** (high risk of progression)
- **Temporary pacing** if acute (e.g., MI)
- **Do NOT use Atropine** (may worsen block)

### Third-Degree (Complete) AV Block

**Definition:** Complete dissociation between atria and ventricles

**ECG Features:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      COMPLETE HEART BLOCK                       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • P waves regular                               │
│ • QRS complexes regular                         │
│ • No relationship between P and QRS             │
│ • "P and QRS march independently"               │
│ • Ventricular rate slower (20-40 bpm)           │
│ • Wide QRS if ventricular escape                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Escape Rhythms:**
- **Junctional escape:** 40-60 bpm, narrow QRS (if block at AV node)
- **Ventricular escape:** 20-40 bpm, wide QRS (if block below AV node)

**Causes:**
- **Congenital:** Maternal SLE (anti-Ro antibodies)
- **Acquired:**
  - **Degenerative** (Lev's disease, Lenègre's disease)
  - **Ischemic:** MI (inferior or anterior)
  - **Drugs:** Beta-blockers, CCBs, Digoxin
  - **Infiltrative:** Sarcoidosis, amyloidosis
  - **Infections:** Lyme disease, endocarditis
  - **Post-cardiac surgery**

**Clinical Features:**
- **Bradycardia** (20-40 bpm)
- **Syncope** (Stokes-Adams attacks)
- Fatigue, dyspnea
- Heart failure
- **Sudden cardiac death**

**Management:**

**Acute:**
1. **Atropine 0.5 mg IV** (if narrow QRS/junctional escape)
2. **Temporary pacing** (transcutaneous or transvenous)
3. **Isoproterenol infusion** (if pacing delayed)

**Chronic:**
- **Permanent pacemaker** (Definitive treatment)
- **Indications:**
  - Symptomatic complete heart block
  - Asymptomatic with pauses >3 seconds
  - HR <40 bpm while awake
  - Congenital CHB with wide QRS, symptoms, or ventricular dysfunction

### Comparison Table

```
┌───────────────┬──────────────┬───────────────┬──────────────────┐
│   BLOCK       │  PR INTERVAL │  QRS DROPPED  │  PACEMAKER       │
├───────────────┼──────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ 1° AV Block   │  >200 ms     │      No       │      No          │
│               │  (constant)  │               │                  │
├───────────────┼──────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤

├───────────────┼──────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Mobitz II     │  Constant    │  Yes (sudden) │   YES (Always)   │
│               │              │               │                  │
├───────────────┼──────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ 3° (CHB)      │  No relation │  Many dropped │   YES (Always)   │
│               │  (AV dissoc) │  (indep P,QRS)│                  │
└───────────────┴──────────────┴───────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

## Other Important Arrhythmias

### Atrial Flutter

**Definition:** Regular atrial rhythm at 250-350 bpm with "sawtooth" pattern

**ECG Features:**
- **Sawtooth F waves** (best in II, III, aVF)
- Atrial rate: 300 bpm (typically)
- **Variable AV block:** 2:1, 3:1, 4:1
  - 2:1 block → Ventricular rate 150 bpm
  - 3:1 block → Ventricular rate 100 bpm

**Management:**
- Similar to AF
- **Rate control:** Beta-blockers, CCBs
- **Rhythm control:** Cardioversion (lower energy needed, ~50-100 J)
- **Anticoagulation:** Same as AF (CHA₂DS₂-VASc)
- **Catheter ablation:** Very high success rate (>90%) - **CURATIVE**

### Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)

**Types:**
1. **AVNRT** (AV Nodal Reentrant Tachycardia) - Most common (60%)
2. **AVRT** (AV Reentrant Tachycardia) - Accessory pathway (WPW)
3. **Atrial tachycardia**

**AVNRT:**
- **Mechanism:** Re-entry within AV node (slow-fast pathway)
- **ECG:** Narrow QRS, regular, rate 150-250, **pseudo-r' in V1, pseudo-s in II/III/aVF**
- **Acute management:**
  - **Vagal maneuvers** (Carotid massage, Valsalva) - First
  - **Adenosine 6 mg IV rapid bolus** → 12 mg → 12 mg
  - **Verapamil/Diltiazem** if adenosine fails
  - **DC cardioversion** if unstable
- **Long-term:** Catheter ablation (curative)

**Adenosine:**
- **Mechanism:** Blocks AV node transiently
- **Dose:** 6 mg → 12 mg → 12 mg (rapid IV push)
- **Side effects:** Transient asystole, flushing, chest discomfort
- **Contraindications:** Asthma (bronchospasm), WPW with AF
- **Use:** Diagnostic (unmasks atrial activity) and therapeutic (terminates SVT)

### Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) Syndrome

**Definition:** Pre-excitation syndrome with accessory pathway (Bundle of Kent)

**ECG Features (Sinus Rhythm):**
```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      WPW ECG                                 │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Short PR interval (<120 ms)                │
│ • Delta wave (slurred QRS upstroke)          │
│ • Wide QRS (>120 ms)                         │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Complications:**
- **AVRT** (Orthodromic - narrow QRS; Antidromic - wide QRS)
- **AF with rapid ventricular response** → May degenerate to VF

**Management:**
- **Acute AVRT:**
  - Vagal maneuvers, Adenosine (if narrow QRS)
  - **DC cardioversion** if unstable

- **AF with WPW:**
  - **Procainamide or Ibutilide** (slow accessory pathway)
  - **DC cardioversion** if unstable
  - **AVOID:** Digoxin, Beta-blockers, CCBs, Adenosine (may accelerate conduction through accessory pathway → VF)

- **Long-term:** **Catheter ablation of accessory pathway** (Curative)

### Premature Beats

**PACs (Premature Atrial Contractions):**
- Early P wave (different morphology)
- Followed by QRS (usually narrow)
- **Benign** - no treatment needed

**PVCs (Premature Ventricular Contractions):**
- Wide QRS, no preceding P wave
- **Compensatory pause** (full pause)
- **Benign in structurally normal heart**
- **Concerning if:**
  - Frequent (>10/hour)
  - Multifocal
  - R-on-T phenomenon
  - Associated with structural heart disease
- **Management:** Treat underlying cause, Beta-blockers

## Anti-Arrhythmic Drugs

### Vaughan Williams Classification

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              VAUGHAN WILLIAMS CLASSIFICATION                   │
├───────┬────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┤
│ CLASS │  MECHANISM                 │  DRUGS                    │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  Ia   │ Na+ channel blocker        │ Quinidine, Procainamide,  │
│       │ (Moderate)                 │ Disopyramide              │
│       │ ↑ QRS, ↑ QT                │                           │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  Ib   │ Na+ channel blocker        │ Lidocaine, Mexiletine     │
│       │ (Weak)                     │ **Post-MI VT**            │
│       │ ↓ QT                       │                           │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  Ic   │ Na+ channel blocker        │ Flecainide, Propafenone   │
│       │ (Strong)                   │ **AF cardioversion**      │
│       │ ↑↑ QRS, No QT change       │ (structurally normal)     │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  II   │ Beta-blockers              │ Metoprolol, Esmolol,      │
│       │                            │ Propranolol, Carvedilol   │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  III  │ K+ channel blocker         │ Amiodarone, Sotalol,      │
│       │ ↑ APD, ↑ QT                │ Ibutilide, Dofetilide     │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  IV   │ Ca2+ channel blocker       │ Verapamil, Diltiazem      │
│       │ (Non-DHP)                  │                           │
├───────┼────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│ Other │ Adenosine (AV node block)  │ **SVT**                   │
│       │ Digoxin (↑ vagal)          │ **AF with HF**            │
│       │ Ivabradine (If channel)    │ **Sinus node inhibitor**  │
└───────┴────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Drug Details

**Amiodarone:**
- **Most effective** anti-arrhythmic
- **Uses:** VT, AF, SVT
- **Side effects:** "ABCDEFG"
  - A - Arrhythmias (Bradycardia, TdP)
  - B - Blue-gray skin (photosensitivity)
  - C - Corneal deposits
  - D - Deranged LFTs
  - E - Everything else (Neuropathy, myopathy)
  - F - Fibrosis (Pulmonary - most serious)
  - G - Goiter (Hypo/Hyperthyroidism)
- **Monitoring:** TFTs, LFTs, PFTs, Chest X-ray

**Sotalol:**
- Beta-blocker + Class III
- **Proarrhythmic:** May cause TdP (dose-dependent)
- **Contraindicated:** CrCl <40 ml/min, QT >450 ms

**Digoxin:**
- **Use:** AF with heart failure (rate control)
- **Narrow therapeutic window**
- **Toxicity:** "Yellow vision," Nausea, Arrhythmias (bidirectional VT)
- **Precipitants:** Hypokalemia, Hypercalcemia, Hypomagnesemia, Renal failure
- **Treatment of toxicity:** Digoxin-specific Fab fragments

## NEET PG High-Yield Points

### 🔥 MUST KNOW

1. **AF is the most common sustained arrhythmia**

2. **CHA₂DS₂-VASc score:**
   - **≥2:** Anticoagulation mandatory
   - **Score 0 (male) or 1 (female):** No anticoagulation

3. **DOACs preferred over Warfarin** (except mechanical valves, mitral stenosis)

4. **AF <48 hours:** Cardioversion safe without prior anticoagulation

5. **VT vs SVT with aberrancy:** VT more likely if history of MI, age >35, wide QRS >140 ms

6. **AV dissociation, Capture/Fusion beats → VT**

7. **Torsades de Pointes treatment:** **Magnesium sulfate** (DOC)

8. **Mobitz II requires permanent pacemaker** (unlike Mobitz I)

9. **Complete heart block:** Permanent pacemaker indicated

10. **WPW with AF:** AVOID Digoxin, Beta-blockers, CCBs → Use **Procainamide**

11. **ICD indications:**
    - Post-MI EF ≤30%
    - Survived cardiac arrest
    - Sustained VT with hemodynamic compromise

12. **Adenosine:**
    - Dose: 6 mg → 12 mg → 12 mg (rapid IV)
    - Contraindicated in asthma, WPW with AF

13. **Amiodarone most common side effect:** Pulmonary fibrosis (most serious), Thyroid dysfunction (most common)

14. **Flecainide:** Only in structurally normal heart (CAST study - ↑ mortality post-MI)

### Clinical Scenarios

**Scenario 1:** Irregularly irregular pulse → **AF**

**Scenario 2:** Wide QRS tachycardia in post-MI patient → **VT** (until proven otherwise)

**Scenario 3:** Young patient with palpitations, delta wave on ECG → **WPW**

**Scenario 4:** Progressive PR prolongation, then dropped QRS in inferior MI → **Mobitz I** (Wenckebach)

**Scenario 5:** Constant PR with sudden dropped QRS in anterior MI → **Mobitz II** → Needs **pacemaker**

**Scenario 6:** Patient on Sotalol develops "twisting" QRS tachycardia → **Torsades** → Give **Magnesium**

**Scenario 7:** AF patient, CHA₂DS₂-VASc = 3 → **Anticoagulation mandatory**

### Question 3
A 70-year-old female with inferior MI on day 2 develops bradycardia. ECG shows progressive PR interval prolongation followed by a dropped QRS, then the cycle repeats. Management?

**A)** Permanent pacemaker
**B)** Temporary pacing
**C)** Observation only
**D)** Atropine infusion

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: C) Observation only**

**Explanation:**
- Diagnosis: **Mobitz Type I (Wenckebach)** - progressive PR prolongation until dropped QRS
- Context: **Inferior MI** (RCA occlusion affects AV node)
- Mobitz I is usually **benign** and often **resolves spontaneously**
- No treatment needed if asymptomatic
- If symptomatic (rare): Consider temporary pacing
- **No permanent pacemaker** needed (unlike Mobitz II)
- Atropine may help if symptomatic, but not as infusion
</details>

### Question 4
A patient on Sotalol develops palpitations. ECG shows polymorphic ventricular tachycardia with QRS complexes that appear to "twist" around the baseline. QTc is 580 ms. What is the treatment?

**A)** Amiodarone IV
**B)** Magnesium sulfate 2g IV
**C)** Lidocaine IV
**D)** Increase Sotalol dose

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: B) Magnesium sulfate 2g IV**

**Explanation:**
- Diagnosis: **Torsades de Pointes** (polymorphic VT with "twisting" QRS, prolonged QT)
- Cause: Sotalol (Class III anti-arrhythmic - prolongs QT)
- **Magnesium sulfate** is the **drug of choice** for Torsades
- Give even if serum Mg2+ is normal
- Also:
  - **Stop Sotalol**
  - Correct K+ (>4.5) and Mg2+ (>2)
  - Consider temporary pacing to increase HR (shortens QT)
- **Avoid:** Standard anti-arrhythmics (may worsen by prolonging QT further)
</details>

### Question 6
A patient with WPW syndrome develops AF with rapid ventricular rate of 220 bpm. He is hypotensive. What should be avoided?

**A)** DC cardioversion
**B)** Procainamide
**C)** Ibutilide
**D)** Verapamil

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: D) Verapamil**

**Explanation:**
- **WPW with AF** is a medical emergency (risk of VF)
- **AVOID:** Digoxin, Beta-blockers, CCBs (Verapamil, Diltiazem), Adenosine
  - These block AV node → More conduction through accessory pathway → ↑ Ventricular rate → VF
- **Appropriate treatments:**
  - **DC cardioversion** (if hemodynamically unstable) - **CORRECT ANSWER HERE**
  - **Procainamide or Ibutilide** (slow accessory pathway conduction)
- This patient is **unstable (hypotensive)** → **Immediate DC cardioversion** is the treatment
- Verapamil could be **fatal** in this scenario
</details>

A 60-year-old male with dilated cardiomyopathy and EF 25% presents after syncope. Holter shows non-sustained VT. What is the most appropriate long-term management?

**A)** Amiodarone only
**B)** ICD implantation
**C)** EP study with catheter ablation

  - Symptomatic (syncope) with NSVT

### Question 8
A patient presents with palpitations. ECG shows narrow complex regular tachycardia at 180 bpm. Carotid sinus massage terminates the arrhythmia. What is the most likely diagnosis?

**A)** Atrial fibrillation
**B)** Atrial flutter with 2:1 block
**C)** AVNRT
**D)** Ventricular tachycardia

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: C) AVNRT**

**Explanation:**
- **AVNRT** (AV Nodal Reentrant Tachycardia):
  - **Most common SVT** (60%)
  - **Narrow QRS, regular** tachycardia
  - **Terminates with vagal maneuvers** (carotid massage, Valsalva)
  - Mechanism: Re-entry within AV node
- AF is **irregular**
- Atrial flutter has **sawtooth** pattern and usually doesn't terminate with vagal maneuvers (may slow temporarily)
- VT has **wide QRS** and doesn't respond to vagal maneuvers
</details>
