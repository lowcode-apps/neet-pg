## Overview & Classification

### Definition
**Pneumonia** is **acute infection of the pulmonary parenchyma** (alveoli) characterized by consolidation.

### Classification

**By Acquisition:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. COMMUNITY-ACQUIRED (CAP)                        │
│    • Most common                                   │
│    • Onset outside hospital or <48h admission      │
│                                                    │
│ 2. HOSPITAL-ACQUIRED (HAP)                         │
│    • ≥48 hours after admission                     │
│    • Includes Ventilator-Associated (VAP)          │
│                                                    │
│ 3. ASPIRATION PNEUMONIA                            │
│    • Inhalation of gastric/oropharyngeal contents  │
│                                                    │
│ 4. IMMUNOCOMPROMISED                               │
│    • HIV, Transplant, Chemotherapy                 │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**By Pathology:**
- **Lobar:** Entire lobe (S. pneumoniae)
- **Bronchopneumonia:** Patchy, multiple lobes (S. aureus, Gram-negative)
- **Interstitial:** Alveolar walls (Atypical - Mycoplasma, Viruses)

## Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)

### Definition
**Pneumonia acquired outside hospital** or **<48 hours** after admission.

### Epidemiology
- **Leading infectious cause of death** worldwide
- All ages, more severe in elderly, immunocompromised

### Etiology

**Most Common Organism OVERALL:**
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae (Pneumococcus)** - 30-50%

**Other Bacteria:**
- **Haemophilus influenzae** (COPD, Smokers)
- **Staphylococcus aureus** (Post-influenza, IVDU)
- **Mycoplasma pneumoniae** (Young adults, Atypical)
- **Chlamydophila pneumoniae** (Elderly, Atypical)
- **Legionella pneumophila** (Contaminated water, Atypical)
- **Klebsiella pneumoniae** (Alcoholics, Diabetics - "Currant jelly" sputum)
- **Moraxella catarrhalis** (COPD)

**Viral:**
- **Influenza, RSV, SARS-CoV-2** (COVID-19)

**Fungal/Other:**
- **Pneumocystis jirovecii** (HIV with CD4 <200)

---

### Clinical Features

**Classic (Typical) Pneumonia:**

**Symptoms:**
- **Fever, Rigors** (High-grade fever, Shaking chills)
- **Productive cough** (Purulent sputum)
  - **"Rusty" sputum:** S. pneumoniae
  - **"Currant jelly" sputum:** Klebsiella
- **Pleuritic chest pain** (Sharp, worse with breathing)
- **Dyspnea**

**Signs:**
- **Tachypnea, Tachycardia**
- **Consolidation:**
  - **Inspection:** ↓ Chest expansion (affected side)
  - **Palpation:** ↑ Tactile fremitus
  - **Percussion:** Dull
  - **Auscultation:**
    - **Bronchial breath sounds** (High-pitched, Tubular)
    - **Crackles** (Coarse, Inspiratory)
    - **Bronchophony, Egophony, Whispered pectoriloquy**
- **Cyanosis** (Severe)

**Atypical Pneumonia:** (See separate section)

---

### Diagnosis

**Clinical + Radiological + Microbiological**

#### **1. Chest X-ray - ESSENTIAL**

**Findings:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│      CHEST X-RAY IN PNEUMONIA                      │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Lobar consolidation (Air-space opacity)          │
│   - Homogeneous opacity                            │
│   - Air bronchograms (Air-filled bronchi visible)  │
│ • Patchy/Multifocal opacities (Bronchopneumonia)   │
│ • Pleural effusion (Parapneumonic)                 │
│ • Cavitation (S. aureus, Klebsiella, Anaerobes)    │
│ • Interstitial pattern (Atypical)                  │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Patterns by Organism:**
- **S. pneumoniae:** Lobar consolidation
- **Klebsiella:** Upper lobe, Bulging fissure (Heavy consolidation)
- **S. aureus:** Cavitation, Pneumatoceles
- **Mycoplasma:** Interstitial, Reticulonodular

#### **2. Sputum Examination**

**Gram Stain + Culture:**
- **Quality:** >25 PMNs, <10 epithelial cells per LPF (Valid sample)
- **S. pneumoniae:** Gram-positive diplococci (Lancet-shaped)
- **H. influenzae:** Gram-negative coccobacilli
- **S. aureus:** Gram-positive cocci in clusters
- **Klebsiella:** Gram-negative bacilli (Mucoid)

**Low Yield if:**
- Already on antibiotics
- Atypical organisms (Mycoplasma, Legionella - don't grow on standard culture)

#### **3. Blood Cultures**

**Indications:**
- **Severe CAP** (ICU, Septic shock)
- **Before antibiotics** (If not delaying treatment)

**Positive:** 5-15% of CAP cases

#### **4. Urine Antigen Tests**

**Streptococcus pneumoniae:**
- **Binax NOW** (Rapid antigen test)
- **High sensitivity/specificity**
- Positive even after antibiotics started

**Legionella pneumophila:**
- Urine antigen (Detects serogroup 1 - 80% of cases)

#### **5. Laboratory Tests**

**CBC:**
- **Leukocytosis** with **left shift** (Bacterial)
  - **>15,000/μL:** Suggests bacterial
  - **<4,000 or >30,000:** Poor prognosis
- **Normal/Low WBC:** Viral, Atypical, Immunocompromised

**Inflammatory Markers:**
- **CRP:** Elevated (↑ In bacterial, Less in viral/atypical)
- **Procalcitonin:** ↑ In bacterial (Guide antibiotic therapy)

**ABG:**
- **Hypoxemia** (PaO₂ <60 mmHg or SpO₂ <90%)
- Assess severity

**Renal, LFTs:** Assess complications

#### **6. Thoracentesis**

**If significant pleural effusion** (>10 mm on lateral decubitus)
- **Parapneumonic effusion** (Exudate - meets Light's criteria)
- **Empyema:** Pus (pH <7.2, Glucose <40 mg/dL, LDH >1000)
- **Complicated:** Needs drainage

### Severity Assessment

#### **CURB-65 Score (Outpatient vs Inpatient Decision)**

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              CURB-65 SCORE                         │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ C - Confusion (New onset)                     1    │
│ U - Urea >7 mmol/L (BUN >20 mg/dL)            1    │
│ R - Respiratory rate ≥30/min                  1    │
│ B - Blood pressure (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60)       1    │
│ 65 - Age ≥65 years                            1    │
│                                                    │
│ Total: 0-5                                         │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

INTERPRETATION:
• Score 0-1: Outpatient treatment (Mortality <3%)
• Score 2: Consider hospitalization (Mortality 9%)
• Score ≥3: Hospitalization (Severe - Mortality 15-40%)
  - Consider ICU
```

**Simplified CRB-65:** (Without Urea - Outpatient setting)
- Same, but exclude Urea
- Score ≥1: Consider hospitalization

#### **Pneumonia Severity Index (PSI/PORT Score)**

**More complex** (20 variables - Age, Comorbidities, Vitals, Labs)
- Classes I-V
- **Class I-II:** Outpatient
- **Class III:** Inpatient brief stay or observation
- **Class IV-V:** Hospitalization

#### **SMART-COP (ICU Admission)**

**Severe CAP requiring ICU if score ≥3:**
- **S**ystolic BP <90 mmHg (2 points)
- **M**ultilobar involvement on CXR (1)
- **A**lbumin <3.5 g/dL (1)
- **R**espiratory rate: Age ≤50: ≥25/min, Age >50: ≥30/min (1)
- **T**achycardia ≥125/min (1)
- **C**onfusion (1)
- **O**xygenation: Age ≤50: PaO₂ <70, Age >50: <60 (2)
- **P**H <7.35 (2)

---

### Management

#### **General Measures**

**Supportive:**
- **Oxygen:** Target SpO₂ ≥90% (92-96% if not COPD)
- **IV Fluids:** Hydration
- **Antipyretics:** Paracetamol
- **Chest physiotherapy**

---

#### **Antibiotic Therapy**

**Principles:**
- **Start empirically** (Don't wait for culture)
- **Within 4-8 hours** of diagnosis (↓ Mortality)
- **De-escalate** based on culture results

**Duration:** **5-7 days** (Minimum 5 days, Afebrile ≥48-72 hours)

---

**OUTPATIENT (CURB-65 0-1):**

**Previously Healthy, No Antibiotics in 3 months:**
- **Amoxicillin 1 g TID** (First-line)
  - OR **Doxycycline 100 mg BD**
  - OR **Azithromycin 500 mg OD × 3 days** (Covers atypical)

**Comorbidities OR Antibiotics in 3 months:**
- **Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 625 mg TID** (or 1g BD)
  - **+ Azithromycin 500 mg OD** (Add if atypical suspected)
- **OR Respiratory fluoroquinolone** (Levofloxacin 750 mg OD, Moxifloxacin 400 mg OD)

---

**INPATIENT NON-ICU (CURB-65 2):**

- **IV β-Lactam (Ampicillin-Sulbactam 1.5 g q6h, Ceftriaxone 1-2 g OD, Cefotaxime 1 g q8h)**
  - **+ Azithromycin 500 mg OD** (or Doxycycline)
- **OR Respiratory fluoroquinolone** alone (Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin)

**Switch to PO** when clinically improving, tolerating oral

---

**ICU (CURB-65 ≥3 or Severe):**

- **IV β-Lactam (Ceftriaxone 2 g OD OR Cefotaxime 2 g q8h OR Ampicillin-Sulbactam 3 g q6h)**
  - **+ Azithromycin 500 mg OD** (or Levofloxacin)

**If Pseudomonas Risk** (Bronchiectasis, Severe COPD, Recent hospitalization, Prior Pseudomonas):
- **Antipseudomonal β-Lactam:**
  - **Piperacillin-Tazobactam 4.5 g q6h** OR
  - **Cefepime 2 g q8h** OR
  - **Meropenem 1 g q8h**
  - **+ Fluoroquinolone (Levofloxacin) OR Aminoglycoside (Gentamicin)**

**If MRSA Risk** (Recent hospitalization, IVDU, Severe influenza):
- **Add Vancomycin 15 mg/kg q12h** (or Linezolid)

---

#### **Prevention**

**Pneumococcal Vaccine:**
- **PCV13** (Conjugate) + **PPSV23** (Polysaccharide)
- **All ≥65 years**, Chronic diseases, Immunocompromised

**Influenza Vaccine:**
- **Annual** (All adults, especially elderly, Chronic diseases)

**Smoking Cessation**

---

### Complications

**1. Pleural Effusion:**
- **Parapneumonic:** Exudate (Sterile initially)
- **Empyema:** Pus (pH <7.2, Glucose <40, LDH >1000)
  - **Treatment:** Chest tube drainage + Antibiotics

**2. Lung Abscess:**
- **Cavitation with air-fluid level**
- **Organisms:** Anaerobes (Aspiration), S. aureus, Klebsiella
- **Treatment:** Prolonged antibiotics (4-6 weeks), Drainage if large

**3. Septic Shock:**
- **Severe sepsis** → Hypotension, Organ dysfunction
- **ICU, Vasopressors, Broad-spectrum antibiotics**

**4. Respiratory Failure:**
- **ARDS** (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome)
- **Mechanical ventilation**

**5. Metastatic Infections:**
- **Meningitis, Endocarditis, Septic arthritis** (S. pneumoniae)

## Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia (HAP)

### Definition
**Pneumonia occurring ≥48 hours after hospital admission** (Not incubating at admission)

**Includes:**
- **Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP):** ≥48 hours after intubation

### Epidemiology
- **2nd most common nosocomial infection** (After UTI)
- **Highest mortality** among nosocomial infections

### Risk Factors

**"MIST-HAP"**
```
M - Mechanical ventilation (Biggest risk for VAP)
I - Intubation, ICU stay
S - Supine position (Head of bed <30°)
T - Tracheostomy

H - H2 blockers, PPI (↑ Gastric pH → Bacterial overgrowth)
A - Antibiotics (Prior use → Resistant organisms)
P - Poor hand hygiene
```

**Other:**
- Age >70, Chronic lung disease, Immunosuppression, Malnutrition

### Etiology

**Different from CAP - More resistant organisms**

**Early HAP (<5 days):**
- **S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae** (Similar to CAP)
- **MSSA** (Methicillin-sensitive S. aureus)

**Late HAP (≥5 days) or Risk Factors:**
- **Gram-negative bacilli:**
  - **Pseudomonas aeruginosa** (Most common)
  - **Klebsiella pneumoniae** (ESBL-producing)
  - **Acinetobacter baumannii**
  - **E. coli, Enterobacter**
- **MRSA** (Methicillin-resistant S. aureus)
- **Polymicrobial** (Common)

### Diagnosis

**Clinical:**
- **New infiltrate on CXR**
- **+ ≥2 of:** Fever, Leukocytosis/Leukopenia, Purulent sputum

**Microbiological:**
- **Tracheal aspirate** (Intubated patients)
- **Bronchoalveolar lavage (BAL)** or **Protected specimen brush (PSB)** (Gold standard)
  - **Quantitative cultures:**
    - BAL: ≥10⁴ CFU/mL
    - PSB: ≥10³ CFU/mL
- **Blood cultures**

**Clinical Pulmonary Infection Score (CPIS):**
- Helps diagnose VAP (Temperature, WBC, Tracheal secretions, Oxygenation, CXR, Culture)
- **Score >6:** Likely pneumonia

### Management

**Empiric Antibiotics (Broad-spectrum):**

**Cover Pseudomonas + MRSA (If risk factors):**
```
Antipseudomonal β-Lactam:
  • Piperacillin-Tazobactam 4.5 g q6h, OR
  • Cefepime 2 g q8h, OR
  • Meropenem 1 g q8h
           +
Antipseudomonal Fluoroquinolone (Levofloxacin) or Aminoglycoside
           +
Anti-MRSA: Vancomycin or Linezolid
```

**De-escalate** based on culture (within 48-72 hours)

**Duration:** **7-8 days** (Shorter if good response, Non-fermenting GNB may need 14 days)

### Prevention

**VAP Bundle:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         VAP PREVENTION BUNDLE                      │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Elevate head of bed 30-45°                       │
│ • Daily sedation vacation + Spontaneous breathing  │
│   trial (Assess extubation readiness)              │
│ • Oral care with chlorhexidine                     │
│ • Subglottic suctioning (ETT with suction port)    │
│ • Avoid gastric overdistension                     │
│ • DVT prophylaxis                                  │
│ • Stress ulcer prophylaxis (Only if indicated)     │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Early Extubation** (Most effective)

## Aspiration Pneumonia

### Definition
**Pneumonia following aspiration of gastric or oropharyngeal contents**

### Risk Factors

**"ASPIRATION"**
```
A - Altered consciousness (Stroke, Seizure, Anesthesia, Alcohol)
S - Swallowing dysfunction (Dysphagia - Stroke, Parkinson's, Esophageal)
P - Poor dentition
I - Intubation (Prolonged)
R - Recumbent position
A - Achalasia, GERD
T - Tracheostomy
I - Impaired gag reflex
O - Obstruction (Esophageal tumor)
N - NG tube
```

### Pathophysiology

**Two Syndromes:**

**1. Chemical Pneumonitis (Mendelson's Syndrome):**
- **Aspiration of gastric acid** (pH <2.5)
- **Acute inflammation** (Not infection)
- **Rapid onset** (Within hours)
- **Clinical:** Dyspnea, Tachypnea, Hypoxemia, Bilateral infiltrates
- **Management:** Supportive (Oxygen, Bronchodilators), **Antibiotics NOT needed** (Unless secondary bacterial infection)

**2. Aspiration Pneumonia (Bacterial):**
- **Aspiration of oropharyngeal bacteria**
- **Anaerobes** (Most common): Bacteroides, Peptostreptococcus, Fusobacterium
- **Develops over days**
- **Predilection:** **Right lower lobe** (if upright), **Superior segments of lower lobes or posterior segments of upper lobes** (if supine)

### Clinical Features

- **Subacute onset** (Days)
- **Fever, Productive cough** (Foul-smelling sputum - Anaerobes)
- **CXR:** Infiltrate in dependent areas (RLL if upright, Posterior/Superior segments if supine)
- **May progress to lung abscess** (Cavitation with air-fluid level)

### Management

**Aspiration Pneumonitis:**
- **Supportive care** (Oxygen, Fluids)
- **Bronchodilators**
- **Antibiotics:** Only if secondary bacterial infection suspected (Fever persists >48-72 hours)

**Aspiration Pneumonia (Bacterial):**
- **Antibiotics covering anaerobes:**
  - **Ampicillin-Sulbactam 3 g q6h** (First-line)
  - **OR Clindamycin 600 mg q8h + Cephalosporin**
  - **OR Moxifloxacin 400 mg OD** (Covers anaerobes)
- **Duration:** 7-14 days (Longer if lung abscess - 4-6 weeks)

**Prevention:**
- **Head of bed elevation** (30-45°)
- **Swallowing assessment** before oral feeding (Stroke patients)
- **Thickened liquids** (if dysphagia)
- **Oral care**

## Atypical Pneumonia

### Definition
**Pneumonia caused by "atypical" organisms** that don't respond to β-lactams and have distinct clinical features.

**Organisms:**
1. **Mycoplasma pneumoniae**
2. **Chlamydophila pneumoniae**
3. **Legionella pneumophila**
4. **Viruses** (Influenza, RSV, Adenovirus, SARS-CoV-2)

### Features Distinguishing from Typical

```
┌────────────────┬──────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│                │ TYPICAL          │ ATYPICAL        │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Onset          │ Acute            │ Gradual         │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Fever          │ High-grade       │ Low-grade       │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Cough          │ Productive       │ Dry (Non-prod)  │
│                │ (Purulent)       │                 │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Sputum Gram    │ Organisms seen   │ No organisms    │
│ stain          │                  │                 │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ CXR            │ Lobar consol.    │ Interstitial/   │
│                │                  │ Patchy          │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ WBC            │ High (>15,000)   │ Normal/Mild ↑   │
├────────────────┼──────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Response to    │ Good             │ Poor            │
│ β-lactam       │                  │                 │
└────────────────┴──────────────────┴─────────────────┘
```

---

### Mycoplasma pneumoniae

**Epidemiology:**
- **Young adults** (5-40 years) - "Walking pneumonia"
- Outbreaks in closed communities (Schools, Military)

**Clinical:**
- **Gradual onset**
- **Dry cough** (Persistent, Paroxysmal)
- **Low-grade fever, Headache, Malaise**
- **Extrapulmonary manifestations** (20%):
  - **Bullous myringitis** (Ear - Hemorrhagic blisters on TM)
  - **Hemolytic anemia** (Cold agglutinins - IgM anti-I)
  - **Erythema multiforme** (Skin - Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
  - **Encephalitis, Guillain-Barré syndrome** (Rare)

**CXR:**
- **Interstitial/Reticulonodular pattern**
- **Patchy infiltrates** (Often looks worse than clinical symptoms - "Walking pneumonia")

**Diagnosis:**
- **Cold agglutinins:** Positive (Agglutination at 4°C) - Non-specific
- **IgM antibodies** (Serology - Acute infection)
- **PCR** (Most sensitive)

**Treatment:**
- **Macrolides:** Azithromycin 500 mg OD × 3-5 days (DOC)
- **OR Doxycycline** 100 mg BD × 7-10 days
- **OR Respiratory fluoroquinolone**

---

### Legionella pneumophila

**Epidemiology:**
- **Contaminated water sources** (Air conditioning, Cooling towers, Hot tubs)
- **Elderly, Smokers, Immunocompromised**

**Clinical:**
- **Severe pneumonia**
- **High fever, Rigors**
- **Dry cough** (Later productive)
- **Extrapulmonary features:**
  - **GI:** Diarrhea, Nausea, Vomiting (Common - 30-50%)
  - **CNS:** Confusion, Headache
  - **Hyponatremia** (SIADH)
  - **↑ Transaminases**
  - **Relative bradycardia** (Fever-pulse dissociation)

**CXR:**
- **Patchy/Lobar consolidation**
- **Pleural effusion** (Common)

**Diagnosis:**
- **Urine antigen test** (Detects L. pneumophila serogroup 1 - 80%) - **Best initial test**
  - Rapid, Sensitive, Remains positive even after antibiotics
- **Culture:** Buffered charcoal yeast extract (BCYE) agar (Gold standard but slow)
- **PCR**

**Treatment:**
- **Respiratory fluoroquinolone:** Levofloxacin 750 mg OD × 7-10 days (DOC)
- **OR Azithromycin** 500 mg OD × 5-7 days
- **Severe:** Add Rifampin to fluoroquinolone

---

### Chlamydophila pneumoniae

**Epidemiology:**
- **Elderly**
- **Sore throat** preceding pneumonia (Common)

**Clinical:**
- **Mild atypical pneumonia**
- **Dry cough, Hoarseness**
- **Pharyngitis**

**Diagnosis:**
- **Serology** (IgM, IgG)
- **PCR**

**Treatment:**
- **Macrolides** (Azithromycin) or **Doxycycline**

## NEET PG High-Yield Points

### 🔥 MUST KNOW

1. **Most Common Cause of CAP:** **Streptococcus pneumoniae** (30-50%)

2. **Rusty Sputum:** S. pneumoniae

3. **Currant Jelly Sputum:** Klebsiella (Alcoholics, Diabetics)

4. **CURB-65 Score:**
   - **0-1:** Outpatient
   - **2:** Consider hospitalization
   - **≥3:** Hospitalize (ICU if needed)

5. **Empiric Antibiotics CAP (Outpatient):**
   - **Healthy:** Amoxicillin or Doxycycline
   - **Comorbid:** Amoxicillin-Clavulanate + Azithromycin (or Respiratory FQ)

6. **Empiric Antibiotics CAP (Inpatient):**
   - **β-Lactam + Azithromycin** (or Respiratory FQ alone)

7. **Empiric Antibiotics CAP (ICU):**
   - **Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin** (or Levofloxacin)
   - Add Vancomycin if MRSA risk

8. **HAP/VAP Organisms:** **Pseudomonas, MRSA** (Most common)

9. **HAP/VAP Treatment:** Antipseudomonal β-lactam + Antipseudomonal FQ/Aminoglycoside + Vancomycin

10. **VAP Prevention:** Elevate head 30-45°, Oral care, Daily sedation vacation, Early extubation

11. **Aspiration Pneumonia:**
    - **Chemical pneumonitis:** NO antibiotics (Supportive)
    - **Bacterial:** Anaerobes → **Ampicillin-Sulbactam or Clindamycin**
    - **Predilection:** Right lower lobe (upright), Posterior/Superior segments (supine)

12. **Mycoplasma pneumoniae:**
    - **Young adults**, "Walking pneumonia"
    - **Dry cough**, **Bullous myringitis**, **Cold agglutinins**
    - **Treatment:** Azithromycin (DOC)

13. **Legionella:**
    - **Water source**, **Diarrhea**, **Hyponatremia**, **Relative bradycardia**
    - **Urine antigen test** (Best initial)
    - **Treatment:** Levofloxacin (DOC) or Azithromycin

14. **Pneumococcal Vaccine:** All ≥65 years (PCV13 + PPSV23)

15. **Empyema Criteria:** pH <7.2, Glucose <40 mg/dL, LDH >1000 → **Chest tube drainage**

### Clinical Scenarios

**Scenario 1:** Fever, rusty sputum, lobar consolidation → **S. pneumoniae CAP**

**Scenario 2:** Alcoholic, currant jelly sputum, upper lobe consolidation with bulging fissure → **Klebsiella pneumoniae**

**Scenario 3:** Post-influenza, cavitation on CXR → **S. aureus pneumonia**

**Scenario 4:** Young adult, dry cough, bullous myringitis, cold agglutinins → **Mycoplasma**

**Scenario 5:** Elderly, severe pneumonia, diarrhea, hyponatremia, relative bradycardia → **Legionella**

**Scenario 6:** ICU patient, ≥48 hours post-intubation, new infiltrate → **VAP** (Pseudomonas, MRSA)

**Scenario 7:** Stroke patient, aspiration, foul-smelling sputum, RLL infiltrate → **Aspiration pneumonia** (Anaerobes) → Ampicillin-Sulbactam

**Scenario 8:** Empyema with pH 7.1, glucose 30 → **Chest tube drainage**

### Quick Revision Checklist

- [ ] S. pneumoniae: Most common CAP, Rusty sputum
- [ ] CURB-65: 0-1 outpatient, 2 consider admission, ≥3 admit
- [ ] CAP antibiotics: Amoxicillin (healthy), Amox-Clav + Azithro (comorbid), β-lactam + Azithro (inpatient)
- [ ] HAP/VAP: Pseudomonas, MRSA
- [ ] VAP prevention: Head elevation, Oral care, Early extubation
- [ ] Aspiration: Anaerobes, Ampicillin-Sulbactam
- [ ] Mycoplasma: Young, Dry cough, Bullous myringitis, Azithromycin
- [ ] Legionella: Water, Diarrhea, Hyponatremia, Levofloxacin
- [ ] Empyema: pH <7.2, Glucose <40 → Drain

## Practice Questions

### Question 1
A 65-year-old presents with fever, productive cough with rusty sputum, and pleuritic chest pain. CXR shows right lower lobe consolidation. CURB-65 score is 2. Most appropriate management?

**A)** Outpatient Amoxicillin
**B)** Hospitalize, IV Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin
**C)** Outpatient Azithromycin only
**D)** ICU admission

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: B) Hospitalize, IV Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin**

**Explanation:**
- **CURB-65 = 2:** Consider hospitalization (Mortality ~9%)
- **Likely S. pneumoniae** (Rusty sputum, Lobar consolidation)
- **Inpatient treatment:**
  - **IV β-Lactam** (Ceftriaxone, Ampicillin-Sulbactam) + **Azithromycin**
  - OR Respiratory FQ alone

**Why not others:**
- Outpatient: CURB-65 ≥2 → Consider admission
- ICU: CURB-65 ≥3 or severe (This patient is moderate)
</details>

**A)** Streptococcus pneumoniae

**D)** Mycoplasma pneumoniae

<details>

**Answer: B) Klebsiella pneumoniae**

**Explanation:**
- **Klebsiella pneumoniae** pneumonia:
  - **"Currant jelly" sputum** (Mucoid, Bloody)
  - **Upper lobe** predilection
  - **Bulging fissure** (Heavy, gelatinous consolidation)
  - **Risk factors:** Alcoholics, Diabetics, Aspiration
  - **Gram-negative bacilli** (Mucoid colonies)

**Others:**
- S. pneumoniae: Rusty sputum
- S. aureus: Post-influenza, Cavitation
- Mycoplasma: Atypical, Dry cough

### Question 3
A 25-year-old presents with gradual onset dry cough, low-grade fever, and bullous myringitis. CXR shows patchy interstitial infiltrates. Cold agglutinins are positive. What is the most appropriate treatment?

**A)** Amoxicillin
**B)** Ceftriaxone
**C)** Azithromycin
**D)** Vancomycin

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: C) Azithromycin**

**Explanation:**
- **Diagnosis:** **Mycoplasma pneumoniae** pneumonia
  - **Young adult**, **Gradual onset**
  - **Dry cough**, **Bullous myringitis** (Hemorrhagic blisters on TM)
  - **Cold agglutinins** positive
  - **CXR:** Interstitial/Patchy

**Treatment:**
- **Macrolides:** **Azithromycin 500 mg OD × 3-5 days** (DOC)
- OR Doxycycline, Respiratory FQ

**Why not β-lactams:**
- Mycoplasma lacks cell wall → β-lactams (Amoxicillin, Ceftriaxone) ineffective
</details>
