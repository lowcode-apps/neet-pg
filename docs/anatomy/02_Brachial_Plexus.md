# BRACHIAL PLEXUS - NEET PG Study Material

## Overview
Network of nerves supplying upper limb
**Roots**: C5, C6, C7, C8, T1
**Location**: Posterior triangle of neck → Axilla → Upper limb

---

## Structure - "**R**oots **T**runks **D**ivisions **C**ords **B**ranches"

```
C5  ─┐
     ├──── Upper Trunk ───┬─── Anterior Division ───┐
C6  ─┘                    │                         ├─── Lateral Cord
                          └─── Posterior Division ──┼─── Posterior Cord
C7  ────── Middle Trunk ──┬─── Anterior Division ───┤
                          └─── Posterior Division ──┤
C8  ─┐                    │                         │
     ├──── Lower Trunk ───┬─── Anterior Division ───┘
T1  ─┘                    └─── Posterior Division ───┘
```

**Mnemonic for organization**: "**R**obert **T**aylor **D**rinks **C**old **B**eer"

---

## 1. ROOTS (5) - C5, C6, C7, C8, T1

**Branches from Roots**:
- **Dorsal scapular nerve** (C5) → Rhomboids, Levator scapulae
- **Long thoracic nerve** (C5, C6, C7) → Serratus anterior

**NEET PG**: Long thoracic nerve injury → **Winged scapula**

---

## 2. TRUNKS (3)

### Upper Trunk (C5 + C6)
**Branches**:
- **Suprascapular nerve** → Supraspinatus + Infraspinatus
- **Nerve to subclavius** → Subclavius

### Middle Trunk (C7)
**No branches**

### Lower Trunk (C8 + T1)
**No direct branches**

---

## 3. DIVISIONS (6)
- 3 Anterior divisions
- 3 Posterior divisions

**No branches from divisions** - just rearrangement!

---

## 4. CORDS (3)

Named according to relation with **Axillary artery**

### Lateral Cord (Anterior divisions of Upper + Middle trunks)

**Branches**:
1. **Lateral pectoral nerve** → Pectoralis major
2. **Musculocutaneous nerve** (terminal)
3. **Lateral root of median nerve** (terminal)

### Medial Cord (Anterior division of Lower trunk)

**Branches**:
1. **Medial pectoral nerve** → Pectoralis major + minor
2. **Medial cutaneous nerve of arm**
3. **Medial cutaneous nerve of forearm**
4. **Ulnar nerve** (terminal)
5. **Medial root of median nerve** (terminal)

### Posterior Cord (All 3 Posterior divisions)

**Branches**:
1. **Upper subscapular nerve** → Subscapularis
2. **Lower subscapular nerve** → Subscapularis + Teres major
3. **Thoracodorsal nerve** → Latissimus dorsi
4. **Axillary nerve** (terminal)
5. **Radial nerve** (terminal)

**Mnemonic for Posterior cord branches**: "**U**pper **L**ower **T**horaco **A**xillary **R**adial"

---

## 5. TERMINAL BRANCHES (5)

### MUSCULOCUTANEOUS NERVE (C5, C6, C7)
**Origin**: Lateral cord
**Course**: Pierces coracobrachialis → Between biceps & brachialis
**Motor**:
- Coracobrachialis
- Biceps brachii
- Brachialis

**Sensory**: Lateral cutaneous nerve of forearm

**Testing**: Elbow flexion with forearm supinated

**Injury**: Weak elbow flexion, loss of sensation over lateral forearm

---

### MEDIAN NERVE (C5-T1)
**Origin**: Medial + Lateral cords ("M" shaped formation)
**Course**: Arm → Cubital fossa → Forearm (between FDS & FDP) → Carpal tunnel → Hand

**Motor**:

**Forearm** (All flexors EXCEPT FCU & medial half FDP):
- Pronator teres (PT)
- Flexor carpi radialis (FCR)
- Palmaris longus (PL)
- Flexor digitorum superficialis (FDS)
- Lateral half of FDP (to index & middle fingers)
- Flexor pollicis longus (FPL)
- Pronator quadratus (PQ)

**Mnemonic**: "**P**lease **F**ind **P**almaris **F**DS **F**DP **F**PL **P**Q"

**Hand** (Thenar + Lumbricals):
- Abductor pollicis brevis (APB)
- Flexor pollicis brevis (FPB) - superficial head
- Opponens pollicis (OP)
- Lateral 2 lumbricals

**Mnemonic for Thenar**: "**A**ll **F**or **O**ne" (APB, FPB, OP)

**Sensory**: Palmar side of lateral 3½ digits + nail beds

**Branches**:
- **Anterior interosseous nerve** (AIN): FPL, lateral FDP, PQ

**Testing**:
- **Pen test**: Ask to hold pen between thumb & index finger
- **OK sign**: Cannot make "OK" sign (AIN lesion)
- **Ape hand**: Thenar wasting, thumb in plane of hand

**Injuries**:

| Site | Features | Name |
|------|----------|------|
| Elbow/Forearm | Cannot flex DIP of index, cannot flex IP of thumb, loss of pronation | AIN syndrome |
| Wrist (Carpal tunnel) | Thenar wasting, ape hand, nocturnal pain, +Tinel's, +Phalen's | Carpal Tunnel Syndrome |
| Complete | Cannot oppose thumb, cannot flex wrist radially, sensory loss | Median nerve palsy |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Carpal tunnel syndrome**: Most common nerve compression, affects median nerve at wrist
- **Diagnosis**: Tinel's sign (tapping), Phalen's test (wrist flexion 60 sec)
- **Treatment**: Wrist splint, steroid injection, surgical release
- **AIN syndrome**: Cannot make "OK" sign, no sensory loss

---

### ULNAR NERVE (C8, T1)
**Origin**: Medial cord
**Course**: Medial arm → Behind medial epicondyle (funny bone) → Ulnar side of forearm → Guyon's canal → Hand

**Motor**:

**Forearm**:
- Flexor carpi ulnaris (FCU)
- Medial half of FDP (to ring & little fingers)

**Hand**:
- **Hypothenar muscles**: Abductor digiti minimi, Flexor digiti minimi, Opponens digiti minimi
- **Interossei**: Palmar (PAD) + Dorsal (DAB)
- **Lumbricals**: Medial 2 (to ring & little fingers)
- **Adductor pollicis**
- **Deep head of FPB**

**Mnemonic**: "Ulnar does it **A**ll" (All intrinsic hand muscles except LOAF)
**LOAF** = Median supplies → Lateral 2 lumbricals, Opponens pollicis, Abductor pollicis brevis, Flexor pollicis brevis

**Sensory**: Medial 1½ digits (both palmar & dorsal)

**Testing**:
- **Froment's sign**: Paper between thumb & index, thumb IP flexes (using FPL as adductor pollicis weak)
- **Card test**: Cannot adduct/abduct fingers
- **Wartenberg's sign**: Little finger abducted

**Injuries**:

| Site | Features | Name |
|------|----------|------|
| Elbow | Complete ulnar palsy | Cubital tunnel syndrome |
| Wrist (Guyon's canal) | Spares dorsal sensory branch | Cyclist's palsy |
| Complete | Claw hand (hyperextended MCP, flexed PIP/DIP of 4th & 5th digits), Froment's +ve | Ulnar claw |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Claw hand**: MORE clawed with distal lesion (Paradox of ulnar nerve)
  - Proximal lesion: FDP paralyzed → cannot flex → less clawed
  - Distal lesion: FDP intact → can flex → more clawed
- **Froment's sign**: Pathognomonic for ulnar nerve injury

---

### AXILLARY NERVE (C5, C6)
**Origin**: Posterior cord
**Course**: Quadrangular space (with posterior circumflex humeral artery)

**Motor**:
- **Deltoid**: Shoulder abduction (15-90°)
- **Teres minor**: Lateral rotation of shoulder

**Sensory**: Regimental badge area (over lateral deltoid)

**Testing**: Shoulder abduction

**Injury**:
- **Causes**: Anterior shoulder dislocation, fracture surgical neck of humerus
- **Features**: Cannot abduct shoulder beyond 15° (supraspinatus does 0-15°)
- **Sensation loss**: Regimental badge area

**NEET PG**: Quadrangular space contains axillary nerve + posterior circumflex humeral artery

---

### RADIAL NERVE (C5-T1)
**Origin**: Posterior cord (largest branch)
**Course**: Posterior arm (spiral groove of humerus) → Lateral epicondyle → Forearm → Hand

**Motor**:

**Arm**:
- Triceps brachii (all 3 heads)
- Anconeus
- Brachioradialis
- Extensor carpi radialis longus (ECRL)

**Forearm** (All extensors):
- Extensor carpi radialis brevis (ECRB)
- Supinator
- Extensor digitorum (ED)
- Extensor digiti minimi (EDM)
- Extensor carpi ulnaris (ECU)
- Abductor pollicis longus (APL)
- Extensor pollicis brevis (EPB)
- Extensor pollicis longus (EPL)
- Extensor indicis (EI)

**Sensory**: Posterior arm, forearm, dorsal lateral 3½ digits (excluding nail beds)

**Branches**:
- **Posterior interosseous nerve** (PIN): Deep branch, motor to all extensors in forearm

**Testing**:
- Extend wrist/fingers
- Extend thumb at IP joint
- Anatomical snuffbox sensation

**Injuries**:

| Site | Features | Name |
|------|----------|------|
| Axilla | Complete palsy: wrist drop, finger drop, thumb drop, triceps weak | Crutch palsy |
| Spiral groove | Wrist drop, finger drop, triceps spared (branches above) | Saturday night palsy |
| Forearm (PIN) | Cannot extend fingers/thumb, wrist extension present (ECRL spared) | Posterior interosseous syndrome |

**Saturday Night Palsy**: Arm hanging over chair while drunk → radial nerve compression in spiral groove

**Wrist Drop**: Cannot extend wrist, pathognomonic for radial nerve injury

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Most commonly injured nerve** in humeral shaft fracture
- **Wrist drop**: Classic sign of radial nerve palsy
- **Triceps preserved**: If injury below spiral groove

## Brachial Plexus Injuries

### ERB'S PALSY (Upper Trunk - C5, C6)
**Causes**:
- Birth injury (shoulder dystocia)
- Trauma (motorcycle accident)

**Muscles affected**:
- Deltoid, supraspinatus, infraspinatus (shoulder movements)
- Biceps, brachialis (elbow flexion)
- Supinator, brachioradialis (forearm supination)

**Position**: **"Waiter's Tip"** or **"Porter's Tip"**
- Shoulder adducted & internally rotated
- Elbow extended
- Forearm pronated
- Wrist flexed

**NEET PG**: Birth injury, shoulder dystocia

---

### KLUMPKE'S PALSY (Lower Trunk - C8, T1)
**Causes**:
- Birth injury (arm pulled up during delivery)
- Trauma (grabbing overhead object while falling)

**Muscles affected**:
- Intrinsic hand muscles
- Long finger flexors

**Position**: **"Claw Hand"**
- Hyperextended MCP
- Flexed IP joints

**Associated**: **Horner's syndrome** (T1 involvement affects sympathetic chain)
- Ptosis
- Miosis
- Anhidrosis

**NEET PG**: Associated with Horner's syndrome

---

### COMPLETE BRACHIAL PLEXUS INJURY
**Cause**: Severe trauma (motorcycle accident)
**Features**: Entire upper limb paralyzed, anesthetic, flail arm

---

## Important Relations - HIGH YIELD

### Quadrangular Space
**Boundaries**:
- Superior: Teres minor
- Inferior: Teres major
- Medial: Long head of triceps
- Lateral: Surgical neck of humerus

**Contents**:
- Axillary nerve
- Posterior circumflex humeral artery

---

### Triangular Space
**Boundaries**:
- Superior: Teres minor
- Inferior: Teres major
- Lateral: Long head of triceps

**Contents**: Circumflex scapular artery

---

### Triangular Interval
**Boundaries**:
- Superior: Teres major
- Medial: Long head of triceps
- Lateral: Lateral head of triceps/humerus

**Contents**:
- Radial nerve
- Profunda brachii artery

## Mnemonics - Must Know

### Terminal Branches (Lateral to Medial)
"**M**y **A**unt **R**aised **M**y **U**ncle"
- **Musculocutaneous
- **Axillary
- **Radial
- **Median
- **Ulnar

### Cords Formation
- **L**ateral cord: **L**ateral + **L**ateral (C5, C6, C7 anterior divisions)
- **M**edial cord: **M**edial (C8, T1 anterior division)
- **P**osterior cord: **P**osterior **P**osterior **P**osterior (All 3 posterior divisions)

### Median vs Ulnar in Hand
**LOAF** supplied by **M**edian:
- **L**ateral 2 lumbricals
- **O**pponens pollicis
- **A**bductor pollicis brevis
- **F**lexor pollicis brevis (superficial head)

**Rest** supplied by **U**lnar

### Rotator Cuff Muscles
"**S**its **I**n **T**he **S**uper **B**owl"
- **S**upraspinatus: Abduction 0-15°
- **I**nfraspinatus: Lateral rotation
- **T**eres minor: Lateral rotation
- **S**ubscapularis: Medial rotation

## Practice MCQs

1. **Long thoracic nerve palsy causes?**
   Answer: Winged scapula

2. **Nerve passing through carpal tunnel?**
   Answer: Median nerve

3. **Most common site of radial nerve injury?**
   Answer: Spiral groove (mid-shaft humerus fracture)

4. **Erb's palsy involves which roots?**
   Answer: C5, C6 (Upper trunk)

5. **Froment's sign tests which nerve?**
   Answer: Ulnar nerve

6. **Nerve passing through quadrangular space?**
   Answer: Axillary nerve

7. **Inability to make OK sign indicates?**
   Answer: Anterior interosseous nerve lesion

8. **Claw hand is more clawed in?**
   Answer: Distal ulnar nerve lesion (paradox)

## Clinical Testing Summary

| Sign/Test | Nerve | What it shows |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Wrist drop | Radial | Cannot extend wrist |
| Ape hand | Median | Thenar wasting |
| Claw hand | Ulnar | Hyperextended MCP, flexed IP |
| Waiter's tip | C5, C6 | Erb's palsy |
| Froment's sign | Ulnar | Thumb IP flexes when holding paper |
| OK sign | Median (AIN) | Cannot flex DIP index, IP thumb |
| Tinel's sign | Median | Tapping wrist causes paresthesia |
| Phalen's test | Median | Wrist flexion causes symptoms |
| Winged scapula | Long thoracic | Scapula protrudes posteriorly |
| Wartenberg's sign | Ulnar | Little finger abducted |
