# ADRENERGIC AGONISTS (Sympathomimetics) - NEET PG Study Material

## SYMPATHETIC NEUROTRANSMISSION

### Catecholamine Synthesis

```
Tyrosine
  ↓ [Tyrosine hydroxylase] ← Rate-limiting step
DOPA
  ↓ [DOPA decarboxylase]
Dopamine
  ↓ [Dopamine β-hydroxylase]
Norepinephrine (NE)
  ↓ [PNMT - only in adrenal medulla]
Epinephrine (E)
```

**NEET PG**: Tyrosine hydroxylase = Rate-limiting enzyme

### Catecholamine Metabolism

**Enzymes**:
1. **MAO** (Monoamine oxidase) - intracellular
2. **COMT** (Catechol-O-methyltransferase) - extracellular

**End product**: VMA (Vanillylmandelic acid) - excreted in urine

**NEET PG**: VMA in urine - diagnostic for pheochromocytoma

## CLASSIFICATION OF SYMPATHOMIMETICS

### By Mechanism

**A. Direct-acting**: Directly stimulate receptors
- Epinephrine, Norepinephrine, Dopamine
- Phenylephrine, Clonidine
- Salbutamol, Dobutamine

**B. Indirect-acting**: Release stored NE
- Amphetamine, Tyramine, Ephedrine (partial)

**C. Mixed**: Both direct + indirect
- Ephedrine, Pseudoephedrine

### By Chemical Structure

**A. Catecholamines** (3,4-dihydroxybenzene):
- Epinephrine, Norepinephrine, Dopamine
- Dobutamine, Isoprenaline
- **Characteristics**:
  - Cannot cross BBB (polar)
  - Metabolized by MAO & COMT
  - **Short duration** of action
  - Cannot be given orally (first-pass metabolism)

**B. Non-catecholamines**:
- Phenylephrine, Ephedrine, Amphetamine
- Salbutamol, Terbutaline
- **Characteristics**:
  - Can cross BBB
  - Longer duration
  - Can be given orally

**NEET PG**: Catecholamines have short duration, can't cross BBB

## DIRECT-ACTING SYMPATHOMIMETICS

### EPINEPHRINE (Adrenaline)

**Receptor activity**: **α1, α2, β1, β2** (all receptors)

---

#### Cardiovascular Effects (Dose-dependent)

**Low dose** (β effects predominate):
- **β1**: ↑ HR, ↑ contractility, ↑ cardiac output
- **β2**: Vasodilation (skeletal muscle vessels)
- **Net effect**: ↑ SBP, ↓ DBP, ↓ Total peripheral resistance (TPR)

**High dose** (α effects predominate):
- **α1**: Vasoconstriction (all vessels)
- **Net effect**: ↑ SBP, ↑ DBP, ↑ TPR

**NEET PG**: Low-dose epinephrine ↓ DBP (β2 vasodilation)

---

#### Other Actions

**Respiratory**:
- **Bronchodilation** (β2) - powerful
- ↓ Mediator release (mast cells)

**Metabolic**:
- ↑ Glycogenolysis (β2) → hyperglycemia
- ↑ Lipolysis (β3)
- ↑ Glucagon, ↓ insulin secretion

**Eye**: Mydriasis (α1)

**GU**: Uterine relaxation (β2)

**CNS**: Minimal (doesn't cross BBB)

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Anaphylaxis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Drug of choice**
- IM/IV administration
- Reverses bronchospasm, hypotension, angioedema

**2. Cardiac arrest**
- ACLS protocol
- IV/Intraosseous/Endotracheal

**3. Acute asthma** (severe)
- Nebulized/IM
- Rapid bronchodilation

**4. With local anesthetics**
- ↓ Absorption (vasoconstriction)
- Prolongs duration
- ↓ Systemic toxicity
- **Contraindicated**: Fingers, toes, penis (risk of gangrene)

**5. Glaucoma**
- ↓ Aqueous humor production

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Epinephrine = Drug of choice for anaphylaxis
- Don't use with LA in digits

---

#### Adverse Effects

- Anxiety, tremor, palpitations
- **Cerebral hemorrhage** (hypertensive crisis)
- **Arrhythmias** (ventricular)
- Pulmonary edema
- Hyperglycemia

**Contraindications**:
- Hypertension
- Hyperthyroidism
- Coronary artery disease
- Closed-angle glaucoma

---

### NOREPINEPHRINE (Noradrenaline)

**Receptor activity**: **α1, α2, β1** (NO β2)

---

#### Cardiovascular Effects

- **α1**: Intense vasoconstriction (all vessels)
- **β1**: ↑ Contractility
- **Net effect**:
  - ↑ SBP, ↑ DBP, ↑ TPR
  - **Reflex bradycardia** (baroreceptor response to ↑ BP)
  - ↓ or no change in cardiac output

**NEET PG**: NE causes reflex bradycardia (vs epinephrine ↑ HR)

---

#### Clinical Uses

**Shock** (cardiogenic, septic):
- Potent **vasoconstrictor**
- Maintains BP
- Preferred in septic shock (over dopamine)

**NEET PG**: NE = Drug of choice for septic shock

---

#### Adverse Effects

- **Severe hypertension**
- **Tissue necrosis** if extravasation (α1-mediated)
  - **Antidote**: **Phentolamine** (α-blocker) - local infiltration
- Reflex bradycardia
- Reduced organ perfusion (intense vasoconstriction)

**NEET PG**: Phentolamine for NE extravasation

---

### DOPAMINE

**Receptor activity** (dose-dependent):
- **Low dose** (1-2 mcg/kg/min): **D1** receptors
- **Medium dose** (2-10 mcg/kg/min): **β1** receptors
- **High dose** (>10 mcg/kg/min): **α1** receptors

---

#### Effects by Dose

**Low dose ("Renal dose")**:
- **D1**: Renal & mesenteric vasodilation
- ↑ Renal blood flow
- ↑ GFR, ↑ Na⁺ excretion

**Medium dose**:
- **β1**: ↑ Contractility, ↑ HR, ↑ cardiac output
- Minimal vasoconstriction

**High dose**:
- **α1**: Vasoconstriction
- Similar to norepinephrine

**NEET PG**: Dopamine dose-dependent effects

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Shock** (cardiogenic, septic)
- Start medium dose
- Adjust based on response

**2. Heart failure** (acute)
- ↑ Cardiac output

**NEET PG**: Dopamine for cardiogenic shock

---

#### Adverse Effects

- Tachycardia, arrhythmias
- Nausea, vomiting (D2 receptors - CTZ)
- Tissue necrosis (if extravasation)
- ↑ Pulmonary shunt (worsen hypoxemia)

---

### DOBUTAMINE

**Receptor activity**: **β1 >> β2** (selective β1 agonist)

---

#### Actions

- **β1**: ↑ Contractility (↑ inotropy)
- Minimal ↑ HR (less than dopamine)
- **β2**: Mild vasodilation
- **Net**: ↑ Cardiac output, ↓ preload, ↓ afterload

**NEET PG**: Dobutamine = ↑ contractility without much ↑ HR

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Acute heart failure** (decompensated)
- ↑ Cardiac output
- **Preferred over dopamine** (less arrhythmogenic)

**2. Cardiogenic shock**

**3. Stress echocardiography**
- Assess cardiac reserve

**NEET PG**: Dobutamine preferred over dopamine in heart failure

---

### ISOPRENALINE (Isoproterenol)

**Receptor activity**: **β1, β2** (pure β-agonist, NO α)

---

#### Actions

- **β1**: ↑ HR, ↑ contractility, ↑ cardiac output
- **β2**: Bronchodilation, vasodilation
- **Net**: ↑ SBP, ↓ DBP, ↓ TPR

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Heart block** (AV block, bradycardia)
- Emergency, until pacemaker

**2. Shock** (rarely used now)

**NEET PG**: Isoprenaline for AV block (emergency)

---

### PHENYLEPHRINE

**Receptor activity**: **Pure α1 agonist**

---

#### Actions

- **α1**: Vasoconstriction
- ↑ BP (systolic + diastolic)
- **Reflex bradycardia**
- Mydriasis (if topical on eye)

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Hypotension** (shock, anesthesia-induced)

**2. Nasal decongestant**
- Vasoconstriction of nasal mucosa

**3. Mydriatic** (eye drops)
- Pupil dilation (fundoscopy)

**4. Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)**
- ↑ BP → reflex vagal stimulation → ↓ HR

**NEET PG**: Phenylephrine = Pure α1 agonist, causes reflex bradycardia

---

### CLONIDINE

**Receptor activity**: **α2 agonist** (central + peripheral)

---

#### Mechanism & Actions

**Central α2 agonist**:
- Stimulates α2 in medulla
- ↓ Sympathetic outflow
- **↓ BP, ↓ HR**

**Peripheral α2**:
- Initial vasoconstriction (transient ↑ BP)

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Hypertension**
- Especially with renal disease

**2. Opioid withdrawal**
- ↓ Sympathetic symptoms

**3. ADHD** (off-label)

**4. Prophylaxis of migraine**

**NEET PG**: Clonidine = Central α2 agonist, antihypertensive

---

#### Adverse Effects

- Sedation, dry mouth
- **Rebound hypertension** (if stopped abruptly)
- Bradycardia

**NEET PG**: Clonidine rebound hypertension if stopped suddenly

---

## SELECTIVE β2 AGONISTS

### Short-Acting β2 Agonists (SABA)

**SALBUTAMOL (Albuterol), TERBUTALINE, LEVOSALBUTAMOL**

**Receptor**: **Selective β2**

---

#### Actions

- **Bronchodilation** (β2 on bronchial smooth muscle)
- Uterine relaxation (tocolytic)
- Tremor (β2 on skeletal muscle)
- ↑ K⁺ uptake (hypokalemia)

---

#### Clinical Uses

**1. Asthma** (acute attack)
- **Reliever/Rescue medication**
- Nebulized/MDI

**2. COPD** (symptomatic relief)

**3. Hyperkalemia** (emergency)
- Shifts K⁺ into cells

**4. Preterm labor** (tocolytic - terbutaline)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Salbutamol = Asthma reliever
- Salbutamol for hyperkalemia (shifts K⁺ intracellularly)

---

#### Adverse Effects

- **Tremor** (most common)
- Tachycardia, palpitations
- Hypokalemia
- Hyperglycemia

**NEET PG**: SABA cause tremor, hypokalemia

---

### Long-Acting β2 Agonists (LABA)

**SALMETEROL, FORMOTEROL**

**Duration**: 12 hours

---

#### Clinical Uses

**Asthma & COPD** (chronic maintenance):
- **Controller medication**
- **Always used with inhaled corticosteroids** (not alone)
- Given twice daily

**NEET PG**: LABA never used alone in asthma (risk of death)

---

## INDIRECT & MIXED ACTING SYMPATHOMIMETICS

### AMPHETAMINE

**Mechanism**: Indirect (releases stored NE, blocks reuptake)

**Actions**:
- CNS stimulation (alertness, ↓ appetite)
- Cardiovascular: ↑ BP, ↑ HR
- **Tolerance** develops

**Uses**:
- **ADHD** (methylphenidate preferred)
- **Narcolepsy**
- Weight loss (off-label, not recommended)

**Adverse effects**:
- **Addiction, dependence**
- Psychosis
- Hypertension, arrhythmias

**NEET PG**: Amphetamine = ADHD, narcolepsy, abuse potential

---

### EPHEDRINE

**Mechanism**: Mixed (direct + indirect)

**Actions**:
- Similar to epinephrine but:
  - Oral bioavailability (non-catecholamine)
  - Longer duration
  - CNS effects (crosses BBB)

**Uses**:
- **Nasal decongestant**
- **Hypotension** (anesthesia-induced)
- Bronchodilator (now rarely used)

**NEET PG**: Ephedrine = Mixed-acting, nasal decongestant

## High-Yield Drug Comparison

| Drug | Receptors | Key Use | Key Point |
|------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Epinephrine** | α, β1, β2 | Anaphylaxis | DOC for anaphylaxis |
| **Norepinephrine** | α, β1 | Septic shock | Causes reflex bradycardia |
| **Dopamine** | D1, β1, α1 (dose-dependent) | Cardiogenic shock | Dose-dependent effects |
| **Dobutamine** | β1 >> β2 | Heart failure | ↑ Contractility, preferred in HF |
| **Isoprenaline** | β1, β2 | AV block | Pure β-agonist |
| **Phenylephrine** | α1 | Nasal decongestant | Pure α1, reflex bradycardia |
| **Salbutamol** | β2 | Asthma (acute) | SABA, reliever |
| **Salmeterol** | β2 | Asthma (maintenance) | LABA, never alone |
| **Clonidine** | α2 (central) | Hypertension | Rebound HTN if stopped |

## Practice MCQs

1. **Drug of choice for anaphylaxis?**
   Answer: Epinephrine

2. **Drug of choice for septic shock?**
   Answer: Norepinephrine

3. **Which sympathomimetic causes reflex bradycardia?**
   Answer: Norepinephrine, Phenylephrine

4. **Antidote for norepinephrine extravasation?**
   Answer: Phentolamine (local infiltration)

5. **Beta-blocker for acute asthma attack?**
   Answer: Salbutamol (SABA)

6. **LABA should never be used alone in asthma because?**
   Answer: Increases risk of asthma-related death

7. **Selective β1 agonist used in heart failure?**
   Answer: Dobutamine

8. **Central α2 agonist causing rebound hypertension?**
   Answer: Clonidine
