# Pneumonia - Pathology

## DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Pneumonia**: **Acute inflammation of lung parenchyma** (alveoli) caused by infectious agents ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Characterized by**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Alveolar consolidation** (air spaces filled with inflammatory exudate) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Neutrophilic infiltration** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pulmonary edema**

---

## CLASSIFICATION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### BY ANATOMIC DISTRIBUTION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Lobar Pneumonia**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Entire lobe** involved ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae** (most common) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Homogeneous consolidation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**2. Bronchopneumonia**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Patchy, multifocal consolidation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Centered around bronchioles** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bilateral, lower lobes** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Multiple organisms** (Staph, H. flu, Pseudomonas) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**3. Interstitial (Atypical) Pneumonia**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Interstitium** primarily affected ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Minimal alveolar exudate** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Viruses, Mycoplasma, Chlamydia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### BY CLINICAL SETTING ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**2. Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia (HAP)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**3. Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**4. Aspiration Pneumonia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## LOBAR PNEUMONIA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### ETIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Streptococcus pneumoniae** (95%) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Others: Klebsiella pneumoniae, Legionella, H. influenzae

---

### PATHOLOGY - STAGES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Classic 4 Stages** (correlate with days): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Stage 1: CONGESTION** (Day 1-2): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Heavy, **red, boggy lung** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Vascular congestion** (dilated capillaries) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Proteinaceous fluid** in alveoli
  - **Few bacteria, few neutrophils** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Stage 2: RED HEPATIZATION** (Day 3-4): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Red, firm, airless** (liver-like consistency) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Dry, granular cut surface**
- **Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Alveoli filled with: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Neutrophils** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **RBCs** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (extravasated - gives red color)
    - **Fibrin** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - Bacteria
  - **Fibrinous pleuritis** (pleuritic chest pain) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Stage 3: GRAY HEPATIZATION** (Day 5-7): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Gray-brown, firm, dry** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Massive neutrophils** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Fibrin** (prominent) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **RBCs disintegrate** (gray color) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Few bacteria** (being cleared) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Stage 4: RESOLUTION** (Day 8-10+): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Normal/slightly firm**
  - **Moist cut surface**
- **Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Macrophages** phagocytose debris ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Enzymatic digestion** of exudate ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Resorption/coughing out** of exudate
  - **Lung architecture restored** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Complete recovery** (no scarring in uncomplicated cases) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### KEY HIGH-YIELD POINTS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

- **Red hepatization**: RBCs ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gray hepatization**: No RBCs (disintegrated), massive neutrophils ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Complete resolution** without scarring (if uncomplicated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fibrinous pleuritis** in Stage 2-3 → pleuritic chest pain ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## BRONCHOPNEUMONIA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### ETIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Multiple organisms**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Staphylococcus aureus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **H. influenzae** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pseudomonas aeruginosa** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Klebsiella pneumoniae** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Risk Factors**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Extremes of age (infants, elderly)
- Debilitation, immunosuppression
- Chronic lung disease (COPD)

---

### PATHOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Patchy, scattered consolidation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bilateral**, **lower lobes** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gray-red to yellow** (suppurative)
- **Centered around bronchi/bronchioles** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Acute suppurative exudate** in bronchioles/alveoli ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Neutrophils** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Purulent material**

**Complications**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Abscess formation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Empyema** (pus in pleural space) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organization** → scarring (vs lobar - complete resolution) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## SPECIFIC PNEUMONIAS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### KLEBSIELLA PNEUMONIAE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**"Friedländer's Pneumonia"**

**Risk Factors**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Alcoholics** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diabetics** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Chronic lung disease
- Nursing home residents

**Features**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Upper lobes** (esp. right upper lobe) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thick, mucoid, gelatinous sputum** ("currant jelly") ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bulging fissure** on CXR ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Necrotizing** → **abscess/cavitation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- High mortality

---

### STAPHYLOCOCCUS AUREUS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Settings**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Post-influenza** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (most common)
- **IV drug users** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hospitalized patients** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cystic fibrosis** ⭐⭐⭐

**Features**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bronchopneumonia** pattern
- **Necrotizing** → **multiple abscesses** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pneumatoceles** (thin-walled cysts) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Empyema** common ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bilateral, multilobar** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### LEGIONELLA PNEUMOPHILA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**"Legionnaires' Disease"**

**Source**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Water systems** (cooling towers, air conditioning, hot water tanks) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Risk Factors**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Elderly, smokers
- Immunosuppressed
- Chronic lung disease

**Pathology**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Lobar consolidation**
- **Fibrinopurulent exudate**
- **Neutrophils + macrophages**

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Urinary antigen** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (most sensitive/specific)
- **Silver stain** (Dieterle, Warthin-Starry) to visualize bacteria ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Culture on **BCYE agar** (buffered charcoal yeast extract) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Macrolides** (azithromycin) or **fluoroquinolones** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### MYCOPLASMA PNEUMONIAE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**"Walking Pneumonia"**

**Pathology**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Interstitial pneumonia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mononuclear infiltrate** (lymphocytes, plasma cells) in interstitium ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Minimal alveolar exudate** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**See Microbiology Topic 17 for full clinical details** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## COMPLICATIONS OF PNEUMONIA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Abscess**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Suppurative destruction** of lung tissue ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organisms**: Anaerobes, Staph, Klebsiella ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Aspiration** most common cause ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cavity with air-fluid level** on CXR ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Foul-smelling sputum** (anaerobes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**2. Empyema**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pus in pleural space** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Staph, Strep pneumoniae** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**3. Organization**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Granulation tissue** fills alveoli → **fibrosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organized pneumonia** (formerly "BOOP")

**4. Bacteremia/Sepsis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Can lead to **meningitis, endocarditis, septic arthritis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**5. Respiratory Failure**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **ARDS** (acute respiratory distress syndrome) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## HIGH-YIELD PATHOLOGY PEARLS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

1. **Lobar pneumonia** = homogeneous consolidation, Strep pneumoniae, complete resolution ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
2. **Red hepatization** = RBCs + neutrophils + fibrin ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
3. **Gray hepatization** = neutrophils + fibrin, NO RBCs ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
4. **Bronchopneumonia** = patchy, bilateral, lower lobes, abscess-prone ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
5. **Klebsiella** = alcoholics, currant jelly sputum, upper lobes, bulging fissure ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
6. **Staph** = post-influenza, abscesses, pneumatoceles ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
7. **Legionella** = water source, urinary antigen, silver stain, BCYE agar ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
8. **Mycoplasma** = interstitial pattern, mononuclear cells ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## MNEMONICS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Lobar Pneumonia Stages**: "**C**an **R**eally **G**et **R**esolution" (Congestion, Red hepatization, Gray hepatization, Resolution)

**Red vs Gray Hepatization**: "**R**ed has **R**BCs, **G**ray **G**one (no RBCs)"

**Klebsiella Risk**: "**K**lebsiella likes **A**lcoholics" (Alcoholics, upper lobes, currant jelly)

**Legionella Diagnosis**: "**L**egionella = **U**rinary antigen, **S**ilver stain, **B**CYE" (LUS-B)

## NEET PG MCQS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Q1.** Pneumonia with red hepatization. Microscopic finding?
A) Only neutrophils B) Neutrophils + RBCs + fibrin C) Macrophages only D) Granulomas

**Answer: B** - Red hepatization (Stage 2) has neutrophils + RBCs + fibrin

**Q2.** Alcoholic with upper lobe pneumonia, thick gelatinous sputum. Organism?
A) Strep pneumoniae B) Klebsiella C) Staph aureus D) Legionella

**Answer: B** - Klebsiella: Alcoholics, upper lobes, currant jelly sputum

**Q3.** Post-influenza pneumonia with multiple abscesses. Most likely organism?
A) Strep pneumoniae B) Klebsiella C) Staph aureus D) Mycoplasma

**Answer: C** - Staph aureus: Post-influenza, necrotizing, multiple abscesses

**Q4.** Pneumonia with minimal alveolar exudate, mononuclear interstitial infiltrate. Pattern?
A) Lobar B) Bronchopneumonia C) Interstitial D) Necrotizing

**Answer: C** - Interstitial (atypical) pneumonia: Minimal alveolar exudate, interstitial mononuclear cells (Mycoplasma, viruses)

**Q5.** Best diagnostic test for Legionella pneumonia?
A) Sputum culture B) Blood culture C) Urinary antigen D) Gram stain

**Answer: C** - Urinary antigen is most sensitive/specific for Legionella
