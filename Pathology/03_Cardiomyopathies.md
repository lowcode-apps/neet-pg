# Cardiomyopathies

## DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Cardiomyopathy**: Primary disease of **myocardium** (heart muscle) causing structural and functional abnormalities ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Excludes**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Ischemic heart disease
- Hypertensive heart disease  
- Valvular heart disease
- Congenital heart disease

---

## CLASSIFICATION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**By Morphology & Function** (WHO/ISFC): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

1. **Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
2. **Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
3. **Restrictive Cardiomyopathy (RCM)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
4. **Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC)** ⭐⭐⭐
5. **Unclassified** (Takotsubo, Left Ventricular Non-compaction)

---

## 1. DILATED CARDIOMYOPATHY (DCM) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Ventricular dilatation + systolic dysfunction** (↓ EF) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Most common cardiomyopathy** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### ETIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Genetic** (30-50%): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Autosomal dominant** (most common) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mutations**: Titin, lamin A/C, desmin, dystrophin
- **X-linked**: Duchenne/Becker muscular dystrophy ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Toxic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Alcohol** (most common identifiable cause) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Chemotherapy**: Doxorubicin (Adriamycin), trastuzumab ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Cocaine, methamphetamine
- **Cobalt** (beer drinker's cardiomyopathy)

**Infectious**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Viral myocarditis** (Coxsackie B, adenovirus, parvovirus B19) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Chagas disease** (*Trypanosoma cruzi*) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- HIV

**Metabolic/Nutritional**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thiamine deficiency** (beriberi) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Selenium deficiency** (Keshan disease)
- Diabetes mellitus
- Thyroid disease (hypo/hyperthyroidism)

**Peripartum Cardiomyopathy**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Last month of pregnancy** to **5 months postpartum** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- No pre-existing heart disease
- **Risk factors**: Multiparity, advanced maternal age, African descent, preeclampsia
- **50% recover**, 25% remain stable, 25% worsen
- **High risk of recurrence** in subsequent pregnancies ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Idiopathic** (most cases): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- No identifiable cause (likely genetic)

---

### PATHOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Globular, enlarged heart** (↑ weight, often >500g) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **All chambers dilated** (LV and RV) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Normal/thin wall thickness** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mural thrombi** (esp. LV apex) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Myocyte hypertrophy
- **Interstitial fibrosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Variable myocyte degeneration

---

### CLINICAL FEATURES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Age**: 20-60 years (younger than ischemic)

**Presentation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Progressive heart failure** (biventricular) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Dyspnea** (exertional → rest)
  - **Fatigue**
  - **Peripheral edema**
- **Arrhythmias** (atrial fibrillation, ventricular arrhythmias) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thromboembolism** (mural thrombi → stroke, PE) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sudden cardiac death** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Physical Exam**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **S3 gallop** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Displaced apex** (laterally and inferiorly)
- **Mitral/tricuspid regurgitation** murmurs (functional - dilated annulus)
- Signs of CHF (↑ JVP, rales, edema)

---

### DIAGNOSIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Echocardiography** (diagnostic): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Dilated ventricles** (LV end-diastolic diameter >55 mm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Reduced ejection fraction** (**EF <40%**) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Global hypokinesis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thin walls** (normal thickness)

**ECG**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sinus tachycardia**
- **Left ventricular hypertrophy** (voltage criteria)
- **Atrial fibrillation** (common)
- **Conduction abnormalities** (LBBB, AV blocks)
- **Q waves** (mimic old MI)

**Chest X-ray**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cardiomegaly** (↑ cardiothoracic ratio >0.5) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Pulmonary congestion

**Cardiac MRI**: ⭐⭐⭐
- Gold standard for volumes, EF
- Late gadolinium enhancement (mid-wall fibrosis)

**Endomyocardial Biopsy**: ⭐⭐⭐
- Rarely needed
- To diagnose specific causes (sarcoid, giant cell myocarditis, hemochromatosis)

---

### TREATMENT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Standard Heart Failure Therapy**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. ACE Inhibitors/ARBs**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **First-line** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- ↓ Mortality, ↓ remodeling

**2. Beta-Blockers**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- ↓ Mortality, improve EF

**3. Diuretics**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Symptom relief** (pulmonary congestion, edema)
- Furosemide, bumetanide

**4. Aldosterone Antagonists**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Spironolactone, eplerenone** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- If EF <35% despite ACE-I + β-blocker

**5. Anticoagulation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- If **atrial fibrillation** or **mural thrombus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Warfarin (INR 2-3) or DOACs

**6. ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **EF ≤35%** despite optimal therapy ≥3 months ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Prevents sudden cardiac death

**7. CRT (Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Biventricular pacing**
- If EF ≤35% + **LBBB** (QRS ≥150 ms) + symptomatic despite therapy

**8. Heart Transplantation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **End-stage** refractory to medical therapy

**9. Treat Underlying Cause**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Alcohol cessation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thiamine** (if deficiency)

---

## 2. HYPERTROPHIC CARDIOMYOPATHY (HCM) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Inappropriate LV hypertrophy** (in absence of HTN/AS) + **diastolic dysfunction** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Hallmark**: **Asymmetric septal hypertrophy** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### ETIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Genetic** (>50%): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Autosomal dominant** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sarcomeric protein mutations**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **β-myosin heavy chain** (most common) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Myosin-binding protein C** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Troponin T, α-tropomyosin

**Sporadic** (no family history)

---

### PATHOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Massive LV hypertrophy** (wall thickness ≥15 mm, often >30 mm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Asymmetric septal hypertrophy** (septum : posterior wall ratio ≥1.3) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Small LV cavity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Normal/supranormal EF** (hyperdynamic) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mitral valve thickening** (SAM - systolic anterior motion) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Myocyte disarray** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (chaotic, haphazard arrangement - pathognomonic)
- **Interstitial fibrosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Myocyte hypertrophy

---

### PATHOPHYSIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Diastolic Dysfunction**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Stiff, hypertrophied LV** → impaired relaxation → ↓ filling ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Main problem** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**2. LV Outflow Tract Obstruction (LVOTO)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Subaortic obstruction** (60-70% of HCM) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mechanisms**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Systolic anterior motion (SAM) of mitral valve** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - Hypertrophied septum narrows outflow tract → Venturi effect → anterior leaflet pulled toward septum
  - **Hypertrophied septum** bulges into outflow tract
- **Dynamic obstruction**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **↑ Contractility** → ↑ obstruction (exercise, inotropes, tachycardia)
  - **↓ Preload** → ↑ obstruction (Valsalva, standing, dehydration) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **↓ Afterload** → ↑ obstruction (vasodilators)

**3. Mitral Regurgitation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- From SAM (incomplete coaptation)

**4. Myocardial Ischemia**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- ↑ O₂ demand (massive hypertrophy) + ↓ supply (small vessels, diastolic dysfunction)

**5. Arrhythmias**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sudden cardiac death** (ventricular arrhythmias - VT/VF) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### CLINICAL FEATURES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Age**: Adolescents/young adults (also older)

**Presentation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Asymptomatic** (50%): ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Detected on screening (FHx, murmur, ECG)

**2. Symptomatic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Dyspnea** (exertional) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Diastolic dysfunction → ↑ LA pressure → pulmonary congestion
- **Angina** (exertional) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Myocardial ischemia (↑ demand, ↓ supply)
- **Syncope/presyncope** (exertional) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - LVOTO (inadequate CO with exercise)
  - Arrhythmias
- **Palpitations** (atrial fibrillation, VT) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**3. Sudden Cardiac Death**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most common cause of sudden death in young athletes** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Often **first presentation** (no prior symptoms)
- **During/after exercise** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Mechanism: **Ventricular fibrillation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Physical Exam**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Harsh systolic murmur** (crescendo-decrescendo) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Left sternal border** (LVOT obstruction)
  - Radiates to axilla (NOT carotids - vs AS)
- **Dynamic murmur**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **↑ With maneuvers that ↓ preload/↑ contractility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Valsalva** (strain phase) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Standing** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - Amyl nitrite
  - **↓ With maneuvers that ↑ preload/↓ contractility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Squatting** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Passive leg raise** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Handgrip** (↑ afterload) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bisferiens pulse** (double systolic peak) ⭐⭐⭐
- **Prominent "a" wave** (JVP - forceful atrial contraction against stiff RV)
- **S4** (atrial kick against stiff ventricle) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### DIAGNOSIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Echocardiography** (diagnostic): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **LV wall thickness ≥15 mm** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (in absence of HTN/AS)
- **Asymmetric septal hypertrophy** (septum ≥1.3× posterior wall) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Small LV cavity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hyperdynamic systolic function** (EF >65%) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **SAM of mitral valve** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **LVOT gradient** (at rest or with provocation):
  - ≥30 mmHg = obstruction ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**ECG**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **LV hypertrophy** (voltage criteria) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Deep, narrow Q waves** (septal depolarization) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Lateral leads (I, aVL, V5-V6), inferior leads (II, III, aVF)
- **T-wave inversion** (giant in apical HCM)
- **Atrial enlargement** (broad P wave in lead II)

**Cardiac MRI**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Better assessment of apex, RV
- Late gadolinium enhancement (fibrosis - marker of sudden death risk)

**Genetic Testing**: ⭐⭐⭐
- Family screening

---

### RISK STRATIFICATION FOR SCD ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Major Risk Factors**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Prior cardiac arrest/sustained VT** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Family history of premature SCD** (<50 years) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Unexplained syncope** (recent, recurrent, exertional) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Massive LVH** (≥30 mm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Non-sustained VT** on Holter ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Abnormal BP response** to exercise (fail to ↑ ≥20 mmHg) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**≥1 major risk factor** → **Consider ICD** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### TREATMENT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Goals**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
1. **Relieve symptoms** (diastolic dysfunction, LVOTO)
2. **Prevent sudden cardiac death**

**A. Medical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Beta-Blockers**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **First-line** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Metoprolol, atenolol, propranolol** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mechanism**: ↓ HR (↑ filling time), ↓ contractility (↓ LVOTO)

**2. Calcium Channel Blockers**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Verapamil, diltiazem** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- If β-blockers ineffective/contraindicated
- **Avoid dihydropyridines** (vasodilation → ↓ afterload → ↑ LVOTO)

**3. Disopyramide**: ⭐⭐⭐
- **Negative inotrope** (↓ LVOTO)
- Add-on if symptoms persist

**AVOID**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Nitrates** (↓ preload → ↑ LVOTO) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **ACE inhibitors/ARBs** (↓ afterload → ↑ LVOTO) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Digoxin** (↑ contractility → ↑ LVOTO) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diuretics** (↓ preload → ↑ LVOTO) - use cautiously ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**B. Invasive/Surgical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. ICD**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Primary prevention**: High risk for SCD (≥1 major risk factor) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Secondary prevention**: Survived cardiac arrest, sustained VT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**2. Septal Reduction Therapy** (if severe obstruction + refractory symptoms): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**a) Surgical Myectomy**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Septal myectomy** (resect hypertrophied septum) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Gold standard** for severe LVOTO ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- ± Mitral valve repair

**b) Alcohol Septal Ablation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Inject ethanol into septal branch → controlled infarction → ↓ septum thickness
- **Alternative to surgery** (older, high surgical risk)

**C. Other**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anticoagulation** if atrial fibrillation ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Avoid strenuous exercise/competitive sports** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Genetic counseling, family screening** ⭐⭐⭐

---

## 3. RESTRICTIVE CARDIOMYOPATHY (RCM) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Impaired ventricular filling** (diastolic dysfunction) due to **rigid, stiff ventricles** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Normal systolic function** (EF preserved) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Least common cardiomyopathy** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### ETIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Infiltrative**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Amyloidosis** (most common in developed countries) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **AL amyloid** (light chains - myeloma)
  - **Transthyretin** (senile, hereditary)
- **Sarcoidosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hemochromatosis** (iron deposition) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Storage Diseases**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Glycogen storage diseases**
- **Fabry disease** (α-galactosidase deficiency)

**Endomyocardial**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Endomyocardial fibrosis** (tropical, children/young adults) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hypereosinophilic syndrome** (Löffler endocarditis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Eosinophilic infiltration → necrosis → fibrosis

**Idiopathic** ⭐⭐⭐

---

### PATHOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Normal/mildly thickened ventricular walls** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Enlarged atria** (biatrial) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Normal LV cavity size** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Depends on cause**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Amyloidosis**: Apple-green birefringence (Congo red, polarized light) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Sarcoidosis**: Noncaseating granulomas ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Hemochromatosis**: Iron deposits (Prussian blue stain) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Interstitial fibrosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### CLINICAL FEATURES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Presentation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Right-sided heart failure** (predominantly) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **↑ JVP** (prominent)
  - **Kussmaul sign** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (JVP rises with inspiration - vs normal ↓)
  - **Hepatomegaly, ascites, peripheral edema**
- **Left-sided failure** (dyspnea, orthopnea)
- **Arrhythmias** (atrial fibrillation - dilated atria) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thromboembolic events** ⭐⭐⭐

**Physical Exam**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Kussmaul sign** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **S3, S4** (early diastolic filling sound, atrial kick)
- **Hepatomegaly, ascites, edema**

---

### DIAGNOSIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Echocardiography**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Normal/mildly thickened LV walls** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Biatrial enlargement** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Normal LV cavity size, preserved EF** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diastolic dysfunction** (restrictive filling pattern) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **"Sparkling" myocardium** (amyloidosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**ECG**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Low voltage** (esp. amyloidosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Conduction abnormalities**
- **Atrial arrhythmias**

**Cardiac Catheterization**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **"Dip-and-plateau" (square root sign)** in ventricular pressure tracing ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Differentiates from constrictive pericarditis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
  - **RCM**: Discordant RV/LV pressures (RV systolic <50 mmHg) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Constrictive**: Concordant (equalized diastolic pressures)

**Endomyocardial Biopsy**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Definitive diagnosis** (esp. amyloid, sarcoid, hemochromatosis)

**Cardiac MRI**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Late gadolinium enhancement (amyloid - diffuse subendocardial)

---

### TREATMENT ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Treat Underlying Cause**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sarcoidosis**: Steroids ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hemochromatosis**: Phlebotomy, chelation ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hypereosinophilic**: Steroids, hydroxyurea

**Symptomatic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diuretics** (cautious - need high filling pressures) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Heart rate control** (allow filling time)
- **Anticoagulation** (if atrial fibrillation)

**Prognosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Poor** (especially amyloidosis)
- **Heart transplantation** (end-stage)

---

## 4. ARRHYTHMOGENIC RV CARDIOMYOPATHY (ARVC) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Progressive replacement of RV myocardium with fibrofatty tissue** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Arrhythmogenic** → ventricular tachycardia ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### ETIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Genetic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Autosomal dominant** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Desmosomal mutations** (plakoglobin, desmoglein, plakophilin) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### PATHOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Gross**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Dilated, thin-walled RV** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fibrofatty replacement** (RV free wall, apex, outflow tract - "triangle of dysplasia") ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Microscopic**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Myocyte loss**
- **Fibrofatty infiltration** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### CLINICAL FEATURES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Age**: Adolescents/young adults

**Presentation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Ventricular tachycardia** (LBBB morphology - from RV) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Palpitations, syncope** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sudden cardiac death** (young athletes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **RV failure** (late)

---

### DIAGNOSIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Echocardiography/MRI**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Dilated, hypokinetic RV** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fibrofatty infiltration** (MRI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**ECG**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Epsilon wave** (terminal notch in QRS - V1-V3) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T-wave inversion** (V1-V3) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **VT with LBBB pattern** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### TREATMENT ⭐⭐⭐⭐

- **ICD** (prevent sudden death) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Antiarrhythmics** (sotalol, amiodarone)
- **Avoid competitive sports** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## MNEMONICS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**HCM Murmur Changes**: "**V**alsalva & **S**tanding **U**p murmur" (↑ murmur with ↓ preload)

**HCM AVOID**: "**D**igoxin, **D**iuretics, **N**itrates, **A**CE-I" (all worsen LVOTO)

**RCM Causes**: "**A**myloid **S**arcoid **H**emochromatosis **E**osinophils" (ASHE)

**Kussmaul Sign**: "RCM & constrictive - JVP **K**limbs with inspiration"

## NEET PG MCQS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Q1.** Dilated heart, reduced EF, globular appearance. Diagnosis?
A) HCM B) DCM C) RCM D) ARVC

**Answer: B** - Dilated cardiomyopathy: Dilated ventricles, ↓ EF, globular heart

**Q2.** Young athlete with sudden death. Autopsy shows asymmetric septal hypertrophy. Diagnosis?
A) DCM B) HCM C) ARVC D) Myocarditis

**Answer: B** - HCM: Most common cause of sudden death in young athletes, asymmetric septal hypertrophy

**Q3.** Systolic murmur that increases with Valsalva maneuver. Diagnosis?
A) Aortic stenosis B) Mitral regurgitation C) HCM D) VSD

**Answer: C** - HCM: Dynamic LVOT obstruction murmur ↑ with ↓ preload (Valsalva)

**Q4.** Patient with restrictive cardiomyopathy. JVP rises with inspiration. This sign is:
A) Hepatojugular reflux B) Kussmaul sign C) Pulsus paradoxus D) Beck's triad

**Answer: B** - Kussmaul sign (JVP ↑ with inspiration) - seen in RCM and constrictive pericarditis

**Q5.** Pathognomonic histologic finding in HCM?
A) Foam cells B) Myocyte disarray C) Granulomas D) Fibrofatty replacement

**Answer: B** - Myocyte disarray (chaotic, haphazard arrangement) is pathognomonic of HCM
