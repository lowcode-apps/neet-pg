    NEOPLASIA - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Definition¶

    Neoplasm: New growth with uncontrolled proliferation, autonomous (independent of physiological signals), irreversible

    Tumor: Swelling (can be neoplastic or non-neoplastic)

    Cancer: Malignant neoplasm

    BENIGN vs MALIGNANT TUMORS¶

    Feature Benign Malignant
    Differentiation Well-differentiated Poorly differentiated/anaplastic
    Growth rate Slow Fast
    Mitoses Rare, normal Numerous, atypical
    Nuclear features Normal Hyperchromatic, pleomorphic, ↑ N:C ratio
    Capsule Usually present Absent
    Local invasion Absent (expansile growth) Present (infiltrative)
    Metastasis Never Characteristic
    Necrosis Rare Common
    Recurrence Rare (after excision) Common
    Effect on host Minimal (unless location/hormone) Cachexia, death

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Metastasis = Hallmark of malignancy - Benign tumors NEVER metastasize - Anaplasia: Lack of differentiation (pleomorphism, ↑ N:C ratio, atypical mitoses)

    NOMENCLATURE¶

    Benign Tumors¶

    Epithelial: - -oma: Adenoma, papilloma - Cyst: Cystadenoma

    Mesenchymal: - Cell type + -oma: - Lipoma (fat) - Fibroma (fibrous tissue) - Chondroma (cartilage) - Osteoma (bone) - Leiomyoma (smooth muscle) - Rhabdomyoma (skeletal muscle)

    NEET PG: Benign tumors end in "-oma" (with exceptions)

    Malignant Tumors¶

    Epithelial (Carcinoma): - Squamous cell carcinoma: From squamous epithelium - Adenocarcinoma: From glandular epithelium - Transitional cell carcinoma: From transitional epithelium (bladder)

    Mesenchymal (Sarcoma): - Cell type + -sarcoma: - Liposarcoma (fat) - Fibrosarcoma (fibrous tissue) - Chondrosarcoma (cartilage) - Osteosarcoma (bone) - Leiomyosarcoma (smooth muscle) - Rhabdomyosarcoma (skeletal muscle)

    Mixed: - Teratoma: All 3 germ layers (mature = benign, immature = malignant) - Carcinosarcoma: Mixed carcinoma + sarcoma (e.g., uterus)

    NEET PG: - Carcinoma: Epithelial malignancy (80% of cancers) - Sarcoma: Mesenchymal malignancy (rare)

    Exceptions to Nomenclature¶

    Benign but NOT "-oma": - Hamartoma: Disorganized tissue (normal to site) - e.g., pulmonary hamartoma - Choristoma: Normal tissue in abnormal location - e.g., pancreatic tissue in stomach wall

    Malignant but end in "-oma": - Melanoma (malignant) - Lymphoma (malignant) - Mesothelioma (malignant) - Hepatoma (hepatocellular carcinoma) - Seminoma (testicular cancer)

    NEET PG: Melanoma, lymphoma, mesothelioma are MALIGNANT (despite "-oma")

    CARCINOGENESIS - MOLECULAR BASIS¶

    Oncogenes (Accelerators)¶

    Definition: Mutated proto-oncogenes → Promote cell proliferation

    Mechanisms of activation: 1. Point mutation: RAS (most common oncogene in human cancers) 2. Gene amplification: MYCN (neuroblastoma), HER2/NEU (breast cancer) 3. Chromosomal translocation: BCR-ABL (CML), c-MYC (Burkitt's lymphoma)

    Common oncogenes:

    Oncogene Tumor Mechanism
    RAS Colon, pancreas, lung Point mutation (most common oncogene)
    MYC Burkitt's lymphoma t(8;14) translocation
    BCR-ABL CML t(9;22) Philadelphia chromosome
    HER2/NEU (ERBB2) Breast cancer Gene amplification
    N-MYC Neuroblastoma Amplification (poor prognosis)
    RET MEN 2, medullary thyroid Ca Point mutation

    NEET PG High-Yield: - RAS: Most common oncogene (30% of cancers) - Philadelphia chromosome t(9;22): BCR-ABL in CML - Burkitt's lymphoma: c-MYC translocation t(8;14) - HER2: Breast cancer (trastuzumab target)

    Tumor Suppressor Genes (Brakes)¶

    Definition: Inhibit cell proliferation, promote apoptosis Loss of function → Cancer ("two-hit hypothesis" - Knudson)

    Common tumor suppressor genes:

    Gene Tumors Function Syndrome
    p53 Most cancers (50%) Cell cycle arrest, apoptosis, DNA repair Li-Fraumeni
    RB Retinoblastoma, osteosarcoma Cell cycle regulation (G1/S checkpoint) Familial retinoblastoma
    APC Colorectal cancer Wnt signaling inhibition FAP (Familial Adenomatous Polyposis)
    BRCA1/BRCA2 Breast, ovarian cancer DNA repair Familial breast/ovarian cancer
    WT1 Wilms tumor Kidney development WAGR syndrome
    NF1 Neurofibromatosis RAS inhibition Neurofibromatosis type 1
    VHL Renal cell carcinoma HIF degradation Von Hippel-Lindau
    PTEN Breast, prostate, endometrial PI3K/AKT pathway inhibition Cowden syndrome

    NEET PG High-Yield: - p53: "Guardian of the genome" (most commonly mutated gene in cancer) - RB: Retinoblastoma, osteosarcoma - APC: Familial adenomatous polyposis → Colorectal cancer - BRCA1/BRCA2: Breast & ovarian cancer (prophylactic mastectomy/oophorectomy) - Two-hit hypothesis: Both alleles must be lost for tumor suppressor gene loss

    DNA Repair Genes¶

    Function: Repair DNA damage

    Defects: - HNPCC (Lynch syndrome): Mismatch repair genes (MLH1, MSH2) - Tumors: Colorectal cancer (right-sided), endometrial, ovarian - Xeroderma pigmentosum: Nucleotide excision repair - Tumors: Skin cancers (UV sensitivity) - BRCA1/BRCA2: Homologous recombination repair - Tumors: Breast, ovarian

    NEET PG: HNPCC (Lynch syndrome) = Mismatch repair defect → Right-sided colon cancer

    HALLMARKS OF CANCER (Hanahan & Weinberg)¶

    1. Sustained proliferative signaling (oncogenes)
    2. Evading growth suppressors (loss of tumor suppressors)
    3. Resisting cell death (apoptosis evasion)
    4. Enabling replicative immortality (telomerase activation)
    5. Inducing angiogenesis (VEGF)
    6. Activating invasion & metastasis
    7. Genome instability & mutation
    8. Tumor-promoting inflammation
    9. Deregulating cellular energetics (Warburg effect)
    10. Avoiding immune destruction

    NEET PG: Know at least 6 hallmarks

    METASTASIS - HIGH YIELD¶

    Definition: Spread of tumor to distant sites (hallmark of malignancy)

    Routes of Metastasis¶

    1. Lymphatic Spread¶

    Common in: Carcinomas Spread: Regional lymph nodes → Distant lymph nodes

    Sentinel lymph node: First draining lymph node - Breast cancer: Axillary lymph node - Melanoma: Regional lymph node basin

    NEET PG: Carcinomas spread via lymphatics (sarcomas via blood)

    2. Hematogenous Spread¶

    Common in: Sarcomas, some carcinomas (RCC, HCC, follicular thyroid) Target organs: Liver, lungs, bone, brain

    Portal of entry: - Liver: GI tumors (via portal vein), breast, lung - Lungs: Most tumors (first capillary bed) - Bone: Breast, prostate, lung, kidney, thyroid - Brain: Lung, breast, melanoma, RCC

    NEET PG: Lung = Most common site of hematogenous metastasis (first capillary bed)

    3. Seeding (Transcoelomic)¶

    Spread: Across body cavities (peritoneal, pleural, pericardial) Examples: - Ovarian cancer → Peritoneal cavity (Krukenberg tumor - ovarian met to stomach) - Gastric cancer → Peritoneum, ovaries (Krukenberg) - Mesothelioma → Pleural cavity

    NEET PG: Krukenberg tumor = Ovarian metastasis (usually from stomach/GI)

    Common Metastatic Patterns - MUST KNOW¶

    Tumors with bone metastases (mnemonic: "P**lease **T**ake **K**ids **B**ut **L**eave **T**hem"): - **P**rostate - **T**hyroid (follicular) - **K**idney (RCC) - **B**reast - **L**ung - **T**estis

    Osteoblastic (sclerotic): - Prostate (most common) - Breast (mixed)

    Osteolytic: - Breast (most common) - Lung, kidney, thyroid, multiple myeloma

    NEET PG: - Prostate: Osteoblastic metastases - Breast: Mixed (osteolytic > osteoblastic) - Lung, kidney, thyroid: Osteolytic

    TUMOR MARKERS - HIGH YIELD¶

    Marker Tumor Notes
    AFP (α-fetoprotein) Hepatocellular Ca, yolk sac tumor, testicular (non-seminoma) Neural tube defects (high in maternal serum)
    β-hCG Choriocarcinoma, hydatidiform mole, testicular (choriocarcinoma) Also elevated in pregnancy
    CEA Colorectal Ca, pancreatic, gastric, lung Non-specific, monitoring recurrence
    CA 125 Ovarian Ca (epithelial), endometrial Screening NOT recommended
    CA 19-9 Pancreatic Ca, cholangiocarcinoma, gastric Highest in pancreatic Ca
    CA 15-3 Breast Ca Monitoring, not screening
    PSA Prostate Ca, BPH, prostatitis Screening controversial
    Calcitonin Medullary thyroid Ca Also used for screening in MEN 2
    Thyroglobulin Differentiated thyroid Ca (papillary, follicular) Post-thyroidectomy monitoring
    S-100 Melanoma, schwannoma, Langerhans cell histiocytosis Neural crest origin
    NSE Small cell lung Ca, neuroblastoma Neuroendocrine tumors
    LDH Germ cell tumors, lymphoma, melanoma Non-specific, prognostic
    Chromogranin A Neuroendocrine tumors (carcinoid, pheochromocytoma)
    5-HIAA Carcinoid tumor In urine (serotonin metabolite)
    VMA, HVA Neuroblastoma, pheochromocytoma In urine (catecholamine metabolites)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - AFP: Hepatocellular Ca, yolk sac tumor - CA 125: Ovarian Ca (not for screening) - CA 19-9: Pancreatic Ca - PSA: Prostate (DRE + PSA for screening) - Calcitonin: Medullary thyroid Ca - S-100: Melanoma

    PARANEOPLASTIC SYNDROMES¶

    Definition: Symptom complexes due to cancer but NOT from tumor mass/metastasis

    Endocrine Syndromes¶

    Syndrome Hormone Tumor
    Cushing's syndrome ACTH Small cell lung Ca (most common), carcinoid
    SIADH ADH Small cell lung Ca, pancreatic Ca
    Hypercalcemia PTHrP Squamous cell lung Ca, breast, RCC, multiple myeloma
    Hypoglycemia Insulin-like factors Hepatocellular Ca, mesenchymal tumors
    Polycythemia Erythropoietin RCC, HCC, cerebellar hemangioblastoma
    Carcinoid syndrome Serotonin (5-HT) Carcinoid tumor (small intestine, bronchial)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Small cell lung cancer: ACTH (Cushing's), ADH (SIADH) - Squamous cell lung cancer: PTHrP (hypercalcemia) - RCC: EPO (polycythemia), PTHrP (hypercalcemia)

    Neurological Syndromes¶

    Syndrome Antibody Tumor
    Lambert-Eaton myasthenic syndrome Anti-VGCC Small cell lung Ca
    Myasthenia gravis Anti-AChR Thymoma
    Cerebellar degeneration Anti-Yo, Anti-Hu Ovarian, breast, small cell lung
    Limbic encephalitis Anti-Hu Small cell lung

    NEET PG: - Lambert-Eaton: Small cell lung Ca (improves with exercise) - Myasthenia gravis: Thymoma (worsens with exercise)

    Hematological Syndromes¶

    • Trousseau syndrome: Migratory thrombophlebitis (pancreatic, lung Ca)
    • DIC: Acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL), mucin-secreting adenocarcinoma
    • Polycythemia: RCC, HCC

    Dermatological Syndromes¶

    Syndrome Tumor
    Acanthosis nigricans GI malignancies (gastric adenocarcinoma)
    Dermatomyositis Lung, ovarian, breast
    Erythema gyratum repens Lung cancer
    Tripe palms Gastric, lung

    NEET PG: Acanthosis nigricans = Gastric adenocarcinoma

    GRADING vs STAGING¶

    Grading¶

    Definition: Histological assessment of differentiation Basis: Cytology, architecture, mitoses Grades: 1 (well-differentiated) → 4 (poorly differentiated/anaplastic)

    Staging¶

    Definition: Extent of tumor spread System: TNM classification - T: Primary tumor size - N: Regional lymph node involvement - M: Distant metastasis

    NEET PG: - Grading: Degree of differentiation (microscopic) - Staging: Extent of spread (clinical + imaging) - Prognosis: Staging > Grading

    TUMOR IMMUNOLOGY¶

    Tumor Antigens¶

    1. Tumor-specific antigens (TSA): Unique to tumor (viral antigens, neoantigens)
    2. Tumor-associated antigens (TAA): Overexpressed in tumor (CEA, AFP, HER2)

    Immune Evasion Mechanisms¶

    1. Reduced MHC expression
    2. PD-L1 expression (inhibits T cells)
    3. Immunosuppressive microenvironment (Tregs, MDSCs)
    4. Antigen loss variants

    NEET PG: PD-L1 expression → Immune evasion (target for immunotherapy)

    Immunotherapy¶

    Checkpoint inhibitors: - Anti-PD-1/PD-L1: Pembrolizumab, nivolumab (melanoma, lung cancer) - Anti-CTLA-4: Ipilimumab (melanoma)

    CAR-T cells: Engineered T cells (B-cell lymphomas, ALL)

    NEET PG: Checkpoint inhibitors (anti-PD-1) for melanoma, lung cancer

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Hallmark of malignancy? Answer: Metastasis

    2. Most common oncogene mutated in cancers? Answer: RAS

    3. Guardian of the genome? Answer: p53

    4. Philadelphia chromosome seen in? Answer: CML (BCR-ABL)

    5. Carcinomas spread via? Answer: Lymphatics

    6. Most common site of hematogenous metastasis? Answer: Lungs

    7. Tumor marker for ovarian cancer? Answer: CA 125

    8. Small cell lung cancer paraneoplastic syndrome? Answer: SIADH, Cushing's (ACTH), Lambert-Eaton

    • Understand nomenclature rules
    • Learn oncogenes and tumor suppressors
    • Know metastatic routes
    • Memorize tumor markers
    • Understand paraneoplastic syndromes
    • Learn grading vs staging
