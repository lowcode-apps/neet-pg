    Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD)¶

    DEFINITION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Ischemic Heart Disease: Group of clinical syndromes resulting from myocardial ischemia - an imbalance between myocardial oxygen supply and demand ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Underlying Cause: Coronary artery atherosclerosis in >90% of cases ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Synonym: Coronary artery disease (CAD), Coronary heart disease (CHD)

    EPIDEMIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    • Leading cause of death worldwide ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    • Accounts for 30-40% of all deaths in developed countries
    • India: Epidemic proportions, younger age of onset (40s-50s vs 60s-70s in West)
    • Men > Women (until menopause)

    CLINICAL SYNDROMES OF IHD ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Classification: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. Angina Pectoris: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Stable angina ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Prinzmetal (variant) angina ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    2. Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Unstable angina ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Non-ST elevation MI (NSTEMI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ST elevation MI (STEMI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    3. Chronic IHD with Heart Failure ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    4. Sudden Cardiac Death ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. STABLE ANGINA PECTORIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    PATHOPHYSIOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Cause: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Fixed atherosclerotic stenosis (usually >70%) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↑ Oxygen demand (exercise, stress) exceeds supply → ischemia

    Critical Stenosis: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - >50% stenosis: Reduced maximal flow (symptomatic with exertion) - >70% stenosis: Reduced resting flow (may have symptoms at rest) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    CLINICAL FEATURES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Classic Angina: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Substernal chest discomfort/pressure ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - "Heavy," "squeezing," "tightness" (NOT sharp/stabbing) - Levine's sign: Patient clenches fist over chest ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Radiation: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Left arm (most common) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Jaw, neck, shoulder, epigastrium - Provoked by: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Exertion ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Emotional stress - Cold weather - Heavy meals - Relieved by: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Rest (within 5-10 minutes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Nitroglycerin (within 1-3 minutes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Duration: <10 minutes (usually 2-5 minutes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Associated Symptoms: ⭐⭐⭐ - Dyspnea, diaphoresis, nausea

    Atypical Presentations (esp. elderly, women, diabetics): ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Dyspnea (angina equivalent) - Fatigue, weakness - Epigastric discomfort ("indigestion")

    DIAGNOSIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Clinical Diagnosis: Based on history ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Resting ECG: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Often normal between episodes ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - May show old MI, LVH

    Stress Testing: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. Exercise ECG (Treadmill Test): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Gold standard for stable angina ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Positive: ≥1 mm ST depression (horizontal/downsloping) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Sensitivity 68%, Specificity 77% - Duke Treadmill Score predicts prognosis ⭐⭐⭐ - Contraindications: Acute MI, unstable angina, severe AS, uncontrolled HTN

    2. Stress Echocardiography: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Wall motion abnormalities with stress ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - More sensitive than exercise ECG - Used if unable to exercise or baseline ECG abnormalities (LBBB, LVH)

    3. Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (Nuclear Scan): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Thallium-201 or Technetium-99m sestamibi ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Reversible perfusion defect (defect on stress, normalizes at rest) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - More sensitive than exercise ECG (90% sensitivity)

    Coronary Angiography: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Gold standard for coronary anatomy ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Indications: High-risk features, failed medical therapy, before revascularization

    CT Coronary Angiography: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Non-invasive alternative - High negative predictive value (rules out significant CAD) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    TREATMENT ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Goals: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 1. Relieve symptoms 2. Prevent MI and death

    A. Lifestyle Modification: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Smoking cessation, diet, exercise, weight loss

    B. Pharmacologic: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Symptomatic Relief: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. Nitrates: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Sublingual nitroglycerin ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Acute episodes (0.3-0.6 mg SL, repeat q5min × 3) - Onset <3 min, duration 15-30 min - Long-acting nitrates (isosorbide mononitrate/dinitrate) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Prophylaxis (before exertion) - Nitrate-free interval (10-12h) to prevent tolerance ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Mechanism: Venodilation → ↓ preload → ↓ O₂ demand ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    2. Beta-Blockers: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - First-line for symptom control ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Atenolol, metoprolol, bisoprolol ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Mechanism: ↓ HR, ↓ contractility → ↓ O₂ demand ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Contraindications: Severe bradycardia, heart block, asthma/COPD (use cardioselective)

    3. Calcium Channel Blockers: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Non-dihydropyridines (diltiazem, verapamil) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↓ HR, ↓ contractility (similar to β-blockers) - Alternative if β-blockers contraindicated ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Dihydropyridines (amlodipine, nifedipine) ⭐⭐⭐ - Vasodilation (↓ afterload) - Add-on to β-blockers (NOT monotherapy - can cause reflex tachycardia)

    4. Ranolazine: ⭐⭐⭐ - Inhibits late sodium current - Add-on if symptoms persist despite other drugs

    Prevent MI & Death ("Secondary Prevention"): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. Antiplatelet: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Aspirin 75-100 mg daily ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↓ MI, stroke, death by 25% - All CAD patients unless contraindicated - Clopidogrel (if aspirin intolerant)

    2. Statins: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 40-80 mg, rosuvastatin 20-40 mg) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↓ LDL, stabilize plaques - All CAD patients regardless of baseline LDL

    3. ACE Inhibitors: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - All CAD patients (especially if DM, HTN, LV dysfunction) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↓ MI, stroke, death

    4. Risk Factor Management: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - BP control, diabetes control, smoking cessation

    C. Revascularization: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Indications: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Failed medical therapy (persistent symptoms) - High-risk anatomy (left main disease, 3-vessel disease, proximal LAD)

    1. Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Balloon angioplasty + stent ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Drug-eluting stents (DES) preferred ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↓ Restenosis vs bare-metal stents - Requires dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) for ≥12 months ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (clopidogrel, prasugrel, ticagrelor) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    2. Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Superior to PCI for: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Left main disease ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - 3-vessel disease (especially with DM) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Reduced ejection fraction - Grafts: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - LIMA to LAD (best patency - >90% at 10 years) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Saphenous vein grafts (50% occluded by 10 years) - Radial artery

    2. PRINZMETAL (VARIANT) ANGINA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Pathophysiology: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Coronary artery vasospasm ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Usually in arteries without significant atherosclerosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Clinical: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Angina at rest (NOT with exertion) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Cyclic pattern (same time each day, often early morning) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Young patients, often smokers

    ECG During Episode: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Transient ST elevation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (mimics STEMI) - Resolves when spasm ends - Arrhythmias (VT, VF) may occur

    Diagnosis: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ST elevation during symptoms (Holter monitoring) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Coronary angiography + provocative testing (ergonovine, acetylcholine) ⭐⭐⭐

    Treatment: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Calcium channel blockers (diltiazem, verapamil) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - First-line ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Long-acting nitrates ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Avoid β-blockers (may worsen vasospasm - unopposed α-mediated vasoconstriction) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    3. ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME (ACS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Definition: Spectrum of acute myocardial ischemia due to plaque rupture/thrombosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Pathophysiology: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Unstable plaque rupture → thrombus formation → acute occlusion ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Degree of occlusion determines syndrome: - Partial/transient occlusion → Unstable angina / NSTEMI ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Complete occlusion → STEMI ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Classification (Based on ECG & Biomarkers): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Type ECG Troponin
    Unstable Angina ST depression/T-wave inversion/Normal Negative ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    NSTEMI ST depression/T-wave inversion Positive ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    STEMI ST elevation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Positive ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    UNSTABLE ANGINA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Clinical (Braunwald Classification): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - New-onset angina (within 2 months, severe) - Crescendo angina (increasing frequency/severity/duration) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Rest angina (angina at rest, prolonged >20 min) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    ECG: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ST depression or T-wave inversion ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - May be normal

    Cardiac Biomarkers: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Troponin NEGATIVE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (vs NSTEMI)

    NON-ST ELEVATION MI (NSTEMI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Pathophysiology: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Partial coronary occlusion → subendocardial infarction ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Clinical: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Similar to unstable angina (chest pain at rest)

    ECG: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ST depression (≥0.5 mm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - T-wave inversion (≥2 mm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - NO ST elevation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Cardiac Biomarkers: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Troponin POSITIVE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (defines NSTEMI) - Troponin I/T (more specific than CK-MB) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Rises 3-4h, peaks 24-48h, returns to normal 7-14 days

    ST ELEVATION MI (STEMI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Pathophysiology: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Complete coronary occlusion → transmural infarction ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Clinical: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Prolonged chest pain (>20 min, unrelieved by nitroglycerin) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Dyspnea, diaphoresis, nausea/vomiting - Levine's sign (clenched fist over chest) ⭐⭐⭐

    ECG (Diagnostic): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ST elevation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ≥1 mm in ≥2 contiguous limb leads OR - ≥2 mm in ≥2 contiguous precordial leads ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - New LBBB (LBBB + chest pain = STEMI until proven otherwise) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Cardiac Biomarkers: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. Troponin I/T: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Most sensitive and specific ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Rises 3-4h, peaks 24-48h, normalizes 7-14 days ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - High-sensitivity troponin (detects earlier) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    2. CK-MB: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Rises 4-6h, peaks 24h, normalizes 2-3 days ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Useful for detecting re-infarction (normalizes faster than troponin) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    3. Myoglobin: ⭐⭐⭐ - Earliest (rises 1-2h) - Least specific (also elevated in skeletal muscle injury)

    4. LDH: ⭐⭐⭐ - Rises late (24-48h), peaks 3-6 days - Rarely used now

    ACUTE MI - DETAILED PATHOLOGY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    GROSS & MICROSCOPIC CHANGES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Timeline: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Time Gross Microscopic Complications
    <4-6 hours None ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ None ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Arrhythmias ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    6-12 hours None/slight pallor Wavy fibers ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Arrhythmias
    12-24 hours Pale/mottled Coagulative necrosis begins ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    Neutrophils ⭐⭐⭐⭐    		 Arrhythmias, HF
    1-3 days Yellow-tan center, hyperemic border ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Massive neutrophils ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Fibrinous pericarditis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    Rupture (3-7 days) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    3-7 days Soft, yellow-tan Necrosis + macrophages begin ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Rupture (peak risk) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    7-10 days Soft, red-tan rim Granulation tissue (macrophages, fibroblasts, vessels) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Rupture
    10-14 days Red-gray rim Granulation tissue ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Rupture
    2-8 weeks Gray-white scar Collagen deposition ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Ventricular aneurysm ⭐⭐⭐⭐
    >2 months White scar ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Dense collagen ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Aneurysm, Dressler ⭐⭐⭐

    Key Microscopic Changes: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - <4-6h: NO CHANGES (light microscopy) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Electron microscopy: Sarcolemmal blebs, mitochondrial swelling - 6-12h: Wavy fibers ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (stretched necrotic myocytes) - 12-24h: Coagulative necrosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ + neutrophils ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - 3-7 days: Macrophages phagocytose debris ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - 7-10 days: Granulation tissue (young capillaries, fibroblasts) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - >2 weeks: Collagen scar ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    DISTRIBUTION OF INFARCTS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    By Artery: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Artery Region ECG Leads % of MIs
    LAD ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Anterior wall, apex, anterior septum ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ V1-V4 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 40-50% ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    RCA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Inferior wall, posterior septum ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ II, III, aVF ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 30-40% ⭐⭐⭐⭐
    LCx ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Lateral wall ⭐⭐⭐⭐ I, aVL, V5-V6 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 15-20% ⭐⭐⭐

    LAD = Most common ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    COMPLICATIONS OF MI ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    IMMEDIATE (<24 HOURS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    1. Arrhythmias: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Most common cause of death in first 24-48h ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Ventricular fibrillation (most lethal) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Ventricular tachycardia ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Heart blocks (esp. inferior MI - RCA supplies AV node) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    2. Pump Failure (Cardiogenic Shock): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - If >40% LV infarcted ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Hypotension, poor perfusion (cold extremities, oliguria, altered mental status) - Killip Class IV ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    3. Acute Heart Failure: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Pulmonary edema - Killip classification (STEMI prognosis): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Class I: No HF (mortality 5%) - Class II: Mild HF (S3, rales <50%) (mortality 10-15%) - Class III: Pulmonary edema (rales >50%) (mortality 30-40%) - Class IV: Cardiogenic shock (mortality 60-80%) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    EARLY (1-3 DAYS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    4. Fibrinous Pericarditis: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Days 2-4 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Transmural MI (STEMI) only ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Pleuritic chest pain (worsens with inspiration, lying flat; relieved sitting forward) - Pericardial friction rub ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Treatment: Aspirin (avoid NSAIDs, steroids - impair healing) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    DAYS 3-7 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    5. Cardiac Rupture: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Peak risk: Days 3-7 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (soft, weakened myocardium) - Risk factors: First MI, large transmural MI, elderly, hypertension, no reperfusion

    Types: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    a) Free Wall Rupture: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Most common (50% of ruptures) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Hemopericardium → Cardiac tamponade → Sudden death ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Beck's triad: Hypotension, ↑ JVP, muffled heart sounds ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    b) Ventricular Septal Rupture (VSR): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - New harsh holosystolic murmur (left sternal border) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Thrill palpable ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Left-to-right shunt → acute right heart failure - Step-up in O₂ saturation (RV > RA) on right heart cath ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Treatment: Surgical repair ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    c) Papillary Muscle Rupture: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Posteromedial papillary muscle > anterolateral ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Posteromedial has single blood supply (RCA) - Anterolateral has dual supply (LAD + LCx) - Acute severe mitral regurgitation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - New holosystolic murmur (apex → axilla) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Acute pulmonary edema, cardiogenic shock ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Treatment: Surgical repair/replacement ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    LATE (WEEKS-MONTHS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    6. Ventricular Aneurysm: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Weeks-months after MI - Outpouching of scarred ventricular wall ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Anterior wall (LAD occlusion) most common ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Clinical: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Persistent ST elevation (months after MI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - HF (paradoxical wall motion) - Arrhythmias (VT) - Mural thrombus → embolization ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Does NOT rupture (unlike pseudoaneurysm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    7. Dressler Syndrome (Post-MI Syndrome): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Weeks-months after MI (typically 2-10 weeks) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Autoimmune pericarditis (antibodies against myocardial antigens) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Clinical: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Fever, pleuritic chest pain, pericardial friction rub ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Pericardial effusion, pleural effusion - ↑ ESR, ↑ WBC ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Treatment: NSAIDs, colchicine, steroids ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    8. Mural Thrombus: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Forms on endocardial surface of infarcted wall ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Anterior MI (40% develop thrombus) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Risk of systemic embolization (stroke) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Prophylaxis: Anticoagulation (warfarin × 3-6 months) if large akinetic segment ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    TREATMENT OF ACUTE MI (STEMI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Goal: Restore coronary blood flow ASAP ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - "Time is muscle" ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Immediate Management (MONA-BAG): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **M**orphine (pain, anxiety) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **O**xygen (if SpO₂ <90%) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **N**itroglycerin (unless hypotension, RV infarct) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **A**spirin 300 mg (chewed) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **B**eta-blocker (if no contraindications) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **A**CE inhibitor ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - a**G**ents (antiplatelet, anticoagulant) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Reperfusion Therapy: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    1. Primary PCI (Preferred): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Treatment of choice ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Within 90 min of first medical contact (door-to-balloon) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Superior to fibrinolysis (lower mortality, reinfarction, stroke) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Dual antiplatelet therapy: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor, prasugrel, or clopidogrel) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    2. Fibrinolytic Therapy: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - If PCI not available within 120 min ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Within 12 hours of symptom onset (ideally <3h) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Agents: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Fibrin-specific: Tenecteplase (TNK), Alteplase (tPA) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Non-fibrin-specific: Streptokinase (rarely used now)

    Contraindications: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Absolute: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Prior ICH (intracranial hemorrhage) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Ischemic stroke <3 months ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Active bleeding ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Suspected aortic dissection ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Intracranial neoplasm/AVM - Relative: ⭐⭐⭐ - Severe uncontrolled HTN (>180/110) - Recent surgery/trauma (<3 weeks) - Active peptic ulcer - Pregnancy

    Success Criteria: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ↓ Chest pain - ST segment resolution (>50% reduction in ST elevation) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Reperfusion arrhythmias (e.g., accelerated idioventricular rhythm - AIVR) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Early peak of cardiac biomarkers ⭐⭐⭐

    Adjunctive Therapy: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Anticoagulation: Heparin (UFH or enoxaparin) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Statins: High-intensity (atorvastatin 80 mg) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - ACE inhibitors: Within 24h (all STEMI, especially anterior, HF, LV dysfunction) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Beta-blockers: Oral (unless contraindicated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    TREATMENT OF NSTEMI/UNSTABLE ANGINA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Risk Stratification (TIMI Score, GRACE Score): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Medical Management: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Antiplatelet: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Aspirin ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor, prasugrel, clopidogrel) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Anticoagulation: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - LMWH (enoxaparin) or fondaparinux ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Nitrates, beta-blockers, statins, ACE inhibitors

    Invasive Strategy (Early Angiography + Revascularization): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - High-risk patients: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Refractory angina - Hemodynamic instability - Arrhythmias - ↑ Troponin - Dynamic ST changes - GRACE score >140 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Timing: Within 24-72 hours ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Conservative Strategy (Medical Management First): ⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Low-risk patients - Angiography only if recurrent ischemia

    HIGH-YIELD POINTS FOR NEET PG ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    1. LAD occlusion = anterior MI (most common, V1-V4) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    2. RCA occlusion = inferior MI (II, III, aVF) + AV blocks ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    3. Troponin = most sensitive/specific biomarker (gold standard) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    4. Unstable angina = troponin negative; NSTEMI = troponin positive ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    5. <4-6h MI: NO gross/microscopic changes on light microscopy ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    6. Wavy fibers = earliest microscopic change (6-12h) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    7. Cardiac rupture: Peak risk days 3-7 (soft myocardium) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    8. Free wall rupture → tamponade; VSR → harsh murmur + step-up O₂; Papillary muscle → acute MR ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    9. Ventricular aneurysm = persistent ST elevation, does NOT rupture ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    10. Dressler syndrome = autoimmune pericarditis weeks-months after MI ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    11. Primary PCI preferred over fibrinolysis (if available within 90 min) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    12. Prinzmetal angina = coronary spasm, ST elevation at rest, treat with CCB (avoid β-blockers) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    MNEMONICS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    MONA-BAG (Immediate MI Management): - **M**orphine, **O**xygen, **N**itroglycerin, **A**spirin, **B**eta-blocker, **A**CE inhibitor, a**G**ents

    Cardiac Biomarker Timing: "**T**roponin **T**akes **T**ime (3-4h rise), stays **L**onger (7-14d)"

    Cardiac Rupture Types: "**F**ree wall → **F**atal (tamponade); **S**eptum → **S**hrill murmur; **P**apillary → **P**ulmonic edema (MR)"

    Killip Class: "**N**o failure, **S**3/rales, **P**ulmonary edema, **S**hock" (Classes I-IV)

    NEET PG MCQS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Q1. Patient with chest pain for 3 hours. ECG shows ST elevation in V1-V4. Which artery is occluded? A) RCA B) LAD C) LCx D) PDA

    Answer: B - LAD occlusion → anterior MI (V1-V4)

    Q2. Patient with MI 5 days ago develops sudden hypotension, ↑ JVP, muffled heart sounds. Most likely complication? A) VSR B) Free wall rupture C) Papillary muscle rupture D) LV aneurysm

    Answer: B - Beck's triad (hypotension, ↑JVP, muffled sounds) = tamponade from free wall rupture (days 3-7)

    Q3. Patient with inferior MI develops complete heart block. Which artery supplies the AV node? A) LAD B) RCA C) LCx D) PDA

    Answer: B - RCA supplies AV node in 90% → inferior MI (RCA) often causes AV blocks

    Q4. Most sensitive and specific biomarker for MI? A) Myoglobin B) CK-MB C) Troponin D) LDH

    Answer: C - Troponin I/T (gold standard - most sensitive and specific)

    Q5. Patient with MI 6 weeks ago has persistent ST elevation on ECG. Most likely diagnosis? A) Re-infarction B) Dressler syndrome C) Ventricular aneurysm D) Pericarditis

    Answer: C - Persistent ST elevation months after MI = ventricular aneurysm
