    ANTIDIABETIC DRUGS - NEET PG Study Material¶

    DIABETES MELLITUS - Brief Overview¶

    Types¶

    Type 1 DM (10%): - Absolute insulin deficiency - Autoimmune destruction of β-cells - Requires insulin

    Type 2 DM (90%): - Insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency - Progressive β-cell dysfunction - Initially managed with oral drugs

    NEET PG: Type 2 DM = Insulin resistance + β-cell dysfunction

    CLASSIFICATION OF ANTIDIABETIC DRUGS¶

    INSULIN & ANALOGS¶

    • Rapid-acting, Short-acting, Intermediate-acting, Long-acting

    ORAL HYPOGLYCEMIC AGENTS¶

    1. Insulin Secretagogues: - Sulfonylureas (Glibenclamide, Gliclazide, Glimepiride) - Meglitinides (Repaglinide, Nateglinide)

    2. Insulin Sensitizers: - Biguanides (Metformin) - Thiazolidinediones (Pioglitazone)

    3. α-Glucosidase Inhibitors: - Acarbose, Voglibose, Miglitol

    4. Incretin-based: - GLP-1 agonists (Exenatide, Liraglutide, Dulaglutide, Semaglutide) - DPP-4 inhibitors (Sitagliptin, Vildagliptin, Linagliptin)

    5. SGLT-2 Inhibitors: - Dapagliflozin, Canagliflozin, Empagliflozin

    6. Amylin analog: - Pramlintide

    INSULIN PREPARATIONS¶

    Classification by Duration¶

    1. Rapid-acting (Analogs): - Insulin lispro, aspart, glulisine - Onset: 5-15 min - Peak: 30-90 min - Duration: 3-5 hours - Use: Mealtime bolus, IV infusion

    2. Short-acting (Regular/Soluble insulin): - Regular human insulin - Onset: 30-60 min - Peak: 2-4 hours - Duration: 6-8 hours - Use: Mealtime, IV (DKA, HHS), SC

    3. Intermediate-acting: - NPH (Neutral Protamine Hagedorn) - Onset: 1-2 hours - Peak: 4-8 hours - Duration: 12-18 hours - Use: Basal insulin (twice daily)

    4. Long-acting (Analogs): - Insulin glargine, detemir, degludec - Onset: 1-2 hours - Peak: No peak (peakless) - Duration: 20-24 hours (degludec: >40 hours) - Use: Basal insulin (once daily)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Only regular & rapid-acting can be given IV - Long-acting = Peakless (glargine, detemir, degludec)

    Basal-Bolus Regimen¶

    Standard approach for Type 1 DM: - Basal: Long-acting insulin (glargine/detemir) once daily OR NPH twice daily - Bolus: Rapid-acting insulin (lispro/aspart) before each meal

    Total daily dose: 0.5-1 unit/kg - 50% basal, 50% bolus (divided among meals)

    NEET PG: Basal-bolus = Standard for Type 1 DM

    Insulin Administration¶

    Routes: - SC (most common) - abdomen, thigh, arm - IV - Only regular or rapid-acting (DKA, HHS, perioperative) - Inhaled - Technosphere insulin (rarely used)

    Insulin pen: Convenient, accurate dosing

    Insulin pump: Continuous SC infusion (CSII)

    Adverse Effects of Insulin¶

    1. Hypoglycemia ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Most common & serious - Symptoms: Sweating, tremor, palpitations, confusion, seizures, coma - Treatment: - Conscious: Oral glucose/sugar - Unconscious: IV dextrose (50%) or IM glucagon

    2. Weight gain: - Anabolic effect

    3. Lipodystrophy: - Lipoatrophy (fat loss) - rare (human insulin) - Lipohypertrophy (fat accumulation) - injection site rotation needed

    4. Insulin allergy: - Rare (human insulin) - Local or systemic

    5. Hypokalemia: - Insulin shifts K⁺ into cells - Monitor K⁺ in DKA treatment

    6. Insulin resistance: - High-dose requirement (>200 units/day) - Due to antibodies (rare with human insulin)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Hypoglycemia = Most common/serious insulin complication - Treatment: Conscious = oral glucose, Unconscious = IV dextrose/IM glucagon

    SULFONYLUREAS¶

    Examples: Glibenclamide (Glyburide), Gliclazide, Glimepiride, Glipizide

    Mechanism¶

    Close ATP-sensitive K⁺ channels on β-cell membrane: 1. K⁺ channels close 2. Depolarization 3. Ca²⁺ influx 4. Insulin release

    Effect: ↑ Insulin secretion (insulin secretagogue)

    NEET PG: Sulfonylureas close K⁺ channels → insulin release

    Uses¶

    Type 2 DM: - NOT effective in Type 1 (no β-cells) - Monotherapy or combination

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. Hypoglycemia ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Most serious - Glibenclamide > Glimepiride (risk) - Prolonged (long-acting) - Especially: Elderly, renal/hepatic impairment, missed meals

    2. Weight gain ⭐⭐⭐⭐: - ↑ Insulin → anabolic effect

    3. SIADH (hyponatremia): - Rare

    4. Disulfiram-like reaction: - Chlorpropamide (obsolete drug)

    5. Hypersensitivity: - Sulfonamide allergy

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Sulfonylureas cause hypoglycemia, weight gain - Contraindicated in Type 1 DM, pregnancy

    Contraindications¶

    • Type 1 DM (ineffective)
    • Pregnancy (teratogenic - use insulin)
    • Severe renal/hepatic impairment
    • Sulfonamide allergy

    Drug Interactions¶

    Potentiate hypoglycemia: - Alcohol, NSAIDs, sulfonamides, warfarin

    Reduce effect: - Thiazides, corticosteroids, phenytoin

    MEGLITINIDES (Glinides)¶

    Examples: Repaglinide, Nateglinide

    Mechanism¶

    Similar to sulfonylureas (close K⁺ channels) but: - Faster onset - Shorter duration - Different binding site

    Effect: ↑ Insulin secretion (mealtime)

    Uses¶

    Type 2 DM: - Postprandial hyperglycemia - Taken with meals (15-30 min before)

    Advantage: - Flexible dosing (skip if skip meal) - Less hypoglycemia than sulfonylureas

    NEET PG: Meglitinides = Short-acting, mealtime insulin secretagogues

    Adverse Effects¶

    • Hypoglycemia (less than sulfonylureas)
    • Weight gain

    METFORMIN - First-Line for Type 2 DM¶

    Class: Biguanide

    Mechanism¶

    Multiple actions (exact mechanism unclear): 1. ↓ Hepatic gluconeogenesis (main) → ↓ glucose production 2. ↑ Peripheral glucose uptake (muscle) → ↑ insulin sensitivity 3. ↓ Intestinal glucose absorption 4. Does NOT ↑ insulin secretion (no hypoglycemia)

    NEET PG: Metformin ↓ hepatic gluconeogenesis, doesn't cause hypoglycemia

    Uses¶

    1. Type 2 DM ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - First-line (monotherapy) - Especially if obese (↓ weight)

    2. Prediabetes (prevention)

    3. PCOS: - ↑ Insulin sensitivity - ↑ Ovulation

    4. Gestational diabetes (off-label, some use)

    NEET PG High-Yield: Metformin = First-line for Type 2 DM

    Advantages¶

    1. No hypoglycemia (doesn't ↑ insulin)

    2. Weight neutral or ↓ weight ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Only antidiabetic that ↓ weight - ↓ Appetite

    3. Cardiovascular benefits: - ↓ Mortality (UKPDS trial) - ↓ Macrovascular complications

    4. ↓ HbA1c (1-2%)

    5. Inexpensive

    NEET PG: Metformin = Only antidiabetic that ↓ weight, no hypoglycemia

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. GI disturbances ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Most common (30%) - Nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort - Reduce by: Start low dose, with meals, extended-release

    2. Lactic acidosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Rare but SERIOUS (often fatal) - Risk factors: - Renal failure (eGFR <30) - Liver failure - Hypoxic states (heart failure, respiratory failure) - Alcohol abuse - Contrast dye (IV iodinated) - Sepsis, shock - Stop metformin 48 hours before contrast (restart after 48 hours if renal function normal)

    3. Vitamin B12 deficiency: - Long-term use - ↓ Absorption - Monitor, supplement

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Metformin most common = GI side effects - Lactic acidosis (rare, serious) - stop before contrast dye - Contraindicated in renal failure (eGFR <30)

    Contraindications¶

    Absolute: - Severe renal impairment (eGFR <30) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Liver failure - Heart failure (severe, unstable) - Respiratory failure - Sepsis, shock - Alcohol abuse - Lactic acidosis history

    Relative: - eGFR 30-45 (reduce dose)

    NEET PG: Stop metformin 48 hours before IV contrast

    THIAZOLIDINEDIONES (TZDs, Glitazones)¶

    Example: Pioglitazone (Rosiglitazone withdrawn)

    Mechanism¶

    PPAR-γ (Peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor-gamma) agonist: - ↑ Insulin sensitivity (muscle, fat, liver) - ↑ Glucose uptake - ↓ Hepatic gluconeogenesis - Does NOT ↑ insulin secretion

    NEET PG: TZDs = PPAR-γ agonist, ↑ insulin sensitivity

    Uses¶

    Type 2 DM: - Combination therapy (not first-line)

    Advantages: - No hypoglycemia - ↓ Triglycerides (pioglitazone)

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. Fluid retention, edema ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Common (5%) - Worsens heart failure

    2. Heart failure ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Contraindicated in NYHA class III-IV - Black box warning

    3. Weight gain ⭐⭐⭐⭐: - ↑ Subcutaneous fat

    4. Bone fractures ⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Especially in women - ↓ Bone density

    5. Bladder cancer: - Pioglitazone (controversial association)

    6. Hepatotoxicity: - Monitor LFTs - (Troglitazone withdrawn due to liver failure)

    7. Macular edema

    Onset of action: Slow (weeks to months)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - TZDs cause fluid retention, weight gain, heart failure, bone fractures - Contraindicated in heart failure

    Contraindications¶

    Absolute: - Heart failure (NYHA III-IV) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - Liver disease

    Relative: - Osteoporosis - Bladder cancer history

    α-GLUCOSIDASE INHIBITORS¶

    Examples: Acarbose, Voglibose, Miglitol

    Mechanism¶

    Inhibit α-glucosidase enzymes in intestinal brush border: - ↓ Breakdown of complex carbohydrates → glucose - ↓ Glucose absorption - ↓ Postprandial hyperglycemia

    NEET PG: Acarbose ↓ carbohydrate digestion → ↓ postprandial glucose

    Uses¶

    Type 2 DM: - Postprandial hyperglycemia - Add-on therapy

    Prediabetes: - Delay progression

    Advantage: - No hypoglycemia (unless with insulin/sulfonylurea) - No weight gain

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. GI disturbances ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Flatulence, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort (very common - 50-70%) - Undigested carbohydrates → colonic fermentation → gas - Limits use

    2. ↑ Liver enzymes: - Monitor LFTs

    Important: If hypoglycemia occurs (with other drugs), treat with GLUCOSE (NOT sucrose - won't be digested)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Acarbose causes GI side effects (flatulence, diarrhea) - Treat hypoglycemia with glucose (NOT sucrose)

    Contraindications¶

    • Inflammatory bowel disease
    • Intestinal obstruction
    • Cirrhosis

    GLP-1 RECEPTOR AGONISTS (Incretins)¶

    Examples: Exenatide, Liraglutide, Dulaglutide, Semaglutide

    Mechanism¶

    Mimic GLP-1 (Glucagon-like peptide-1): 1. ↑ Glucose-dependent insulin secretion (no hypoglycemia) 2. ↓ Glucagon secretion 3. Slow gastric emptying 4. ↑ Satiety (central effect) 5. ↓ Appetite → Weight loss

    NEET PG: GLP-1 agonists ↑ insulin, ↓ glucagon, ↓ appetite

    Uses¶

    Type 2 DM: - Add-on therapy - Especially if obese

    Obesity (Liraglutide 3 mg, Semaglutide): - Significant weight loss

    Route¶

    SC injection: - Daily (exenatide, liraglutide) - Weekly (exenatide LAR, dulaglutide, semaglutide)

    Oral (Semaglutide): - First oral GLP-1 agonist

    Advantages¶

    1. Weight loss ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - 3-5 kg average - Best for weight loss (with SGLT2-i)

    2. No hypoglycemia (glucose-dependent)

    3. Cardiovascular benefits ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - ↓ CV events, ↓ mortality (liraglutide, semaglutide) - Proven benefit

    NEET PG: GLP-1 agonists cause weight loss, ↓ CV events

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. GI disturbances ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (very common - 40%) - Improves with time

    2. Pancreatitis ⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Rare but serious - Contraindicated in pancreatitis history

    3. Thyroid C-cell tumors (medullary thyroid cancer): - Animal studies (rats) - Contraindicated in personal/family history of MTC, MEN 2

    4. Injection site reactions

    NEET PG High-Yield: - GLP-1 agonists cause nausea (very common) - Contraindicated in pancreatitis, MTC/MEN 2 history

    DPP-4 INHIBITORS (Gliptins)¶

    Examples: Sitagliptin, Vildagliptin, Linagliptin, Saxagliptin

    Mechanism¶

    Inhibit DPP-4 (Dipeptidyl peptidase-4): - ↓ Degradation of GLP-1 & GIP (endogenous incretins) - ↑ Incretin levels → ↑ insulin, ↓ glucagon

    NEET PG: DPP-4 inhibitors ↑ endogenous GLP-1

    Uses¶

    Type 2 DM: - Monotherapy or combination

    Advantages¶

    1. Well tolerated: - Minimal side effects

    2. Weight neutral

    3. No hypoglycemia

    4. Oral (vs GLP-1 agonists - injection)

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. Nasopharyngitis, headache (mild)

    2. Pancreatitis (rare)

    3. Heart failure: - Saxagliptin, alogliptin (↑ HF hospitalizations) - Avoid in heart failure

    4. Arthralgia (joint pain)

    NEET PG: DPP-4 inhibitors well tolerated, some ↑ heart failure risk

    SGLT-2 INHIBITORS (Gliflozins)¶

    Examples: Dapagliflozin, Canagliflozin, Empagliflozin

    Mechanism¶

    Inhibit SGLT-2 (Sodium-glucose cotransporter-2) in proximal tubule: - ↓ Glucose reabsorption from urine - Glycosuria (glucose in urine) - Osmotic diuresis - Insulin-independent

    NEET PG: SGLT-2 inhibitors cause glycosuria, osmotic diuresis

    Uses¶

    1. Type 2 DM: - Monotherapy or combination

    2. Heart failure ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Heart failure with reduced EF (HFrEF) - ↓ Hospitalizations, ↓ mortality - Proven benefit (even without diabetes)

    3. Chronic kidney disease: - Renoprotective (↓ albuminuria, slow progression)

    NEET PG High-Yield: SGLT-2 inhibitors proven benefit in heart failure, CKD

    Advantages¶

    1. Weight loss ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - 2-3 kg (calories lost in urine)

    2. ↓ Blood pressure: - Osmotic diuresis

    3. No hypoglycemia: - Insulin-independent

    4. Cardiovascular benefits ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - ↓ CV death, HF hospitalizations (empagliflozin, canagliflozin, dapagliflozin)

    5. Renal benefits: - ↓ Albuminuria, slow CKD progression

    NEET PG: SGLT-2 inhibitors cause weight loss, ↓ BP, CV/renal benefits

    Adverse Effects¶

    1. Genital mycotic infections ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Vulvovaginal candidiasis (women) - Balanitis (men) - Most common (10-15%)

    2. UTI ⭐⭐⭐⭐: - ↑ Risk

    3. Euglycemic DKA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - DKA with normal glucose (<200 mg/dl) - Rare but serious - Stop SGLT-2i before surgery

    4. Fournier's gangrene: - Necrotizing fasciitis of perineum - Rare but life-threatening

    5. Hypotension (volume depletion): - Osmotic diuresis

    6. Bone fractures, amputation: - Canagliflozin (controversial)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - SGLT-2 inhibitors cause genital infections (most common) - Euglycemic DKA (stop before surgery)

    Contraindications¶

    • eGFR <30 (ineffective in severe renal failure)
    • Type 1 DM (↑ DKA risk)
    • Recurrent UTIs

    SUMMARY: DRUG OF CHOICE¶

    Type 1 DM¶

    Insulin (basal-bolus)

    Type 2 DM¶

    First-line: Metformin ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    If metformin contraindicated: - Sulfonylurea (if cost concern) - DPP-4 inhibitor (well tolerated)

    Add-on (if HbA1c not at goal): - If ASCVD or high CV risk: GLP-1 agonist or SGLT-2 inhibitor (CV benefit) - If heart failure: SGLT-2 inhibitor (↓ HF hospitalizations) - If CKD: SGLT-2 inhibitor or GLP-1 agonist (renoprotective) - If obesity: GLP-1 agonist or SGLT-2 inhibitor (weight loss) - Otherwise: Sulfonylurea, DPP-4i, TZD, basal insulin

    NEET PG: Metformin first-line; if CV disease → GLP-1/SGLT-2; if HF → SGLT-2

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. First-line drug for Type 2 DM? Answer: Metformin

    2. Only insulin that can be given IV? Answer: Regular insulin (and rapid-acting analogs)

    3. Antidiabetic drug causing weight loss? Answer: Metformin, GLP-1 agonists, SGLT-2 inhibitors

    4. Treatment for unconscious hypoglycemia? Answer: IV dextrose or IM glucagon

    5. When to stop metformin before contrast? Answer: 48 hours before

    6. Most common side effect of metformin? Answer: GI disturbances (nausea, diarrhea)

    7. Antidiabetic contraindicated in heart failure? Answer: Thiazolidinediones (TZDs)

    8. Most common side effect of SGLT-2 inhibitors? Answer: Genital mycotic infections

    9. Antidiabetic with proven CV mortality benefit? Answer: GLP-1 agonists (liraglutide, semaglutide), SGLT-2 inhibitors (empagliflozin)

    10. If acarbose user develops hypoglycemia, give? Answer: Glucose (NOT sucrose)

    Memory Aids¶

    Metformin Contraindications (LASH)¶

    • **L**iver failure
    • **A**lcohol abuse
    • **S**evere renal failure (eGFR <30)
    • **H**eart failure (severe)

    SGLT-2 Inhibitor Side Effects (GEU)¶

    • **G**enital infections (most common)
    • **E**uglycemic DKA
    • **U**TI

    GLP-1 Benefits (WCV)¶

    • **W**eight loss
    • **C**ardiovascular benefit
    • **V**omiting/nausea (side effect)

    Revision Checklist¶

    • Know insulin types & duration (rapid, short, intermediate, long)
    • Understand metformin mechanism, advantages, contraindications
    • Learn sulfonylurea adverse effects (hypoglycemia, weight gain)
    • Know SGLT-2 inhibitor benefits & side effects
    • Understand GLP-1 agonist uses (weight loss, CV benefit)
    • Learn when to use which drug (CV disease, HF, obesity)
    • Know treatment for hypoglycemia
    • Practice 50+ MCQs on antidiabetic drugs
