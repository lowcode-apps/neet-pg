    CORTICOSTEROIDS - NEET PG Study Material¶

    CLASSIFICATION¶

    By Source¶

    1. Natural (from adrenal cortex): - Glucocorticoids: Cortisol (Hydrocortisone) - Mineralocorticoids: Aldosterone

    2. Synthetic: - Glucocorticoids: Prednisolone, Methylprednisolone, Dexamethasone, Betamethasone - Mineralocorticoids: Fludrocortisone

    By Duration of Action¶

    Short-acting (8-12 hours): - Hydrocortisone (Cortisol)

    Intermediate-acting (12-36 hours): - Prednisolone, Prednisone, Methylprednisolone

    Long-acting (36-72 hours): - Dexamethasone, Betamethasone

    NEET PG: Dexamethasone = Longest-acting glucocorticoid

    By Potency¶

    Drug Glucocorticoid Potency Mineralocorticoid Activity Half-life
    Cortisol (Hydrocortisone) 1 1 8-12 hr
    Prednisolone 4 0.8 12-36 hr
    Methylprednisolone 5 0.5 12-36 hr
    Dexamethasone 25-30 0 36-72 hr
    Betamethasone 25-30 0 36-72 hr
    Fludrocortisone 10 125 -

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Dexamethasone = Highest glucocorticoid potency, NO mineralocorticoid effect - Fludrocortisone = Highest mineralocorticoid activity

    MECHANISM OF ACTION¶

    Glucocorticoid Receptor (GR)¶

    Genomic effects (slow, hours): 1. Steroid diffuses into cell (lipophilic) 2. Binds to glucocorticoid receptor (cytoplasmic) 3. Translocates to nucleus 4. Transactivation: ↑ Gene transcription (anti-inflammatory proteins) 5. Transrepression: ↓ Gene transcription (pro-inflammatory cytokines)

    Non-genomic effects (rapid, minutes): - Membrane effects

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids act via nuclear receptors → gene transcription

    PHARMACOLOGICAL EFFECTS¶

    1. Anti-inflammatory ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Mechanisms: - ↓ Phospholipase A2 (↓ arachidonic acid → ↓ prostaglandins, leukotrienes) - ↓ Pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1, IL-2, IL-6, TNF-α, IFN-γ) - ↓ COX-2 expression - ↓ Vascular permeability, edema - ↓ Leukocyte migration (neutrophils, monocytes) - ↑ Anti-inflammatory proteins (lipocortin, IL-10)

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids ↓ phospholipase A2, ↓ cytokines

    2. Immunosuppressive ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Effects: - ↓ T-cell function (↓ IL-2) - ↓ B-cell function (↓ antibody production) - Lymphopenia (redistribution) - ↓ Cell-mediated immunity - Thymic involution

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids suppress cell-mediated immunity

    3. Metabolic Effects¶

    Carbohydrate metabolism: - ↑ Gluconeogenesis (liver) - ↓ Glucose uptake (peripheral tissues) - Insulin resistance - → Hyperglycemia ("steroid diabetes")

    Protein metabolism: - ↑ Protein catabolism (muscle) - → Muscle wasting, negative nitrogen balance

    Fat metabolism: - Lipolysis (limbs) - Lipogenesis (face, trunk) - → Central obesity (buffalo hump, moon facies)

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids cause hyperglycemia, muscle wasting, central obesity

    4. Bone & Calcium¶

    • ↓ Bone formation (↓ osteoblast activity)
    • ↑ Bone resorption (↓ osteoclast apoptosis)
    • ↓ Ca²⁺ absorption (intestine)
    • ↑ Ca²⁺ excretion (kidney)
    • → Osteoporosis

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids cause osteoporosis (↓ bone formation, ↓ Ca²⁺ absorption)

    5. Cardiovascular¶

    • Permissive effect on catecholamines (↑ responsiveness)
    • ↑ Blood pressure (mineralocorticoid effect)

    6. CNS¶

    • Mood elevation (euphoria)
    • Insomnia
    • Psychosis (high dose)

    7. Hematological¶

    • ↑ Neutrophils (demargination, ↓ apoptosis)
    • ↓ Lymphocytes, eosinophils, basophils (redistribution, apoptosis)
    • ↑ RBCs, platelets

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids cause neutrophilia, lymphopenia, eosinopenia

    8. GI¶

    • ↑ Gastric acid, pepsin secretion
    • ↓ Mucus production
    • → ↑ Risk of peptic ulcer

    9. Fetal Lung Maturation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    • ↑ Surfactant production
    • Prevent respiratory distress syndrome (RDS) in preterm infants

    NEET PG: Antenatal steroids for fetal lung maturity

    CLINICAL USES¶

    1. Replacement Therapy ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Adrenal insufficiency: - Primary (Addison's disease): Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone - Secondary (pituitary): Hydrocortisone alone

    Congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CAH): - Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone

    NEET PG: Addison's disease = Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone

    2. Anti-inflammatory/Immunosuppressive¶

    Allergic disorders: - Asthma (inhaled/systemic) - Anaphylaxis (adjunct to epinephrine) - Allergic rhinitis (topical) - Urticaria, angioedema

    Autoimmune disorders: - SLE (systemic lupus) - Rheumatoid arthritis - Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's, UC) - Autoimmune hemolytic anemia - ITP (immune thrombocytopenic purpura)

    Organ transplantation: - Prevent rejection (with other immunosuppressants)

    Dermatological: - Eczema, psoriasis (topical) - Pemphigus, pemphigoid

    Ocular: - Uveitis, optic neuritis (topical/systemic)

    Renal: - Nephrotic syndrome (minimal change disease)

    Respiratory: - COPD exacerbation - Pulmonary fibrosis - Sarcoidosis

    NEET PG: Glucocorticoids used in asthma, SLE, RA, IBD, nephrotic syndrome

    3. Cerebral Edema ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Dexamethasone: - Brain tumors (↓ vasogenic edema) - Bacterial meningitis (↓ inflammation)

    NOT for: Head injury, stroke (not effective)

    NEET PG: Dexamethasone for cerebral edema (brain tumor), bacterial meningitis

    4. Hematological Malignancies¶

    Acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL): - Lympholytic effect

    Multiple myeloma, lymphoma: - Part of chemotherapy regimens

    5. Fetal Lung Maturity ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Antenatal corticosteroids: - Betamethasone or Dexamethasone (IM) - Between 24-34 weeks gestation - Risk of preterm delivery within 7 days - ↓ Respiratory distress syndrome (RDS) - ↓ Intraventricular hemorrhage, neonatal death

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Antenatal steroids = Betamethasone/Dexamethasone (24-34 weeks) - Prevent RDS in preterm infants

    6. Other Uses¶

    Diagnostic: - Dexamethasone suppression test (Cushing's syndrome)

    Hypercalcemia: - ↓ Ca²⁺ (vitamin D toxicity, sarcoidosis)

    Chemotherapy-induced nausea (antiemetic)

    Laryngeal edema (croup, post-extubation)

    ADVERSE EFFECTS (Cushing's Syndrome)¶

    Short-term Use (<3 weeks)¶

    • Hyperglycemia
    • GI upset, peptic ulcer
    • Insomnia, mood changes
    • Acne
    • Increased appetite, weight gain

    Long-term Use (>3 weeks) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    1. Cushing's syndrome (iatrogenic): - Moon facies (round face) - Buffalo hump (fat deposition - upper back) - Central obesity (truncal) - Purple striae (abdomen) - Skin thinning, easy bruising - Hirsutism, acne

    2. Metabolic: - Hyperglycemia, diabetes mellitus - Dyslipidemia - Negative nitrogen balance

    3. Musculoskeletal ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Osteoporosis (most serious long-term effect) - Fractures (vertebral, hip) - Prevention: Calcium + Vitamin D, bisphosphonates - Myopathy (proximal muscle weakness) - Avascular necrosis (femoral head)

    4. Cardiovascular: - Hypertension - Atherosclerosis

    5. Ophthalmic ⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Posterior subcapsular cataract - Glaucoma (↑ IOP)

    6. Immunosuppression: - ↑ Infection risk - Opportunistic infections (TB, fungal) - Impaired wound healing - Mask infection signs (no fever)

    7. GI: - Peptic ulcer, GI bleeding

    8. Psychiatric: - Psychosis, depression, mania - Insomnia

    9. Growth retardation (children): - ↓ Growth hormone effect

    10. HPA axis suppression ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - Adrenal suppression - Cannot respond to stress - Risk of adrenal crisis (infection, surgery, trauma)

    11. Skin: - Thinning, striae, bruising - Poor wound healing

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Long-term glucocorticoids = Cushing's, osteoporosis, HPA suppression - Most serious = Osteoporosis, adrenal suppression, immunosuppression

    HPA AXIS SUPPRESSION ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐¶

    Mechanism¶

    Normal HPA axis: 

    Hypothalamus (CRH)
    ↓
Pituitary (ACTH)
    ↓
Adrenal cortex (Cortisol)
    ↓
Negative feedback

    Exogenous glucocorticoids: - Suppress CRH & ACTH (negative feedback) - Adrenal atrophy (lack of ACTH stimulation) - Cannot produce endogenous cortisol

    Risk factors: - Duration >3 weeks (any dose) - High dose (even short duration) - Evening dosing (↑ suppression)

    Clinical Significance¶

    Adrenal insufficiency if: - Sudden withdrawal (acute adrenal crisis) - Stress (infection, surgery, trauma) - cannot mount cortisol response

    Symptoms of adrenal crisis: - Hypotension, shock - Hypoglycemia - Fever - Abdominal pain - Confusion

    NEET PG: HPA suppression = Cannot mount stress response, risk of adrenal crisis

    Prevention & Management¶

    1. Alternate-day therapy: - Single morning dose every 48 hours (intermediate-acting) - Allows HPA axis recovery on off-days - ↓ Suppression, ↓ adverse effects

    2. Morning dosing: - Mimics physiological rhythm - ↓ Suppression vs evening dosing

    3. Gradual tapering ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - NEVER stop abruptly (>3 weeks use) - Taper over weeks to months - Allows adrenal recovery

    4. Stress dose coverage ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: - During illness, surgery, trauma - Increase dose 2-3× or IV hydrocortisone - Prevent adrenal crisis

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Glucocorticoids >3 weeks = Taper gradually (NEVER stop abruptly) - Stress dose coverage during illness/surgery

    CONTRAINDICATIONS¶

    Relative (weigh risk-benefit): - Systemic fungal infection (absolute for immunosuppression) - Peptic ulcer disease - Diabetes mellitus - Osteoporosis - Psychosis - Live vaccines (avoid during immunosuppression) - Viral infections (herpes, varicella)

    Note: Often benefits outweigh risks in acute situations

    NEET PG: Live vaccines contraindicated during glucocorticoid therapy

    DRUG INTERACTIONS¶

    CYP3A4 inducers (↓ glucocorticoid effect): - Rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine - → May need ↑ dose

    CYP3A4 inhibitors (↑ glucocorticoid effect): - Ketoconazole, ritonavir - → Risk of toxicity

    SPECIAL FORMULATIONS¶

    Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS)¶

    Examples: Beclomethasone, Budesonide, Fluticasone

    Uses: - Asthma (controller medication) - COPD

    Advantages: - Topical effect (lungs) - Minimal systemic absorption (↓ adverse effects)

    Adverse effects: - Oropharyngeal candidiasis (thrush) - common - Prevention: Rinse mouth after use, spacer - Hoarseness, cough - Growth retardation (children - high dose)

    NEET PG: ICS cause oral thrush - prevent by rinsing mouth

    Topical Corticosteroids¶

    Uses: - Eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis

    Adverse effects (prolonged use): - Skin atrophy, telangiectasia - Hypopigmentation - Striae - Systemic absorption (if large area, occlusive dressing)

    Potency classification: - Low: Hydrocortisone - Medium: Triamcinolone - High: Betamethasone - Very high: Clobetasol

    NEET PG: Topical steroids cause skin atrophy (prolonged use)

    MINERALOCORTICOIDS¶

    FLUDROCORTISONE¶

    Properties: - Highest mineralocorticoid activity - Weak glucocorticoid activity

    Mechanism: - Binds mineralocorticoid receptor (kidney) - ↑ Na⁺ reabsorption, ↑ K⁺ excretion - ↑ Water retention, ↑ blood volume

    Uses: - Primary adrenal insufficiency (Addison's) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - With hydrocortisone - Congenital adrenal hyperplasia - Orthostatic hypotension

    Adverse effects: - Hypertension - Hypokalemia - Edema

    NEET PG: Fludrocortisone = Mineralocorticoid for Addison's disease

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Most potent glucocorticoid? Answer: Dexamethasone (25-30× cortisol)

    2. Glucocorticoid for cerebral edema? Answer: Dexamethasone

    3. Antenatal corticosteroid for fetal lung maturity? Answer: Betamethasone or Dexamethasone (24-34 weeks)

    4. Most common adverse effect of long-term glucocorticoids? Answer: Osteoporosis (also Cushing's features)

    5. Most serious long-term complication? Answer: HPA axis suppression, osteoporosis, immunosuppression

    6. How to prevent HPA suppression? Answer: Alternate-day therapy, morning dosing, gradual taper

    7. Treatment for Addison's disease? Answer: Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone

    8. Adverse effect of inhaled corticosteroids? Answer: Oropharyngeal candidiasis (thrush)

    9. How to prevent oral thrush with ICS? Answer: Rinse mouth after use, use spacer

    10. Why taper glucocorticoids gradually? Answer: Prevent adrenal crisis (HPA suppression)

    Memory Aids¶

    Cushing's Features (HOMBAS)¶

    • **H**ypertension, Hirsutism
    • **O**besity (central), Osteoporosis
    • **M**oon facies, Myopathy
    • **B**uffalo hump, Bruising
    • **A**bdominal striae
    • **S**kin thinning, Striae

    Glucocorticoid Adverse Effects (CUSHINGOID)¶

    • **C**ataract
    • **U**lcer (peptic)
    • **S**kin thinning, Striae
    • **H**ypertension, Hyperglycemia
    • **I**nfections (↑ risk)
    • **N**ecrosis (avascular - femoral head)
    • **G**rowth retardation
    • **O**steoporosis
    • **I**nsomnia, Immunosuppression
    • **D**iabetes

    HPA Suppression Risk (3-3-3)¶

    • >3 weeks duration
    • 3× physiologic dose
    • Need to taper for 3× duration of use
