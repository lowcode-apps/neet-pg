# LUNGS & RESPIRATORY SYSTEM - NEET PG Study Material

## Overview
**Function**: Gas exchange (O2 and CO2)
**Location**: Thoracic cavity
**Number**: 2 (right & left)

---

## TRACHEA

**Extent**: C6 (lower border of cricoid cartilage) → T4/5 (carina)
**Length**: 10-12 cm
**Structure**: 16-20 C-shaped cartilaginous rings
**Diameter**: 2-2.5 cm

**Relations** (IMPORTANT):

| Level | Anterior | Posterior |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Neck** | Isthmus of thyroid (2nd-4th rings), sternothyroid | Esophagus |
| **Thorax** | Left brachiocephalic vein, thymus, arch of aorta | Esophagus, left recurrent laryngeal nerve |

**Bifurcation**: T4/5 vertebral level (sternal angle)
**Carina**: Ridge at bifurcation (highly sensitive - cough reflex)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Sternal angle (Angle of Louis)**: Landmark for tracheal bifurcation (T4/5)
- **Foreign body aspiration**: Right main bronchus (wider, shorter, more vertical)

---

## BRONCHI

### Main Bronchi

| Feature | Right Main Bronchus | Left Main Bronchus |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Length** | Shorter (2.5 cm) | Longer (5 cm) |
| **Width** | Wider | Narrower |
| **Angle** | More vertical (25°) | More horizontal (45°) |
| **Branches** | 3 lobar bronchi | 2 lobar bronchi |
| **Eparterial** | Present (above pulmonary artery) | Absent |

**NEET PG**: Right main bronchus - Wider, Shorter, More vertical → Foreign bodies enter right side

### Lobar (Secondary) Bronchi
**Right**: 3 (upper, middle, lower)
**Left**: 2 (upper, lower)

**Eparterial bronchus**: Right upper lobe bronchus (only bronchus above pulmonary artery)

### Segmental (Tertiary) Bronchi
**Right lung**: 10 segments
**Left lung**: 8-10 segments

---

## Bronchopulmonary Segments - HIGH YIELD

### Right Lung (10 segments)

**Upper lobe** (3):
1. Apical
2. Posterior
3. Anterior

**Middle lobe** (2):
4. Lateral
5. Medial

**Lower lobe** (5):
6. Superior (apical)
7. Medial basal
8. Anterior basal
9. Lateral basal
10. Posterior basal

### Left Lung (8-10 segments)

**Upper lobe** (4-5):
1. Apico-posterior
2. Anterior
3. Superior lingular
4. Inferior lingular

**Lower lobe** (4-5):
5. Superior (apical)
6. Anteromedial basal
7. Lateral basal
8. Posterior basal

**NEET PG**: Bronchopulmonary segments are surgical units (can be resected individually)

## LUNGS

### General Features

| Feature | Right Lung | Left Lung |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Lobes** | 3 (upper, middle, lower) | 2 (upper, lower) |
| **Fissures** | 2 (oblique, horizontal) | 1 (oblique) |
| **Weight** | Heavier (625g) | Lighter (565g) |
| **Segments** | 10 | 8-10 |
| **Cardiac notch** | Absent | Present (lingula) |

**NEET PG**: Right lung - 3 lobes, 2 fissures; Left lung - 2 lobes, 1 fissure

---

### Surfaces of Lung

#### 1. Costal Surface
**Relations**: Ribs, intercostal muscles

#### 2. Mediastinal Surface
**Important structure**: **Hilum** (root of lung)

#### 3. Diaphragmatic Surface (Base)
**Relations**: Diaphragm
**Note**: Right base higher (liver beneath)

#### 4. Apex
**Extends**: 2.5 cm above medial 1/3 of clavicle
**Relations**: Subclavian artery, T1 nerve root

---

### Fissures

#### Oblique Fissure (Both lungs)
**Surface marking**: 5th rib posteriorly → 6th costal cartilage anteriorly
**Separates**:
- **Right**: Upper & middle lobes from lower lobe
- **Left**: Upper lobe from lower lobe

#### Horizontal Fissure (Right lung only)
**Surface marking**: 4th costal cartilage (sternal end) → 5th rib (mid-axillary)
**Separates**: Upper lobe from middle lobe

**NEET PG**: Accessory fissures common (seen on imaging)

---

## ROOT OF LUNG (Hilum)

### Structures (Anterior to Posterior)
**Arrangement**:

**Right lung**:
- **Anterior to posterior**: Pulmonary artery → Bronchi → Pulmonary veins
- **Superior to inferior**: Bronchus → Pulmonary artery → Pulmonary veins

**Left lung**:
- **Anterior to posterior**: Pulmonary artery → Bronchi → Pulmonary veins
- **Superior to inferior**: Pulmonary artery → Bronchus → Pulmonary veins

**Mnemonic**: "**A**rtery **B**ronchus **V**ein" (Anterior to Posterior on both sides)
**But**: Right - BAV (superior to inferior), Left - ABV (superior to inferior)

### Other structures at hilum:
- Bronchial vessels
- Pulmonary nerve plexus
- Lymph nodes
- Lymphatics

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Right**: Eparterial bronchus (above artery)
- **Left**: All bronchi below artery (hyparterial)

---

## BLOOD SUPPLY

### Pulmonary Circulation (Functional)
**Pulmonary arteries**: Deoxygenated blood from right ventricle
**Pulmonary veins**: Oxygenated blood to left atrium

**Right pulmonary artery**: Passes **behind** SVC & ascending aorta
**Left pulmonary artery**: Connected to arch of aorta by **ligamentum arteriosum**

**Pulmonary veins**: 4 veins (2 from each lung) → Left atrium

**NEET PG**: Ligamentum arteriosum = Remnant of ductus arteriosus

### Bronchial Circulation (Nutritive)
**Bronchial arteries**: From thoracic aorta
- Supply bronchi, visceral pleura, lymph nodes
**Bronchial veins**: Drain into azygos/hemiazygos veins

**NEET PG**: Bronchial arteries supply bronchi up to terminal bronchioles (not alveoli)

---

## LYMPHATIC DRAINAGE - HIGH YIELD

**Superficial plexus**: Visceral pleura → Hilum
**Deep plexus**: Along bronchi & vessels

**Lymph nodes** (in order):
1. **Pulmonary nodes**: Within lung substance
2. **Bronchopulmonary nodes**: At hilum
3. **Tracheobronchial nodes**: Angle of bifurcation (carina)
4. **Paratracheal nodes**: Along trachea
5. **Mediastinal trunks** → Thoracic duct (left) or Right lymphatic duct

**NEET PG**:
- **Tracheobronchial nodes**: Most commonly involved in bronchogenic carcinoma
- Right lung drains to right side, Left lung to left side (BUT lower lobes can drain to opposite side)

---

## PLEURA

### Types

#### 1. Visceral Pleura
**Covers**: Lung surface (including fissures)
**Innervation**: Autonomic (no pain sensation)
**Blood supply**: Bronchial arteries

#### 2. Parietal Pleura
**Parts**:
1. **Costal pleura**: Lines ribs & intercostal muscles
2. **Diaphragmatic pleura**: Covers diaphragm
3. **Mediastinal pleura**: Covers mediastinum
4. **Cervical pleura (Cupola)**: Covers apex

**Innervation**:
- **Costal & peripheral diaphragmatic**: Intercostal nerves (pain sensation)
- **Central diaphragmatic & mediastinal**: Phrenic nerve

**Blood supply**: Intercostal & internal thoracic arteries

**NEET PG**: Parietal pleura has pain sensation (visceral does not)

---

### Pleural Recesses

**Definition**: Areas where parietal pleura folds on itself (potential spaces)

#### Costodiaphragmatic Recess
**Location**: Between costal & diaphragmatic pleura
**Importance**: Deepest part (accumulation of fluid - pleural effusion)
**Level**:
- Mid-clavicular line: 8th rib
- Mid-axillary line: 10th rib
- Scapular line: 12th rib

#### Costomediastinal Recess
**Location**: Between costal & mediastinal pleura (anterior)
**Cardiac notch**: Left side (space for heart)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Pleural effusion**: Gravitates to costodiaphragmatic recess
- **Thoracocentesis**: Done at costodiaphragmatic recess (safe area)

---

## Surface Markings - CLINICAL IMPORTANCE

### Pleural Margins

**Anterior**:
- Both sides descend behind sternoclavicular joints
- Meet at sternal angle (2nd costal cartilage)
- Right: Descends to 6th costal cartilage
- Left: Deviates laterally at 4th cartilage (cardiac notch)

**Inferior** (important for thoracocentesis):
- **Mid-clavicular line**: 8th rib
- **Mid-axillary line**: 10th rib
- **Scapular line**: 12th rib

**Posterior**: 12th rib to T1

**Mnemonic for inferior margin**: "**8-10-12**" (MCL-MAL-Scapular line)

### Lung Margins
**Lower border** (2 ribs higher than pleura):
- **Mid-clavicular line**: 6th rib
- **Mid-axillary line**: 8th rib
- **Scapular line**: 10th rib

**NEET PG**: Pleura extends 2 ribs below lung (costodiaphragmatic recess)

---

## Mechanism of Respiration

### Inspiration (Active)
**Muscles**:
- **Primary**: Diaphragm (most important), External intercostals
- **Accessory**: SCM, scalenes, pectoralis major/minor

**Mechanism**:
- Diaphragm contracts → Descends
- External intercostals contract → Ribs elevate
- Thoracic cavity volume ↑
- Intrathoracic pressure ↓
- Air enters lungs

**NEET PG**: Diaphragm contributes 75% of inspiration

### Expiration (Passive at rest)
**Muscles** (active expiration):
- Internal intercostals
- Abdominal muscles

**Mechanism**:
- Elastic recoil of lungs
- Relaxation of inspiratory muscles
- Thoracic cavity volume ↓
- Intrathoracic pressure ↑
- Air expelled

## Clinical Conditions - HIGH YIELD

### Pneumothorax
**Definition**: Air in pleural cavity

**Types**:
1. **Spontaneous**: Primary (young, tall, thin males) or Secondary (COPD, TB)
2. **Traumatic**: Rib fracture, penetrating injury
3. **Tension**: One-way valve → Mediastinal shift → Circulatory collapse

**Clinical features**:
- Sudden chest pain, dyspnea
- Decreased breath sounds
- Hyperresonant percussion
- Absent tactile fremitus

**NEET PG**:
- **Tension pneumothorax**: Emergency (needle decompression at 2nd ICS, MCL)
- **Treatment**: Chest tube insertion (5th ICS, anterior to mid-axillary line)

---

### Pleural Effusion
**Definition**: Fluid in pleural cavity

**Types**:
- **Transudate**: CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome
- **Exudate**: Infection (empyema), malignancy, PE

**Clinical features**:
- Dyspnea
- Decreased breath sounds
- Dull percussion (Stony dull)
- Decreased tactile fremitus

**Diagnosis**: **Light's criteria** (exudate vs transudate)

**NEET PG**:
- **Thoracocentesis site**: 7th-8th ICS, posterior axillary line (costodiaphragmatic recess)
- **Complications**: Pneumothorax, hemothorax, infection

---

### Lung Collapse (Atelectasis)
**Causes**:
- **Obstructive**: Mucus plug, foreign body, tumor
- **Compressive**: Pleural effusion, pneumothorax
- **Contraction**: Pulmonary fibrosis
- **Adhesive**: Surfactant deficiency (ARDS, NRDS)

**Clinical**: Decreased breath sounds, dull percussion, mediastinal shift toward collapse

**NEET PG**: Post-operative atelectasis common (inadequate cough, pain)

---

### Aspiration Pneumonia
**Most common site**: **Right lower lobe** (posterior segment)
**Reason**:
- Right main bronchus more vertical
- Posterior segment dependent when supine

**NEET PG**: Foreign body/aspiration → Right lower lobe (posterior segment)

---

## Thoracocentesis (Pleural Tap) - PROCEDURE

**Indications**: Pleural effusion (diagnostic/therapeutic)

**Site**: 7th-8th ICS, mid-axillary/posterior axillary line
**Approach**: **Above the rib** (avoid neurovascular bundle below rib)

**Contraindications**:
- Bleeding disorders
- Small effusion (<1 cm on US)
- Mechanical ventilation

**Complications**:
- **Pneumothorax** (most common)
- Hemothorax
- Re-expansion pulmonary edema
- Infection

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Insert needle ABOVE the rib** (neurovascular bundle runs below)
- **Maximum safe drainage**: 1-1.5 L (avoid re-expansion edema)

---

## Bronchoscopy

**Indications**:
- Hemoptysis
- Persistent cough
- Suspected malignancy
- Foreign body removal
- Biopsy

**Anatomy**:
- Carina easily visualized
- Right main bronchus at 25° angle
- Left main bronchus at 45° angle

**NEET PG**: Carina highly sensitive (touch → cough reflex)

## Practice MCQs

   Answer: Right main bronchus (wider, shorter, more vertical)

2. **Number of bronchopulmonary segments in right lung?**
   Answer: 10

3. **Only eparterial bronchus?**
   Answer: Right upper lobe bronchus

4. **Nerve supply of central diaphragmatic pleura?**
   Answer: Phrenic nerve

5. **Most common site for pleural effusion accumulation?**
   Answer: Costodiaphragmatic recess

6. **Thoracocentesis - needle inserted where?**
   Answer: Above the rib (7th-8th ICS)

7. **Most common site for aspiration pneumonia?**
   Answer: Right lower lobe (posterior segment)

   Answer: Tracheal bifurcation (carina)
