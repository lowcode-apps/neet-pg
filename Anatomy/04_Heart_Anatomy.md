# HEART ANATOMY - NEET PG Study Material

## Overview
**Location**: Middle mediastinum
**Shape**: Pyramid lying on its side
**Weight**: 250-350g (Male), 200-300g (Female)
**Size**: Closed fist

---

## Position & Boundaries

### Surface Anatomy
**Apex**: 5th left intercostal space, 9cm from midline (mid-clavicular line)
**Base**: Opposite T6-T9 vertebrae
**Right border**: Right atrium
**Left border**: Left ventricle
**Superior border**: Great vessels

### Borders (X-ray)
- **Right border**: Right atrium, SVC
- **Left border**: Left ventricle, left atrial appendage, pulmonary trunk
- **Inferior border**: Right ventricle (mainly)

**NEET PG**: Apex beat - 5th intercostal space, mid-clavicular line

---

## Chambers of Heart

### RIGHT ATRIUM

**Parts**:
1. **Sinus venarum**: Smooth posterior part
2. **Atrium proper**: Rough anterior part (pectinate muscles)
3. **Auricle**: Ear-like projection

**Openings**:
- **SVC**: Superior, no valve
- **IVC**: Inferior, guarded by Eustachian valve (rudimentary)
- **Coronary sinus**: Between IVC & tricuspid valve, guarded by Thebesian valve
- **Tricuspid orifice**: Opening to right ventricle

**Important Structures**:
- **Crista terminalis**: Ridge between sinus venarum & atrium proper
- **Fossa ovalis**: Remnant of foramen ovale (interatrial septum)
- **Limbus**: Margin of fossa ovalis
- **SA node**: At junction of SVC & RA (near crista terminalis)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **SA node location**: Junction of SVC & right atrium
- **Fossa ovalis**: Patent in 20-25% (probe patent foramen ovale)
- **Thebesian valve**: Guards coronary sinus opening

---

### RIGHT VENTRICLE

**Parts**:
1. **Inflow tract**: From tricuspid to apex
2. **Outflow tract (Infundibulum)**: To pulmonary trunk

**Internal Features**:
- **Trabeculae carneae**: Irregular muscular ridges (3 types)
  - Ridges, bridges, pillars (papillary muscles)
- **Papillary muscles**: 3 (anterior, posterior, septal)
- **Chordae tendineae**: Attach papillary muscles to tricuspid valve cusps
- **Moderator band (Septomarginal trabecula)**: Bridge from septum to anterior papillary muscle
  - Carries **right bundle branch**
- **Supraventricular crest**: Separates inflow from outflow tract

**Tricuspid Valve**:
- **3 cusps**: Anterior, posterior (inferior), septal (medial)
- **Annulus**: Fibrous ring
- **Papillary muscles prevent prolapse** during systole

**Pulmonary Valve**:
- **3 semilunar cusps**: Anterior, right, left
- No chordae tendineae
- **Sinuses of Valsalva**: Behind each cusp

**NEET PG**:
- **Moderator band**: Carries right bundle branch
- **Most anterior chamber**: Right ventricle (injured in stab wounds)

---

### LEFT ATRIUM

**Parts**:
1. **Main chamber**: Smooth (from embryonic pulmonary veins)
2. **Auricle**: Rough (pectinate muscles)

**Openings**:
- **4 pulmonary veins**: 2 right, 2 left (no valves)
- **Mitral orifice**: To left ventricle

**Important Structures**:
- **Smooth walls**: No crista terminalis
- **Interatrial septum**: Fossa ovalis (from right side)

**Relations** (IMPORTANT):
- **Anterior**: Transverse pericardial sinus, aorta, pulmonary trunk
- **Posterior**: Esophagus, descending aorta
- **Left**: Left lung
- **Right**: Right atrium (interatrial septum)

**NEET PG**: Left atrium posterior to esophagus (enlarged LA → dysphagia)

---

### LEFT VENTRICLE

**Parts**:
1. **Inflow tract**: From mitral valve
2. **Outflow tract**: To aorta

**Internal Features**:
- **Trabeculae carneae**: Finer than right ventricle
- **Papillary muscles**: 2 (anterolateral, posteromedial)
  - Larger & stronger than RV
- **Chordae tendineae**: To mitral valve
- **Thickest wall**: 8-12 mm (3× thicker than RV)

**Mitral Valve**:
- **2 cusps**: Anterior (aortic), Posterior (mural)
- **Anterior cusp larger**
- **Annulus**: Fibrous ring
- Each papillary muscle → Chordae to BOTH cusps

**Aortic Valve**:
- **3 semilunar cusps**: Right coronary, Left coronary, Non-coronary (posterior)
- **Sinuses of Valsalva**: Behind each cusp
- **Coronary ostia**: In right & left sinuses (just above cusps)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **Left ventricle**: Thickest wall (high systemic pressure)
- **Mitral valve**: 2 cusps (bicuspid)
- **Aortic sinuses**: Right & left coronary sinuses give coronary arteries

---

## Fibrous Skeleton of Heart

**Components**:
1. **4 fibrous rings**: Around all 4 valves
2. **Right & left fibrous trigones**: Connect rings
3. **Membranous interventricular septum**: Upper part

**Functions**:
- **Attachment** for valve cusps
- **Separation** between atria & ventricles (electrical insulation)
- **Support** for myocardium
- **Only pathway** for conduction: AV node → Bundle of His

**NEET PG**: Fibrous skeleton electrically insulates atria from ventricles

---

## Interventricular Septum

**Parts**:
1. **Muscular part** (lower 2/3): Thick, muscular
2. **Membranous part** (upper 1/3): Thin, fibrous

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **VSD**: 90% in membranous part (most common congenital heart defect)
- **Membranous septum relations**:
  - Right side: Septal cusp of tricuspid valve
  - Left side: Left ventricular outflow tract

---

## Coronary Circulation

### RIGHT CORONARY ARTERY (RCA)

**Origin**: Right aortic sinus

**Course**:
- Right AV groove (atrioventricular groove)
- Continues to posterior surface
- Ends as **posterior descending artery (PDA)** in 70% (right dominant)

**Branches**:
1. **SA nodal artery** (60% from RCA, 40% from LCx)
2. **Right marginal artery**: Along right border
3. **Posterior descending artery (PDA)**: In posterior interventricular groove
4. **AV nodal artery**: From RCA at crux (90%)

**Supplies**:
- **Right atrium & ventricle**
- **SA node** (60%)
- **AV node** (90%)
- **Posterior 1/3 of interventricular septum**
- **Posterior wall of LV** (if dominant)

---

### LEFT CORONARY ARTERY (LCA)

**Origin**: Left aortic sinus

**Course**: Short main stem (1-2 cm) → Bifurcates into LAD & LCx

#### A. Left Anterior Descending (LAD)
**Course**: Anterior interventricular groove → Apex → Posterior surface

**Branches**:
1. **Diagonal branches** (1-3): To anterior LV wall
2. **Septal perforators**: To anterior 2/3 of interventricular septum

**Supplies**:
- **Anterior wall of LV**
- **Anterior 2/3 of interventricular septum**
- **Apex**
- **Bundle branches** (via septal perforators)

**NEET PG**: LAD = "Widow maker" (occlusion → massive anterior MI)

#### B. Left Circumflex (LCx)
**Course**: Left AV groove → Posterior surface

**Branches**:
1. **Left marginal artery** (Obtuse marginal): To lateral LV wall
2. **SA nodal artery** (40%)
3. **PDA** (10-15% - left dominant system)

**Supplies**:
- **Lateral wall of LV**
- **Posterolateral wall**
- **SA node** (40%)

---

### Coronary Dominance

| Type | Frequency | PDA Origin | AV Nodal Artery |
|------|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Right dominant** | 70% | RCA | RCA |
| **Left dominant** | 10% | LCx | LCx |
| **Co-dominant** | 20% | Both | RCA (usually) |

**NEET PG**: Right dominant system most common (70%)

---

### Coronary Veins

**Great cardiac vein**: Parallels LAD → Continues as **coronary sinus**
**Coronary sinus**:
- Largest vein
- Lies in posterior AV groove
- Opens into **right atrium** (between IVC & tricuspid)
- Guarded by **Thebesian valve**

**Tributaries**:
1. Great cardiac vein
2. Middle cardiac vein (parallels PDA)
3. Small cardiac vein (parallels RCA)
4. Oblique vein of left atrium
5. Posterior vein of LV

**Other drainage**:
- **Anterior cardiac veins**: Directly into RA
- **Thebesian veins**: Directly into chambers

**NEET PG**: Coronary sinus drains into right atrium

---

## Conducting System

### 1. SA NODE (Pacemaker)
**Location**: Junction of SVC & right atrium (subepicardial)
**Blood supply**: SA nodal artery (RCA 60%, LCx 40%)
**Rate**: 60-100 bpm
**Structure**: P cells (pacemaker cells)

### 2. INTERNODAL PATHWAYS
**3 Pathways**: Anterior, middle, posterior (Bachmann's bundle)
**Function**: Conduct impulse from SA node to AV node

### 3. AV NODE
**Location**: Right atrium, above coronary sinus opening (in triangle of Koch)
**Blood supply**: AV nodal artery (from RCA 90%)
**Function**: **Delays impulse** (0.1 sec) - allows atrial contraction before ventricular

**Triangle of Koch** (NEET PG):
- **Anterior**: Tendon of Todaro
- **Posterior**: Coronary sinus opening
- **Base**: Tricuspid valve annulus (septal cusp)
- **Contains**: AV node

### 4. BUNDLE OF HIS
**Location**: Passes through fibrous skeleton (only electrical connection)
**Length**: 1-2 cm
**Divides into**: Right & left bundle branches

### 5. BUNDLE BRANCHES
**Right bundle branch**:
- Passes through **moderator band**
- To right ventricle

**Left bundle branch**:
- Divides into **anterior & posterior fascicles**
- To left ventricle

### 6. PURKINJE FIBERS
**Location**: Subendocardial
**Function**: Rapid conduction (fastest: 2-4 m/sec)
**Distribution**: Throughout ventricular myocardium

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **SA node**: Pacemaker (60-100 bpm)
- **AV node**: Delays conduction (0.1 sec)
- **Purkinje fibers**: Fastest conduction (2-4 m/sec)
- **Triangle of Koch**: Contains AV node

## Cardiac Valves - Clinical Correlations

### Auscultation Areas

| Valve | Auscultation Area | Position |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| **Aortic** | 2nd right intercostal space | Right sternal border |
| **Pulmonary** | 2nd left intercostal space | Left sternal border |
| **Tricuspid** | 4th-5th left intercostal space | Lower left sternal border |
| **Mitral** | 5th intercostal space | Apex (mid-clavicular line) |

**Mnemonic**: "**A**ll **P**eople **T**ake **M**edicine" (from right to left, top to bottom)

### Valve Lesions - NEET PG

#### Mitral Stenosis (MS)
**Cause**: Rheumatic heart disease (most common)
**Murmur**: Mid-diastolic, low-pitched, rumbling (apex)
**Signs**: Opening snap, loud S1, malar flush
**Complication**: Atrial fibrillation, LA thrombus → Embolism

#### Mitral Regurgitation (MR)
**Causes**: RHD, MVP, ischemic (papillary muscle rupture)
**Murmur**: Pansystolic, high-pitched, blowing (apex → axilla)
**Signs**: Soft S1, S3 gallop

#### Aortic Stenosis (AS)
**Causes**: Calcific (elderly), bicuspid valve, RHD
**Murmur**: Ejection systolic, crescendo-decrescendo (2nd right ICS → carotids)
**Triad**: Angina, Syncope, Heart failure (SAD)
**Signs**: Slow-rising pulse, narrow pulse pressure, LV heave

#### Aortic Regurgitation (AR)
**Causes**: RHD, infective endocarditis, aortic dissection, Marfan
**Murmur**: Early diastolic, high-pitched, blowing (left sternal border)
**Signs**:
- **Wide pulse pressure**, **Water-hammer pulse**
- **Head nodding** (De Musset's sign)
- **Corrigan's sign**, **Quincke's sign**, **Hill's sign**

**NEET PG**: AR has maximum eponymous signs

## Pericardium

### Fibrous Pericardium
**Structure**: Tough, non-distensible sac
**Attachments**:
- **Superior**: Great vessels
- **Inferior**: Central tendon of diaphragm
- **Anterior**: Sternum (sternopericardial ligaments)

### Serous Pericardium
**Two layers**:
1. **Parietal layer**: Lines fibrous pericardium
2. **Visceral layer (Epicardium)**: Covers heart

**Pericardial cavity**: Between parietal & visceral layers
**Fluid**: 15-50 ml (lubricant)

### Pericardial Sinuses

#### Transverse Sinus
**Location**: Behind aorta & pulmonary trunk, in front of SVC & LA
**Boundaries**:
- **Anterior**: Aorta, pulmonary trunk
- **Posterior**: SVC, left atrium
- **Superior**: Pericardial reflection
- **Inferior**: Pericardial reflection

**Clinical**: Finger passed through during cardiac surgery (to clamp vessels)

#### Oblique Sinus
**Location**: Behind left atrium
**Boundaries**:
- **Anterior**: Left atrium
- **Lateral**: Pulmonary veins
- **Inferior**: Diaphragm

**NEET PG**: Transverse sinus used in cardiac surgery

---

## Clinical Conditions - HIGH YIELD

### Pericardial Effusion
**Causes**: Infection, malignancy, uremia, post-MI
**Signs**: Muffled heart sounds, enlarged cardiac shadow (water bottle shape on X-ray)
**ECG**: Low voltage QRS, electrical alternans

### Cardiac Tamponade
**Triad (Beck's triad)**:
1. **Hypotension**
2. **Elevated JVP** (distended neck veins)
3. **Muffled heart sounds**

**Signs**:
- **Pulsus paradoxus** (>10 mmHg drop in BP during inspiration)
- Kussmaul's sign (JVP rises on inspiration)

**Treatment**: **Pericardiocentesis** (drain fluid)

**NEET PG**: Beck's triad + Pulsus paradoxus = Tamponade

### Constrictive Pericarditis
**Causes**: TB (most common in developing countries), post-cardiac surgery, radiation
**Signs**:
- **Kussmaul's sign** (JVP rises on inspiration)
- **Pericardial knock** (early diastolic sound)
- **Calcified pericardium** on X-ray

**Treatment**: Pericardiectomy

## Myocardial Infarction - Territory

| Coronary Artery | Territory | ECG Leads |
|----------------|-----------|-----------|
| **LAD** | Anteroseptal | V1-V4 |
| **LAD (distal)** | Apical, Anterior | V3-V4, I, aVL |
| **LCx** | Lateral | I, aVL, V5-V6 |
| **RCA** | Inferior | II, III, aVF |
| **RCA (proximal)** | RV infarction | V1, V4R |
| **LCx or RCA** | Posterior | V7-V9 (or tall R in V1-V2) |

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- **LAD occlusion**: Anterior MI (most common, worst prognosis)
- **RCA occlusion**: Inferior MI (bradycardia, heart block)
- **RV infarction**: With inferior MI, avoid nitrates (preload dependent)

## Practice MCQs

1. **SA node location?**
   Answer: Junction of SVC and right atrium

2. **Most common coronary dominance?**
   Answer: Right dominant (70%)

3. **Most common site of VSD?**
   Answer: Membranous interventricular septum

4. **Moderator band carries?**
   Answer: Right bundle branch

5. **Most anterior chamber of heart?**
   Answer: Right ventricle

6. **Triangle of Koch contains?**
   Answer: AV node

7. **Fastest conducting tissue?**
   Answer: Purkinje fibers (2-4 m/sec)

8. **Beck's triad seen in?**
   Answer: Cardiac tamponade
