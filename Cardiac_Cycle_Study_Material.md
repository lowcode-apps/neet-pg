# CARDIAC CYCLE - NEET PG Study Material

## Overview
The cardiac cycle represents all events that occur from the beginning of one heartbeat to the beginning of the next. Duration: **0.8 seconds** (at 75 bpm)

---

## Phases of Cardiac Cycle

### 1. ATRIAL SYSTOLE (0.1 sec)
- Atria contract
- AV valves open
- Blood flows from atria → ventricles
- Contributes **~30%** of ventricular filling
- "a" wave in JVP
- Fourth heart sound (S4) if present

### 2. VENTRICULAR SYSTOLE (0.3 sec)

#### A. Isovolumetric Contraction (0.05 sec)
- All valves CLOSED
- Ventricular pressure ↑ rapidly
- No change in volume
- First heart sound (S1) - Closure of AV valves

#### B. Rapid Ejection (0.15 sec)
- Semilunar valves OPEN
- Blood ejected into aorta/pulmonary artery
- ~70% of stroke volume ejected
- Ventricular pressure > Aortic pressure

#### C. Reduced Ejection (0.10 sec)
- Continued ejection but slower
- Ventricular pressure starts to fall
- ~30% of stroke volume ejected

### 3. VENTRICULAR DIASTOLE (0.4 sec)

#### A. Isovolumetric Relaxation (0.08 sec)
- All valves CLOSED
- Ventricular pressure ↓ rapidly
- No change in volume
- Second heart sound (S2) - Closure of semilunar valves

#### B. Rapid Filling (0.12 sec)
- AV valves OPEN
- Blood rushes from atria → ventricles
- ~70% of ventricular filling occurs
- Third heart sound (S3) if present (physiological in children)

#### C. Slow Filling/Diastasis (0.20 sec)
- Continued slow filling
- ~30% of passive filling
- Atria and ventricles act as common chamber

## Volume Changes

**End Diastolic Volume (EDV)**: 120-130 mL
**End Systolic Volume (ESV)**: 50-60 mL
**Stroke Volume (SV)**: EDV - ESV = **70 mL**
**Ejection Fraction (EF)**: (SV/EDV) × 100 = **55-65%**

## Heart Sounds - Clinical Correlation

### S1 (LUB) - Mitral & Tricuspid Closure
- Start of ventricular systole
- Best heard at apex
- **Split S1**: RBBB, Ebstein anomaly

### S2 (DUB) - Aortic & Pulmonary Closure
- End of ventricular systole
- Best heard at base
- **Physiological splitting**: Inspiration (↑ venous return → delayed P2)
- **Wide split**: RBBB, Pulmonary stenosis
- **Fixed split**: ASD
- **Paradoxical split**: LBBB, Aortic stenosis

### S3 (Kentucky - Ken-TUC-ky)
- Early diastolic filling
- **Physiological**: Children, pregnancy
- **Pathological**: Heart failure, MR, VSD

### S4 (Tennessee - TEN-nes-see)
- Atrial contraction against stiff ventricle
- **Always pathological**: HTN, AS, HOCM
- Absent in atrial fibrillation

## High-Yield Points for NEET PG

### 1. Frank-Starling Law
- ↑ Preload (EDV) → ↑ Stroke Volume
- Mechanism: Optimal sarcomere length (1.8-2.2 μm)

### 2. Factors Affecting Cardiac Output

**CO = HR × SV**

| Factor | Effect on CO |
|--------|--------------|
| ↑ Sympathetic tone | ↑ |
| ↑ Preload | ↑ |
| ↓ Afterload | ↑ |
| ↑ Contractility | ↑ |
| Fever | ↑ |
| Hypoxia | ↑ |
| Athletic training | ↑ SV, ↓ HR |

### 3. JVP Waves Correlation

- **a wave**: Atrial systole
- **c wave**: Tricuspid bulging (isovolumetric contraction)
- **x descent**: Atrial relaxation
- **v wave**: Atrial filling (AV valve closed)
- **y descent**: AV valve opens

### 4. Common NEET PG Questions

**Q: Maximum coronary blood flow occurs in?**
A: Early diastole (isovolumetric relaxation)

**Q: Dicrotic notch represents?**
A: Closure of aortic valve (S2)

**Q: First chamber to depolarize?**
A: Right atrium (SA node)

**Q: Fastest conducting tissue?**
A: Purkinje fibers (2-4 m/sec)

## Clinical Applications

### Mitral Stenosis
- ↓ Diastolic filling
- ↑ LA pressure
- Opening snap after S2
- Mid-diastolic murmur

### Aortic Stenosis
- ↑ Afterload
- LV hypertrophy
- Ejection systolic murmur
- Paradoxical split S2

### Heart Failure
- ↓ Ejection fraction
- S3 gallop
- ↑ EDV, ↑ ESV
- ↓ Stroke volume

## Practice MCQs

1. **What percentage of ventricular filling occurs before atrial systole?**
   - Answer: 70% (passive filling during diastole)

2. **S2 is best heard at which area?**
   - Answer: 2nd intercostal space (aortic & pulmonary areas)

3. **Normal ejection fraction is?**
   - Answer: 55-65%

4. **Which sound indicates ventricular failure?**
   - Answer: S3 gallop

---

## Key Formulas

1. **Stroke Volume** = EDV - ESV
2. **Cardiac Output** = HR × SV
3. **Ejection Fraction** = (SV/EDV) × 100
4. **Mean Arterial Pressure** = DBP + 1/3(SBP - DBP)
5. **Pulse Pressure** = SBP - DBP
