    CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE (CKD) - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Definition¶

    CKD: Kidney damage or GFR <60 ml/min/1.73m² for ≥3 months

    Kidney damage markers: - Albuminuria (≥30 mg/24hr) - Hematuria - Structural abnormalities (imaging) - Pathological abnormalities (biopsy)

    NEET PG: Duration ≥3 months differentiates CKD from AKI

    KDIGO CLASSIFICATION (2012) - HIGH YIELD¶

    Based on GFR (G Stage)¶

    Stage GFR (ml/min/1.73m²) Description Management
    G1 ≥90 Normal/high (with kidney damage) Treat underlying, slow progression
    G2 60-89 Mildly decreased Estimate progression
    G3a 45-59 Mild to moderately decreased Evaluate & treat complications
    G3b 30-44 Moderately to severely decreased Evaluate & treat complications
    G4 15-29 Severely decreased Prepare for RRT
    G5 <15 Kidney failure (ESRD) RRT (dialysis/transplant)

    Based on Albuminuria (A Stage)¶

    Stage Albumin excretion ACR (mg/g) Description
    A1 <30 mg/24hr <30 Normal to mildly increased
    A2 30-300 mg/24hr 30-300 Moderately increased
    A3 >300 mg/24hr >300 Severely increased

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Stage 3: Most common (subdivided into 3a & 3b) - Stage 5 (GFR <15): ESRD, requires dialysis - Albuminuria: Independent risk factor for progression

    ETIOLOGY¶

    Top causes (vary by region):

    Developed Countries¶

    1. Diabetes mellitus (30-40%) - Most common
    2. Hypertension (25-30%)
    3. Glomerulonephritis (10-15%)
    4. Polycystic kidney disease
    5. Others: Obstructive uropathy, reflux nephropathy

    Developing Countries (India)¶

    1. Chronic glomerulonephritis (30-40%)
    2. Diabetic nephropathy (30-35%)
    3. Hypertensive nephrosclerosis
    4. Obstructive uropathy

    NEET PG: Diabetes = Leading cause of ESRD worldwide

    PATHOPHYSIOLOGY¶

    Mechanisms of Progression¶

    1. Hyperfiltration theory:
    2. Nephron loss → Remaining nephrons hyperfiltrate

    3. ↑ Intraglomerular pressure → Further damage

    4. Proteinuria: Tubular toxicity

    5. Hypertension: Accelerates progression

    6. Tubulointerstitial fibrosis: Final common pathway

    NEET PG: ACE-I/ARBs slow progression (↓ intraglomerular pressure)

    CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS¶

    Uremia (Symptomatic Renal Failure)¶

    Usually symptomatic when GFR <10-15 ml/min

    1. Fluid & Electrolyte Disturbances¶

    • Hyperkalemia: Life-threatening (ECG changes, arrhythmias)
    • Metabolic acidosis: High anion gap (↓ H+ excretion)
    • Hyperphosphatemia + Hypocalcemia
    • Volume overload: Edema, pulmonary edema, hypertension

    2. Cardiovascular¶

    • Hypertension (90%)
    • Pericarditis: Uremic pericarditis (friction rub, ECG: diffuse ST elevation)
    • Accelerated atherosclerosis: #1 cause of death
    • Left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH)
    • Heart failure

    NEET PG: #1 cause of death in CKD = Cardiovascular disease

    3. Hematological¶

    • Anemia: Normocytic normochromic
    • Cause: ↓ Erythropoietin production
    • Treatment: Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (ESA - epoetin alfa)
    • Bleeding tendency: Platelet dysfunction (↑ BT, normal PT/aPTT)
    • Treatment: Desmopressin (DDAVP), dialysis

    4. Renal Osteodystrophy (Mineral & Bone Disorder)¶

    Pathophysiology: 

    ↓ GFR → ↓ Phosphate excretion → Hyperphosphatemia
        ↓
↓ Calcitriol (Vit D) → Hypocalcemia
        ↓
Secondary hyperparathyroidism (↑ PTH)
        ↓
Bone disease

    Types: 1. Osteitis fibrosa cystica: High turnover (↑ PTH) - Brown tumors, bone cysts, subperiosteal resorption 2. Osteomalacia: Low turnover (aluminum toxicity) 3. Adynamic bone disease: Very low turnover 4. Mixed

    X-ray findings: - Rugger-jersey spine: Sclerosis of vertebral endplates - Salt & pepper skull: Granular osteopenia - Subperiosteal resorption: Phalanges, clavicle

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Hyperphosphatemia + Hypocalcemia + ↑ PTH = CKD - Treatment: Phosphate binders, Vitamin D, Calcimimetics (cinacalcet)

    5. Neurological¶

    • Uremic encephalopathy: Confusion, asterixis, seizures, coma
    • Peripheral neuropathy: Restless leg syndrome, sensorimotor neuropathy
    • Autonomic neuropathy

    NEET PG: Asterixis (flapping tremor) in uremia

    6. Gastrointestinal¶

    • Anorexia, nausea, vomiting
    • Uremic fetor: Ammonia breath (urea → ammonia)
    • Gastritis, peptic ulcer
    • GI bleeding

    7. Dermatological¶

    • Pruritus (itching)
    • Uremic frost: Urea crystals on skin (severe uremia)
    • Skin pigmentation: Yellowish-brown

    8. Endocrine¶

    • Sexual dysfunction: Impotence, amenorrhea, infertility
    • Glucose intolerance

    NEET PG: Uremic frost = Severe uremia (rare now)

    INVESTIGATIONS¶

    Assessment of Kidney Function¶

    1. Serum Creatinine: - ↑ Creatinine (inversely proportional to GFR) - Doubles when GFR halves

    2. GFR Estimation:

    Cockcroft-Gault formula: 

    CrCl = [(140 - Age) × Weight in kg] / (72 × Serum Cr)
       × 0.85 (for females)

    MDRD (Modification of Diet in Renal Disease): More accurate CKD-EPI: Most accurate (recommended)

    3. Urine Analysis: - Proteinuria: Albumin-creatinine ratio (ACR) - Hematuria - Urine sediment: Broad waxy casts (chronic), RBC casts (glomerular)

    NEET PG: Broad waxy casts = Chronic kidney disease

    Laboratory Findings in CKD¶

    Parameter Finding
    Hemoglobin ↓ (Normocytic normochromic anemia)
    Creatinine ↑
    BUN ↑ (BUN:Cr ratio >20:1 if volume depleted)
    Potassium ↑ (Hyperkalemia)
    Calcium ↓ (Hypocalcemia)
    Phosphate ↑ (Hyperphosphatemia)
    PTH ↑ (Secondary hyperparathyroidism)
    Bicarbonate ↓ (Metabolic acidosis)
    Anion gap ↑
    Albumin ↓ (Malnutrition, proteinuria)

    Imaging¶

    USG Kidneys: - Bilateral small kidneys (<9 cm) = Chronic disease - Large kidneys: Diabetes, ADPKD, amyloidosis, HIV nephropathy - Increased echogenicity - Loss of corticomedullary differentiation

    NEET PG: Small echogenic kidneys = CKD

    MANAGEMENT¶

    Goals¶

    1. Slow progression
    2. Treat complications
    3. Prepare for RRT

    Slow Progression¶

    1. Blood Pressure Control¶

    Target: <130/80 mmHg (if proteinuria <140/90)

    Drugs: - ACE inhibitors or ARBs: First-line (especially if proteinuria/diabetes) - ↓ Intraglomerular pressure - Antiproteinuric effect - Slow progression - Others: CCBs, diuretics, β-blockers

    NEET PG: ACE-I/ARBs = First-line in CKD (renoprotective)

    2. Glycemic Control¶

    Target HbA1c: <7% (individualized) - Prevents/delays nephropathy

    3. Dietary Protein Restriction¶

    Target: 0.6-0.8 g/kg/day (Stage 3-5) - ↓ Uremic toxins - Slow progression

    4. Control Proteinuria¶

    Target: <0.5 g/day - ACE-I/ARBs - SGLT2 inhibitors (new evidence)

    Treat Complications¶

    1. Anemia¶

    Target Hb: 10-11.5 g/dl (avoid >13 g/dl)

    Treatment: - Iron supplementation (oral/IV): Maintain ferritin >100 ng/ml, TSAT >20% - ESA (Erythropoietin): Epoetin alfa, darbepoetin - Start if Hb <10 g/dl despite iron repletion - Adverse effects: Hypertension, thrombosis, pure red cell aplasia (PRCA)

    NEET PG: - Correct iron deficiency FIRST before starting ESA - Target Hb 10-11.5 g/dl (↑ mortality if >13)

    2. Mineral & Bone Disorder¶

    Step 1: Control Phosphate - Dietary restriction: <800-1000 mg/day - Phosphate binders (with meals): - Calcium-based: Calcium carbonate, calcium acetate (cheap, first-line) - Non-calcium based: Sevelamer, lanthanum (if hypercalcemia, vascular calcification) - Target: 3.5-5.5 mg/dl

    Step 2: Correct Hypocalcemia - Vitamin D supplementation: Calcitriol, alfacalcidol - Target Ca: 8.5-9.5 mg/dl

    Step 3: Control PTH - Calcimimetics: Cinacalcet (sensitizes Ca-sensing receptor) - ↓ PTH secretion - Use if PTH persistently high despite Vit D

    NEET PG: - Phosphate binders: Take with meals - Sevelamer: Non-calcium based (if vascular calcification) - Cinacalcet: Calcimimetic (↓ PTH)

    3. Metabolic Acidosis¶

    Target HCO3-: 22-26 mEq/L

    Treatment: - Sodium bicarbonate: Oral - Dialysis (if severe)

    4. Hyperkalemia¶

    Dietary restriction: <2 g/day Avoid: NSAIDs, K-sparing diuretics Treatment: - Diuretics (loop) - Sodium polystyrene sulfonate (kayexalate) - Patiromer, sodium zirconium cyclosilicate (newer) - Dialysis (if severe)

    5. Volume Overload¶

    Dietary salt restriction: <2 g/day Diuretics: Loop diuretics (furosemide) Dialysis: If refractory

    Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT)¶

    Indications for Dialysis (mnemonic: AEIOU): - **A**cidosis (severe, refractory) - **E**lectrolyte abnormalities (hyperkalemia >6.5 mEq/L, refractory) - **I**ngestions (toxins - methanol, ethylene glycol, lithium, aspirin) - **O**verload (volume - pulmonary edema, refractory) - **U**remia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding)

    Start dialysis when: GFR <10-15 ml/min + symptoms

    NEET PG: AEIOU = Indications for emergent dialysis

    Types of RRT¶

    1. Hemodialysis (HD)¶

    Frequency: 3 times/week, 4 hours/session Access: AV fistula (preferred), AV graft, tunneled catheter

    Complications: - Hypotension (most common during dialysis) - Muscle cramps - Disequilibrium syndrome: Cerebral edema (rapid urea removal) - Access-related: Infection, thrombosis, steal syndrome

    NEET PG: AV fistula = Preferred vascular access (mature in 6-8 weeks)

    2. Peritoneal Dialysis (PD)¶

    Types: - CAPD (Continuous Ambulatory PD): 4-5 exchanges/day - APD (Automated PD): Cycler at night

    Advantages: - Home-based - Hemodynamic stability - Better residual renal function preservation

    Complications: - Peritonitis (most common): Cloudy fluid, abdominal pain - Treatment: Intraperitoneal antibiotics (vancomycin + ceftazidime) - Catheter-related: Exit site infection, tunnel infection - Ultrafiltration failure - Hernias - Malnutrition (protein loss in dialysate)

    NEET PG: Peritonitis most common complication of PD

    3. Kidney Transplantation¶

    Best RRT (survival & quality of life)

    Donor types: - Living related (best outcomes) - Living unrelated - Deceased donor (brain-dead, cardiac death)

    Immunosuppression: - Induction: Basiliximab (IL-2 receptor antagonist), ATG - Maintenance: - Calcineurin inhibitors: Tacrolimus (preferred) or Cyclosporine - Antimetabolites: Mycophenolate mofetil (MMF) - Steroids: Prednisolone

    Complications: - Acute rejection: Within 6 months - Treatment: Pulse steroids, ATG - Chronic rejection: Months to years - Infections: CMV (most common), PCP, fungal - Malignancy: Skin cancer, PTLD (post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder) - Drug toxicity: Nephrotoxicity (calcineurin inhibitors)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Tacrolimus: Preferred calcineurin inhibitor - CMV: Most common opportunistic infection - PTLD: EBV-related lymphoma

    CKD vs AKI - Differentiation¶

    Feature CKD AKI
    Duration ≥3 months <3 months
    Kidney size Small (<9 cm) Normal/large
    Anemia Present Absent (unless chronic)
    Renal osteodystrophy Present Absent
    Reversibility Irreversible Potentially reversible
    Progression Gradual Rapid

    NEET PG: Small kidneys + anemia = CKD (NOT AKI)

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Leading cause of ESRD worldwide? Answer: Diabetes mellitus

    2. Leading cause of death in CKD? Answer: Cardiovascular disease

    3. GFR defining ESRD? Answer: <15 ml/min/1.73m² (Stage 5)

    4. Target Hb in CKD with ESA therapy? Answer: 10-11.5 g/dl

    5. First-line antihypertensive in CKD? Answer: ACE inhibitors or ARBs

    6. Indications for emergent dialysis? Answer: AEIOU

    7. Most common complication of peritoneal dialysis? Answer: Peritonitis

    8. Preferred vascular access for hemodialysis? Answer: AV fistula

    Memory Aids¶

    AEIOU (Dialysis Indications)¶

    • **A**cidosis
    • **E**lectrolytes (hyperkalemia)
    • **I**ngestions (toxins)
    • **O**verload (volume)
    • **U**remia (symptoms)

    CKD Complications (5 H's)¶

    • **H**ypertension
    • **H**yperkalemia
    • **H**yperphosphatemia
    • **H**ypocalcemia
    • **H**eart disease

    Revision Checklist¶

    • Know KDIGO classification (stages 1-5)
    • Understand pathophysiology of progression
    • Learn causes of CKD
    • Know complications (anemia, bone disease, CVD)
    • Understand mineral & bone disorder
    • Learn management strategies
    • Know dialysis indications (AEIOU)
    • Understand transplant immunosuppression
    • Differentiate CKD from AKI
