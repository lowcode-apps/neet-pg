    ISCHEMIC HEART DISEASE (IHD) - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Definition¶

    Ischemic heart disease results from myocardial ischemia due to imbalance between oxygen supply and demand, primarily caused by coronary artery disease (CAD).

    Spectrum of IHD¶

    Stable Angina → Unstable Angina → NSTEMI → STEMI
    ↓              ↓                 ↓        ↓
  Reversible   Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)
  ischemia     ↓                      ↓        ↓
            No necrosis        Partial  Complete
                              thickness thickness
                               necrosis  necrosis

    Risk Factors¶

    Non-Modifiable¶

    • Age: ♂ >45 years, ♀ >55 years
    • Gender: Male > Female (until menopause)
    • Family history: CAD in first-degree relative (♂ <55, ♀ <65)
    • Genetics: Familial hypercholesterolemia

    Modifiable¶

    • Hypertension (most important)
    • Diabetes mellitus
    • Dyslipidemia: ↑ LDL, ↓ HDL
    • Smoking (most preventable)
    • Obesity (BMI >30)
    • Sedentary lifestyle
    • Stress

    NEET PG: Diabetes = CAD equivalent (same risk as patient with prior MI)

    STABLE ANGINA (Chronic Stable Angina)¶

    Definition¶

    Predictable chest pain triggered by exertion, relieved by rest/nitroglycerin

    Clinical Features¶

    Typical Angina (must have ALL 3): 1. Substernal chest discomfort (pressure, tightness, heaviness) 2. Provoked by exertion or emotional stress 3. Relieved by rest or nitroglycerin within 5 minutes

    Atypical Angina: 2 out of 3 criteria Non-cardiac chest pain: ≤1 criterion

    Levine's Sign: Clenched fist over chest (classic)

    Radiation: Left arm, jaw, neck, back, epigastrium

    Associated Symptoms: Dyspnea, diaphoresis, nausea

    NEET PG: Stable angina does NOT occur at rest

    Classification¶

    Canadian Cardiovascular Society (CCS) Classification:

    Class Activity Limitation
    I Ordinary activity does not cause angina (angina with strenuous exertion)
    II Slight limitation (angina walking >2 blocks, stairs >1 flight)
    III Marked limitation (angina walking 1-2 blocks)
    IV Inability to perform any activity without angina (angina at rest)

    Investigations¶

    ECG¶

    • At rest: Usually normal
    • During angina: ST depression, T-wave inversion (reversible)

    Exercise Stress Test (TMT - Treadmill Test)¶

    Indications: Diagnosis of stable angina, risk stratification Positive test: - ≥1 mm horizontal/downsloping ST depression - Chest pain during test - Hypotension, arrhythmias

    Contraindications: Acute MI (<2 days), unstable angina, severe AS, uncontrolled arrhythmias

    NEET PG: TMT positive = ≥1 mm ST depression

    Stress Imaging¶

    • Stress Echo: Wall motion abnormalities
    • Myocardial perfusion scan (Thallium/Technetium): Reversible perfusion defect

    Coronary Angiography¶

    Gold standard for diagnosis Indications: - High-risk features on non-invasive tests - Failed medical therapy - Pre-revascularization assessment

    Significant stenosis: ≥70% in major coronary artery OR ≥50% in left main

    Treatment¶

    Immediate Relief¶

    Sublingual nitroglycerin: 0.4 mg (repeat every 5 min × 3 doses)

    Long-term Management¶

    Goals: 1. Relieve symptoms 2. Prevent MI 3. Improve survival

    Medications:

    Drug Class Mechanism Drugs Benefits
    Antiplatelet ↓ Platelet aggregation Aspirin 75-150 mg (all patients) ↓ MI, ↓ Mortality
    Beta-blockers ↓ HR, ↓ BP, ↓ O2 demand Metoprolol, Atenolol 1st line, ↓ Mortality
    Nitrates Vasodilation Long-acting nitrates, Isosorbide mononitrate Symptom relief
    CCB Vasodilation, ↓ HR Amlodipine, Diltiazem If β-blockers contraindicated
    Statins ↓ LDL, plaque stabilization Atorvastatin 40-80 mg (all patients) ↓ MI, ↓ Mortality
    ACE inhibitors ↓ Remodeling Ramipril, Enalapril If LV dysfunction, DM, HTN

    NEET PG High-Yield: - All IHD patients: Aspirin + Statin - 1st line antianginal: Beta-blocker - Alternative: CCB (if β-blocker contraindicated) - Target LDL: <70 mg/dL (high-risk), <55 mg/dL (very high-risk)

    Revascularization¶

    PCI (Percutaneous Coronary Intervention): - Indications: 1-2 vessel disease, failed medical therapy - Procedure: Balloon angioplasty + Stent (Drug-eluting stent preferred) - Post-PCI: Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) - Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (Clopidogrel/Ticagrelor) × 12 months

    CABG (Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting): - Indications: - Left main disease (>50% stenosis) - 3-vessel disease - 2-vessel disease with proximal LAD involvement - LV dysfunction with multivessel disease - Diabetes with multivessel disease - Conduits: LIMA (left internal mammary artery) to LAD (best graft patency)

    NEET PG: CABG preferred over PCI in left main disease, 3-vessel disease, diabetes with multivessel disease

    ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME (ACS)¶

    Definition¶

    Spectrum of conditions caused by acute myocardial ischemia: 1. Unstable Angina (UA) 2. NSTEMI (Non-ST elevation MI) 3. STEMI (ST elevation MI)

    Pathophysiology¶

    Atherosclerotic plaque rupture/erosion
         ↓
    Thrombus formation
         ↓
   Partial occlusion ────→ UA/NSTEMI
         ↓
   Complete occlusion ───→ STEMI

    UNSTABLE ANGINA (UA)¶

    Definition¶

    Angina at rest or with minimal exertion, without myocardial necrosis (normal troponins)

    Braunwald Classification¶

    Class Definition
    I New-onset, severe, or accelerated angina
    II Angina at rest (subacute - within 1 month but not within 48 hours)
    III Angina at rest (acute - within 48 hours)

    Clinical Features¶

    • Crescendo angina: Increasing frequency, severity, duration
    • Angina at rest: >20 minutes
    • New-onset angina: Severe (CCS III or IV)
    • Post-MI angina: <2 weeks after MI

    Investigations¶

    • ECG: ST depression, T-wave inversion (NO ST elevation)
    • Cardiac biomarkers: Troponin NEGATIVE

    MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION (MI)¶

    Definition¶

    Myocardial necrosis due to prolonged ischemia

    Universal Definition (requires ≥1): 1. ↑ Cardiac troponin + ≥1 of: - Symptoms of ischemia - ECG changes (ST-T changes, LBBB, Q waves) - Imaging evidence of new loss of viable myocardium 2. Cardiac death with symptoms suggestive of MI

    Types of MI¶

    Type Cause
    Type 1 Spontaneous (plaque rupture, erosion) - MOST COMMON
    Type 2 Secondary to ischemia (↑ demand or ↓ supply): Anemia, tachycardia, hypotension, vasospasm
    Type 3 Cardiac death before biomarkers obtained
    Type 4a PCI-related
    Type 4b Stent thrombosis
    Type 5 CABG-related

    NEET PG: Type 1 MI = Spontaneous (atherosclerotic plaque rupture)

    STEMI (ST Elevation Myocardial Infarction)¶

    Diagnostic Criteria¶

    ECG (must have ≥1): 1. New ST elevation at J-point in ≥2 contiguous leads: - ≥2.5 mm in men <40 years (V2-V3) - ≥2 mm in men ≥40 years (V2-V3) - ≥1.5 mm in women (V2-V3) - ≥1 mm in other leads 2. New LBBB (Sgarbossa criteria)

    ECG Localization¶

    ECG Leads Territory Artery Complications
    V1-V4 Anterior/Anteroseptal LAD LV dysfunction, CHF, arrhythmias
    V5-V6, I, aVL Lateral LCX Usually benign
    II, III, aVF Inferior RCA (80%) Bradycardia, AV blocks, RV infarct
    V7-V9 Posterior RCA/LCX Often with inferior MI
    V3R-V4R RV infarction RCA Hypotension, ↑ JVP

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Anterior MI: Worst prognosis (large infarct size) - Inferior MI: Associated with bradycardia, AV blocks (vagal stimulation) - RV infarct: Hypotension, ↑ JVP, clear lungs (triad) - Posterior MI: Tall R waves in V1-V2, ST depression (reciprocal)

    ECG Evolution of STEMI¶

    Time ECG Changes
    Minutes-hours Hyperacute T waves → ST elevation
    Hours-days ST elevation, Q wave development
    Days-weeks Q waves persist, ST normalizes, T-wave inversion
    Weeks-months Persistent Q waves (pathological Q = ≥0.04 sec width, ≥25% of R wave depth)

    Cardiac Biomarkers¶

    Marker Rise Peak Return to Normal Notes
    Troponin I/T 3-4 hours 24-48 hours 7-14 days Most sensitive & specific (Gold standard)
    CK-MB 4-6 hours 24 hours 48-72 hours Useful for re-infarction
    Myoglobin 1-2 hours 6-8 hours 24 hours Earliest, but non-specific
    LDH 12-24 hours 48-72 hours 10-14 days Delayed marker

    NEET PG: - Troponin: Most sensitive & specific (diagnostic) - CK-MB: Useful for re-infarction (normalizes faster) - Myoglobin: Earliest marker, but not specific

    Management of STEMI¶

    Immediate Management (MONA)¶

    **M**orphine (if severe pain, pulmonary edema) **O**xygen (if SpO2 <90%) **N**itroglycerin (sublingual, then IV) **A**spirin 300 mg (chew immediately)

    NEET PG: Aspirin is the FIRST drug to give (chew, not swallow)

    Reperfusion Therapy - TIME IS MUSCLE¶

    Goal: Door-to-needle (thrombolysis) <30 min OR Door-to-balloon (PCI) <90 min

    Indications: STEMI within 12 hours of symptom onset

    Primary PCI (Preferred if available): - Advantages: ↓ Mortality, ↓ Reinfarction, ↓ Stroke vs thrombolysis - Indications: - Available within 90-120 minutes - Cardiogenic shock - Contraindications to thrombolysis - Failed thrombolysis - DAPT: Aspirin + Ticagrelor/Prasugrel (preferred) or Clopidogrel

    Thrombolytic Therapy (if PCI not available):

    Agents: - Streptokinase: 1.5 million units IV over 60 min (non-fibrin specific, antigenic) - Tenecteplase (TNK): Single IV bolus (fibrin-specific, preferred) - Alteplase (tPA): 90-min infusion

    Contraindications:

    Absolute Relative
    Prior ICH Ischemic stroke >3 months ago
    Ischemic stroke <3 months BP >180/110 mmHg
    Intracranial neoplasm/AVM Active bleeding (not menses)
    Active internal bleeding Traumatic CPR
    Suspected aortic dissection Recent surgery (<3 weeks)
    Significant head trauma <3 months Pregnancy

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Primary PCI > Thrombolysis (if available within 120 min) - Thrombolysis: If PCI not available, give within 12 hours - Streptokinase: Cannot repeat within 5 years (antigenic) - Absolute contraindication: Prior ICH, ischemic stroke <3 months

    Adjunctive Therapy¶

    Antiplatelet: - Aspirin: 75-150 mg daily (lifelong) - P2Y12 inhibitor: Ticagrelor/Prasugrel (preferred) or Clopidogrel × 12 months

    Anticoagulation: - Heparin: UFH or Enoxaparin (with thrombolysis or PCI)

    Beta-blockers: - Start within 24 hours (if no contraindications) - Contraindications: Cardiogenic shock, heart block, severe bronchospasm

    ACE inhibitors: - Start within 24 hours (all patients, especially if anterior MI, LV dysfunction, HTN, DM) - Contraindications: Hypotension, renal failure

    Statins: - High-intensity statin: Atorvastatin 80 mg (all patients) - Start immediately

    NEET PG: All post-MI patients get Aspirin + Statin + Beta-blocker + ACE inhibitor

    NSTEMI (Non-ST Elevation MI)¶

    Diagnostic Criteria¶

    • Symptoms of acute ischemia
    • ↑ Troponin (myocardial necrosis)
    • NO ST elevation on ECG (may have ST depression, T-wave inversion)

    Risk Stratification¶

    GRACE Score (Global Registry of Acute Coronary Events): - Age, HR, BP, creatinine, cardiac arrest, ST deviation, troponin - High-risk: >140 (early invasive strategy)

    TIMI Risk Score (0-7 points): - Age ≥65 - ≥3 CAD risk factors - Known CAD (stenosis ≥50%) - Aspirin use in past 7 days - ≥2 anginal events in 24 hours - ST deviation ≥0.5 mm - ↑ Cardiac biomarkers

    High-risk features: - Recurrent angina - Hemodynamic instability - LV dysfunction (EF <40%) - Sustained VT - ↑↑ Troponin - Dynamic ST-T changes

    Management¶

    Medical Therapy (Same as STEMI)¶

    • Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (DAPT)
    • Anticoagulation: Fondaparinux/Enoxaparin/UFH
    • Beta-blockers
    • ACE inhibitors
    • Statins
    • Nitrates (symptom relief)

    Invasive Strategy¶

    Early invasive (<24 hours): High-risk patients Delayed invasive (25-72 hours): Intermediate-risk Conservative: Low-risk, stable patients

    NEET PG: NSTEMI = Medical therapy + Risk stratification → Invasive strategy if high-risk

    Complications of MI¶

    Early (<24 hours)¶

    1. Arrhythmias (most common cause of death in 1st hour):
    2. VF/VT: Leading cause of sudden death
    3. Bradycardia: Inferior MI (vagal stimulation)

    4. AV blocks: Inferior MI (transient, usually resolves)

    5. Cardiogenic Shock: Pump failure (>40% LV damage)

    6. LV Failure: Pulmonary edema

    Subacute (1-7 days)¶

    1. Free Wall Rupture (3-7 days):
    2. Sudden hemodynamic collapse, cardiac tamponade

    3. Treatment: Emergency surgery

    4. Ventricular Septal Rupture (3-7 days):

    5. Harsh pansystolic murmur, thrill
    6. Diagnosis: Echo showing VSD

    7. Treatment: Surgery

    8. Papillary Muscle Rupture (3-7 days):

    9. Sudden-onset severe MR
    10. Posteromedial papillary muscle (RCA) more common

    11. Treatment: Surgery

    12. Pericarditis (2-4 days): Fibrinous pericarditis

    13. Pleuritic chest pain, pericardial rub
    14. Treatment: Aspirin/NSAIDs

    Late (>1 week)¶

    1. Dressler's Syndrome (2-10 weeks):
    2. Autoimmune pericarditis (antibodies against myocardium)
    3. Fever, pericardial rub, pleural effusion

    4. Treatment: Aspirin/NSAIDs, steroids if severe

    5. LV Aneurysm:

    6. Persistent ST elevation (weeks after MI)
    7. Echo: Dyskinetic wall motion
    8. Complications: Mural thrombus, arrhythmias, CHF

    9. Treatment: Anticoagulation, surgery if symptomatic

    10. Mural Thrombus: Risk of embolization

      • Treatment: Anticoagulation × 3-6 months

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Most common cause of death in 1st hour: VF - Free wall rupture: Sudden death, tamponade (3-7 days) - VSD rupture: Pansystolic murmur, step-up in O2 (3-7 days) - Papillary muscle rupture: Acute severe MR (3-7 days) - Dressler's syndrome: Autoimmune, 2-10 weeks, fever + pericarditis

    Secondary Prevention (Post-MI)¶

    Medications (Lifelong unless contraindicated): 1. Aspirin 75-150 mg 2. P2Y12 inhibitor (Ticagrelor/Clopidogrel) × 12 months 3. Beta-blocker (Metoprolol, Carvedilol) 4. ACE inhibitor (or ARB if intolerant) 5. Statin (Atorvastatin 80 mg)

    Mnemonic: "**A**ll **P**ost-MI **B**aby **A**ce **S**tatin"

    Lifestyle Modifications: - Smoking cessation - Cardiac rehabilitation - Diet (Mediterranean diet) - Exercise (30 min, 5 days/week) - Weight loss (BMI <25) - BP control (<130/80) - LDL goal (<70 mg/dL) - HbA1c <7% (if diabetic)

    NEET PG: All post-MI patients: ABCDE (Aspirin + Beta-blocker + Clopidogrel + ACEI + Statin) + Lifestyle

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Most important modifiable risk factor for IHD? Answer: Hypertension

    2. Gold standard investigation for CAD? Answer: Coronary angiography

    3. First drug to give in STEMI? Answer: Aspirin 300 mg (chew)

    4. Preferred reperfusion in STEMI? Answer: Primary PCI (if available within 120 min)

    5. Most common cause of death in first hour of MI? Answer: Ventricular fibrillation

    6. Difference between UA and NSTEMI? Answer: Troponin (negative in UA, positive in NSTEMI)

    7. Most common complication in first 48 hours post-MI? Answer: Arrhythmias

    8. Dressler's syndrome occurs when? Answer: 2-10 weeks post-MI (autoimmune)

    Memory Aids¶

    STEMI Management¶

    "MONA GREETS HER PATIENT" - **M**orphine - **O**xygen - **N**itroglycerin - **A**spirin - **G**lycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitors (if PCI) - **R**eperfusion (PCI/Thrombolysis) - **E**noxaparin (anticoagulation) - **H**eparin - **P**rasugrel/Ticagrelor (P2Y12 inhibitor) - **A**CE inhibitor - **T**icagrelor - **S**tatin - **B**eta-blocker

    Complications of MI (Timeline)¶

    "Very Bad Faulty Valve Pump Died" - **V**F/VT (hours) - **B**radycardia (hours) - **F**ree wall rupture (3-7 days) - **V**SD (3-7 days) - **P**apillary rupture (3-7 days) - **D**ressler's (2-10 weeks)

    Revision Checklist¶

    • Know all risk factors for IHD
    • Understand stable vs unstable angina
    • Learn ECG localization of MI
    • Memorize cardiac biomarker timeline
    • Know indications for PCI vs CABG
    • Understand STEMI management algorithm
    • Learn all MI complications and timeline
    • Know secondary prevention medications
    • Practice ECG interpretation
    • Solve 50+ MCQs on IHD/ACS
