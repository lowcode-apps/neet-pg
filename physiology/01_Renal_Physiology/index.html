    RENAL PHYSIOLOGY - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Overview¶

    Kidneys regulate: - Fluid & electrolyte balance - Acid-base balance - Blood pressure - RBC production (EPO) - Vitamin D activation - Excretion of waste products

    Nephron Structure¶

    Glomerulus → PCT → Loop of Henle → DCT → Collecting Duct
               ↓         ↓           ↓          ↓
            65-70%     15-20%      5-10%      5-10%
          (Na+ reabs) (Concen)  (Fine-tune) (ADH/Aldo)

    Types of Nephrons: 1. Cortical (85%): Short loop of Henle 2. Juxtamedullary (15%): Long loop, important for concentration

    Renal Blood Flow (RBF)¶

    Normal RBF: 1200 ml/min (20-25% of cardiac output) Renal Plasma Flow (RPF): 600-700 ml/min

    Formula: RBF = CO × 0.20-0.25

    Autoregulation: Maintains constant RBF despite BP changes (80-180 mmHg)

    Mechanisms: 1. Myogenic: Stretch of afferent arteriole → vasoconstriction 2. Tubuloglomerular feedback: ↑NaCl at macula densa → ↓GFR

    NEET PG: Kidney has best autoregulation among all organs

    Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR)¶

    Normal GFR: 125 ml/min or 180 L/day Filtration Fraction (FF): GFR/RPF = 125/600 = 20%

    Starling Forces¶

    Favoring Filtration: - Glomerular capillary hydrostatic pressure (PGC) = 60 mmHg (main driving force)

    Opposing Filtration: - Bowman's capsule hydrostatic pressure (PBS) = 18 mmHg - Glomerular capillary oncotic pressure (πGC) = 32 mmHg

    Net Filtration Pressure = PGC - (PBS + πGC) = 60 - (18 + 32) = 10 mmHg

    GFR = Kf × Net Filtration Pressure

    Factors Affecting GFR¶

    Factor Effect on GFR Mechanism
    ↑ Afferent arteriole dilation ↑ ↑ PGC
    ↑ Efferent arteriole constriction ↑ ↑ PGC
    ↓ Plasma proteins ↑ ↓ πGC
    Urinary obstruction ↓ ↑ PBS
    ↓ Renal blood flow ↓ ↓ PGC
    ACE inhibitors ↓ Dilate efferent (↓ PGC)
    NSAIDs ↓ Constrict afferent (↓ PGC)

    NEET PG: - Angiotensin II: Constricts efferent > afferent → Maintains GFR - ACE inhibitors: Dilate efferent → ↓ GFR (contraindicated in bilateral RAS)

    GFR Measurement¶

    Inulin Clearance (Gold Standard)¶

    • Freely filtered
    • Not reabsorbed or secreted
    • GFR = Urine [Inulin] × Urine flow / Plasma [Inulin]

    Creatinine Clearance (Clinical)¶

    • Slightly overestimates GFR (small amount secreted by PCT)
    • Normal: 90-140 ml/min

    Cockcroft-Gault Formula: 

    CrCl = [(140 - Age) × Weight in kg] / (72 × Serum Cr)
       × 0.85 (for females)

    NEET PG: Creatinine clearance overestimates true GFR (secreted in PCT)

    Tubular Function¶

    PROXIMAL CONVOLUTED TUBULE (PCT)¶

    Reabsorbs 65-70% of filtered load

    Substances Reabsorbed: - Na+ (65-70%): Na+/K+ ATPase (basolateral) - Water (65-70%): Follows Na+ (osmosis via aquaporin-1) - Glucose (100%): SGLT2 (sodium-glucose cotransporter 2) - Amino acids (100%) - Bicarbonate (80-90%): Via carbonic anhydrase - Phosphate, uric acid, K+

    Substances Secreted: - Organic acids: Uric acid, drugs (penicillin, probenecid) - Organic bases: Creatinine - H+ ions

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Glucose Tm (Transport maximum) = 375 mg/min - Renal threshold for glucose = 180 mg/dl - SGLT2 inhibitors (gliflozins): Block glucose reabsorption → glycosuria

    LOOP OF HENLE¶

    Descending Limb¶

    • Permeable to water (aquaporin-1)
    • Impermeable to NaCl
    • Water reabsorbed → Urine becomes concentrated

    Thin Ascending Limb¶

    • Permeable to NaCl
    • Impermeable to water

    Thick Ascending Limb (TAL)¶

    • Impermeable to water ("diluting segment")
    • Active Na+ reabsorption via Na-K-2Cl cotransporter (NKCC2)
    • Reabsorbs 15-20% of filtered Na+
    • Creates medullary hypertonicity (countercurrent multiplier)

    NEET PG: - Loop diuretics (furosemide): Block NKCC2 in TAL - TAL impermeable to water → "Diluting segment"

    DISTAL CONVOLUTED TUBULE (DCT)¶

    Reabsorbs 5-10% of filtered Na+

    Mechanisms: - Na-Cl cotransporter (NCC) - Impermeable to water - Ca2+ reabsorption (stimulated by PTH)

    NEET PG: - Thiazide diuretics: Block NCC in DCT - Site of Ca2+ reabsorption (PTH-dependent) - Gitelman syndrome: Defect in NCC

    COLLECTING DUCT¶

    Site of fine-tuning (reabsorbs 5-10% Na+)

    Principal Cells¶

    Function: Na+ reabsorption, K+ secretion, water reabsorption

    Transporters: - ENaC (Epithelial Na+ Channel): Na+ reabsorption - ROMK: K+ secretion - Aquaporin-2 (AQP2): Water reabsorption (ADH-dependent)

    NEET PG: - Aldosterone: ↑ ENaC → ↑ Na+ reabsorption, ↑ K+ secretion - ADH (Vasopressin): Inserts AQP2 → ↑ water reabsorption - Amiloride: Blocks ENaC (K+ sparing diuretic)

    Intercalated Cells¶

    Function: Acid-base balance

    Type A (in acidosis): - Secrete H+ (via H+-ATPase) - Reabsorb HCO3-

    Type B (in alkalosis): - Secrete HCO3- - Reabsorb H+

    Countercurrent Mechanism¶

    Countercurrent Multiplier (Loop of Henle)¶

    Function: Creates medullary hypertonicity (300 → 1200 mOsm/kg)

    Mechanism: 1. TAL actively pumps out NaCl (NKCC2) 2. Water cannot follow (impermeable) 3. Interstitium becomes hypertonic 4. Descending limb loses water to interstitium 5. Urine becomes concentrated

    NEET PG: Loop diuretics abolish countercurrent multiplier

    Countercurrent Exchanger (Vasa Recta)¶

    Function: Maintains medullary hypertonicity - Minimizes washout of solutes from medullary interstitium

    Urine Concentration & Dilution¶

    Maximum Urine Concentration¶

    With ADH: 1200 mOsm/kg (4× plasma) - Collecting duct permeable to water (AQP2 inserted) - Water reabsorbed into hypertonic medulla - Small volume, concentrated urine

    Maximum Urine Dilution¶

    Without ADH: 50 mOsm/kg - Collecting duct impermeable to water - Water not reabsorbed - Large volume, dilute urine

    NEET PG: Urine can be 24× diluted (50 mOsm) but only 4× concentrated (1200 mOsm)

    Hormonal Regulation¶

    1. ADH (Vasopressin)¶

    Source: Posterior pituitary Stimulus: ↑ Plasma osmolality (>280 mOsm), ↓ Blood volume

    Mechanism: - Inserts aquaporin-2 in collecting duct - ↑ Water reabsorption - Concentrated urine

    NEET PG: - Central DI: No ADH production - Nephrogenic DI: Kidneys don't respond to ADH - SIADH: Excess ADH → Hyponatremia

    2. Aldosterone¶

    Source: Adrenal cortex (zona glomerulosa) Stimulus: ↑ Angiotensin II, ↑ K+, ↓ Na+

    Mechanism: - ↑ ENaC in collecting duct - ↑ Na+ reabsorption, ↑ K+ secretion - ↑ Blood pressure

    NEET PG: - Primary hyperaldosteronism: Hypertension, hypokalemia - Conn's syndrome: Aldosterone-secreting adenoma

    3. PTH (Parathyroid Hormone)¶

    Effects on Kidney: - ↑ Ca2+ reabsorption (DCT) - ↓ Phosphate reabsorption (PCT) - ↑ 1α-hydroxylase → Active vitamin D

    4. ANP (Atrial Natriuretic Peptide)¶

    Source: Atrial myocytes Stimulus: ↑ Atrial stretch (volume overload)

    Effects: - ↑ GFR (dilates afferent, constricts efferent) - ↓ Na+ reabsorption - ↓ Renin, ↓ Aldosterone - ↓ ADH - Net: Natriuresis & diuresis

    Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS)¶

    ↓ BP, ↓ Na+, ↑ Sympathetic
         ↓
   Juxtaglomerular cells
         ↓
      RENIN
         ↓
Angiotensinogen → Angiotensin I
                      ↓ (ACE in lungs)
                 Angiotensin II
         ┌────────┴────────┐
    Vasoconstriction   Aldosterone
    (↑ BP)             (↑ Na+, ↑ BP)

    Effects of Angiotensin II: 1. Systemic vasoconstriction 2. Efferent > afferent arteriole constriction (maintains GFR) 3. ↑ Aldosterone secretion 4. ↑ ADH secretion 5. ↑ Proximal tubule Na+ reabsorption 6. ↑ Thirst

    NEET PG: - ACE inhibitors: Block formation of Angiotensin II - ARBs: Block Angiotensin II receptors - Both cause: ↓ BP, ↑ K+ (less aldosterone), ↓ GFR

    Potassium Handling¶

    Reabsorption¶

    • PCT: 65% (paracellular)
    • TAL: 25% (via NKCC2)
    • DCT: 5%

    Secretion¶

    • Collecting duct: Principal cells (via ROMK)
    • Regulated by aldosterone

    Factors ↑ K+ Secretion: - ↑ Aldosterone - ↑ Tubular flow rate - ↑ Plasma K+ - Metabolic alkalosis

    NEET PG: - Loop diuretics: ↑ K+ loss (↑ flow to collecting duct) - Aldosterone antagonists (spironolactone): Hyperkalemia

    Calcium & Phosphate¶

    Calcium¶

    Reabsorption: - PCT: 60-70% (paracellular) - TAL: 20% (paracellular) - DCT: 10% (PTH-stimulated, active transport)

    NEET PG: - Thiazides: ↑ Ca2+ reabsorption → Hypocalciuria - Loop diuretics: ↓ Ca2+ reabsorption → Hypercalciuria

    Phosphate¶

    Reabsorption: - PCT: 80% (via Na-Pi cotransporter) - Inhibited by PTH & FGF-23

    NEET PG: PTH causes phosphaturia (↓ phosphate reabsorption)

    Acid-Base Handling¶

    H+ Secretion¶

    • PCT: 80-90% HCO3- reabsorption (via carbonic anhydrase)
    • Collecting duct: H+ secretion (type A intercalated cells)

    HCO3- Reabsorption¶

    Tubular lumen    |    Cell    |    Blood
                 |            |
H+ + HCO3- → H2CO3 → CO2 + H2O → H2CO3 → H+ + HCO3-
                 |  (CA)      |   (CA)    |
                 |            |           → HCO3- reabsorbed

    NEET PG: Acetazolamide inhibits carbonic anhydrase → ↓ HCO3- reabsorption → Alkaline urine

    Clearance Concepts - HIGH YIELD¶

    Clearance: Volume of plasma completely cleared of a substance per unit time

    Formula: C = (U × V) / P - U = Urine concentration - V = Urine flow rate - P = Plasma concentration

    Renal Handling¶

    Substance Clearance vs GFR Handling
    Inulin C = GFR Filtered only
    Glucose C = 0 Filtered, 100% reabsorbed
    PAH C > GFR Filtered + secreted
    Urea C < GFR Filtered, partially reabsorbed
    Creatinine C ≈ GFR (slight >) Filtered, small secretion

    NEET PG: - PAH clearance = Renal plasma flow (RPF) when fully secreted - Inulin clearance = GFR (gold standard)

    Diuretics - Quick Reference¶

    Diuretic Site Mechanism Effects
    Acetazolamide PCT ↓ Carbonic anhydrase Alkaline urine, ↓ HCO3-
    Loop (Furosemide) TAL Block NKCC2 Hypokalemia, hypocalcemia, metabolic alkalosis
    Thiazides DCT Block NCC Hypokalemia, hypercalcemia, metabolic alkalosis
    K-sparing (Amiloride) CD Block ENaC Hyperkalemia
    Spironolactone CD Block aldosterone Hyperkalemia, metabolic acidosis

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Normal GFR? Answer: 125 ml/min

    2. Filtration fraction? Answer: 20% (GFR/RPF)

    3. Maximum glucose reabsorption occurs at? Answer: PCT (via SGLT2)

    4. Diluting segment of nephron? Answer: Thick ascending limb

    5. ADH acts on which aquaporin? Answer: Aquaporin-2 (collecting duct)

    6. Loop diuretics block which transporter? Answer: NKCC2 (Na-K-2Cl)

    7. Site of PTH action for Ca2+ reabsorption? Answer: DCT

    8. Gold standard for GFR measurement? Answer: Inulin clearance

    Memory Aids¶

    PCT Reabsorption¶

    "PCT Gets All the Good Stuff" - 65-70% of Na+, water - 100% glucose, amino acids - 80-90% HCO3-

    Diuretics Order (Proximal to Distal)¶

    "A**ce **L**oopy **T**hieves **S**pared **K**idneys" - **A**cetazolamide (PCT) - **L**oop diuretics (TAL) - **T**hiazides (DCT) - **S**pironolactone (CD) - **K-sparing (CD)

    ADH Effects¶

    "ADH Adds H2O" - Inserts AQP2 → Water reabsorption

    • Memorize normal values (GFR, RBF, RPF, FF)
    • Know functions of each nephron segment
    • Learn diuretic sites of action
    • Understand RAAS pathway
    • Know ADH and aldosterone mechanisms
    • Learn clearance concepts
    • Understand countercurrent mechanism
    • Practice calculations (CrCl, clearance)
