    HYPERTENSIVE DISORDERS OF PREGNANCY - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Definition¶

    Hypertension in pregnancy: BP ≥140/90 mmHg on 2 occasions, 4 hours apart

    Classification (ACOG 2020)¶

    1. CHRONIC HYPERTENSION¶

    Definition: HTN before 20 weeks OR pre-existing

    BP: ≥140/90 mmHg

    Management: - Continue antihypertensive (avoid ACE-I, ARBs) - Monitor for superimposed pre-eclampsia - Target BP: <160/110 mmHg

    NEET PG: ACE inhibitors & ARBs contraindicated in pregnancy (teratogenic)

    2. GESTATIONAL HYPERTENSION¶

    Definition: HTN after 20 weeks without proteinuria/end-organ damage

    Features: - BP ≥140/90 mmHg - NO proteinuria - NO symptoms (headache, visual disturbances, epigastric pain)

    Progression: 25-50% → Pre-eclampsia

    Management: - Monitor BP, urine protein, symptoms - Deliver at 37 weeks if stable

    NEET PG: Gestational HTN = HTN after 20 weeks without proteinuria

    3. PRE-ECLAMPSIA¶

    Definition: HTN after 20 weeks + Proteinuria OR end-organ dysfunction

    Criteria: HTN + ANY of: - Proteinuria: ≥300 mg/24hr OR Protein:Cr ratio ≥0.3 - Thrombocytopenia: Platelets <100,000 - Renal insufficiency: Creatinine >1.1 mg/dL - Liver dysfunction: Transaminases 2× normal, RUQ pain - Pulmonary edema - Cerebral symptoms: Headache, visual disturbances

    NEET PG: Proteinuria NOT required for diagnosis (end-organ damage sufficient)

    PRE-ECLAMPSIA WITHOUT SEVERE FEATURES¶

    BP: 140-159/90-109 mmHg No severe features

    Management: - <34 weeks: Expectant (if stable) - 34-37 weeks: Individualized - ≥37 weeks: Delivery

    PRE-ECLAMPSIA WITH SEVERE FEATURES¶

    BP: ≥160/110 mmHg OR severe symptoms

    Severe Features (any ONE): - BP: ≥160/110 mmHg - Symptoms: - Severe headache - Visual disturbances (blurred vision, scotoma) - Epigastric/RUQ pain (liver capsule distension) - Thrombocytopenia: <100,000 - Elevated liver enzymes: >2× normal - Renal insufficiency: Cr >1.1 mg/dL - Pulmonary edema - Fetal growth restriction

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Headache + visual disturbances + epigastric pain = Impending eclampsia - RUQ pain = Liver involvement (HELLP syndrome)

    Management: - MgSO4 (seizure prophylaxis) - Antihypertensive if BP ≥160/110 - Delivery: - ≥34 weeks: Immediate delivery - <34 weeks: Corticosteroids + delivery within 48 hours

    4. ECLAMPSIA¶

    Definition: Seizures in pre-eclampsia (NOT attributable to other causes)

    Timing: - Antepartum: 50% - Intrapartum: 20% - Postpartum: 30% (up to 6 weeks)

    Management: 1. Airway protection: Left lateral position 2. MgSO4: 4g IV bolus → 1g/hr infusion 3. BP control: Labetalol, hydralazine, nifedipine 4. Delivery: After stabilization (definitive treatment)

    NEET PG: - MgSO4 = Drug of choice for eclampsia - Antidote: Calcium gluconate (if MgSO4 toxicity)

    5. CHRONIC HTN WITH SUPERIMPOSED PRE-ECLAMPSIA¶

    Definition: Pre-existing HTN + new-onset proteinuria/end-organ dysfunction after 20 weeks

    Diagnosis: - Sudden ↑ in BP (≥160/110) - New proteinuria - Thrombocytopenia - Elevated liver enzymes

    Management: Same as severe pre-eclampsia

    HELLP SYNDROME¶

    Definition: Severe form of pre-eclampsia

    HELLP: - **H**emolysis (schistocytes, ↑ LDH, ↑ indirect bilirubin) - **E**levated **L**iver enzymes (AST, ALT >2× normal) - **L**ow **P**latelets (<100,000)

    Clinical: - RUQ/epigastric pain (most common symptom) - Nausea, vomiting - Malaise - May occur without severe hypertension (15%)

    Complications: - Hepatic rupture (subcapsular hematoma) - DIC - Acute renal failure - Placental abruption

    NEET PG High-Yield: - RUQ pain in pregnancy = Think HELLP - Can occur without severe HTN - Most common symptom: RUQ pain - Delivery = Only cure

    Management: - MgSO4 (seizure prophylaxis) - BP control - Delivery: - ≥34 weeks: Immediate - <34 weeks: Corticosteroids + delivery within 48 hours - Platelet transfusion: If <20,000 or bleeding

    Pathophysiology of Pre-eclampsia¶

    Two-Stage Model¶

    Stage 1 (8-18 weeks): Abnormal placentation - Inadequate trophoblast invasion - Failure of spiral artery remodeling - Placental ischemia

    Stage 2 (>20 weeks): Maternal syndrome - ↑ sFlt-1 (soluble fms-like tyrosine kinase-1): Anti-angiogenic - ↓ PlGF (Placental growth factor): Pro-angiogenic - Endothelial dysfunction - Systemic manifestations

    NEET PG: ↑ sFlt-1/PlGF ratio = Pre-eclampsia

    Risk Factors¶

    High Risk (any ONE → Aspirin prophylaxis)¶

    • Previous pre-eclampsia
    • Chronic hypertension
    • Diabetes mellitus (Type 1 or 2)
    • Renal disease
    • Autoimmune disease (SLE, APS)

    Moderate Risk (≥2 → Aspirin prophylaxis)¶

    • First pregnancy
    • Age ≥35 years
    • BMI >30
    • Family history of pre-eclampsia
    • Multiple pregnancy
    • Interval >10 years between pregnancies

    NEET PG: Low-dose aspirin (75-150 mg) started <16 weeks reduces pre-eclampsia risk

    Prevention¶

    Aspirin Prophylaxis¶

    Indication: High-risk OR ≥2 moderate risk factors Dose: 75-150 mg daily Timing: Start <16 weeks, continue until delivery Benefit: ↓ Pre-eclampsia by 10-15%

    Calcium Supplementation¶

    For: Low dietary calcium intake (<600 mg/day) Dose: 1.5-2g elemental calcium daily

    NEET PG: Aspirin prophylaxis for high-risk women

    Management of Hypertension in Pregnancy¶

    Target BP¶

    Chronic HTN: <160/110 mmHg Pre-eclampsia: <160/110 mmHg (treat if ≥160/110)

    Antihypertensive Drugs¶

    Drug Dose Route Side Effects Notes
    Labetalol 20-80mg q10min IV Bronchospasm DOC for acute HTN
    100-400mg TDS Oral
    Hydralazine 5-10mg q20min IV Headache, tachycardia Alternative
    Nifedipine 10-20mg q30min Oral Flushing Immediate-release
    Methyldopa 250-500mg TDS Oral Sedation 1st line for chronic HTN

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Labetalol: DOC for acute severe HTN - Methyldopa: 1st line for chronic HTN (safe in pregnancy) - Avoid: ACE-I, ARBs (teratogenic), atenolol (IUGR)

    Magnesium Sulfate (MgSO4)¶

    Indications¶

    • Eclampsia (treatment)
    • Severe pre-eclampsia (prophylaxis)

    Regimen¶

    Loading dose: 4g IV over 15-20 min Maintenance: 1g/hr IV infusion

    Duration: 24 hours after delivery OR last seizure

    Monitoring¶

    • Urine output: ≥30 ml/hr
    • Respiratory rate: ≥12/min
    • Deep tendon reflexes: Present
    • Serum Mg2+: 4-8 mEq/L (therapeutic range)

    Toxicity Signs (in order)¶

    1. Loss of deep tendon reflexes (7-10 mEq/L)
    2. Respiratory depression (10-13 mEq/L)
    3. Cardiac arrest (>15 mEq/L)

    Antidote: Calcium gluconate (10 ml of 10% IV over 3 min)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - MgSO4: DOC for eclampsia - First sign of toxicity: Loss of deep tendon reflexes - Antidote: Calcium gluconate - Contraindication: Myasthenia gravis

    Timing of Delivery¶

    Condition Gestational Age Timing
    Gestational HTN ≥37 weeks Deliver
    Pre-eclampsia without severe features <37 weeks Expectant (if stable)
    ≥37 weeks Deliver
    Pre-eclampsia with severe features <34 weeks Corticosteroids + deliver within 48hr
    ≥34 weeks Immediate delivery
    Eclampsia Any Stabilize → Deliver
    HELLP syndrome <34 weeks Corticosteroids + deliver within 48hr
    ≥34 weeks Immediate delivery

    NEET PG: - Delivery = Only definitive treatment - Severe pre-eclampsia at ≥34 weeks: Immediate delivery - <34 weeks: Corticosteroids (lung maturity) + delivery within 48 hours

    Corticosteroids for Fetal Lung Maturity¶

    Indication: Delivery anticipated <34 weeks

    Regimen: - Betamethasone: 12mg IM × 2 doses, 24 hours apart - Dexamethasone: 6mg IM q12h × 4 doses

    Benefit: ↓ Respiratory distress syndrome, intraventricular hemorrhage, NEC

    NEET PG: Give corticosteroids if delivery expected <34 weeks

    Maternal Complications¶

    1. Eclampsia: Seizures
    2. Cerebrovascular accident: ICH, thrombosis
    3. HELLP syndrome
    4. Pulmonary edema
    5. Acute kidney injury
    6. Hepatic rupture (subcapsular hematoma)
    7. Placental abruption
    8. DIC

    NEET PG: Leading cause of maternal mortality in pre-eclampsia = ICH

    Fetal Complications¶

    1. Intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR)
    2. Oligohydramnios
    3. Placental abruption
    4. Preterm delivery
    5. Intrauterine fetal death

    Postpartum Management¶

    Monitoring: - BP: Every 4 hours for 48 hours, then daily - Urine output - Symptoms: Headache, visual changes, RUQ pain

    MgSO4: Continue for 24 hours postpartum

    Antihypertensives: - Continue if needed - Safe in breastfeeding: Labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa, enalapril

    Follow-up: - BP check at 7-10 days postpartum - If persistent HTN: Evaluate for secondary causes

    Contraception: Avoid estrogen-containing pills (thrombosis risk)

    NEET PG: - Eclampsia can occur up to 6 weeks postpartum - Safe antihypertensives in breastfeeding: Labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa

    Future Pregnancy¶

    Counseling: - Recurrence risk: 10-15% (higher if severe or early-onset) - Aspirin prophylaxis: Start <16 weeks in next pregnancy - Cardiovascular risk: ↑ Lifetime risk of HTN, CVD, stroke

    NEET PG: Pre-eclampsia = Risk factor for future CVD

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Drug of choice for eclampsia? Answer: Magnesium sulfate

    2. Antidote for MgSO4 toxicity? Answer: Calcium gluconate

    3. First sign of MgSO4 toxicity? Answer: Loss of deep tendon reflexes

    4. Delivery timing for severe pre-eclampsia at ≥34 weeks? Answer: Immediate delivery

    5. Most common symptom of HELLP syndrome? Answer: RUQ pain

    6. Prophylaxis for high-risk pre-eclampsia? Answer: Low-dose aspirin (75-150 mg) <16 weeks

    7. Drugs contraindicated in pregnancy? Answer: ACE inhibitors, ARBs

    8. Leading cause of maternal death in pre-eclampsia? Answer: Intracranial hemorrhage

    Memory Aids¶

    Severe Features of Pre-eclampsia¶

    "HEPATIC VISION" - **H**eadache (persistent, severe) - **E**pigastric pain - **P**latelets <100,000 - **A**ST/ALT elevated - **T**hrombocytopenia - **I**ncreased creatinine >1.1 - **C**erebral symptoms - **V**isual disturbances - **I**ncreased BP ≥160/110 - **S**hortness of breath (pulmonary edema) - **I**UGR (fetal) - **(O)**liguria - **(N)**ephrotic range proteinuria

    HELLP Syndrome¶

    "**H**elpless **E**ating **L**iver **L**osing **P**latelets" - **H**emolysis - **E**levated - **L**iver enzymes - **L**ow - **P**latelets

    • Learn severe features of pre-eclampsia
    • Memorize MgSO4 dosing and monitoring
    • Know HELLP syndrome criteria
    • Understand delivery timing for each condition
    • Learn safe antihypertensives in pregnancy
    • Know aspirin prophylaxis indications
    • Practice 40+ MCQs on hypertensive disorders
