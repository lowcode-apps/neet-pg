    BLOOD SUPPLY OF GI TRACT - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Overview¶

    GI tract supplied by 3 unpaired branches of abdominal aorta: 1. Celiac trunk (T12) → Foregut 2. Superior mesenteric artery (L1) → Midgut 3. Inferior mesenteric artery (L3) → Hindgut

    Embryological Divisions¶

    Division Extent Artery Venous Drainage
    Foregut Esophagus → 2nd part of duodenum (up to ampulla) Celiac trunk Portal vein
    Midgut 2nd part duodenum → Proximal ⅔ of transverse colon Superior mesenteric Superior mesenteric vein
    Hindgut Distal ⅓ transverse colon → Upper anal canal Inferior mesenteric Inferior mesenteric vein

    1. CELIAC TRUNK (Foregut)¶

    Origin: Abdominal aorta at T12 (aortic hiatus level) Length: 1-2 cm Branches: "LHS" - Left gastric, Hepatic, Splenic

            Celiac Trunk (T12)
              |
     ┌────────┼────────┐
     |        |         |
  Left     Hepatic   Splenic
 Gastric   Artery    Artery

    A. LEFT GASTRIC ARTERY (Smallest)¶

    Course: Ascends → Esophagogastric junction → Lesser curvature Supplies: - Lower esophagus - Lesser curvature of stomach (upper part)

    Anastomoses: Right gastric artery

    B. COMMON HEPATIC ARTERY¶

    Course: Runs right along upper border of pancreas Branches:

    Common Hepatic Artery
         |
    ┌────┴────┐
Proper    Gastroduodenal
Hepatic      (GDA)
    |
 ┌──┴──┐

Hepatic Hepatic
    |
Right Gastric
Cystic (via Right Hepatic)

    1. Proper Hepatic Artery¶

    • Right hepatic → Right lobe of liver
    • Cystic artery → Gallbladder (Calot's triangle)
    • Left hepatic → Left lobe of liver
    • Right gastric → Lesser curvature (anastomoses with left gastric)

    2. Gastroduodenal Artery (GDA)¶

    Branches: - Right gastroepiploic → Greater curvature (right side) - Superior pancreaticoduodenal (anterior + posterior) → Pancreatic head + duodenum

    NEET PG: GDA erosion → massive upper GI bleed in chronic pancreatitis

    C. SPLENIC ARTERY¶

    Course: Tortuous, along upper border of pancreas → Spleen Branches: 1. Pancreatic branches → Body & tail of pancreas 2. Short gastric arteries (5-7) → Fundus of stomach 3. Left gastroepiploic → Greater curvature (left side)

    NEET PG: Most tortuous artery, splenic artery aneurysm common

    Stomach Blood Supply Summary¶

              Short Gastric
          (from Splenic)
               ↓
    ┌──────────────────┐
    │                  │  ← Left Gastric
    │    STOMACH       │
    │                  │  ← Right Gastric
    └──────────────────┘
         ↑          ↑
    Left GE      Right GE
   (Splenic)    (Hepatic)

    Four Arcades: 1. Lesser curvature: Left gastric + Right gastric 2. Greater curvature: Left gastroepiploic + Right gastroepiploic 3. Fundus: Short gastric arteries 4. Cardia: Branches of left gastric

    2. SUPERIOR MESENTERIC ARTERY (Midgut)¶

    Origin: L1 (1 cm below celiac trunk) Course: Behind neck of pancreas → Between uncinate process & left renal vein Emerges: At lower border of pancreas

    Branches (from medial to lateral):

    Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA)
         |
    ┌────┼────┬────┬─────┬────┐
    |    |    |    |     |    |
   IPD  Jejunal Ileal Ileo- RCA MCA
        branches     colic

    1. Inferior Pancreaticoduodenal (IPD)¶

    Supplies: Pancreatic head, duodenum (3rd & 4th parts) Anastomoses: Superior pancreaticoduodenal (from GDA)

    2. Jejunal & Ileal Branches (12-15)¶

    Supplies: Jejunum & ileum Arrangement: Arterial arcades in mesentery - Jejunum: 1-2 arcades, long vasa recta - Ileum: 3-4 arcades, short vasa recta

    NEET PG: Jejunum has fewer arcades but longer vasa recta

    3. Ileocolic Artery¶

    Supplies: - Terminal ileum - Cecum - Appendix (via appendicular artery) - Ascending colon (lower part)

    NEET PG: Appendicular artery is end artery (thrombosis → appendiceal gangrene)

    4. Right Colic Artery¶

    Supplies: Ascending colon Note: May be absent in 10% (then supplied by ileocolic + middle colic)

    5. Middle Colic Artery¶

    Supplies: Transverse colon (proximal ⅔) Anastomoses: Left colic (forms marginal artery of Drummond)

    3. INFERIOR MESENTERIC ARTERY (Hindgut)¶

    Origin: L3 (3-4 cm above aortic bifurcation) Course: Descends in front of left common iliac artery

    Branches:

    Inferior Mesenteric Artery (IMA)
         |
    ┌────┼────┬─────┐
    |    |    |     |
   Left  Sig- Supe-
   Colic moid rior
         Art  Rectal

    1. Left Colic Artery¶

    Supplies: Distal ⅓ transverse colon, descending colon Branches: Ascending + Descending Anastomoses: Middle colic (forms marginal artery)

    2. Sigmoid Arteries (2-3)¶

    Supplies: Sigmoid colon

    3. Superior Rectal Artery (Terminal branch)¶

    Supplies: Upper ⅓ rectum Anastomoses: Middle & inferior rectal arteries

    Collateral Circulation - HIGH YIELD¶

    1. Marginal Artery of Drummond¶

    Location: Along mesenteric border of colon Formation: Anastomoses between SMA & IMA branches Significance: Protects colon from ischemia

    Weak Point: Griffith's point (splenic flexure - junction of SMA & IMA territories)

    2. Arc of Riolan (Meandering Artery)¶

    Location: Direct connection between SMA (middle colic) & IMA (left colic) Significance: Alternative collateral if marginal artery blocked

    3. Pancreaticoduodenal Arcade¶

    Formation: Superior PD (celiac) + Inferior PD (SMA) Significance: Connects celiac trunk & SMA

    Watershed Areas (Ischemia-Prone) - NEET PG¶

    Area Junction Clinical Significance
    Splenic flexure SMA + IMA Griffith's point, most common site of ischemic colitis
    Rectosigmoid IMA + Internal iliac Sudeck's point
    Duodenum Celiac + SMA Rare ischemia

    NEET PG: Splenic flexure most vulnerable to ischemia

    Rectal Blood Supply¶

    Arteries (3)¶

    1. Superior rectal (from IMA) → Upper ⅓
    2. Middle rectal (from internal iliac) → Middle ⅓
    3. Inferior rectal (from internal pudendal) → Lower ⅓

    Veins (3)¶

    1. Superior rectal vein → IMV → Portal system
    2. Middle rectal vein → Internal iliac vein → Systemic
    3. Inferior rectal vein → Internal pudendal → Systemic

    Portosystemic Anastomosis: Superior rectal (portal) + Middle & Inferior rectal (systemic)

    NEET PG: Hemorrhoids due to portosystemic shunting in portal hypertension

    Portal Vein Formation¶

        Splenic Vein (from spleen, stomach, pancreas)
              +
Superior Mesenteric Vein (from SMA territory)
              ↓
        PORTAL VEIN
         (behind neck of pancreas)
              ↓
           LIVER

    Tributaries of Portal Vein: - Superior mesenteric vein (SMV) - Splenic vein (main tributaries) - Inferior mesenteric vein (IMV) → Usually joins splenic vein - Left gastric vein (coronary vein) - Right gastric vein - Cystic vein

    NEET PG: Portal vein formed by SMV + Splenic vein behind neck of pancreas

    Portosystemic Anastomoses - HIGH YIELD¶

    Site Portal Systemic Clinical
    Lower esophagus Left gastric vein Esophageal veins (azygos) Esophageal varices
    Rectum Superior rectal Middle & Inferior rectal Hemorrhoids
    Umbilicus Paraumbilical veins Superficial epigastric Caput medusae
    Retroperitoneum Colic veins Retroperitoneal veins Dilated veins
    Bare area of liver Portal branches Phrenic veins Dilated veins

    NEET PG: 5 sites of portosystemic anastomoses, esophageal varices most dangerous

    Clinical Correlations¶

    1. Mesenteric Ischemia¶

    Acute SMA Occlusion: - Sudden severe abdominal pain ("pain out of proportion") - Bloody diarrhea - Peritonitis - Treatment: Emergency laparotomy

    Chronic Mesenteric Ischemia: - Postprandial pain (intestinal angina) - Weight loss (fear of eating) - Treatment: Revascularization

    NEET PG: "Pain out of proportion to physical findings" = Mesenteric ischemia

    2. Ischemic Colitis¶

    Most common site: Splenic flexure (Griffith's point) Causes: Atherosclerosis, hypotension, vasculitis Features: Bloody diarrhea, abdominal pain, thumbprinting on X-ray

    3. Peptic Ulcer Bleeding¶

    Posterior gastric ulcer: Can erode into splenic artery Posterior duodenal ulcer: Can erode into gastroduodenal artery

    4. Appendicitis¶

    Appendicular artery: End artery (no collaterals) Thrombosis: Leads to gangrenous appendicitis

    Calot's Triangle - NEET PG Favorite¶

    Boundaries: - Inferior: Cystic duct - Superior: Inferior surface of liver - Medial: Common hepatic duct

    Contents: - Cystic artery (from right hepatic artery in 75% cases) - Cystic lymph node (Lund's node)

    Clinical Significance: Identified during cholecystectomy to avoid CBD injury

    NEET PG: Cystic artery normally arises from right hepatic artery

    Mesenteric Angiography Features¶

    Vessel Characteristic Clinical Use
    Celiac trunk Seagull appearance Identify celiac stenosis
    SMA Arborization pattern GI bleeding localization
    IMA Smaller caliber Colonic ischemia evaluation

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Watershed area most prone to ischemia? Answer: Splenic flexure (Griffith's point)

    2. Artery supplying appendix? Answer: Appendicular artery (from ileocolic)

    3. Portal vein formed behind? Answer: Neck of pancreas

    4. Most tortuous artery? Answer: Splenic artery

    5. End artery to appendix? Answer: Appendicular artery

    6. Contents of Calot's triangle? Answer: Cystic artery, cystic lymph node

    7. Marginal artery of Drummond formed by? Answer: Anastomosis between SMA and IMA branches

    8. Most common site of ischemic colitis? Answer: Splenic flexure

    Celiac Trunk Branches¶

    "**L**eft **H**and **S**hake" - **L**eft gastric - **H**epatic - **S**plenic

    SMA Branches (Medial to Lateral)¶

    "I **J**ust **I**magine **I**ce **R**eally **M**elts" - **I**nferior pancreaticoduodenal - **J**ejunal - **I**leal - **I**leocolic - **R**ight colic - **M**iddle colic

    IMA Branches¶

    "**L**eft **S**ide **S**ucks **R**ectally" - **L**eft colic - **S**igmoid - **S**uperior **R**ectal

    Portosystemic Anastomoses (5 Sites)¶

    "**E**very **R**ed **U**mbrella **B**rings **L**uck" - **E**sophagus (varices) - **R**ectum (hemorrhoids) - **U**mbilicus (caput medusae) - **B**are area of liver - **L**igamentum teres

    • Know foregut, midgut, hindgut divisions
    • Understand celiac trunk branches (LHS)
    • Learn SMA and IMA branch patterns
    • Memorize watershed areas
    • Know portosystemic anastomoses (5 sites)
    • Understand Calot's triangle anatomy
    • Learn marginal artery of Drummond
    • Know rectal blood supply
    • Practice 25+ MCQs on GI blood supply
