    CRANIAL NERVES - NEET PG Study Material¶

    Overview¶

    12 pairs of cranial nerves arising from the brain Mnemonic for names: "**O**h **O**h **O**h **T**o **T**ouch **A**nd **F**eel **V**ery **G**ood **V**elvet **A**h **H**eaven"

    Classification by Function¶

    Sensory Only (3)¶

    1. I - Olfactory
    2. II - Optic
    3. VIII - Vestibulocochlear

    Motor Only (5)¶

    1. III - Oculomotor
    2. IV - Trochlear
    3. VI - Abducens
    4. XI - Accessory
    5. XII - Hypoglossal

    Mnemonic: "**S**ome **S**ay **M**arry **M**oney **B**ut **M**y **B**rother **S**ays **B**ig **B**rains **M**atter **M**ore" (S=Sensory, M=Motor, B=Both)

    Mixed (4)¶

    1. V - Trigeminal
    2. VII - Facial
    3. IX - Glossopharyngeal
    4. X - Vagus

    Detailed Description¶

    CN I - OLFACTORY (Sensory)¶

    Origin: Olfactory epithelium → Cribriform plate → Olfactory bulb Function: Smell Testing: Identify odors (coffee, vanilla) Lesion: Anosmia Causes: Head trauma, meningioma, COVID-19

    CN II - OPTIC (Sensory)¶

    Origin: Retina → Optic chiasma → Lateral geniculate body Function: Vision Testing: Visual acuity, visual fields, fundoscopy

    Visual Field Defects - HIGH YIELD 

    Lesion Site          |  Visual Field Defect
---------------------|-------------------------
Optic nerve          |  Ipsilateral blindness
Optic chiasma        |  Bitemporal hemianopia
Optic tract          |  Contralateral homonymous hemianopia
Optic radiation      |  Quadrantanopia
Occipital cortex     |  Macular sparing hemianopia

    NEET PG Favorite: Bitemporal hemianopia = Pituitary tumor

    CN III - OCULOMOTOR (Motor + Parasympathetic)¶

    Origin: Midbrain (at superior colliculus level) Exit: Superior orbital fissure

    Functions: - Motor: All extraocular muscles EXCEPT SO & LR - Superior rectus (SR) - Inferior rectus (IR) - Medial rectus (MR) - Inferior oblique (IO) - Levator palpebrae superioris (LPS) - Parasympathetic: Sphincter pupillae, ciliary muscle

    Testing: H-pattern eye movements, pupillary reflex

    Lesion - Complete CN III Palsy: - Ptosis (LPS paralysis) - Eye "down and out" (SO + LR intact) - Dilated pupil (unopposed sympathetic) - Loss of accommodation

    Causes: Posterior communicating artery aneurysm (pupil involved), Diabetes (pupil spared - medical CN III palsy)

    NEET PG Pearl: Pupil involvement → Surgical cause (aneurysm)

    CN IV - TROCHLEAR (Motor)¶

    Origin: Dorsal midbrain (only nerve from dorsal aspect) Exit: Superior orbital fissure Function: Superior oblique (SO) muscle Action: Depression + Intorsion (when eye adducted)

    Testing: Ask patient to look down and in (reading position)

    Lesion: - Diplopia on looking down - Head tilt to opposite side (compensatory) - Cannot descend stairs

    NEET PG: Longest intracranial course, most commonly injured in head trauma

    CN V - TRIGEMINAL (Mixed)¶

    Origin: Pons Ganglion: Trigeminal (Gasserian) ganglion in Meckel's cave

    Three Divisions:

    V1 - Ophthalmic (Sensory)¶

    • Exit: Superior orbital fissure
    • Supply: Forehead, upper eyelid, cornea, nose tip
    • Branch: Nasociliary → Long ciliary → Corneal reflex (afferent)

    V2 - Maxillary (Sensory)¶

    • Exit: Foramen rotundum
    • Supply: Lower eyelid, cheek, upper lip, upper teeth, maxillary sinus

    V3 - Mandibular (Mixed)¶

    • Exit: Foramen ovale
    • Sensory: Lower lip, chin, lower teeth, anterior ⅔ tongue (general sensation)
    • Motor: Muscles of mastication (masseter, temporalis, pterygoids)

    Testing: - Sensory: Touch, pain, temperature on face - Motor: Clench teeth (masseter), jaw jerk reflex - Corneal reflex: V1 afferent, VII efferent

    Lesion: - Trigeminal neuralgia (Tic douloureux) - Jaw deviates to SAME side (unopposed opposite pterygoid)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Largest cranial nerve - Corneal reflex: Afferent = V1, Efferent = VII - Trigeminal neuralgia: Carbamazepine first-line

    CN VI - ABDUCENS (Motor)¶

    Origin: Pontomedullary junction Exit: Superior orbital fissure Course: Longest intradural course Function: Lateral rectus (LR) - ABduction

    Lesion: - Cannot abduct eye - Diplopia on lateral gaze - False localizing sign in raised ICP

    NEET PG: Most commonly affected in raised ICP

    CN VII - FACIAL (Mixed)¶

    Origin: Pontomedullary junction Exit: Internal acoustic meatus → Facial canal → Stylomastoid foramen

    Functions: - Motor: Muscles of facial expression - Sensory: Taste from anterior ⅔ tongue (chorda tympani) - Parasympathetic: Lacrimal, submandibular, sublingual glands - Special: Stapedius muscle (dampens sound)

    Branches - "**T**o **Z**anzibar **B**y **M**otor **C**ar" - Temporal - Zygomatic - Buccal - Marginal mandibular - Cervical

    Testing: - Raise eyebrows, close eyes tight, show teeth, puff cheeks - Taste on anterior ⅔ tongue

    Lesions:

    Feature UMN (Stroke) LMN (Bell's palsy)
    Forehead Spared Affected
    Eye closure Present Absent (Bell's phenomenon)
    Nasolabial fold Lost Lost
    Taste Normal Lost if proximal to chorda tympani
    Hyperacusis Absent Present (stapedius paralysis)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - UMN lesion: Forehead spared (bilateral cortical supply) - LMN lesion: Entire half of face affected - Bell's palsy: Idiopathic LMN facial palsy, treat with steroids + acyclovir - Ramsay Hunt syndrome: VZV reactivation, facial palsy + vesicles in ear

    CN VIII - VESTIBULOCOCHLEAR (Sensory)¶

    Origin: Inner ear Exit: Internal acoustic meatus Two Parts:

    Cochlear (Hearing)¶

    • Testing: Weber, Rinne tests
    • Weber: Tuning fork on forehead
    • Conductive loss: Lateralizes to affected ear
    • Sensorineural loss: Lateralizes to normal ear
    • Rinne: Air conduction vs Bone conduction
    • Normal/SNHL: AC > BC (positive)
    • Conductive: BC > AC (negative)

    Vestibular (Balance)¶

    • Testing: Caloric test, Romberg test, nystagmus
    • Lesion: Vertigo, nystagmus, ataxia

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Acoustic neuroma: Schwannoma at cerebellopontine angle - Presents with: Hearing loss, tinnitus, facial numbness (V), facial palsy (VII) - MRI: Ice cream cone appearance

    CN IX - GLOSSOPHARYNGEAL (Mixed)¶

    Origin: Medulla (posterolateral sulcus) Exit: Jugular foramen

    Functions: - Motor: Stylopharyngeus muscle - Sensory: Posterior ⅓ tongue (taste + general sensation), pharynx, middle ear - Parasympathetic: Parotid gland (via otic ganglion) - Special: Carotid body & sinus (chemoreceptor & baroreceptor)

    Testing: Gag reflex (afferent), taste on posterior ⅓ tongue

    Lesion: Loss of gag reflex, loss of taste posterior tongue

    NEET PG: Jacobson's nerve (tympanic branch) → Tympanic plexus → Lesser petrosal → Otic ganglion → Parotid

    CN X - VAGUS (Mixed)¶

    Origin: Medulla Exit: Jugular foramen Course: Longest cranial nerve (extends to abdomen)

    Functions: - Motor: All muscles of pharynx (except stylopharyngeus) & larynx (except cricothyroid) - Sensory: Larynx, pharynx, thoracic & abdominal viscera - Parasympathetic: Heart, lungs, GI tract up to splenic flexure

    Branches: - Pharyngeal - Superior laryngeal (SLN): External (motor to cricothyroid) + Internal (sensory above vocal cords) - Recurrent laryngeal (RLN): All intrinsic laryngeal muscles except cricothyroid, sensory below vocal cords

    Testing: - Say "Ahhh" - Uvula deviates to NORMAL side - Cough reflex, gag reflex (efferent)

    Lesion: - Unilateral: Hoarseness, uvula deviates to normal side - Bilateral: Aphonia, stridor, dysphagia

    RLN Injury - NEET PG Favorite: - Unilateral: Hoarseness, vocal cord in paramedian position - Bilateral: Respiratory distress (emergency tracheostomy) - Causes: Thyroid surgery, lung cancer, aortic aneurysm - Left RLN more commonly injured (longer course around aortic arch)

    NEET PG High-Yield: - Gag reflex: IX afferent, X efferent - SLN injury: Cannot increase pitch (cricothyroid) - RLN injury: Cannot abduct vocal cords (posterior cricoarytenoid)

    CN XI - ACCESSORY (Motor)¶

    Origin: - Cranial part: Medulla - Spinal part: C1-C5 spinal cord

    Exit: Jugular foramen Function: Sternocleidomastoid (SCM) + Trapezius

    Testing: - Turn head against resistance (SCM) - Shrug shoulders (trapezius)

    Lesion: - Cannot turn head to opposite side - Shoulder droop on same side - Iatrogenic injury: Lymph node biopsy in posterior triangle

    NEET PG: SCM turns head to OPPOSITE side

    CN XII - HYPOGLOSSAL (Motor)¶

    Origin: Medulla Exit: Hypoglossal canal Function: All intrinsic + extrinsic muscles of tongue (except palatoglossus - X)

    Testing: Stick out tongue, move side to side

    Lesion: - Tongue deviates to SAME side (unopposed opposite genioglossus) - Unilateral: Deviation - Bilateral: Tongue paralysis, dysphagia, dysarthria

    NEET PG: Tongue deviates toward the lesion

    Cranial Nerve Nuclei¶

    Midbrain (3, 4)¶

    • CN III: Oculomotor nucleus, Edinger-Westphal nucleus
    • CN IV: Trochlear nucleus

    Pons (5, 6, 7, 8)¶

    • CN V: Motor + Sensory nuclei
    • CN VI: Abducens nucleus
    • CN VII: Facial nucleus, Superior salivatory nucleus
    • CN VIII: Cochlear + Vestibular nuclei

    Medulla (9, 10, 12) + Spinal cord (11)¶

    • CN IX: Nucleus ambiguus, Inferior salivatory nucleus
    • CN X: Nucleus ambiguus, Dorsal motor nucleus
    • CN XI: Accessory nucleus (C1-C5)
    • CN XII: Hypoglossal nucleus

    Clinical Syndromes¶

    1. Cavernous Sinus Syndrome¶

    Affected: III, IV, V1, V2, VI Cause: Cavernous sinus thrombosis, ICA aneurysm Features: Ophthalmoplegia, facial pain/numbness

    2. Cerebellopontine Angle (CPA) Syndrome¶

    Affected: V, VII, VIII Cause: Acoustic neuroma Features: Hearing loss → facial numbness → facial palsy

    3. Jugular Foramen Syndrome (Vernet)¶

    Affected: IX, X, XI Features: Loss of gag, vocal cord palsy, trapezius weakness

    4. Posterior Condylar Syndrome (Collet-Sicard)¶

    Affected: IX, X, XI, XII Features: Vernet + tongue deviation

    5. Weber Syndrome (Midbrain)¶

    Affected: III + corticospinal tract Features: Ipsilateral CN III palsy + Contralateral hemiplegia

    6. Millard-Gubler Syndrome (Pons)¶

    Affected: VI, VII + corticospinal tract Features: Ipsilateral facial palsy + lateral rectus palsy + Contralateral hemiplegia

    7. Wallenberg Syndrome (Lateral Medullary)¶

    Affected: IX, X + spinothalamic + sympathetic Features: Dysphagia, hoarseness, Horner's, loss of pain/temp opposite body, ataxia

    Reflexes - Must Know¶

    Reflex Afferent Efferent Response
    Corneal V1 VII Eye blink
    Gag IX X Pharynx contracts
    Jaw jerk V3 V3 Jaw closes
    Light (Direct) II III Pupil constricts (same side)
    Light (Consensual) II III Pupil constricts (opposite)
    Accommodation II III Pupils constrict, convergence

    Practice MCQs¶

    1. Which nerve passes through foramen rotundum? Answer: Maxillary nerve (V2)

    2. Tongue deviates to which side in hypoglossal nerve palsy? Answer: Same side as lesion

    3. In Bell's palsy, which muscle paralysis causes hyperacusis? Answer: Stapedius

    4. Afferent limb of corneal reflex? Answer: Ophthalmic nerve (V1)

    5. Which is the longest cranial nerve? Answer: Vagus (X)

    6. Pupil sparing in CN III palsy suggests? Answer: Medical cause (Diabetes)

    Memory Palace Technique¶

    Imagine walking through your house:

    1. Door (I) - Smell flowers
    2. Window (II) - Look outside
    3. Three lights (III) - Moving up, down, medially
    4. Four-poster bed (IV) - Look down at pillow
    5. Five fingers touching Face (V)
    6. Six-pack looking Lateral (VI)
    7. Seven dwarfs making Facial expressions (VII)
    8. Eight-ball (VIII) - Hear it crack
    9. Nine lives of cat, Tasting food (IX)
    10. Ten commandments (X) - Vagabond travels far
    11. Eleven o'clock, Shoulder shrug time (XI)
    12. Twelve noon, Tongue out panting (XII)
