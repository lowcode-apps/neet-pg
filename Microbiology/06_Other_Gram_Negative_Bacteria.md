# Other Important Gram-Negative Bacteria

## Overview
This chapter covers clinically important Gram-negative bacteria beyond Enterobacteriaceae, including Pseudomonas, Neisseria, Haemophilus, Bordetella, Legionella, Helicobacter, and Campylobacter.

---

## 1. PSEUDOMONAS AERUGINOSA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Characteristics
- **Gram-negative rod** (bacillus)
- **Obligate aerobe** (requires oxygen)
- **Oxidase positive** (key differentiator from Enterobacteriaceae)
- **Motile** (polar flagella)
- **Non-lactose fermenter**
- Produces **pigments**: Pyocyanin (blue-green), Pyoverdin (yellow-green)
- **Fruity/grape-like odor**
- Grows at **42°C** (distinctive feature)

### Virulence Factors
```
Pseudomonas Virulence Arsenal:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ADHERENCE                           │
│ • Pili & flagella                   │
│ • Biofilm formation                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TOXINS                              │
│ • Exotoxin A (EF-2 inhibitor)       │
│ • Phospholipase C                   │
│ • Elastase (tissue destruction)     │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│ PIGMENTS                            │
│ • Pyocyanin (oxidative damage)      │
│ • Pyoverdin (iron acquisition)      │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Clinical Manifestations

#### 1. **Hospital-Acquired Infections** (Most Common)
- **Ventilator-associated pneumonia** (VAP)
- **Catheter-related UTI**
- **Burn wound infections** (green pus)
- **ICU-related bacteremia**

#### 2. **Community-Acquired Infections**
- **Hot tub folliculitis** (dermatitis)
- **Swimmer's ear** (external otitis, malignant in diabetics)
- **Contact lens-related keratitis**
- **Puncture wound through shoe** → Osteomyelitis

#### 3. **Special Populations**
- **Cystic fibrosis**: Chronic lung colonization (mucoid strains with alginate)
- **Neutropenic patients**: Ecthyma gangrenosum (necrotic skin lesions)
- **Burn patients**: Major cause of morbidity/mortality

### Diagnosis
- **Culture**: MacConkey agar (colorless - NLF), characteristic blue-green pigment
- **Oxidase test**: Positive ⭐
- **Growth at 42°C**: Positive
- **Grape-like odor**: Distinctive

### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
Antipseudomonal Antibiotics (4 P's):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ P - Penicillins (Piperacillin)      │
│ P - (3rd/4th gen) Cephalosporins    │
│     (Ceftazidime, Cefepime)         │
│ P - (Carba)Penems (Meropenem)       │
│ P - (Fluoro)quinolones              │
│     (Ciprofloxacin)                 │
│                                     │
│ + Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin,      │
│   Tobramycin, Amikacin)             │
│                                     │
│ + Polymyxins (Colistin) - last      │
│   resort for MDR                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Note**: Always use **combination therapy** for serious infections to prevent resistance

## 2. NEISSERIA SPECIES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### General Characteristics
- **Gram-negative diplococci** (coffee bean/kidney bean shaped)
- **Oxidase positive**
- **Catalase positive**
- Require **chocolate agar** (enriched medium)
- Two pathogenic species: **N. meningitidis** and **N. gonorrhoeae**

### 2A. Neisseria meningitidis (Meningococcus)

#### Characteristics
- **Capsule**: Polysaccharide (13 serogroups: A, B, C, W-135, Y most common)
- **Transmission**: Respiratory droplets
- **Carrier state**: 5-10% healthy individuals (nasopharynx)

#### Clinical Features
```
Meningococcal Disease Timeline:
0-12 hrs → Bacteremia (fever, malaise)
12-24 hrs → Meningitis (headache, neck stiffness)
            + Petechial rash (non-blanching)
24-48 hrs → Fulminant septicemia
            (Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome)

Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Overwhelming sepsis               │
│ • DIC (disseminated intravascular   │
│   coagulation)                      │
│ • Purpura fulminans (extensive      │
│   hemorrhagic necrosis)             │
│ • Bilateral adrenal hemorrhage      │
│ • Shock → Death (hours)             │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Diagnosis
- **CSF**: ↑ Protein, ↓ Glucose, ↑ PMNs
- **Gram stain**: Gram-negative diplococci (intracellular in WBCs)
- **Culture**: Chocolate agar, Thayer-Martin media
- **PCR**: Rapid diagnosis

#### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **First-line**: **Ceftriaxone** or **Cefotaxime**
- **Prophylaxis** (close contacts):
  - **Rifampicin** (2 days) OR
  - **Ciprofloxacin** (single dose) OR
  - **Ceftriaxone** (single IM dose)

#### Prevention
- **Vaccines**:
  - **Quadrivalent** (A, C, W-135, Y): MenACWY
  - **Serogroup B**: MenB (separate vaccine)
  - **Recommended**: Age 11-12 years + booster at 16 years

### 2B. Neisseria gonorrhoeae (Gonococcus) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### Characteristics
- **No capsule** (unlike meningitidis)
- **No vaccine** (antigenic variation)
- **Strictly human pathogen**
- **Sexually transmitted**
- **Intracellular** (in PMNs)

#### Clinical Manifestations

**Males:**
- **Urethritis**: Purulent discharge, dysuria (2-5 days post-exposure)
- **Epididymitis**, **Prostatitis**

**Females:** (Often asymptomatic - 50%)
- **Cervicitis**: Mucopurulent discharge
- **PID** (Pelvic Inflammatory Disease) → Infertility
- **Salpingitis** → Ectopic pregnancy

**Both Sexes:**
- **Disseminated Gonococcal Infection (DGI)**:
  - Triad: **Tenosynovitis + Dermatitis + Polyarthritis**
  - Pustular skin lesions
- **Pharyngitis** (oral sex)
- **Proctitis** (anal sex)

**Neonates:**
- **Ophthalmia neonatorum** (purulent conjunctivitis)
  - Preventable with **erythromycin** eye ointment at birth

#### Diagnosis
- **Gram stain**: Gram-negative diplococci **inside PMNs** ⭐
- **Culture**: Thayer-Martin agar (VCN - Vancomycin, Colistin, Nystatin)
- **NAAT** (Nucleic Acid Amplification Test): Most sensitive, first-line

#### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (2023 CDC Guidelines)
```
Uncomplicated Gonorrhea:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ SINGLE DOSE:                        │
│ Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM               │
│ (if ≥150 kg: 1 g IM)                │
│                                     │
│ ALTERNATIVE (if severe allergy):    │
│ Gentamicin 240 mg IM                │
│ + Azithromycin 2 g PO               │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘

ALWAYS test and treat for Chlamydia:
+ Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID × 7 days
```

**Increasing resistance**: Previously resistant to penicillin, tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones; now ceftriaxone is last reliable option

## 3. HAEMOPHILUS INFLUENZAE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Characteristics
- **Gram-negative coccobacillus** (pleomorphic)
- **Facultative anaerobe**
- **Fastidious**: Requires **X factor** (hemin) and **V factor** (NAD+)
- **Culture**: Chocolate agar (heated blood releases factors)
- **Satellite phenomenon**: Grows near S. aureus colonies on blood agar

### Classification
- **Typeable**: 6 serotypes (a-f) based on **capsule**
  - **Type b (Hib)**: Most virulent (polyribosylribitol phosphate capsule)
- **Non-typeable**: No capsule (most common now due to vaccination)

### Clinical Manifestations

#### Before Hib Vaccine (Pre-1990s):
```
Classic Hib Infections (Children <5 years):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. MENINGITIS (most common)         │
│    • Peak age: 6 months - 2 years   │
│                                     │
│ 2. EPIGLOTTITIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐             │
│    • "Cherry-red epiglottis"        │
│    • Thumb sign on X-ray            │
│    • Tripod position (drooling)     │
│    • EMERGENCY - secure airway!     │
│                                     │
│ 3. SEPTIC ARTHRITIS                 │
│                                     │
│ 4. CELLULITIS (facial/periorbital)  │
│                                     │
│ 5. PNEUMONIA                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Post-Hib Vaccine (Current):
- **Non-typeable H. influenzae** (NTHI):
  - **Otitis media** (children)
  - **Sinusitis**
  - **COPD exacerbations** (adults)
  - **Pneumonia** (elderly)

### Diagnosis
- **Culture**: Chocolate agar (X + V factors required)
- **CSF**: Gram-negative coccobacilli
- **Quellung reaction**: Capsular swelling (type b)

### Treatment
- **Meningitis/Epiglottitis**: **Ceftriaxone** or **Cefotaxime**
- **COPD exacerbations**: Amoxicillin-clavulanate, fluoroquinolones
- **Prophylaxis** (close contacts of invasive Hib): **Rifampicin**

### Prevention ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hib vaccine** (conjugate vaccine):
  - **Schedule**: 2, 4, 6, 12-15 months
  - **Result**: >99% reduction in invasive Hib disease

## 4. BORDETELLA PERTUSSIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Characteristics
- **Gram-negative coccobacillus**
- **Strictly aerobic**
- **Fastidious**: Requires Bordet-Gengou agar or Regan-Lowe medium
- **Transmission**: Respiratory droplets (highly contagious)
- **Human pathogen only**

### Virulence Factors
```
Pertussis Toxin Mechanisms:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PERTUSSIS TOXIN (PT):               │
│ • ADP-ribosylates Gi protein        │
│ • ↑ cAMP → Lymphocytosis            │
│ • Histamine sensitization           │
│                                     │
│ FILAMENTOUS HEMAGGLUTININ (FHA):    │
│ • Adherence to ciliated epithelium  │
│                                     │
│ ADENYLATE CYCLASE TOXIN:            │
│ • Impairs phagocyte function        │
│                                     │
│ TRACHEAL CYTOTOXIN:                 │
│ • Ciliary paralysis                 │
│ • Epithelial damage                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Clinical Features: Whooping Cough
```
Three Stages (6-10 weeks total):

CATARRHAL STAGE (1-2 weeks):
• Mild URI symptoms
• Most contagious period ⭐
• Often misdiagnosed as common cold

↓

PAROXYSMAL STAGE (2-6 weeks):
• Intense coughing paroxysms
• Inspiratory "whoop" (characteristic)
• Post-tussive vomiting
• Cyanosis during attacks
• Subconjunctival hemorrhages
• Apnea (infants) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

↓

CONVALESCENT STAGE (weeks-months):
• Gradual resolution
• "100-day cough"
```

**Complications:**
- **Infants**: Apnea, seizures, encephalopathy, death
- **Adults**: Rib fractures, pneumonia, weight loss

### Diagnosis
- **PCR**: Most sensitive (early disease)
- **Culture**: Bordet-Gengou or Regan-Lowe (slow, 3-7 days)
- **DFA** (Direct Fluorescent Antibody): Rapid but less sensitive
- **CBC**: **Lymphocytosis** (>10,000) with normal ESR ⭐

### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
Antibiotics (Macrolides):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ FIRST-LINE:                         │
│ • Azithromycin (5 days) - preferred │
│ • Clarithromycin (7 days)           │
│ • Erythromycin (14 days)            │
│                                     │
│ ALTERNATIVE:                        │
│ • TMP-SMX (if macrolide allergy)    │
│                                     │
│ TIMING:                             │
│ • Effective in catarrhal stage      │
│ • Reduces transmission              │
│ • Minimal effect on paroxysmal      │
│   symptoms if started late          │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Prevention ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **DTaP vaccine** (children): 2, 4, 6, 15-18 months, 4-6 years
- **Tdap booster**:
  - Adolescents (11-12 years)
  - Adults (every 10 years)
  - **Pregnant women** (each pregnancy, 27-36 weeks) ⭐
  - Healthcare workers, caregivers of infants
- **Prophylaxis**: Azithromycin for close contacts

## 5. LEGIONELLA PNEUMOPHILA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Characteristics
- **Gram-negative rod** (weakly staining, requires **silver stain**)
- **Intracellular parasite** (alveolar macrophages)
- **Fastidious**: Requires **BCYE agar** (Buffered Charcoal Yeast Extract)
- **Aerobic**
- **Habitat**: Aquatic environments (water towers, air conditioners, hot tubs)

### Epidemiology
- **Transmission**: **Inhalation of contaminated aerosols** (NOT person-to-person)
- **Risk factors**:
  - Smoking
  - COPD
  - Immunosuppression
  - Age >50 years
  - Male sex

### Clinical Manifestations

#### 1. **Legionnaires' Disease** (Severe Pneumonia)
```
Clinical Features:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PULMONARY:                          │
│ • High fever (>40°C)                │
│ • Nonproductive cough → productive  │
│ • Dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain     │
│                                     │
│ GI SYMPTOMS (distinctive): ⭐        │
│ • Diarrhea (25-50%)                 │
│ • Abdominal pain                    │
│ • Nausea/vomiting                   │
│                                     │
│ NEUROLOGICAL:                       │
│ • Confusion, altered mental status  │
│ • Headache                          │
│                                     │
│ LAB ABNORMALITIES: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐         │
│ • Hyponatremia (SIADH)              │
│ • Elevated transaminases            │
│ • Microscopic hematuria             │
│ • Relative bradycardia              │
│   (fever-pulse dissociation)        │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### 2. **Pontiac Fever** (Mild, Self-Limited)
- Flu-like illness without pneumonia
- Resolves in 2-5 days without treatment

### Diagnosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
Diagnostic Tests:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ URINARY ANTIGEN (Best): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐   │
│ • Rapid (hours)                     │
│ • Sensitive & specific              │
│ • Detects only serogroup 1          │
│   (70% of cases)                    │
│ • Remains positive for weeks        │
│                                     │
│ CULTURE (Gold standard):            │
│ • BCYE agar                         │
│ • Slow (3-7 days)                   │
│ • Detects all species/serogroups    │
│                                     │
│ PCR: Rapid, sensitive               │
│                                     │
│ SEROLOGY: Retrospective (4x rise)   │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Gram stain**: Organisms NOT seen (require silver stain)

### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
Antibiotics:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ FIRST-LINE (need intracellular      │
│ penetration):                       │
│                                     │
│ • Azithromycin (preferred)          │
│   500 mg IV/PO daily                │
│                                     │
│ • Levofloxacin                      │
│   750 mg IV/PO daily                │
│                                     │
│ ALTERNATIVE:                        │
│ • Doxycycline                       │
│                                     │
│ DURATION: 7-10 days (mild)          │
│           14-21 days (severe/       │
│           immunocompromised)        │
│                                     │
│ β-LACTAMS INEFFECTIVE ⭐             │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 6. HELICOBACTER PYLORI ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Characteristics
- **Gram-negative spiral/curved rod**
- **Microaerophilic** (requires low oxygen)
- **Highly motile** (multiple flagella)
- **Urease producer** (KEY virulence factor)
- Colonizes **gastric mucosa** only

### Virulence Factors
```
Survival in Acidic Stomach:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ UREASE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:                     │
│ Urea → Ammonia + CO2                │
│ Ammonia neutralizes gastric acid    │
│ Creates alkaline microenvironment   │
│                                     │
│ FLAGELLA:                           │
│ • Motility through mucus layer      │
│                                     │
│ ADHESINS:                           │
│ • Bind to gastric epithelium        │
│                                     │
│ CYTOTOXINS:                         │
│ • CagA (injected via Type IV        │
│   secretion system)                 │
│ • VacA (vacuolating cytotoxin)      │
│ • Inflammation → damage             │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Clinical Manifestations
```
H. pylori-Associated Diseases:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. CHRONIC GASTRITIS (100%)         │
│    • Most asymptomatic              │
│                                     │
│ 2. PEPTIC ULCER DISEASE (10-15%)    │
│    • Duodenal ulcer (90%)           │
│    • Gastric ulcer (70%)            │
│                                     │
│ 3. GASTRIC ADENOCARCINOMA (1-3%)    │
│    • WHO Class I carcinogen         │
│    • Intestinal-type cancer         │
│                                     │
│ 4. MALT LYMPHOMA                    │
│    • Mucosa-Associated Lymphoid     │
│      Tissue lymphoma                │
│    • May regress with eradication   │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Diagnosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
INVASIVE (require endoscopy):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Rapid Urease Test (CLO test)      │
│   Pink color change if positive     │
│                                     │
│ • Histology (gold standard)         │
│   Giemsa or silver stain            │
│                                     │
│ • Culture (rarely done)             │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘

NON-INVASIVE (preferred initial):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Urea Breath Test ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐         │
│   C13 or C14-labeled urea           │
│   Most accurate                     │
│                                     │
│ • Stool Antigen Test                │
│   Good for screening                │
│                                     │
│ • Serology (IgG antibodies)         │
│   Cannot distinguish active from    │
│   past infection                    │
│   Not for post-treatment            │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
Triple Therapy (10-14 days):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PPI (Proton Pump Inhibitor)         │
│ • Omeprazole 20 mg BID              │
│                                     │
│ + Clarithromycin 500 mg BID         │
│                                     │
│ + Amoxicillin 1 g BID               │
│   (or Metronidazole 500 mg BID      │
│    if penicillin allergy)           │
│                                     │
│ Success rate: 70-85%                │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘

Quadruple Therapy (if resistance):
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PPI BID                             │
│ + Bismuth subsalicylate             │
│ + Tetracycline                      │
│ + Metronidazole                     │
│                                     │
│ Duration: 14 days                   │
│ Success rate: 90%+                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Post-treatment testing**: Wait 4-6 weeks after completion (urea breath test or stool antigen)

**Mnemonic - PAC for Triple Therapy**: **P**PI + **A**moxicillin + **C**larithromycin

## 7. CAMPYLOBACTER JEJUNI ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Characteristics
- **Gram-negative curved/spiral rod** ("seagull" or "S-shaped")
- **Microaerophilic** (5% O2, 10% CO2)
- **Motile** (single polar flagellum - "darting motility")
- **Oxidase positive**
- **Thermophilic**: Grows best at **42°C**

### Epidemiology
- **Leading cause of bacterial diarrhea worldwide**
- **Transmission**:
  - Contaminated food (especially undercooked **poultry**)
  - Unpasteurized milk
  - Contaminated water
  - Direct contact with animals (pets, farm animals)
- **Incubation**: 2-5 days

### Clinical Manifestations

#### Acute Gastroenteritis
```
Typical Course:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PRODROME:                           │
│ • Fever, headache, malaise          │
│                                     │
│ DIARRHEAL PHASE (2-7 days):         │
│ • Watery → Bloody diarrhea          │
│ • Severe abdominal cramps           │
│ • Fecal leukocytes present          │
│ • Mimics inflammatory bowel disease │
│                                     │
│ SELF-LIMITED:                       │
│ • Usually resolves in 7-10 days     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Complications ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
POST-INFECTIOUS SEQUELAE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. GUILLAIN-BARRÉ SYNDROME (GBS)    │
│    • Most common trigger (30% of    │
│      GBS cases)                     │
│    • Molecular mimicry              │
│      (GM1 ganglioside)              │
│    • Onset: 1-3 weeks post-         │
│      infection                      │
│    • Ascending paralysis            │
│                                     │
│ 2. REACTIVE ARTHRITIS (ReA)         │
│    • HLA-B27 association            │
│    • Triad: Arthritis + Urethritis  │
│      + Conjunctivitis               │
│    • Weeks after GI infection       │
│                                     │
│ 3. BACTEREMIA (rare)                │
│    • Immunocompromised patients     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Diagnosis
- **Culture**: Campy agar (selective media) at **42°C** in microaerophilic environment
- **Stool microscopy**: "Seagull" shaped organisms, fecal leukocytes
- **PCR/GI panel**: Increasingly used for rapid diagnosis

### Treatment
- **Most cases**: Supportive only (self-limited)
  - Oral rehydration
  - Electrolyte replacement
- **Antibiotics** (if severe, immunocompromised, or persistent):
  - **Azithromycin** (preferred) - 500 mg daily × 3 days
  - **Ciprofloxacin** - increasing resistance
- **Avoid antimotility agents** (may prolong infection)

### Prevention
- Proper cooking of poultry (>165°F / 74°C)
- Pasteurization of milk
- Hand hygiene after animal contact
- Safe water supply

## HIGH-YIELD MNEMONICS

### 1. **Antipseudomonal Antibiotics (4 P's)**
- **P**iperacillin
- 3rd/4th gen Ce**P**halosporins (Ceftazidime, Cefepime)
- Carba**P**enems (Meropenem)
- Fluoro**P**quinolones (Ciprofloxacin)

### 2. **Neisseria on Thayer-Martin (VCN)**
- **V**ancomycin (inhibits Gram-positives)
- **C**olistin (inhibits Gram-negatives except Neisseria)
- **N**ystatin (inhibits fungi)

### 3. **Haemophilus Requires X and V**
- **X** = Hematin (from he**X**ane)
- **V** = NAD+ (**V** for **V**itamin)

### 4. **Legionella Lab Findings (SHAMPOO)**
- **S**IAH (hyponatremia)
- **H**ematuria (microscopic)
- **A**mino transaminases elevated
- **M**acrophages (intracellular)
- **P**oor Gram stain (silver needed)
- **O**ut of water (aerosols)
- **O**lder males, smokers

### 5. **H. pylori Triple Therapy (PAC)**
- **P**PI
- **A**moxicillin
- **C**larithromycin

### 6. **Campylobacter Complications (GAR)**
- **G**uillain-Barré syndrome
- **A**rthritis (reactive)
- **R**eiter syndrome

## NEET PG MCQS

### Q1. A 25-year-old burn patient develops green pus from wound. Culture shows oxidase-positive Gram-negative rods producing blue-green pigment. Most appropriate treatment?
A) Ampicillin
B) Ceftriaxone alone
C) Piperacillin-tazobactam + Gentamicin
D) Vancomycin

**Answer: C**

## CLINICAL PEARLS ⭐

1. **Pseudomonas**: Think in hospital settings, burns, CF, immunocompromised
2. **Green pus = Pseudomonas** (pyocyanin pigment)
3. **Coffee bean diplococci in PMNs = Gonorrhea**
4. **Meningitis + petechial rash = Meningococcus** (emergency!)
5. **Thumb sign on lateral neck X-ray = Epiglottitis** (H. influenzae b) - SECURE AIRWAY!
6. **Never examine the throat** in suspected epiglottitis (can precipitate complete obstruction)
7. **Whooping cough + lymphocytosis** = Pertussis
8. **Pertussis vaccine in EVERY pregnancy** (27-36 weeks) to protect newborn
9. **Pneumonia + diarrhea + hyponatremia** = Legionella
10. **β-lactams don't work for Legionella** (intracellular) - use macrolides or fluoroquinolones
11. **Peptic ulcer = test for H. pylori** (90% duodenal, 70% gastric)
12. **Post-eradication testing**: Urea breath test or stool antigen (NOT serology)
13. **Diarrhea → GBS 1-3 weeks later** = Campylobacter
14. **Gonorrhea resistance increasing** - ceftriaxone last reliable option
15. **Hot tub folliculitis** = Pseudomonas
16. **Puncture wound through shoe** → Osteomyelitis = Pseudomonas
