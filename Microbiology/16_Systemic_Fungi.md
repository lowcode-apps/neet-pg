# Systemic Mycoses

## Overview
Deep fungal infections that can disseminate and cause life-threatening disease, especially in immunocompromised patients.

---

## ENDEMIC MYCOSES

### 1. HISTOPLASMA CAPSULATUM ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Geography**: Mississippi/Ohio River valleys (US), Central/South America ⭐
**Habitat**: Soil enriched with bird/bat droppings

**Morphology**:
- **Dimorphic** ⭐: Mold (25°C) → Yeast (37°C)
- **Intracellular yeast** in macrophages ⭐

**Transmission**: Inhalation of spores (cleaning chicken coops, exploring caves)

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
ACUTE PULMONARY (most common):
• Asymptomatic (90% in immunocompetent)
• Flu-like illness: Fever, cough, chest pain
• CXR: Hilar lymphadenopathy, infiltrates
• Self-limited (2-3 weeks)

CHRONIC PULMONARY: ⭐⭐⭐
• Mimics tuberculosis (cavitary lesions in upper lobes)
• COPD patients, smokers
• Chronic cough, weight loss, hemoptysis
• CXR: Cavities, fibrosis

DISSEMINATED: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Immunocompromised (AIDS CD4 <150, transplant, TNF-α inhibitors)
• Fever, weight loss, hepatosplenomegaly
• Pancytopenia (bone marrow involvement)
• Adrenal insufficiency (Addison's)
• Mucosal ulcers (oral, GI)
• Fatal if untreated
```

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Urine/serum Histoplasma antigen** ⭐⭐⭐ (most sensitive for disseminated, rapid)
- **Culture**: Sabouraud agar (slow, 2-6 weeks), "tuberculate macroconidia"
- **Histopathology**: ⭐
  - **Intracellular yeasts in macrophages** (GMS/PAS stain)
  - **Narrow-based budding yeast** (2-4 μm)
- Serology: Antibodies (complement fixation, immunodiffusion)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mild-moderate pulmonary**: Observation (self-limited) OR **Itraconazole** if symptoms >4 weeks
- **Severe pulmonary/disseminated**: ⭐
  - **Liposomal amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV × 1-2 weeks** →
  - **Itraconazole 200 mg TID × 3 days, then BID × 12 months**
- **AIDS prophylaxis** (CD4 <150 in endemic area): Itraconazole 200 mg daily

### 2. COCCIDIOIDES (C. immitis, C. posadasii) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Geography**: Southwestern US (Arizona, California), Mexico, Central/South America ⭐
- "Valley fever" (San Joaquin Valley)

**Habitat**: Arid soil

**Morphology**:
- **Dimorphic**: Mold (barrel-shaped arthroconidia) → **Spherules** ⭐⭐⭐ (37°C)
- Spherules (20-80 μm) with **endospores** ⭐

**Transmission**: Inhalation (dust storms, earthquakes, construction, archaeology)

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
ACUTE PULMONARY (60% asymptomatic):
• Flu-like illness: Fever, cough, chest pain
• Erythema nodosum ⭐⭐⭐ (painful red nodules on shins - hypersensitivity)
• Erythema multiforme
• Arthralg

ias ("desert rheumatism")
• CXR: Infiltrates, hilar adenopathy
• Self-limited (weeks)

CHRONIC PULMONARY:
• Cavitary disease (thin-walled cavities, upper lobes)
• Mimics TB
• Hemoptysis, chronic cough

DISSEMINATED: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
• <1% overall, higher risk in:
  - African Americans, Filipinos ⭐ (genetic susceptibility)
  - Pregnancy (3rd trimester)
  - Immunosuppressed (HIV, TNF-α inhibitors)
• Sites:
  - Skin: Verrucous lesions, abscesses
  - Bones/joints: Osteomyelitis (vertebrae, long bones)
  - **Meningitis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (most serious)
    - Chronic, basilar meningitis
    - Headache, altered mental status
    - CSF: Lymphocytic pleocytosis, ↓ glucose, ↑ protein, eosinophils
    - Often fatal without treatment
```

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Serology** (most common): ⭐
  - IgM (early, 1-3 weeks)
  - IgG/complement fixation (chronic, disseminated)
  - Rising titers = active disease
- **Culture**: Spherules → arthroconidia (BIOHAZARD - highly infectious)
- **Histopathology**: **Large spherules with endospores** ⭐⭐⭐ (GMS/PAS)
- CSF: PCR, serology, culture (meningitis)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mild pulmonary**: Observation (self-limited)
- **Severe pulmonary/disseminated (non-meningeal)**:
  - **Fluconazole 400-800 mg daily** OR **Itraconazole 200 mg BID** × 3-12 months
  - Amphotericin B if severe/refractory
- **Meningitis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Fluconazole 400-1200 mg daily** × **LIFELONG** ⭐
  - Alternative: Itraconazole
  - **Never stop therapy** (high relapse rate)

### 3. BLASTOMYCES DERMATITIDIS ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Geography**: Southeastern/Midwestern US (Great Lakes, Mississippi/Ohio River), Canada

**Habitat**: Moist soil, decaying wood (near waterways)

**Morphology**:
- **Dimorphic**: Mold → Yeast
- **Broad-based budding yeast** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (8-15 μm, thick wall)

**Transmission**: Inhalation

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
PULMONARY:
• Acute: Flu-like illness
• Chronic: Mimics TB or cancer
• Cough, fever, weight loss, night sweats
• CXR: Mass-like infiltrates, cavitation

DISSEMINATED:
• Skin ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (most common extrapulmonary):
  - **Verrucous (warty), crusted plaques** ⭐
  - Ulcerated, heaped-up borders
  - Face, extremities
• Bone: Osteomyelitis (vertebrae, ribs, long bones)
• GU: Prostatitis, epididymitis (unique among fungi)
• CNS: Meningitis, brain abscesses (rare)
```

**Diagnosis**:
- **Histopathology**: **Broad-based budding yeast** ⭐⭐⭐ (pathognomonic)
- **Culture**: Mold with lollipop conidia
- Serology: Limited utility
- Urine antigen (cross-reacts with Histoplasma)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mild-moderate**: **Itraconazole 200 mg daily/BID × 6-12 months**
- **Severe/CNS**: **Liposomal amphotericin B** → Step down to itraconazole

## OPPORTUNISTIC MYCOSES

### 4. ASPERGILLUS (A. fumigatus most common) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Habitat**: Ubiquitous (soil, decaying vegetation, hospital construction)

**Morphology**:
- **Septate hyphae** with **acute-angle (45°) branching** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Conidiophores**: "Aspergillum" (looks like aspergillum/holy water sprinkler)

**Transmission**: Inhalation of spores

**Clinical Syndromes**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

```
1. ALLERGIC BRONCHOPULMONARY ASPERGILLOSIS (ABPA): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Hypersensitivity to Aspergillus antigens
• Asthma, cystic fibrosis patients
• Wheezing, cough, brown mucus plugs
• Eosinophilia, elevated IgE ⭐
• CXR: Fleeting infiltrates, central bronchiectasis
• Diagnosis: ↑ IgE, Aspergillus IgE/IgG, eosinophilia, imaging
• Treatment: Corticosteroids + itraconazole

2. ASPERGILLOMA ("Fungus Ball"): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Colonization of pre-existing cavity (TB, sarcoid, bullae)
• Usually asymptomatic
• Hemoptysis (can be massive) ⭐
• CXR/CT: Mobile mass within cavity, "air crescent sign" ⭐⭐⭐
• Treatment: Observation (if asymptomatic) OR surgical resection (if massive hemoptysis)

3. INVASIVE ASPERGILLOSIS: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Immunocompromised (neutropenia, transplant, high-dose steroids)
• ANGIOINVASION ⭐ → thrombosis, hemorrhage, infarction
• Pulmonary (most common):
  - Fever, cough, dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain
  - Hemoptysis
  - **"Halo sign"** ⭐ (early - hemorrhage around nodule)
  - **"Air crescent sign"** ⭐ (late - retraction, cavity)
  - **Cavitary lesions**
• Disseminated: CNS (brain abscesses), sinuses, skin
• High mortality (30-90%)
```

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Histopathology**: **Septate hyphae, 45° branching** ⭐⭐⭐ (GMS/PAS)
- **Culture**: Aspergillus species (NOT specific for invasive vs colonization)
- **Galactomannan antigen** ⭐ (serum, BAL): Invasive aspergillosis
- **Beta-D-glucan** (serum): Non-specific (most fungi except Cryptococcus, Mucor)
- CT chest: Halo sign, air crescent, nodules, cavities

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Invasive aspergillosis**:
  - **Voriconazole 6 mg/kg IV Q12h × 2 doses → 4 mg/kg Q12h** ⭐⭐⭐ (first-line)
  - Alternatives: Isavuconazole, liposomal amphotericin B, posaconazole
  - Reverse immunosuppression if possible
- **ABPA**: Corticosteroids + itraconazole
- **Aspergilloma**: Surgery (if symptomatic, massive hemoptysis)

### 5. CRYPTOCOCCUS NEOFORMANS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Habitat**: Soil, bird droppings (especially pigeons) ⭐

**Morphology**:
- **Encapsulated yeast** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (thick polysaccharide capsule)
- **Narrow-based budding** (4-7 μm)

**Transmission**: Inhalation

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

```
MENINGITIS (most common): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Immunocompromised (AIDS CD4 <100, transplant, steroids)
• Subacute/chronic (weeks):
  - Headache (most common) ⭐
  - Fever
  - Altered mental status, confusion
  - Meningismus (may be absent)
  - **Increased ICP** ⭐⭐⭐ (CSF production, impaired absorption)
    - Papilledema, vision changes
    - CN palsies (VI)
    - Requires serial LPs or shunt
• CSF: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Opening pressure often HIGH (>25 cm H2O) ⭐
  - Lymphocytic pleocytosis (may be minimal in AIDS)
  - ↓ Glucose, ↑ protein
  - **India ink** ⭐⭐⭐: Encapsulated yeast (60-80% sensitive in AIDS)
  - **Cryptococcal antigen (CrAg)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: >95% sensitive (CSF/serum)
  - Culture: Gold standard
• Fatal if untreated

PULMONARY:
• Asymptomatic to severe pneumonia
• Immunocompetent: Often self-limited
• Immunocompromised: Disseminates

DISSEMINATED:
• Skin: Umbilicated papules (molluscum-like)
• Bone, prostate
```

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cryptococcal antigen (CrAg)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **CSF** (preferred) or **serum**
  - Lateral flow assay (rapid, point-of-care)
  - >95% sensitive and specific
  - **Screening**: HIV patients with CD4 <100
- **India ink**: ⭐⭐⭐
  - CSF: **Encapsulated yeast** (negative staining of capsule)
  - Less sensitive (60-80%)
- **Culture**: Sabouraud agar
- **Histopathology**: Mucicarmine stain ⭐ (capsule stains red)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
MENINGITIS (AIDS or transplant):

INDUCTION (2 weeks): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Liposomal amphotericin B 3-4 mg/kg IV daily
  + Flucytosine 25 mg/kg PO QID
• Monitor: Renal function, CBC (flucytosine → marrow suppression)

CONSOLIDATION (8 weeks):
• Fluconazole 400 mg PO daily

MAINTENANCE (≥1 year): ⭐
• Fluconazole 200 mg PO daily
• Can stop if:
  - On ART, CD4 >200 × 6 months, undetectable VL
  - Asymptomatic, negative CSF culture

MANAGE INCREASED ICP: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Serial LPs (remove 20-30 mL CSF) to keep pressure <20 cm H2O
• Lumbar drain or VP shunt if refractory
• Crucial for survival (mortality ↑ if not managed)
```

### 6. CANDIDA Species ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Species**: C. albicans (most common), C. glabrata, C. krusei, C. auris

**Morphology**:
- **Yeast** with **pseudohyphae** (elongated buds) ⭐
- True hyphae (C. albicans)
- **Germ tube test** ⭐: C. albicans (positive in 2-3 hours)

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

```
INVASIVE CANDIDIASIS:
• Risk factors:
  - Central venous catheters ⭐⭐⭐
  - Broad-spectrum antibiotics
  - ICU, TPN, surgery (GI surgery, anastomotic leak)
  - Immunosuppression, neutropenia

CANDIDEMIA: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Fever unresponsive to antibiotics
• Hypotension, sepsis
• Mortality: 30-40%
• Complications:
  - **Endophthalmitis** ⭐ (chorioretinal lesions - "fluffy white balls")
    → Requires ophthalmology exam in all candidemia patients
  - Endocarditis (IVDU, prosthetic valves)
  - Hepatosplenic candidiasis (chronic disseminated)
  - Osteomyelitis, septic arthritis

CHRONIC MUCOCUTANEOUS CANDIDIASIS:
• T-cell defects, AIRE gene mutations
• Persistent oral/nail/skin candidiasis
• Does NOT disseminate
```

**Diagnosis**:
- **Blood cultures** ⭐ (may take 2-3 days; insensitive 50%)
- **Beta-D-glucan** (serum): Adjunct
- Culture from sterile sites
- **Remove catheter** and culture tip

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
CANDIDEMIA (non-neutropenic):
• Echinocandins (first-line): ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Caspofungin 70 mg load → 50 mg daily
  - Micafungin 100 mg daily
  - Anidulafungin 200 mg load → 100 mg daily
• Fluconazole 800 mg load → 400 mg daily (if stable, known susceptible)
• **Remove/replace CVC** ⭐
• Duration: 14 days after clearance of bloodstream, resolution of symptoms
• **Ophthalmology exam** ⭐ (rule out endophthalmitis)

CANDIDEMIA (neutropenic):
• Echinocandin preferred
• Amphotericin B if resistant

FLUCONAZOLE-RESISTANT SPECIES:
• C. krusei: Intrinsically resistant ⭐
• C. glabrata: Dose-dependent susceptibility
• C. auris: Multi-drug resistant, emerging threat
```

## MUCORMYCOSIS (Zygomycosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organisms**: Rhizopus, Mucor (Order: Mucorales)

**Risk Factors**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (most important)
- Neutropenia, transplant, iron overload (deferoxamine)
- Burns, trauma

**Morphology**:
- **Broad, ribbon-like, NON-septate hyphae** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Wide-angle (90°) branching** ⭐⭐⭐
- (vs Aspergillus: septate, 45°)

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

```
RHINOCEREBRAL (most common): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• DKA, neutropenia
• ANGIOINVASION → thrombosis, necrosis
• Symptoms:
  - Facial/orbital pain, headache
  - **Black necrotic eschar** on palate/nasal turbinates ⭐⭐⭐
  - Proptosis, ophthalmoplegia (orbital invasion)
  - Facial numbness
  - Altered mental status (brain invasion)
• Rapidly progressive, fatal if untreated
• Invades through cribriform plate → brain

PULMONARY:
• Neutropenia, transplant
• Cavitary lesions, hemoptysis
• Angioinvasion → infarction

DISSEMINATED:
• Multiple organs
• High mortality
```

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Histopathology**: **Broad, non-septate, 90° branching hyphae** ⭐⭐⭐ (GMS)
- **Biopsy with culture** (tissue invasion, NOT just colonization)
- CT/MRI: Sinus/orbital involvement, bone destruction

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
AGGRESSIVE APPROACH:
1. **Reverse predisposing factors**: ⭐⭐⭐
   - Correct DKA, hyperglycemia
   - Reduce immunosuppression
   - Discontinue deferoxamine
   - Restore neutrophil count (G-CSF)

2. **Antifungal**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - **Liposomal amphotericin B 5-10 mg/kg IV daily** ⭐
   - Start immediately (don't wait for confirmation)
   - Alternative: Isavuconazole, posaconazole
   - **NOT echinocandins or fluconazole** (ineffective)

3. **Surgical debridement**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - URGENT, aggressive
   - Remove all necrotic tissue
   - May require multiple surgeries
   - Essential for survival

Mortality: 50-80% even with treatment
```

## PNEUMOCYSTIS JIROVECII ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
*(Formerly Pneumocystis carinii; technically a fungus)*

**Risk**: AIDS (CD4 <200), transplant, chronic steroids

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Subacute (weeks): Progressive dyspnea, dry cough, fever
  - Hypoxemia (↓ PaO2), ↑ LDH ⭐
  - CXR: **Bilateral diffuse interstitial infiltrates** ⭐ ("ground-glass" on CT)
  - May have normal CXR early
  - **Pneumothorax** complication (spontaneous)

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Induced sputum or BAL**: ⭐
  - **Methenamine silver or Giemsa stain** ⭐
  - **Disc-shaped or cup-shaped cysts** (4-6 μm)
  - Immunofluorescence (DFA) - more sensitive
- **PCR**: High sensitivity
- **Beta-D-glucan**: Elevated (adjunct)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
```
MILD-MODERATE (PaO2 >70 mmHg):
• TMP-SMX 15-20 mg/kg/day (TMP component) PO TID × 21 days ⭐⭐⭐

SEVERE (PaO2 <70 or A-a gradient >35):
• TMP-SMX 15-20 mg/kg/day IV QID × 21 days ⭐
  + Prednisone 40 mg BID × 5d → 40 mg daily × 5d → 20 mg daily × 11d ⭐
  (Steroids reduce inflammation, improve outcomes if started within 72h)

ALTERNATIVES (if sulfa allergy):
• Pentamidine IV, Atovaquone, Clindamycin-primaquine

PROPHYLAXIS: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
• Indication: CD4 <200, prior PCP, oral candidiasis
• TMP-SMX DS 1 tab daily ⭐ (or 3×/week)
• Stop when CD4 >200 × 3 months on ART
```

## HIGH-YIELD COMPARISON

```
Fungus          | Morphology             | Geography        | Key Clinical
----------------|------------------------|------------------|------------------
Histoplasma     | Intracellular yeast    | Ohio/Miss Valley | Disseminated (AIDS)
Coccidioides    | Spherules+endospores   | SW US, deserts   | Meningitis (lifelong Rx)
Blastomyces     | Broad-based budding    | Great Lakes      | Verrucous skin lesions
Aspergillus     | Septate, 45° branching | Ubiquitous       | Angioinvasion, halo sign
Cryptococcus    | Encapsulated yeast     | Pigeon droppings | Meningitis, ↑ ICP
Mucor           | Non-septate, 90°       | Ubiquitous       | DKA, black eschar
Pneumocystis    | Cup-shaped cysts       | Ubiquitous       | PCP (AIDS CD4<200)
```

## NEET PG MCQS

**Q1.** Broad, non-septate hyphae with 90° branching in diabetic with facial necrosis. Diagnosis?
A) Aspergillosis B) Mucormycosis C) Candidiasis D) Cryptococcosis

**Answer: B** - Mucormycosis: Non-septate, 90° branching; DKA risk; black eschar

**Q2.** Encapsulated yeast in CSF India ink preparation. Treatment?
A) Fluconazole alone B) Amphotericin B + flucytosine → fluconazole C) TMP-SMX D) Itraconazole

**Answer: B** - Cryptococcal meningitis: Induction (AmB + flucytosine) → consolidation (fluconazole) → maintenance

**Q3.** AIDS patient with CD4 50, bilateral ground-glass infiltrates, elevated LDH. Treatment?
A) Fluconazole B) TMP-SMX + prednisone C) Itraconazole D) Voriconazole

**Answer: B** - PCP: TMP-SMX (21 days) + steroids if severe (PaO2 <70)
