# HEPATITIS VIRUSES - NEET PG Study Material

## OVERVIEW OF VIRAL HEPATITIS

### Hepatotropic Viruses

**Five major types**:
- **Hepatitis A (HAV)** - RNA, fecal-oral, acute
- **Hepatitis B (HBV)** - DNA, parenteral, acute/chronic
- **Hepatitis C (HCV)** - RNA, parenteral, chronic
- **Hepatitis D (HDV)** - RNA, defective (needs HBV), parenteral
- **Hepatitis E (HEV)** - RNA, fecal-oral, acute (severe in pregnancy)

**NEET PG**: Know transmission routes & chronicity potential

---

### Comparison Table ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Feature | HAV | HBV | HCV | HDV | HEV |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| **Genome** | RNA | **DNA** | RNA | RNA | RNA |
| **Family** | Picornavirus | Hepadnavirus | Flavivirus | Deltavirus | Hepeviridae |
| **Transmission** | **Fecal-oral** | **Parenteral**, vertical, sexual | **Parenteral** | Parenteral | **Fecal-oral** |
| **Chronicity** | **NO** | **Yes** (5-10%) | **Yes** (70-85%) | Yes | **NO** (except immunocompromised) |
| **Fulminant** | Rare | 1% | Rare | **10-20%** (co-infection) | **20-30%** (pregnancy) |
| **Vaccine** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **NO** | HBV vaccine protects | **NO** (in development) |
| **Treatment** | Supportive | Antivirals (tenofovir, entecavir) | DAAs (sofosbuvir, etc.) | HBV treatment | Supportive |

**NEET PG High-Yield**: HBV only DNA virus; HAV & HEV fecal-oral, no chronicity; HCV highest chronicity

## HEPATITIS A VIRUS (HAV)

### Characteristics

**Genome**: Single-stranded **RNA**, non-enveloped

**Family**: **Picornavirus** (like poliovirus, enteroviruses)

**Transmission** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Fecal-oral** route
- Contaminated food/water (shellfish, raw vegetables)
- **Person-to-person** (daycare centers)
- **NO vertical or sexual transmission**

**Incubation**: **2-6 weeks** (average 28 days)

**NEET PG**: HAV = Fecal-oral, RNA, no chronic infection

---

### Clinical Features

**Acute hepatitis**:
- **Asymptomatic** (children <6 years - 70%)
- **Symptomatic** (older children, adults)

**Phases**:

**1. Prodromal (Pre-icteric)**:
- Fever, malaise, anorexia
- Nausea, vomiting
- RUQ pain
- **Aversion to smoking** (smokers)

**2. Icteric phase**:
- **Jaundice** (dark urine → jaundice → pale stools)
- Hepatomegaly (tender)
- **Improvement in symptoms** (paradoxical - feel better when jaundiced)

**3. Convalescent**:
- Resolution (4-6 weeks)

**NEET PG**: HAV = Self-limited, children often asymptomatic

---

### Diagnosis

**Serology** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:

**Anti-HAV IgM** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Acute infection** marker
- Appears early (during symptoms)
- Persists 3-6 months

**Anti-HAV IgG**:
- **Past infection or vaccination**
- Lifelong immunity
- Appears after IgM

**HAV RNA**: Research

**LFTs**:
- ↑↑ ALT, AST (10-100× normal)
- ↑ Bilirubin (conjugated)
- ↑ Alkaline phosphatase

**NEET PG High-Yield**: Anti-HAV IgM = Acute infection, IgG = Past infection/immunity

---

### Treatment & Prevention

**Treatment**:
- **Supportive** (no specific antiviral)
- Rest, hydration, nutrition
- **NO alcohol**
- **Self-limited** (full recovery - no chronicity)

**Prevention** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:

**1. Vaccine** (Inactivated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **2 doses** (0, 6-12 months)
- **>12 months of age**
- **Indications**:
  - All children (routine - some countries)
  - Travelers to endemic areas
  - Chronic liver disease (any cause)
  - MSM, IVDU
- **Highly effective** (95-100%)
- **Lifelong immunity**

**2. Immunoglobulin (IHNIG - Immune Globulin)**:
- **Post-exposure prophylaxis** (<2 weeks after exposure)
- **Pre-exposure**: Travelers (if no time for vaccine)
- **Passive immunity** (immediate, short-term - 3 months)

**3. Hygiene**: Handwashing, safe water/food

**NEET PG**: HAV vaccine = Inactivated, 2 doses, lifelong immunity; IHNIG for post-exposure

## HEPATITIS B VIRUS (HBV)

### Characteristics

**Genome**: Partially **double-stranded DNA** (unique - only DNA hepatitis virus)

**Family**: **Hepadnavirus**

**Structure**:
- **HBsAg** (surface antigen) - envelope protein
- **HBcAg** (core antigen) - nucleocapsid
- **HBeAg** (e antigen) - secreted, marker of active replication
- **HBV DNA polymerase** (reverse transcriptase activity)

**Transmission** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Parenteral** (blood, needles - IVDU, transfusion)
- **Vertical** (mother-to-child during delivery) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ - **most important in endemic areas**
- **Sexual** (semen, vaginal fluids)
- **NOT fecal-oral**

**Incubation**: **1-6 months** (average 3 months)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: HBV = Only DNA hepatitis virus, vertical transmission important

---

### Clinical Features

**Acute hepatitis B**:
- **70%**: Asymptomatic or mild
- **30%**: Symptomatic (jaundice, malaise, RUQ pain)
- **1%**: **Fulminant hepatic failure** (high mortality)

**Outcomes** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **90-95%**: **Complete recovery** (adults)
  - Clear virus, develop anti-HBs (immunity)
- **5-10%**: **Chronic HBV** (adults)
  - **90%**: Infants (vertical transmission)
  - **30%**: Children <5 years
  - **5%**: Adults

**Chronic hepatitis B** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Persistent infection** (>6 months)
- **Progressive liver disease**:
  - **Cirrhosis** (20-30% over 20-30 years)
  - **Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (increased risk)
- **Phases**:
  1. **Immune tolerant**: HBeAg+, high HBV DNA, normal ALT (no treatment)
  2. **Immune active**: HBeAg+, high HBV DNA, elevated ALT (treat)
  3. **Inactive carrier**: HBeAg-, low HBV DNA, normal ALT (monitor)
  4. **HBeAg-negative chronic**: HBeAg-, high HBV DNA, elevated ALT (treat)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Acute HBV: 90-95% recover (adults), 5-10% chronic
- Infants: 90% become chronic (vertical transmission)
- Chronic HBV → Cirrhosis → HCC

---

### Serological Markers ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Understanding HBV serology is CRITICAL for NEET PG**

**Antigens**:
- **HBsAg** (surface antigen): Presence indicates **active infection** (acute or chronic)
- **HBeAg** (e antigen): **Active viral replication**, high infectivity
- **HBcAg** (core antigen): NOT detectable in serum (intracellular)

**Antibodies**:
- **Anti-HBs**: **Immunity** (recovery or vaccination)
- **Anti-HBe**: **Seroconversion** (↓ replication), better prognosis
- **Anti-HBc**:
  - **IgM**: **Acute infection** (recent)
  - **IgG**: **Past or chronic infection** (persists lifelong)

---

### Interpretation of HBV Serology ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| HBsAg | Anti-HBs | Anti-HBc IgM | Anti-HBc IgG | Interpretation |
|-------|----------|--------------|--------------|----------------|
| **(+)** | (-) | **(+)** | (+/-) | **Acute HBV** |
| **(+)** | (-) | (-) | **(+)** | **Chronic HBV** |
| (-) | **(+)** | (-) | **(+)** | **Resolved infection** (immunity) |
| (-) | **(+)** | (-) | (-) | **Vaccination** (immunity, never infected) |
| (-) | (-) | (-) | **(+)** | **Window period** OR past infection (anti-HBs waned) |
| (-) | (-) | (-) | (-) | **Never exposed**, susceptible |

**NEET PG High-Yield - MUST KNOW**:
- Acute HBV = HBsAg+ + Anti-HBc IgM+
- Chronic HBV = HBsAg+ >6 months + Anti-HBc IgG+
- Resolved = Anti-HBs+ + Anti-HBc IgG+
- Vaccinated = Anti-HBs+ ONLY (no anti-HBc)
- Window period = ONLY Anti-HBc+ (HBsAg cleared, anti-HBs not yet developed)

---

### Diagnosis

**Serology**: See above

**HBV DNA (Quantitative PCR)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Viral load** measurement
- **Monitor treatment response**
- **>2000 IU/mL**: Consider treatment (with elevated ALT)

**LFTs**:
- ↑ ALT, AST, Bilirubin (variable)

**Liver biopsy** (if needed):
- Assess fibrosis/cirrhosis

**NEET PG**: HBV DNA quantification for treatment decisions

---

### Treatment

**Acute HBV**:
- **Supportive** (usually self-limited)
- **NO antiviral** (unless fulminant)

**Chronic HBV** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:

**Indications for treatment**:
- **HBV DNA >2000 IU/mL** + **Elevated ALT** + **Significant fibrosis**
- **Cirrhosis** (any HBV DNA level)
- **Family history of HCC**
- **Pregnancy** (high viral load - prevent vertical transmission)

**First-line agents** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
1. **Tenofovir** (TDF or TAF):
   - **Nucleotide analog** (reverse transcriptase inhibitor)
   - **High barrier to resistance**
   - **Preferred** (lifelong in most)
   - **Safe in pregnancy**

2. **Entecavir**:
   - **Nucleoside analog**
   - High barrier to resistance
   - **Contraindicated with lamivudine resistance**

**Older agents** (high resistance):
- **Lamivudine** (high resistance - 70% at 5 years)
- **Adefovir** (nephrotoxic)

**Pegylated Interferon-α** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Finite duration** (48 weeks)
- **30-40% response** (seroconversion)
- **Many side effects** (flu-like, depression, cytopenias)
- **Contraindicated**: Decompensated cirrhosis, pregnancy, autoimmune disease

**Goal of treatment**:
- **HBeAg seroconversion** (HBeAg- → Anti-HBe+)
- **Undetectable HBV DNA**
- **↓ Progression to cirrhosis/HCC**
- **Functional cure** (HBsAg loss) - rare

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Chronic HBV treatment = Tenofovir or Entecavir (first-line)
- Indications: HBV DNA >2000 + elevated ALT + fibrosis
- Tenofovir safe in pregnancy

---

### Prevention ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. HBV Vaccine** (Recombinant HBsAg):

**Schedule**:
- **3 doses**: 0, 1-2, 6 months (standard)
- **Birth dose + 3 doses** (0, 6 weeks, 10 weeks, 14 weeks with DPT)

**Indications**:
- **All infants** (universal infant immunization)
- **Healthcare workers**
- **High-risk**: IVDU, MSM, dialysis, chronic liver disease, HIV+

**Efficacy**: **95%** (protective anti-HBs >10 mIU/mL)

**Duration**: **Lifelong** (no booster needed for immunocompetent)

**Non-responders** (5-10%):
- Repeat full series
- Check anti-HBs 1-2 months after completion

**2. Hepatitis B Immune Globulin (HBIG)**:
- **Passive immunity** (immediate, short-term)
- **Post-exposure prophylaxis**:
  - Needlestick injury (with HBV vaccine)
  - **Newborns of HBsAg+ mothers** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (within **12 hours**)
  - Sexual exposure

**3. Prevention of vertical transmission** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **All pregnant women** screened for HBsAg
- If **HBsAg+ mother**:
  - **HBIG + HBV vaccine** (within 12 hours of birth)
  - **95% efficacy** in preventing transmission
  - Complete vaccine series (3-4 doses)
- If **high viral load** (>200,000 IU/mL):
  - **Tenofovir** in 3rd trimester (↓ transmission)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- HBV vaccine = Recombinant HBsAg, 3 doses, 95% efficacy
- Newborn of HBsAg+ mother = HBIG + vaccine within 12 hours
- Tenofovir if maternal high viral load

## HEPATITIS C VIRUS (HCV)

### Characteristics

**Genome**: Single-stranded **RNA**, enveloped

**Family**: **Flavivirus** (like dengue, yellow fever, Zika)

**Genotypes**: **6 major genotypes** (1-6), multiple subtypes
- **Genotype 1** most common (US, Europe)
- **Genotype 3** common (India, Southeast Asia)

**Transmission** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Parenteral** (blood):
  - **IVDU** (most common in developed countries) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Blood transfusion** (pre-1992 screening)
  - Needlestick injury, hemodialysis
- **Vertical** (rare - 5%, higher if HIV co-infection)
- **Sexual** (rare - <5%)
- **NO fecal-oral**

**Incubation**: **2 weeks - 6 months** (average 6-7 weeks)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: HCV = RNA, parenteral (IVDU most common), high chronicity

---

### Clinical Features

**Acute HCV**:
- **70-80%**: Asymptomatic
- **20-30%**: Symptomatic (mild jaundice, malaise)
- Rarely fulminant

**Chronic HCV** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **70-85%** develop chronic infection (highest chronicity rate)
- **20-30%** spontaneous clearance (especially women, jaundice during acute phase)

**Natural history**:
- **Cirrhosis**: 20-30% over 20-30 years
- **Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)**: Increased risk (only from cirrhosis)
- **Decompensation**: Ascites, variceal bleeding, encephalopathy

**Extrahepatic manifestations** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Mixed cryoglobulinemia** (most common)
  - Palpable purpura, arthralgia, glomerulonephritis
  - ↓ C4 complement
- **Membranoproliferative glomerulonephritis (MPGN)**
- **Porphyria cutanea tarda**
- **Lichen planus**
- **B-cell lymphoma**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- HCV = 70-85% chronic (highest)
- Mixed cryoglobulinemia (extrahepatic)
- Cirrhosis → HCC

---

### Diagnosis

**Serology** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:

**Anti-HCV antibody** (ELISA):
- **Screening test**
- Positive: Current or past infection
- **Does NOT differentiate active from resolved**
- Appears **4-10 weeks** after infection (window period)
- **Persists lifelong** (even after cure)

**HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Confirmatory test** (if anti-HCV+)
- **Active infection** if positive
- **Viral load** measurement
- **Genotyping** (guide treatment - now less important with DAAs)
- **Monitor treatment response** (SVR - sustained virologic response)

**Algorithm**:
1. **Screen**: Anti-HCV
2. **If positive**: HCV RNA (confirm active infection)
3. **If HCV RNA positive**: Treat

**LFTs**:
- ↑ ALT, AST (fluctuating, may be normal)

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Anti-HCV = Screening (persists lifelong)
- HCV RNA = Confirms active infection, viral load
- Must do HCV RNA if anti-HCV+ to confirm active infection

---

### Treatment ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Revolution in HCV treatment**:
- **Direct-Acting Antivirals (DAAs)** since 2014
- **Cure >95%** (SVR - Sustained Virologic Response)
- **Short duration** (8-12 weeks)
- **Well-tolerated** (few side effects)
- **No interferon** needed

**DAA Classes**:
1. **NS5A inhibitors**: Ledipasvir, Velpatasvir, Daclatasvir
2. **NS5B polymerase inhibitors**: **Sofosbuvir** (nucleotide), Dasabuvir (non-nucleotide)
3. **NS3/4A protease inhibitors**: Simeprevir, Paritaprevir, Glecaprevir

**Pangenotypic regimens** (treat all genotypes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir** (12 weeks) - **most common**
- **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir** (8-12 weeks)

**Goal**: **SVR12** (undetectable HCV RNA 12 weeks after treatment completion) = **CURE**

**Old treatment** (historical):
- **Pegylated Interferon-α + Ribavirin** (48 weeks)
- **40-50% SVR** (genotype 1)
- Many side effects
- **Obsolete** now

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- HCV treatment = DAAs (Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir), 8-12 weeks, >95% cure
- SVR12 = Cure (undetectable HCV RNA 12 weeks post-treatment)

---

### Prevention

**NO vaccine** (antigenic variability, no animal model)

**Screening recommendations** ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **All adults ≥18 years** (one-time - US guidelines 2020)
- **High-risk**: IVDU, HIV+, hemodialysis, received blood transfusion pre-1992

**Post-exposure**:
- **No HCIG** (immune globulin NOT available)
- Monitor: HCV RNA at 4-6 weeks, anti-HCV at 4-6 months
- Treat if develops infection

**IVDU**: Harm reduction (needle exchange)

**NEET PG**: No HCV vaccine, screen all adults ≥18 years

## HEPATITIS D VIRUS (HDV)

### Characteristics

**Defective virus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Requires HBV** for replication (uses HBsAg as envelope)
- **Cannot infect alone**
- **RNA virus**

**Transmission**: Same as HBV (parenteral, sexual)

**NEET PG**: HDV = Defective, requires HBV (uses HBsAg)

---

### Clinical Significance

**Two scenarios** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:

**1. Co-infection** (HBV + HDV simultaneously):
- **Acute hepatitis** (more severe than HBV alone)
- **Fulminant hepatitis** (5-20%)
- Usually **resolves** (90% clear both)

**2. Superinfection** (HDV infects chronic HBV patient):
- **Severe, rapidly progressive**
- **80-90% chronic HDV**
- **Accelerated cirrhosis**
- **Worse prognosis**

**NEET PG High-Yield**:
- Co-infection = Acute (HBV+HDV together), usually resolves
- Superinfection = Chronic HBV + HDV, severe, high chronicity

---

### Diagnosis

**Serology**:
- **Anti-HDV IgM**: Acute or active infection
- **Anti-HDV IgG**: Past or chronic
- **HDV RNA**: Active replication

**Must have HBsAg+** (cannot have HDV without HBV)

---

### Treatment & Prevention

**Treatment**:
- **Pegylated Interferon-α** (limited efficacy)
- **No DAAs** effective
- **Liver transplantation** (end-stage)

**Prevention**:
- **HBV vaccination protects** against HDV ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- If chronic HBV: Avoid HDV exposure

**NEET PG**: HBV vaccine protects against HDV

---

## HEPATITIS E VIRUS (HEV)

### Characteristics

**Genome**: Single-stranded **RNA**, non-enveloped

**Family**: Hepeviridae

**Transmission** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Fecal-oral** (like HAV)
- Contaminated water (most common)
- **Zoonotic** (pigs, deer)
- **NOT vertical** (but severe in pregnancy)

**Incubation**: **2-9 weeks** (average 40 days)

**Genotypes**:
- **Genotype 1, 2**: Humans only (developing countries)
- **Genotype 3, 4**: Zoonotic (developed countries)

**NEET PG**: HEV = Fecal-oral (like HAV), RNA, severe in pregnancy

---

### Clinical Features

**Acute hepatitis**:
- Similar to HAV
- **Self-limited** (2-6 weeks)
- **NO chronic infection** (except immunocompromised - transplant, HIV)

**Pregnancy** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐:
- **Severe in pregnant women** (especially 3rd trimester)
- **Fulminant hepatic failure** (20-30% mortality) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fetal loss** (high)
- **Unique to HEV** (other hepatitis viruses NOT as severe)

**NEET PG High-Yield**: HEV in pregnancy = 20-30% mortality (fulminant), 3rd trimester

---

### Diagnosis

**Serology**:
- **Anti-HEV IgM**: Acute infection
- **Anti-HEV IgG**: Past infection

**HEV RNA**: Active infection (research)

---

### Treatment & Prevention

**Treatment**:
- **Supportive**
- **Ribavirin** (chronic HEV in immunocompromised)

**Prevention**:
- **Safe water, hygiene**
- **Avoid undercooked pork** (developed countries)
- **Vaccine** (China only - not widely available)
- **Pregnant women**: Avoid endemic areas

**NEET PG**: HEV = Supportive, NO widely available vaccine

## Practice MCQs

1. **Which hepatitis virus is DNA?**
   Answer: Hepatitis B (only DNA hepatitis virus)

2. **Hepatitis virus transmitted fecal-oral?**
   Answer: HAV, HEV

3. **Marker of acute Hepatitis A?**
   Answer: Anti-HAV IgM

4. **Marker of vaccination against HBV?**
   Answer: Anti-HBs ONLY (no anti-HBc)

5. **Marker of acute HBV?**
   Answer: HBsAg+ + Anti-HBc IgM+

6. **Hepatitis virus with highest chronicity?**
   Answer: HCV (70-85%)

7. **Treatment for chronic HCV?**
   Answer: DAAs (Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir), 8-12 weeks, >95% cure

8. **Hepatitis virus requiring HBV for replication?**
   Answer: HDV (defective virus)

9. **Most severe hepatitis in pregnancy?**
   Answer: HEV (20-30% fulminant hepatic failure)

10. **Prevention of vertical HBV transmission?**
    Answer: HBIG + HBV vaccine within 12 hours of birth

## Memory Aids

### Hepatitis Transmission (PPPFF)
- **P**arenteral = B, C, D
- **P**eroral (fecal-oral) = A, E
- **P**regnant severe = E
- **F**ulminant co-infection = D
- **F**amily clustering = A

### HBV Serology - Acute (SIMS)
- **S**urface antigen (HBsAg) = Positive
- **I**gM anti-core (Anti-HBc IgM) = Positive
- **M**arker of acute
- **S**elf-limited if adult

### HBV Vaccine Response (S-only)
- Anti-HB**S** = Positive
- Anti-HBc = Negative (never infected)

### HCV Treatment Goal (SVR)
- **S**ustained
- **V**irologic
- **R**esponse (12 weeks post-treatment = CURE)
