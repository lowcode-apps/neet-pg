# Immunology & Vaccines

## IMMUNE SYSTEM OVERVIEW ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Function**: Defense against pathogens, surveillance for abnormal cells ⭐

**Two Arms**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
1. **Innate Immunity** (Non-specific, immediate) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
2. **Adaptive Immunity** (Specific, delayed, memory) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## INNATE IMMUNITY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Characteristics**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **First line of defense**
- **Non-specific** (recognizes broad patterns)
- **No memory**
- **Immediate** response (minutes-hours)

---

### PHYSICAL & CHEMICAL BARRIERS ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Skin**: ⭐⭐⭐
- Intact epithelium (physical barrier)
- **Low pH** (acidic - inhibits bacteria)
- **Sebum, fatty acids** (antimicrobial)

**Mucous Membranes**: ⭐⭐⭐
- **Mucus traps pathogens**
- **Cilia** (respiratory tract - mucociliary clearance)
- **Lysozyme** in secretions (cleaves peptidoglycan)
- **IgA** (neutralizes pathogens)

**Gastric Acid**: ⭐⭐⭐
- **Low pH** (kills ingested organisms)

**Normal Flora**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Competitive inhibition** (colonization resistance)
- Produce antimicrobial substances

---

### CELLULAR COMPONENTS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Neutrophils (PMNs)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **First responders** (arrive within hours) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Phagocytosis** (ingest bacteria, fungi)
- **Oxidative burst** (respiratory burst) ⭐⭐⭐⭐:
  - **NADPH oxidase** produces **reactive oxygen species (ROS)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Superoxide, H₂O₂, hypochlorous acid** → kill pathogens
- **Degranulation** (release antimicrobial peptides, enzymes)
- **Neutrophil extracellular traps (NETs)** ⭐⭐⭐
- **Deficiency**: Recurrent bacterial/fungal infections ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**2. Macrophages**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Tissue-resident** (derived from monocytes)
- **Phagocytosis** (bacteria, dead cells, debris)
- **Antigen presentation** (link to adaptive immunity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cytokine production** (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Types**: Alveolar (lungs), Kupffer (liver), microglia (brain), osteoclasts (bone)

**3. Dendritic Cells**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Professional antigen-presenting cells (APCs)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Capture antigens** in periphery → migrate to lymph nodes
- **Activate naive T cells** (initiate adaptive immunity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most important for T cell priming** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**4. Natural Killer (NK) Cells**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Kill virus-infected cells, tumor cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Without prior sensitization** (innate)
- **Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Recognize **"missing self"** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (cells with low/absent MHC-I)
  - Release **perforin, granzymes** → apoptosis
- **Antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**5. Eosinophils**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Defense against parasites** (helminths) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Allergic reactions** (Type I hypersensitivity)
- **Major basic protein** (toxic to parasites)

**6. Basophils & Mast Cells**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Allergic/inflammatory responses**
- Release **histamine, heparin, leukotrienes** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **IgE receptor** (FcεRI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### SOLUBLE MEDIATORS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Complement System**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Functions**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Opsonization** (C3b - enhances phagocytosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Inflammation** (C3a, C5a - anaphylatoxins) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Membrane attack complex (MAC)** (C5b-9 - lysis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Pathways**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
1. **Classical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Triggered by **antigen-antibody complexes** (IgG, IgM) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - **C1** binds antibody → C4, C2 → **C3 convertase (C4b2a)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
2. **Alternative**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Triggered by **pathogen surfaces** (LPS, zymosan) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Spontaneous C3 hydrolysis → Factor B, D → **C3 convertase (C3bBb)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
3. **Lectin**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Triggered by **mannose-binding lectin (MBL)** on pathogen ⭐⭐⭐⭐
   - Similar to classical (bypasses C1) → C4, C2 → C3 convertase

**All pathways converge at C3** → C5-9 → MAC ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Deficiencies**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **C1, C2, C4** (classical): SLE-like syndrome, recurrent pyogenic infections ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **C3**: Severe recurrent infections (all organisms) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **C5-9** (MAC): **Recurrent Neisseria infections** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (meningococcus, gonococcus)

**Cytokines**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Fever** (act on hypothalamus) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Acute phase response** (↑ CRP, fibrinogen, ferritin) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Endothelial activation** (adhesion molecules)
- **Interferons (IFNs)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Type I (IFN-α, IFN-β)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Antiviral** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (induce antiviral proteins, ↑ MHC-I, activate NK cells)
    - Produced by virus-infected cells
  - **Type II (IFN-γ)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
    - **Macrophage activation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Th1 response, intracellular pathogens)
    - Produced by T cells, NK cells

**Acute Phase Proteins**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **C-reactive protein (CRP)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Opsonizes bacteria (binds phosphocholine on bacterial surfaces)
  - Activates complement (classical pathway)
  - **Marker of inflammation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Serum amyloid A (SAA)**, **fibrinogen**, **ferritin**

---

## ADAPTIVE IMMUNITY ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Characteristics**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Specific** (recognizes unique antigens) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Memory** (faster, stronger response on re-exposure) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Delayed** (days-weeks for initial response)
- **Two arms**: Cell-mediated (T cells), Humoral (B cells, antibodies) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## T CELLS (Cell-Mediated Immunity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Maturation**: **Thymus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**T Cell Receptor (TCR)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Recognizes **antigen + MHC** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (NOT free antigen)

---

### CD4+ T CELLS (Helper T Cells) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Recognize**: Antigen + **MHC Class II** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- On **APCs** (dendritic cells, macrophages, B cells)

**Function**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **"Orchestrate" immune response** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Activate other immune cells** (via cytokines)
- **Help B cells** make antibodies ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Subsets**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Th1 (Type 1 Helper)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cytokines**: **IFN-γ, IL-2** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Function**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Activate macrophages** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (kill intracellular pathogens)
  - **Cell-mediated immunity** (DTH)
- **Target**: **Intracellular bacteria** (Mycobacteria, Listeria), viruses, protozoa ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Disease**: Overactive → **Type IV hypersensitivity** (contact dermatitis, TB granulomas) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Th2 (Type 2 Helper)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cytokines**: **IL-4, IL-5, IL-13** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Function**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Activate B cells** → antibody production ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Activate eosinophils** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Promote IgE class switching** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Target**: **Extracellular parasites** (helminths) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Disease**: Overactive → **Allergies, asthma** (Type I hypersensitivity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Th17**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cytokines**: **IL-17, IL-22** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Function**: Recruit **neutrophils** (extracellular bacteria, fungi) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Disease**: Overactive → Autoimmune (psoriasis, IBD, MS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Treg (Regulatory T Cells)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cytokines**: **IL-10, TGF-β** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Function**: **Suppress immune responses** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (prevent autoimmunity, limit inflammation)
- **Marker**: **CD25, FOXP3** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Disease**: Deficiency → **IPEX syndrome** (autoimmunity) ⭐⭐⭐

---

### CD8+ T CELLS (Cytotoxic T Cells, CTLs) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Recognize**: Antigen + **MHC Class I** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- On **ALL nucleated cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Function**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Kill virus-infected cells, tumor cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Perforin** (pore formation) + **Granzymes** (induce apoptosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Fas-FasL** interaction (apoptosis)

**Target**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Virus-infected cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Tumor cells** (cancer immunosurveillance)
- **Intracellular bacteria**

---

## B CELLS (Humoral Immunity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Maturation**: **Bone marrow** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Function**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Produce antibodies** (immunoglobulins) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Antigen presentation** (to T cells)

**Activation**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T-dependent**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - B cell binds antigen → presents to CD4+ T cell → T cell help → B cell activation
  - **Requires CD40-CD40L** interaction ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Class switching** (IgM → IgG/IgA/IgE) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Somatic hypermutation** (affinity maturation) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Memory B cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T-independent**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Polysaccharide antigens (bacterial capsules)
  - Direct B cell activation (no T cell help)
  - **NO class switching** (IgM only) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **NO memory** (poor secondary response) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## ANTIBODIES (IMMUNOGLOBULINS) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Structure**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **2 heavy chains + 2 light chains** (κ or λ) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fab** (fragment antigen-binding): Variable region (antigen recognition) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fc** (fragment crystallizable): Constant region (effector functions - complement, opsonization) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### ANTIBODY CLASSES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**IgM**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pentamer** (5 units) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **First antibody** in immune response (primary) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Acute infection** marker ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Best at complement activation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Best at agglutination** (large, multivalent)
- **Cannot cross placenta** (too large) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**IgG**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Monomer** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most abundant** (75% of serum Ig) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Secondary/memory response** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Past infection/immunity** marker ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Functions**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Opsonization** (enhances phagocytosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Complement activation** (classical pathway) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Neutralization** (toxins, viruses)
  - **ADCC** (antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **ONLY antibody that crosses placenta** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (via FcRn receptor)
  - **Passive immunity to newborn** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **4 subclasses**: IgG1 (most abundant), IgG2 (polysaccharides), IgG3 (strongest complement), IgG4

**IgA**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Monomer** (serum), **Dimer** (secretions) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Secretory IgA** (sIgA): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Mucosal immunity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (GI, respiratory, GU tracts, breast milk, saliva, tears)
  - **Prevents pathogen attachment** to mucosa ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Neutralizes toxins**
  - **Contains J chain + secretory component** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **2nd most abundant** (15% of serum Ig)
- **Does NOT activate complement** (classical) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**IgE**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Monomer** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Lowest serum concentration** (<0.001%) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Functions**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Binds to mast cells/basophils** (via FcεRI) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Type I hypersensitivity** (allergies, anaphylaxis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Parasite defense** (helminths) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Elevated in**: Allergies, asthma, atopy, parasitic infections ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**IgD**: ⭐⭐⭐
- **Monomer**
- **B cell receptor** (with IgM on naive B cells)
- **Function unclear** (signaling?)

---

## HYPERSENSITIVITY REACTIONS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Gell and Coombs Classification** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### TYPE I (Immediate, Anaphylactic) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **IgE-mediated** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Sensitization**: First exposure → IgE production → binds mast cells/basophils
- **Re-exposure**: Antigen cross-links IgE → **mast cell degranulation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mediators**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Histamine** (immediate - vasodilation, bronchospasm, ↑ vascular permeability) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Leukotrienes, prostaglandins** (delayed - sustained bronchoconstriction)
  - **Tryptase** (marker of mast cell activation) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Timing**: **Minutes** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anaphylaxis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (systemic - hypotension, bronchospasm, angioedema, urticaria)
- **Allergic rhinitis** (hay fever)
- **Asthma** (allergic)
- **Atopic dermatitis** (eczema)
- **Food allergies**, **drug allergies** (penicillin)

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Bee sting**, **peanuts**, **penicillin**, **latex**

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Skin prick test** (wheal and flare) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Serum IgE** (specific allergen)
- **Serum tryptase** (anaphylaxis)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anaphylaxis**: **Epinephrine IM** (first-line) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ + antihistamines, steroids, fluids
- **Prevention**: Avoid allergen, **desensitization** (immunotherapy) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Chronic**: Antihistamines (H1 blockers), leukotriene inhibitors (montelukast), **omalizumab** (anti-IgE)

---

### TYPE II (Cytotoxic, Antibody-Mediated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **IgG or IgM** antibodies against **cell-surface/matrix antigens** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cell destruction** via: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Complement activation** (MAC) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Opsonization** → phagocytosis ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **ADCC** (NK cells, macrophages) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Timing**: **Minutes-hours** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Hemolytic Transfusion Reactions**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **ABO incompatibility** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Pre-existing **IgM antibodies** against A/B antigens
  - **Acute intravascular hemolysis** (complement-mediated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Fever, flank pain, hemoglobinuria, hypotension, DIC
- **Rh incompatibility** (Rh-negative mother, Rh-positive fetus):
  - First pregnancy: Sensitization (no symptoms)
  - Subsequent pregnancy: **Hemolytic disease of newborn (HDN)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Prevention**: **RhoGAM** (anti-D IgG at 28 weeks + delivery) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Autoimmune Hemolytic Anemia (AIHA)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Warm (IgG)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Extravascular hemolysis** (spleen) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Positive direct Coombs test** (DAT) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Causes: Idiopathic, SLE, drugs (penicillin, methyldopa), CLL
- **Cold (IgM)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Intravascular hemolysis** (complement) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Triggered by cold exposure
  - **Mycoplasma, EBV, lymphoma**

**Goodpasture Syndrome**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anti-basement membrane antibodies** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Glomerulonephritis + pulmonary hemorrhage** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Linear IgG** on immunofluorescence ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Graves' Disease**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anti-TSH receptor antibodies** (stimulating) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hyperthyroidism** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Myasthenia Gravis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anti-acetylcholine receptor antibodies** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Muscle weakness** (fatigable) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Pemphigus Vulgaris**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anti-desmoglein antibodies** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Blistering skin disease** (intraepidermal)

---

### TYPE III (Immune Complex-Mediated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Antigen-antibody complexes** (immune complexes) deposit in tissues ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Complement activation** → **neutrophil recruitment** → **tissue damage** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Timing**: **Hours (3-8 hours)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Serum Sickness**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Antibodies against foreign proteins** (antiserum, drugs) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Fever, rash, arthralgias, lymphadenopathy** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **7-21 days** after exposure ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **↓ Complement (C3, C4)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Arthus Reaction**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Local immune complex deposition** (repeated injections) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Skin necrosis** at injection site

**SLE (Systemic Lupus Erythematosus)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Anti-nuclear antibodies** (ANA), anti-dsDNA, anti-Smith ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Immune complex deposition** (kidneys, joints, skin, brain)
- **↓ Complement (C3, C4)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Immune complexes deposit in glomeruli
- **1-3 weeks after Strep pharyngitis/skin infection** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **↓ C3** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hepatitis B** associated
- Necrotizing vasculitis (medium vessels)

---

### TYPE IV (Delayed, Cell-Mediated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T cell-mediated** (CD4+ Th1) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **NO antibodies** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Macrophage activation** (IFN-γ from Th1 cells) → tissue damage ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Timing**: **48-72 hours** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (delayed)

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Contact Dermatitis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Poison ivy, nickel, cosmetics** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Erythema, vesicles, pruritus** at contact site
- **48-72h after exposure** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Tuberculin Skin Test (TST, PPD)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Mantoux test** for TB exposure ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Induration** (not erythema) at **48-72 hours** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **≥5 mm**: HIV+, close contact, CXR changes
- **≥10 mm**: Healthcare workers, immigrants, IVDU, <5 years old
- **≥15 mm**: No risk factors

**Granulomatous Reactions**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **TB, leprosy, sarcoidosis** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Epithelioid macrophages + giant cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Autoimmune destruction of β-cells** (T cell-mediated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**MS (Multiple Sclerosis)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T cell attack on myelin** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Graft Rejection** (cellular rejection): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Acute rejection**: T cells attack donor tissue ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

## IMMUNODEFICIENCY DISORDERS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### PRIMARY IMMUNODEFICIENCIES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**B Cell Defects**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**X-Linked Agammaglobulinemia (XLA, Bruton)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **BTK gene** mutation (B cell maturation arrest) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **No B cells, no immunoglobulins** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Males only** (X-linked)
- **Recurrent sinopulmonary infections** (6 months of age - maternal IgG wanes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organisms**: Encapsulated bacteria (Strep pneumoniae, H. influenzae), enteroviruses ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Treatment**: **IVIG** (IV immunoglobulin replacement) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Common Variable Immunodeficiency (CVID)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Low IgG, IgA ± IgM** (B cells present but dysfunctional) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Onset**: 20s-30s (vs XLA in infancy)
- **Recurrent sinopulmonary infections**
- **↑ Risk**: Autoimmune disease, lymphoma, GI disease (Giardia) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Treatment**: IVIG

**Selective IgA Deficiency**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most common primary immunodeficiency** (1:500) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **IgA <7 mg/dL**, normal IgG/IgM
- **Most asymptomatic** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Risk**: Sinopulmonary infections, GI infections (Giardia), **anaphylaxis to blood products** (anti-IgA antibodies) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **No treatment** (do NOT give IVIG - anaphylaxis risk)

---

**T Cell Defects**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**DiGeorge Syndrome (22q11.2 deletion)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thymic aplasia** → no T cell maturation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **CATCH-22**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **C**ardiac defects (ToF, truncus arteriosus)
  - **A**bnormal facies
  - **T**hymic aplasia
  - **C**left palate
  - **H**ypocalcemia (parathyroid hypoplasia)
  - **22**q11.2 deletion
- **Recurrent viral/fungal infections** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Treatment**: Thymic transplant (if severe)

---

**Combined B + T Cell Defects**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **No T cells, no B cells** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Types**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **X-linked SCID** (most common): IL-2 receptor γ chain defect ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **ADA deficiency**: Adenosine deaminase (toxic metabolites accumulate) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Presentation**: Infancy (<6 months) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Infections**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Bacterial, viral, fungal, opportunistic** (all types) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Chronic diarrhea** (Cryptosporidium)
  - **PCP pneumonia** (Pneumocystis)
  - **Failure to thrive**
- **Absent thymic shadow** on CXR ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Live vaccines CONTRAINDICATED** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (can cause disseminated infection)
- **Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HSCT)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (curative)
  - **Gene therapy** (ADA deficiency)
  - **PCP prophylaxis** (TMP-SMX)

**Hyper-IgM Syndrome**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **CD40L deficiency** (X-linked) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- B cells cannot class switch (stuck at IgM)
- **↑ IgM, ↓ IgG/IgA/IgE** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Recurrent pyogenic infections** + **opportunistic** (Pneumocystis, Cryptosporidium) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

**Phagocyte Defects**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **NADPH oxidase deficiency** → no respiratory burst ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cannot kill catalase-positive organisms** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organisms**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **S. aureus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Burkholderia cepacia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Serratia, Nocardia** ⭐⭐⭐
  - **Aspergillus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Recurrent abscesses** (skin, liver, lung, lymph nodes) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Granulomas** (non-caseating)
- **Diagnosis**: **Nitroblue tetrazolium (NBT) test** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ or **dihydrorhodamine (DHR) flow cytometry** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Treatment**: Prophylactic **TMP-SMX** + **itraconazole** (Aspergillus), IFN-γ ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Integrin deficiency** (CD18) → neutrophils cannot migrate to tissues ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Delayed umbilical cord separation** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Recurrent skin/mucosal infections** (no pus) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Leukocytosis** (neutrophils in blood, not tissues) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Chédiak-Higashi Syndrome**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Microtubule polymerization defect** ⭐⭐⭐
- **Giant granules** in neutrophils/lysosomes ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Recurrent pyogenic infections**
- **Partial albinism**, **peripheral neuropathy**

---

**Complement Deficiencies**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **C5-C9 (MAC)**: **Recurrent Neisseria** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **C3**: Severe infections (all types)
- **C1 inhibitor deficiency**: **Hereditary angioedema** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### SECONDARY IMMUNODEFICIENCIES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**HIV/AIDS** (see Topic 07): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **CD4+ T cell depletion** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most common cause of immunodeficiency worldwide** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Malnutrition**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most common cause of immunodeficiency globally** (if including developing countries) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Protein-energy malnutrition** (kwashiorkor, marasmus)
- **Impaired cell-mediated immunity** ⭐⭐⭐

**Others**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diabetes mellitus** (neutrophil dysfunction)
- **Chronic kidney disease, liver disease**
- **Splenectomy/asplenia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (↑ risk encapsulated bacteria)
- **Medications**: Corticosteroids, chemotherapy, immunosuppressants ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Malignancy**: Leukemia, lymphoma, myeloma
- **Aging** (immunosenescence)

## VACCINES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Definition**: Biological preparation that provides **active acquired immunity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Goal**: Induce **immunologic memory** without causing disease ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### TYPES OF VACCINES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**1. Live Attenuated**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Weakened live pathogen** (replicates but doesn't cause disease) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Strong, long-lasting immunity** (mimics natural infection) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cellular + humoral immunity** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Usually 1-2 doses** (lifelong immunity often)

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **MMR** (Measles, Mumps, Rubella) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Varicella** (chickenpox) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Yellow Fever** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Rotavirus** (oral) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **BCG** (tuberculosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **OPV** (oral polio) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Typhoid** (oral Ty21a) ⭐⭐⭐
- **Intranasal influenza** (FluMist)

**Contraindications**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Immunocompromised** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (HIV with CD4 <200, chemotherapy, high-dose steroids, SCID)
- **Pregnancy** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (risk of fetal infection - avoid MMR, varicella, yellow fever)
- Recent immunoglobulin (interferes with viral replication)

**Advantages**: Strong, durable immunity; mucosal immunity (oral vaccines) ⭐⭐⭐

**Disadvantages**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Risk of reversion to virulence** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (rare - VAPP from OPV)
- **Cannot use in immunocompromised** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Storage (requires cold chain)

---

**2. Inactivated (Killed)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Dead pathogen** (heat, chemicals) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Cannot replicate** → safe in immunocompromised ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Humoral immunity only** (no cellular) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Weaker, shorter immunity** → **requires boosters** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **IPV** (inactivated polio vaccine, Salk) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hepatitis A** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Rabies** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Influenza** (injectable) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Japanese encephalitis** ⭐⭐⭐

**Advantages**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Safe in immunocompromised** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **No risk of reversion** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Disadvantages**: Weaker immunity, multiple doses needed

---

**3. Subunit/Toxoid/Conjugate**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Subunit** (purified antigen): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Hepatitis B** (HBsAg) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **HPV** (L1 protein) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pertussis** (acellular - DTaP) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Influenza** (recombinant)

**Toxoid** (inactivated toxin): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Diphtheria** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Tetanus** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Induce **antitoxin antibodies** (neutralize toxin, NOT bacterium)

**Polysaccharide** (capsular polysaccharides): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pneumococcal** (PPSV23) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Meningococcal** (MPSV4 - older)
- **T-independent response** (no memory, poor in <2 years) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Conjugate** (polysaccharide + protein carrier): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Overcomes limitations of polysaccharide vaccines** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T-dependent response** → **memory**, effective in infants ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Hib** (H. influenzae type b) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **PCV13** (pneumococcal conjugate) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Meningococcal conjugate** (MenACWY) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Advantages**: Safe (cannot cause infection), safe in immunocompromised

**Disadvantages**: Weaker immunity, adjuvants/boosters needed

---

**4. mRNA Vaccines**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Mechanism**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **mRNA encoding antigen** → host cells translate → produce antigen → immune response ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Does NOT integrate into genome** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Examples**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **COVID-19** (Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna - spike protein) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Advantages**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Rapid development** (no need to culture pathogen)
- **Strong cellular + humoral immunity**
- **Safe** (non-infectious, non-integrating)

**Disadvantages**: Requires ultra-cold storage (initially)

---

### UNIVERSAL IMMUNIZATION PROGRAMME (UIP) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**India's National Schedule**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**At Birth**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **BCG** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **OPV-0** (zero dose) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**6 weeks**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **DTP-1, OPV-1, Hib-1, Hepatitis B-2, PCV-1, Rotavirus-1** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**10 weeks**: DTP-2, OPV-2, Hib-2, PCV-2, Rotavirus-2

**14 weeks**: DTP-3, OPV-3, Hib-3, Hepatitis B-3, PCV-3, Rotavirus-3, IPV-1

**9 months**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Measles-Rubella-1 (MR-1)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**16-24 months**: DTP-Booster 1, OPV-Booster, **MR-2**, **JE-1** (Japanese encephalitis - endemic areas)

**5-6 years**: DTP-Booster 2

**10 years**: **Tdap** (tetanus-diphtheria-pertussis booster)

### SPECIFIC VACCINES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**BCG (Bacille Calmette-Guérin)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Live attenuated** Mycobacterium bovis ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **At birth** (intradermal, left deltoid) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Prevents**: Severe TB in children (miliary TB, TB meningitis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Does NOT prevent pulmonary TB in adults** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **BCG scar**: Presence indicates vaccination (not immunity)
- **Contraindications**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **HIV with symptoms** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - SCID, immunosuppression
  - NOT contraindicated in asymptomatic HIV ⭐⭐⭐

**DTP/DTaP**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **D**iphtheria (toxoid) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **T**etanus (toxoid) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **P**ertussis (whole-cell or acellular) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **5 doses**: 6 weeks, 10 weeks, 14 weeks, 15-18 months, 4-6 years
- **Tdap** (adolescent/adult booster): Lower diphtheria/pertussis dose ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Given in pregnancy** (27-36 weeks) to protect newborn ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Adverse effects**: Fever, local reaction; rare: encephalopathy (whole-cell pertussis)

**Hepatitis B**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Recombinant subunit** (HBsAg) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **At birth** (within 24 hours - prevents vertical transmission) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ + 6 weeks, 14 weeks
- **3 doses** (0, 1-2, 6 months)
- **Check anti-HBs titers** after series (healthcare workers) ⭐⭐⭐
  - **≥10 mIU/mL** = protective ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Non-responders** (5-10%): Give additional doses or double-dose series

**MMR**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Live attenuated** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **In India**: **MR (Measles-Rubella)** used instead ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - 9 months, 16-24 months
- **Contraindications**: Pregnancy, immunocompromised, recent Ig ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **NOT associated with autism** (Wakefield study retracted) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Varicella** (Chickenpox): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Live attenuated** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Not in UIP** (part of IAP schedule)
- 15 months, 4-6 years
- **Post-exposure prophylaxis**: Within 72-96 hours ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Rotavirus**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Live attenuated oral vaccine** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Prevents severe gastroenteritis** (dehydration) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **3 doses**: 6, 10, 14 weeks
- **Contraindication**: **History of intussusception** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**HPV (Human Papillomavirus)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Subunit** (L1 protein) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Prevents cervical cancer, genital warts** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Age**: 9-14 years (before sexual debut) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **2 doses** (0, 6-12 months) if <15 years
  - **3 doses** (0, 1-2, 6 months) if ≥15 years
- **Types**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Bivalent** (16, 18 - cervical cancer)
  - **Quadrivalent** (6, 11, 16, 18 - + genital warts) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Nonavalent** (6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58)

**Influenza**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Inactivated** (injectable, most common) or **live attenuated** (intranasal) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Annual vaccination** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (strain changes yearly)
- **Indications**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **≥6 months** (universal recommendation in many countries)
  - **High-risk**: ≥65 years, pregnancy, chronic diseases, healthcare workers ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Egg allergy**: NOT a contraindication (modern vaccines safe) ⭐⭐⭐

**Pneumococcal**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **PCV13** (conjugate): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Infants/children** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - 6 weeks, 10 weeks, 14 weeks (+ booster 12-15 months)
- **PPSV23** (polysaccharide): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **≥65 years, immunocompromised, asplenia** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Give **after PCV13** (better response) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Meningococcal**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **MenACWY** (conjugate): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Adolescents** (11-12 years, booster 16 years)
  - **High-risk**: Asplenia, complement deficiency, college students, Hajj pilgrims ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **MenB**: Separate vaccine (outbreaks, high-risk)

**Rabies**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Inactivated** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Pre-exposure**: 3 doses (0, 7, 21-28 days) - high-risk (vets, lab workers, travelers)
- **Post-exposure**: See Topic 18 (4 doses + RIG) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Yellow Fever**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Live attenuated** (17D strain) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Single dose = lifelong protection** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Required for travel** to endemic areas (Africa, South America) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Contraindications**: <6 months, pregnancy, immunocompromised, egg allergy

**Typhoid**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Oral live** (Ty21a - 4 doses) OR **Injectable polysaccharide** (Vi - 1 dose) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Endemic areas, travelers**
- **Conjugate vaccine** (Typbar-TCV) - longer duration, effective in children ⭐⭐⭐

**Japanese Encephalitis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Inactivated** ⭐⭐⭐
- **Endemic areas** (Assam, UP, Bihar, etc. in India) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 9 months (with MR-1), 16-24 months

## SPECIAL SITUATIONS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Pregnancy**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Safe (Inactivated)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Tdap** (recommended 27-36 weeks) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Influenza** (inactivated) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B

**Contraindicated (Live)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **MMR, varicella, yellow fever** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Avoid pregnancy × 4 weeks after live vaccine** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Immunocompromised**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Safe (Inactivated/Subunit)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **All inactivated vaccines** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Higher doses/additional boosters** may be needed

**Contraindicated (Live)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **MMR, varicella, yellow fever, rotavirus, BCG, OPV, live influenza** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Exception**: MMR/varicella in HIV if CD4 >15-20% ⭐⭐⭐

**Asplenia/Hyposplenia**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Encapsulated organisms** (Strep pneumoniae, H. influenzae, Neisseria) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Vaccines**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **PCV13 + PPSV23** (pneumococcal) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **MenACWY + MenB** (meningococcal) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Hib** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Influenza** (annual)
- **Prophylactic antibiotics**: Penicillin (children) ⭐⭐⭐

## NEET PG MCQS

**Q1.** CD4+ T cells recognize antigen presented on:
A) MHC Class I B) MHC Class II C) No MHC needed D) Either class

**Answer: B** - CD4+ recognize antigen + MHC-II (on APCs); CD8+ recognize MHC-I (all nucleated cells)

**Q2.** Live vaccines are contraindicated in all EXCEPT:
A) SCID B) Pregnancy C) HIV with CD4 350/μL D) High-dose steroids

**Answer: C** - Live vaccines safe in HIV if CD4 >200 (>15-20% in children). Contraindicated in SCID, pregnancy, severe immunosuppression.

**Q3.** Recurrent Neisseria infections suggest deficiency of:
A) C3 B) C5-C9 C) IgG D) Neutrophils

**Answer: B** - Terminal complement (C5-C9, MAC) deficiency → recurrent meningococcal/gonococcal infections

**Q4.** Vaccine recommended in pregnancy:
A) MMR B) Varicella C) Tdap D) Yellow fever

**Answer: C** - Tdap (27-36 weeks) protects newborn from pertussis. MMR, varicella, yellow fever contraindicated (live).

**Q5.** Child with recurrent Staphylococcus aureus and Aspergillus infections. Abnormal NBT test. Diagnosis?
A) SCID B) CGD C) Bruton agammaglobulinemia D) DiGeorge

**Answer: B** - Chronic granulomatous disease: Catalase-positive organisms (Staph, Aspergillus), abnormal NBT/DHR test (no respiratory burst)
