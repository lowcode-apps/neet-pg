# Rickettsiae & Atypical Bacteria

## RICKETTSIAE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**General Features**:
- **Obligate intracellular** bacteria ⭐
- Gram-negative (poorly staining)
- **Arthropod vectors** (ticks, mites, lice, fleas)
- Target: **Endothelial cells** ⭐ → Vasculitis → Rash, end-organ damage

---

### SPOTTED FEVER GROUP

#### **Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organism**: *Rickettsia rickettsii*
- **Vector**: **Dog tick** (*Dermacentor*) ⭐
- **Geography**: Southeastern US (misnomer - not just Rocky Mountains)

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Triad** (only 3% have all): Fever + Headache + Rash
- **Rash**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Starts **day 3-5** of illness (NOT day 1)
  - **Maculopapular** → **Petechial**
  - **Wrists/ankles** → **Palms/soles** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ → Trunk (centripetal)
  - 10% never develop rash ("spotless fever")
- Severe: Vasculitis → DIC, gangrene, renal failure, CNS involvement
- **Most deadly rickettsial disease** in US ⭐
- Fatal if untreated (20-30%)

**Diagnosis**: Clinical (don't wait for confirmation to treat)
- Serology: IFA (acute/convalescent, 4-fold rise)
- Skin biopsy: Immunofluorescence

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Doxycycline 100 mg BID** (all ages, including children <8) ⭐⭐⭐
- Start empirically if suspected (tick exposure + fever + headache)
- Chloramphenicol (alternative, pregnancy - but doxycycline preferred even in pregnancy for RMSF)

---

### TYPHUS GROUP

#### **Epidemic Typhus** ⭐⭐⭐
- **Organism**: *R. prowazekii*
- **Vector**: **Body louse** ⭐
- Overcrowding, war, famine, refugee camps
- **Rash**: Trunk → extremities (centrifugal), **SPARES palms/soles** ⭐
- Severe: Gangrene, CNS involvement
- **Brill-Zinsser disease**: Recrudescence years later

#### **Endemic (Murine) Typhus** ⭐⭐⭐
- **Organism**: *R. typhi*
- **Vector**: **Rat flea** ⭐
- Milder than epidemic typhus
- Rash: Trunk → extremities, may spare palms/soles

**Treatment**: **Doxycycline**

---

### SCRUB TYPHUS ⭐⭐⭐
- **Organism**: *Orientia tsutsugamushi*
- **Vector**: **Chigger mite** (larval mite) ⭐
- **Geography**: Asia-Pacific ("tsutsugamushi triangle")
- **Eschar** at bite site ⭐ + regional lymphadenopathy
- Rash: Maculopapular, trunk
- Treatment: **Doxycycline**

---

### EHRLICHIA & ANAPLASMA ⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### **Human Monocytic Ehrlichiosis (HME)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organism**: *Ehrlichia chaffeensis*
- **Vector**: **Lone star tick** (*Amblyomma americanum*) ⭐
- **Target cells**: **Monocytes** ⭐

#### **Human Granulocytic Anaplasmosis (HGA)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Organism**: *Anaplasma phagocytophilum*
- **Vector**: **Ixodes tick** ⭐ (same as Lyme, often co-infection)
- **Target cells**: **Neutrophils** ⭐

**Clinical** (similar for both):
- Fever, headache, myalgias
- **No rash** (vs RMSF) ⭐
- Lab: **Leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, elevated transaminases** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Morulae** ⭐ on blood smear (intracytoplasmic inclusions in monocytes/granulocytes)

**Diagnosis**: Blood smear (morulae - low sensitivity), PCR, serology

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7-14 days**
- Empiric if suspected (tick + fever + cytopenias)

---

## ATYPICAL BACTERIA

### MYCOPLASMA PNEUMONIAE ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**:
- **No cell wall** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ → Gram stain negative, resistant to **β-lactams**
- **Smallest free-living organism**
- **Walking pneumonia** (atypical pneumonia)

**Transmission**: Respiratory droplets (close contacts, schools, military)

**Clinical**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Atypical pneumonia**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Gradual onset (days-weeks)
  - Fever, malaise, headache
  - **Persistent dry cough** ⭐ (>3 weeks)
  - **Exam out of proportion to symptoms** ⭐ (minimal findings despite symptoms)
  - CXR: **Patchy infiltrates** (worse than exam suggests)
- **Extrapulmonary**: ⭐⭐⭐
  - **Bullous myringitis** ⭐ (hemorrhagic blebs on tympanic membrane)
  - **Hemolytic anemia** (cold agglutinins - IgM anti-I) ⭐⭐⭐
  - Erythema multiforme, Stevens-Johnson syndrome
  - Myocarditis, pericarditis (rare)
  - Encephalitis, Guillain-Barré, transverse myelitis (rare)

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **PCR** (respiratory sample): Most sensitive
- **Serology**: IgM (acute), IgG (convalescent, 4-fold rise)
- **Cold agglutinins** ⭐: Bedside test (agglutination of RBCs at 4°C, disappears at 37°C)
  - Present in 50% (NOT specific - also EBV, lymphoma)
- Culture: Special media, slow (weeks) - not done routinely

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Macrolides**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Azithromycin 500 mg × 1, then 250 mg daily × 4 days** (preferred)
  - Clarithromycin, erythromycin
- **Alternatives**: Doxycycline, fluoroquinolones
- **NOT β-lactams** (no cell wall)

---

### CHLAMYDIA SPECIES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Features**:
- **Obligate intracellular** bacteria
- No peptidoglycan (resistant to β-lactams)
- **Two forms**: ⭐
  - **Elementary body** (EB): Infectious, extracellular, metabolically inactive
  - **Reticulate body** (RB): Intracellular, replicating

---

#### **CHLAMYDIA TRACHOMATIS** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Serovars**:

**A-C**: **Trachoma** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Leading infectious cause of blindness worldwide** ⭐
- Endemic: Sub-Saharan Africa, Middle East, Asia
- Chronic keratoconjunctivitis → Scarring → Trichiasis (inturned lashes) → Corneal damage
- Flies, fomites, person-to-person
- Treatment: **Azithromycin** (single dose, mass treatment)

**D-K**: **Genital/neonatal infections** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Most common bacterial STI** in US ⭐
- **Men**: Urethritis (NGU - non-gonococcal), epididymitis, proctitis
- **Women**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Often asymptomatic** (70%) ⭐
  - Cervicitis, urethritis
  - **PID** (pelvic inflammatory disease) → Infertility, ectopic pregnancy
  - **Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome** ⭐ (perihepatitis - "violin string" adhesions)
- **Neonatal**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Conjunctivitis** (5-14 days after birth)
  - **Pneumonia** (1-3 months) - **Staccato cough** ⭐, eosinophilia
- **Diagnosis**: **NAAT** ⭐ (urine, endocervical/urethral swab) - most sensitive
- **Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - **Azithromycin 1 g PO single dose** ⭐ OR
  - Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7 days
  - **Treat partners** (expedited partner therapy)
  - **Test of cure** at 3 weeks if pregnant

**L1-L3**: **Lymphogranuloma Venereum (LGV)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Tropical/subtropical (Africa, Asia, South America); MSM (developed countries)
- **Three stages**:
  1. **Painless genital ulcer** (papule → ulcer, heals)
  2. **Painful inguinal lymphadenopathy** ⭐⭐⭐ (buboes - matted, may rupture)
     - **"Groove sign"** ⭐: Depression between inguinal/femoral nodes
  3. **Proctocolitis** (if rectal), genital elephantiasis, strictures
- Treatment: **Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 21 days** ⭐

---

#### **CHLAMYDOPHILA PNEUMONIAE** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Atypical pneumonia** (similar to Mycoplasma)
- Community-acquired pneumonia (5-10%)
- Mild, self-limited
- Pharyngitis, sinusitis, bronchitis
- Treatment: **Macrolides or doxycycline**

#### **CHLAMYDOPHILA PSITTACI** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Psittacosis** (parrot fever)
- **Birds** (parrots, parakeets, turkeys) ⭐
- Atypical pneumonia
- **Headache** (prominent), **hepatosplenomegaly**
- **Faget sign** (relative bradycardia despite high fever)
- Treatment: **Doxycycline**

## HIGH-YIELD MNEMONICS

**RMSF Rash**: "**R**ick climbs from **W**rists to **P**alms" (Wrists/ankles → Palms/soles)

**Mycoplasma**: "**M**ycoplasma = **M**acrolides" (No cell wall = No β-lactams)

**Chlamydia Forms**: "**E**xtracellular **E**lementary" / "**R**eplicating **R**eticulate"

**LGV Groove Sign**: "**L**ine **G**roove in **V**enereal disease"

## NEET PG MCQS

**Q1.** Fever, headache, rash on palms/soles after tick bite. Treatment?
A) Penicillin B) Doxycycline C) Azithromycin D) Ceftriaxone

**Answer: B** - RMSF: Doxycycline (empiric if suspected, even children <8)

**Q2.** Tick bite, fever, thrombocytopenia, NO rash. Morulae in neutrophils. Diagnosis?
A) RMSF B) Lyme disease C) Anaplasmosis D) Babesiosis

**Answer: C** - Anaplasmosis (HGA): Morulae in granulocytes, no rash, cytopenias

**Q3.** Atypical pneumonia with bullous myringitis and cold agglutinins. Organism?
A) Strep pneumoniae B) Mycoplasma pneumoniae C) Legionella D) Chlamydophila

**Answer: B** - Mycoplasma: Bullous myringitis, cold agglutinins (IgM anti-I), persistent dry cough

**Q4.** Treatment for uncomplicated C. trachomatis urethritis?
A) Penicillin B) Ciprofloxacin C) Azithromycin 1 g single dose D) TMP-SMX

**Answer: C** - Chlamydia: Azithromycin 1 g PO × 1 (or doxycycline × 7 days)

**Remember**: Rickettsiae and atypical bacteria are high-yield. Master vector-disease associations, rash patterns, and doxycycline/macrolide treatments!
