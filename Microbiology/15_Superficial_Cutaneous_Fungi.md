# Superficial & Cutaneous Mycoses

## Overview
Fungal infections of skin, hair, and nails caused by dermatophytes and other superficial fungi.

---

## DERMATOPHYTES ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Definition**: Fungi that digest keratin (skin, hair, nails)

**Three Genera**:
1. **Trichophyton** ⭐ (most common) - skin, hair, nails
2. **Microsporum** - skin, hair (NOT nails)
3. **Epidermophyton** - skin, nails (NOT hair)

**Transmission**:
- **Anthropophilic**: Human-to-human (most common)
- **Zoophilic**: Animals (more inflammatory)
- **Geophilic**: Soil

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATIONS (TINEA)

### 1. TINEA CAPITIS (Scalp Ringworm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Age**: Children (rare in adults)
**Organisms**: **T. tonsurans** (US, most common) ⭐, M. canis

**Clinical**:
- **Scaly patches** with alopecia
- **Black dots** ⭐ (broken hair shafts at scalp surface)
- **Kerion** ⭐⭐⭐⭐: Boggy, tender, pustular mass (severe inflammatory response)
  - Risk of permanent scarring alopecia
  - Often from zoophilic organisms
- **Favus**: Honeycomb crusts (T. schoenleinii)

**Diagnosis**:
- **KOH preparation**: Hyphae, arthroconidia
- **Wood's lamp** (UV light): Green fluorescence with M. canis ⭐ (NOT T. tonsurans)
- **Culture**: Gold standard (Sabouraud agar)

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Systemic therapy required** ⭐ (topical ineffective - can't penetrate hair shaft)
- **Griseofulvin 10-20 mg/kg/day × 6-12 weeks** ⭐ (first-line, safe in children)
- Alternatives: Terbinafine, itraconazole, fluconazole
- Kerion: Add **prednisone** (reduce inflammation, prevent scarring)

---

### 2. TINEA CORPORIS (Body Ringworm) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organisms**: T. rubrum (most common), M. canis, T. mentagrophytes

**Clinical**:
- **Annular (ring-shaped) lesion** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ with:
  - Central clearing
  - Raised, scaly, erythematous border
  - Advancing edge
- Pruritic
- Trunk, extremities

**Diagnosis**: KOH, culture

**Treatment**:
- **Topical antifungals** (most cases): ⭐
  - Azoles (clotrimazole, miconazole, ketoconazole)
  - Allylamines (terbinafine, naftifine)
  - BID × 2-4 weeks
- Oral (extensive/resistant): Griseofulvin, terbinafine, itraconazole

---

### 3. TINEA CRURIS (Jock Itch) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organisms**: T. rubrum, E. floccosum

**Clinical**:
- **Inguinal folds**, inner thighs
- Spares scrotum/penis ⭐ (vs candidiasis: involves scrotum)
- Annular, pruritic, erythematous patches
- More common in males, warm/humid climates

**Diagnosis**: KOH

**Treatment**: Topical antifungals × 2-4 weeks

---

### 4. TINEA PEDIS (Athlete's Foot) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organisms**: T. rubrum (most common) ⭐, T. mentagrophytes, E. floccosum

**Clinical Forms**:
- **Interdigital** (most common): ⭐
  - Maceration, scaling, fissuring between toes (especially 4th/5th web space)
  - Pruritus
- **Moccasin**: Chronic, hyperkeratotic soles/heels ("moccasin distribution")
- **Vesiculobullous**: Vesicles/bullae on soles, arches

**Diagnosis**: KOH (hyphae), culture

**Treatment**:
- Topical antifungals × 4 weeks
- Moccasin type: Often requires **oral therapy** (resistant)
- Keep feet dry, good hygiene

---

### 5. TINEA UNGUIUM (Onychomycosis) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organisms**: T. rubrum (most common) ⭐⭐, T. mentagrophytes, Candida (fingernails)

**Clinical**:
- **Thickened, discolored (yellow/white), brittle nails** ⭐
- **Subungual debris**
- **Onycholysis** (separation of nail from bed)
- Toenails > fingernails
- Distal lateral subungual (most common type)

**Diagnosis**:
- **KOH** + **fungal culture** (confirm before treatment) ⭐
- PAS staining of nail clippings

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Systemic therapy required** ⭐ (topical ineffective)
- **Terbinafine 250 mg daily** ⭐:
  - Fingernails: 6 weeks
  - Toenails: 12 weeks
  - Cure rate: 70-80%
- Alternative: Itraconazole pulse therapy (1 week on, 3 weeks off, repeat 3-4 cycles)
- **Monitor LFTs** (hepatotoxicity risk)
- Topical: Ciclopirox, efinaconazole (adjunct or mild cases)

---

### 6. TINEA VERSICOLOR (Pityriasis Versicolor) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organism**: **Malassezia furfur** ⭐ (lipophilic yeast, NOT dermatophyte)

**Clinical**:
- **Hypo- or hyperpigmented macules** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - "Versicolor" = various colors (white, pink, brown)
  - **Hypopigmentation on tan skin** ⭐ (most noticeable)
  - Hyperpigmentation on fair skin
- Fine scale (esp. with scraping)
- Upper trunk, shoulders, neck (sebaceous areas)
- Young adults
- Recurrence common (summer, humid)

**Diagnosis**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **KOH**: **"Spaghetti and meatballs"** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Hyphae ("spaghetti") + yeast cells ("meatballs")
  - Pathognomonic
- Wood's lamp: Yellow-gold fluorescence

**Treatment**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Topical**:
  - **Selenium sulfide 2.5% shampoo** ⭐ (leave on 10 min, daily × 1 week)
  - Ketoconazole shampoo/cream
  - Zinc pyrithione shampoo
- **Oral** (extensive/recurrent):
  - Fluconazole 400 mg single dose OR 200 mg daily × 7 days
  - Itraconazole 200 mg daily × 7 days
- **Note**: Pigment changes persist months after cure (reassure patient)

## OTHER SUPERFICIAL MYCOSES

### TINEA NIGRA ⭐⭐
- **Hortaea werneckii**
- **Brown-black macule on palm/sole** (mimics melanoma)
- KOH: Dematiaceous (pigmented) hyphae
- Treatment: Topical azoles

### PIEDRA ⭐⭐
- **Black piedra**: Piedraia hortae - hard black nodules on hair shafts
- **White piedra**: Trichosporon - soft white nodules on hair
- Treatment: Shave hair, topical azoles

---

## CANDIDA (Cutaneous Infections) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Organism**: **Candida albicans** (most common yeast) ⭐

### Clinical Forms:

**1. ORAL CANDIDIASIS (Thrush)**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **White plaques on tongue, buccal mucosa** that scrape off → erythematous base ⭐
- Infants, dentures, immunocompromised (HIV/AIDS, steroids, antibiotics)
- **Angular cheilitis**: Cracks at mouth corners
- Treatment: **Nystatin oral suspension** or **Fluconazole 100-200 mg daily × 7-14 days**

**2. VULVOVAGINAL CANDIDIASIS**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Thick, white, "cottage cheese" discharge** ⭐
- Intense pruritus, dysuria, dyspareunia
- Erythema, edema of vulva/vagina
- Risk factors: Pregnancy, DM, antibiotics, OCPs
- Treatment:
  - **Topical azoles** (miconazole, clotrimazole) × 1-7 days OR
  - **Fluconazole 150 mg PO single dose** ⭐

**3. DIAPER DERMATITIS**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Beefy red rash with satellite pustules** ⭐
- Involves skin folds (vs irritant: spares folds)
- Treatment: Topical nystatin or azole cream

**4. INTERTRIGO**: ⭐⭐⭐
- Inframammary, axillary, inguinal folds
- Erythema, maceration, satellite pustules
- Obese, diabetic patients
- Treatment: Topical azoles, keep dry

**5. PARONYCHIA**: ⭐⭐⭐
- **Chronic** (Candida) vs Acute (bacteria - S. aureus)
- Painful, swollen nail fold
- Loss of cuticle
- Treatment: Topical/oral azoles, keep hands dry

**Diagnosis**: KOH (budding yeast, pseudohyphae), culture

## DIAGNOSIS OF FUNGAL INFECTIONS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### KOH Preparation: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Scrape scale from advancing edge of lesion
- Place on slide with 10-20% KOH
- Heat gently (dissolves keratin)
- Examine under microscope
- **Dermatophytes**: Septate hyphae, arthroconidia
- **Candida**: Budding yeast + pseudohyphae
- **Malassezia**: "Spaghetti and meatballs"

### Culture:
- **Sabouraud dextrose agar** (SDA) ⭐
- With antibiotics, cycloheximide (inhibits non-dermatophytes)
- Slow growth (1-4 weeks)
- Colony morphology, microscopy (lactophenol cotton blue)

### Wood's Lamp (UV):
- **Microsporum**: Green fluorescence
- Malassezia: Yellow-gold
- Most dermatophytes: No fluorescence

## ANTIFUNGAL AGENTS ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Topical**:
- **Azoles**: Clotrimazole, miconazole, ketoconazole, econazole
- **Allylamines**: Terbinafine, naftifine (more fungicidal)
- **Polyenes**: Nystatin (Candida only, not dermato

phytes)
- **Others**: Ciclopirox, tolnaftate, selenium sulfide

**Oral**:
- **Griseofulvin**: ⭐
  - Fungistatic (disrupts microtubules)
  - **Dermatophytes only** (NOT Candida, NOT Malassezia)
  - Requires prolonged therapy (weeks-months)
  - Well tolerated in children
  - Side effects: Headache, GI upset, photosensitivity
  - Take with fatty meal (↑ absorption)
- **Terbinafine**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Fungicidal (inhibits squalene epoxidase)
  - **First choice for onychomycosis**
  - Dermatophytes > yeast
  - Monitor LFTs
- **Azoles**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
  - Fluconazole, itraconazole, ketoconazole
  - Inhibit ergosterol synthesis
  - Broad spectrum (dermatophytes + yeasts)
  - Drug interactions (CYP450)
  - Monitor LFTs

```
Condition         | Organism          | Key Feature           | Treatment
------------------|-------------------|-----------------------|------------------
Tinea capitis     | T. tonsurans      | Black dots, kerion    | Oral griseofulvin
Tinea corporis    | T. rubrum         | Annular, central clear| Topical azoles
Tinea cruris      | T. rubrum         | Spares scrotum        | Topical azoles
Tinea pedis       | T. rubrum         | Web spaces, moccasin  | Topical/oral
Onychomycosis     | T. rubrum         | Thick, yellow nails   | Oral terbinafine
Tinea versicolor  | Malassezia        | Spaghetti & meatballs | Selenium sulfide
Oral thrush       | C. albicans       | White plaques, scrape | Nystatin/fluconazole
```

## HIGH-YIELD MNEMONICS

**Tinea Capitis Treatment**: "**C**apitis needs **C**entral (oral) therapy" - Systemic required

**Tinea Cruris Spares**: "**C**ruris **C**annot touch private parts" - Spares scrotum

**Malassezia**: "**S**paghetti and **M**eatballs" → **S** = spaghetti (hyphae), **M** = Malassezia

**Terbinafine Duration**: "**6** fingers, **12** toes" - 6 weeks (fingernails), 12 weeks (toenails)

## NEET PG MCQS

**Q1.** KOH preparation shows "spaghetti and meatballs". Diagnosis?
A) Tinea corporis B) Tinea versicolor C) Candidiasis D) Tinea capitis

**Answer: B** - Tinea versicolor (Malassezia): Hyphae (spaghetti) + yeast (meatballs) pathognomonic

**Q2.** Treatment of choice for onychomycosis?
A) Topical antifungals B) Oral terbinafine C) Oral nystatin D) Griseofulvin

**Answer: B** - Oral terbinafine (12 weeks for toenails) - topical ineffective

**Q3.** Which tinea infection requires systemic therapy?
A) Tinea corporis B) Tinea cruris C) Tinea capitis D) Tinea pedis

**Answer: C** - Tinea capitis requires oral therapy (topical can't penetrate hair shaft)
